# Footings, Posts & Connections

Source: 2025 California Residential Code (Title 24, Part 2.5), Sections R403 (Footings), R507.4 (Posts), Appendix H (Patio Covers).

---

## Footing Requirements

### Minimum Footing Dimensions (CRC R403 + Appendix H)

Patio cover footing sizes are governed by Appendix H prescriptive tables, which are based on the tributary area each footing supports.

#### Open (Lattice) Patio Covers — 10 psf Live Load

| Footing Size | Minimum Depth | Max Supported Shaded Area |
|---|---|---|
| 12" x 12" | 12" | 100 sq ft |
| 18" x 18" | 12" | 220 sq ft |

#### Solid Roof Patio Covers — 20 psf Live Load

| Footing Size | Minimum Depth | Max Supported Roof Area |
|---|---|---|
| 12" x 12" | 12" | 70 sq ft |
| 18" x 18" | 12" | 150 sq ft |

> Footings may be **circular** with equivalent cross-sectional area (e.g., 14" diameter circular is approximately equivalent to 12" x 12" square).

### Footing General Rules (CRC R403.1)

| Requirement | Standard |
|---|---|
| Minimum depth below undisturbed ground | 12 inches |
| Concrete compressive strength | 3,000 psi minimum at 28 days |
| Bottom of footing | On undisturbed soil or engineered fill |
| Soil bearing capacity (assumed) | 1,500 psf (unless soil report provides higher value) |
| Reinforcement (18" footings) | Minimum one #4 rebar each direction |
| Frost protection | Not typically required in Southern California (12" depth is sufficient) |

### Footing-to-Post Connection

The post must be connected to the footing with an **approved post base** (e.g., Simpson ABU44, ABA44, CB44, PBS44) that provides:

| Property | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Standoff | Minimum **1 inch** above concrete surface to prevent moisture wicking |
| Attachment to concrete | Minimum one 1/2" anchor bolt or approved concrete anchor |
| Attachment to post | Per manufacturer's specifications (typically 10d nails or SDS screws) |
| Uplift resistance | Per manufacturer's published allowable loads |

> **Critical:** Posts shall NOT be set directly into concrete without an approved post base. Direct embedment traps moisture and accelerates decay. The 1-inch standoff allows water to drain and air to circulate beneath the post.

### Patio Slab Requirements

| Requirement | Standard |
|---|---|
| Slab thickness | 3-1/2 inches minimum |
| Slope | Sloped away from house (min 1/4" per foot / 2% grade) |
| Clearance | 2 inches minimum between weep screed and slab surface |

## Post Requirements

### Minimum Post Sizes

| Cover Type | Minimum Post Size | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Open (lattice) | 4x4 nominal | Adequate for most residential spans |
| Solid roof | 4x6 nominal minimum | 4x4 may be used only with engineering for small covers |

### Post Height and Clearance

| Dimension | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Minimum clearance under beam | 6'-8" from finished floor/slab to bottom of beam |
| Maximum post height (prescriptive) | 12 feet (per Appendix H one-story limit) |
| Typical post height | 7'-0" to 9'-0" (depends on beam depth and desired clearance) |

### Post Material Requirements

| Property | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Species/grade | Douglas Fir-Larch No. 2 or better |
| Treatment | Pressure-preservative-treated per AWPA U1 where within 6" of ground, or where exposed to weather without roof protection |
| Quality mark | Treatment plant, preservative type, retention, end use, standard, and inspection agency |
| Field cuts | All field-cut ends treated per AWPA M4 |

## Post-to-Beam Connections

The post-to-beam connection is a critical structural joint. Two approved methods:

### Method 1: Approved Post Cap (Preferred)

Use an approved post cap connector (e.g., Simpson CC44, BC4, LPC4, AC4):

| Property | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Hardware | ICC-approved post cap rated for the applied loads |
| Fasteners | Per manufacturer's specifications (all fastener holes filled) |
| Bearing | Beam bears fully on top of post |
| Lateral restraint | Post cap provides lateral restraint against beam sliding off post |

### Method 2: Steel Straps

| Property | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Strap material | 12 gauge (minimum) 1-1/2" x 12" steel "T" or "L" straps |
| Attachment | Each side with 1/2" diameter machine bolts |
| Quantity | One strap each side of the connection |

> **Note:** Toenailing alone is NOT an acceptable post-to-beam connection. A positive mechanical connection (post cap, strap, or through-bolt) is required.

## Knee Braces

Knee braces provide lateral stability and resist racking forces at the post-to-beam connection. They are required at every post unless the connection has been engineered for lateral loads.

### Knee Brace Requirements

| Property | Open (Lattice) | Solid Roof |
|---|---|---|
| Minimum size | 4x4 | 4x6 |
| Connection | 1/2" x 6" lag bolts at each end (lattice) or 5/8" x 8" lag bolts (solid) |
| Installation | Lag bolts installed with standard washers into 3/8" (lattice) or 7/16" (solid) pre-drilled holes |
| Bolt heads | Counter-sunk |
| Maximum area per knee brace | 60 sq ft (open) / 35 sq ft light, 22 sq ft heavy (solid) |
| Through-bolt option | 5/8" through-bolt when knee brace occurs at intermediate post on both sides |

### Knee Brace Tributary Area

Each knee brace supports a tributary area of roof. If the actual tributary area exceeds the maximum per knee brace, additional posts and knee braces are required, or engineering is needed.

| Cover Type / Assembly | Max Area per Knee Brace |
|---|---|
| Open (lattice) | 60 sq ft |
| Solid roof — light assembly | 35 sq ft |
| Solid roof — heavy assembly | 22 sq ft |

## Beam Overhang Limits

| Dimension | Limit |
|---|---|
| Maximum beam overhang (cantilever past post) | 1/3 of the adjacent beam span, with 48" maximum |
| Minimum distance from beam end to property line | 3 feet |

## Common Review Findings

1. **Missing post bases** — Posts set directly on concrete or into dirt without approved post bases. Requires approved connector with minimum 1-inch standoff.
2. **Undersized footings for solid roof** — A 12" x 12" footing supports only 70 sq ft of solid roof area. Larger covers need 18" x 18" footings or engineering.
3. **No knee braces shown** — Knee braces are required at every post. Plans that omit them are a FAIL.
4. **Knee brace tributary area exceeded** — For solid heavy covers, each knee brace supports only 22 sq ft. A 10' x 20' solid tile-roof cover (200 sq ft) needs at least 10 knee braces (i.e., many posts), or engineering.
5. **Insufficient post size for solid roof** — 4x4 posts are generally inadequate for solid-roof patio covers. Minimum 4x6 unless engineering proves otherwise.
6. **Patio slab sloping toward house** — Slab must slope away from house. Water ponding against the foundation causes damage. Verify 2" minimum clearance between weep screed and slab.
