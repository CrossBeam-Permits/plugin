| # | Check | Severity | What to verify | Code |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Charger amperage valid?** | ❌ **fail** | Standard breaker size, individual branch circuit, continuous duty (125% rule) | CEC 625.40, 625.41, 625.42, 240.6(A) |
| 2 | **Wire sizing correct?** | ❌ **fail** | Conductor matches amperage per Table 310.16 (75°C column for THHN) | CEC Table 310.16 |
| 3 | **Equipment grounding conductor (EGC) correct?** | ❌ **fail** | EGC sized per Table 250.122 based on breaker rating (21-60A = #10 Cu, 61-100A = #8 Cu) | CEC Table 250.122 |
| 4 | **Conduit sizing correct?** | ❌ **fail** | Conduit type and size appropriate for number/size of conductors + EGC | CEC Chapter 9 Tables |
| 5 | **Load calc arithmetic correct?** | ❌ **fail** | **CRITICAL: Do NOT trust the applicant's stated Total General Load.** Independently extract every checked line item and its wattage, sum them yourself, and compare to the stated total. Flag any discrepancy. Then verify the service load formula (first 10,000W at 100% + remainder at 40% for city worksheets, or first 8,000 VA for CEC default). See detailed sub-steps below. | CEC 220.83(A), 230.42 |
| 6 | **Service panel has capacity?** | ❌ **fail** | Total service load (from verified calc) vs. existing panel size per sizing table | CEC 220.83(A), 230.42 |
| 7 | **Equipment location compliant?** | ❌ **fail** | Coupling means height: indoor min 18" above floor, outdoor min 24" above grade (CEC 625.50). Maximum coupling height 48" above floor/grade per accessibility requirements (ADA / CBC Chapter 11B). | CEC 625.50, CBC 11B |
| 8 | **Working space adequate?** | ⚠️ **flag** | Min. 30" wide x 36" deep x 6'6" high clear space around EVSE and panel; no storage in working space | CEC 110.26 |
| 9 | **GFCI protection?** | ❌ **fail** | Required for garage and outdoor receptacles; all EV receptacles per 625.54 | CEC 210.8(A), 625.54 |
| 10 | **Disconnect means (if >60A)?** | ❌ **fail** | Lockable disconnect in readily accessible location | CEC 625.43 |
| 11 | **Ventilation?** | ⚠️ **flag** | Indoor: vehicle batteries must be listed for indoor charging without ventilation, OR mechanical ventilation required | CEC 625.52 |
| 12 | **Equipment listed?** | ❌ **fail** | All EVSE must be listed by NRTL (UL, etc.) | CEC 625.6 |
| 13 | **Weatherproof enclosure (if outdoor)?** | ❌ **fail** | Wet-location receptacles need weatherproof enclosure with extra-duty hood | CEC 625.56 |
| 1 | **SCE utility coordination** `[CMC]` | ⚠️ **flag** | Coachella is in **Southern California Edison (SCE)** service territory. Any panel upgrade or new service entrance needed to support EV charging requires SCE coordination before final inspection. For Level 2 EVSE where existing panel capacity is adequate, SCE coordination is typically minimal, but verify if a load letter or service agreement is required for larger installs. DCFC (commercial Level 3) always requires SCE utility coordination and may require transformer upgrade. Contact SCE at sce.com. | 2025 CEC; SCE service territory |
| 2 | **Extreme heat — high-temperature rated equipment** `[CMC]` | ⚠️ **flag** | Coachella Valley's extreme summer ambient temperatures — outdoor temps regularly exceed 115°F and equipment surfaces in direct sun can reach 140°F+. **EVSE installed outdoors or in unconditioned garages must be rated for high-temperature operation.** Verify: (1) EVSE operating temperature ceiling exceeds expected site ambient; (2) conduit and wire insulation are rated for the installation location (conduit in direct sun — use THHN/THWN-2 rated conductors; aluminum conduit not preferred for direct-sun desert runs due to thermal expansion); (3) outdoor EVSE has NEMA 3R or better enclosure rating. Flag equipment with insufficient temperature ratings as a critical deficiency. | 2025 CEC 110.3(B); CEC 310.15(B) |
| 3 | **Panel capacity check — aging housing stock** `[CMC]` | ⚠️ **flag** | Coachella has significant older residential housing stock, some with 100A or smaller panels. **EV charging demand (typically 40A continuous for a 50A branch circuit) added to existing high AC loads will frequently exceed panel capacity.** Verify: (1) existing panel rating and available capacity; (2) load calculation per CEC 220.83 accounts for existing loads including HVAC; (3) if panel upgrade is needed, it must be permitted separately or concurrently. Do not approve an EV charger installation where the load calculation shows panel overload. | 2025 CEC 220.83 |
| 4 | **DCFC (Level 3) — additional coordination required** `[CMC]` | ⚠️ **flag** | Commercial or Level 3 DCFC installations require: (1) electrical permit with full plan check (not AB 1236 streamlined track); (2) SCE utility coordination for demand capacity and potentially transformer upgrade; (3) possible fire plan check through Coachella Fire Marshal.
