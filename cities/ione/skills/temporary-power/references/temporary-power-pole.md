# Temporary Power Pole Requirements

Source: 2025 California Electrical Code (Title 24, Part 3), California Fire Code (Title 24, Part 9).

---

## Scope

Temporary power poles are utility-fed temporary service installations used during construction. They receive power from the utility (e.g., SCE, PG&E, LADWP) and distribute it to the construction site through panelboards, receptacles, and temporary wiring.

---

## Service Installation (CEC 590.4(A), Article 230)

Temporary power pole services shall comply with **Article 230 Parts I through VIII**. Key requirements include:

### Service Entrance Conductors

- Service conductors sized per utility and load requirements
- Service entrance conductor installation per Article 230
- Overhead service drops must maintain required clearances from buildings, roadways, and other structures per **CEC 230.24** and utility company standards

### Service Disconnect

- Service disconnecting means required per **CEC 230.70**
- Located at a readily accessible location nearest the point of entrance of service conductors
- Disconnect shall be **suitable for use as service equipment** and marked as such

### Overcurrent Protection

- Service overcurrent protection per **CEC 230.90**
- For solidly grounded wye services >150V to ground, available fault current >10,000A, OCPDs shall be **current limiting** (CEC 590.8(B))

---

## Fire Code Requirements for Temporary Power Poles (CFC 605.10)

### Installation (CFC 605.10.1)

- Shall be installed by a **qualified electrician** in accordance with the CEC and local utility requirements
- Poles shall be **securely anchored and braced** to withstand environmental and operational conditions

### Clearances (CFC 605.10.2)

- Maintain required clearances from buildings, roadways, overhead lines, and other structures
- Minimum clearance per CEC and utility company standards

### Overcurrent Protection (CFC 605.10.3)

- Equipped with OCPDs (circuit breakers or fuses) to protect against overloads
- A **disconnect switch** shall be installed for emergency shut-off

### Grounding (CFC 605.10.4)

- Grounded in accordance with CEC standards
- Grounding conductors and electrodes properly installed and **tested for continuity and resistance**

## Grounding for Temporary Power Poles

Since a temporary power pole is a service entrance, full grounding electrode system requirements apply:

### Service Panel Grounding (CEC 250.24)

- Neutral, EGCs, and enclosure shall be **bonded** in service panels (250.24(B))
- GEC connects service neutral to grounding electrode system (250.24(D))
- GEC connects at any accessible point from load end of service drop to neutral bus in service disconnect (250.24(A)(1))

### Grounding Electrode System (CEC 250.50)

- All available electrodes present at the site shall be bonded together
- Minimum one electrode required; types per 250.52(A)(4) through (A)(8) if none exist

### Typical Power Pole Grounding

For a temporary power pole on a construction site, the typical grounding electrode is a **driven ground rod**:
- Copper-clad, minimum 5/8 inch diameter (CEC 250.52(A)(5))
- Minimum 8 ft in contact with soil (CEC 250.52(A)(5))
- Driven vertically, fully below grade (CEC 250.53(A)(4))
- If resistance exceeds 25 ohms, a **supplemental electrode** is required (CEC 250.53(A)(2))
- Supplemental rod: minimum 6 ft from first rod (16 ft preferred) (CEC 250.53(A)(3))

### GEC Sizing

- GEC sized per Table 250.66 based on service conductor size
- For rod/pipe/plate electrode only: maximum GEC size required is **#6 Cu or #4 Al** (CEC 250.66(A))

## Site Plan Requirements for Temporary Power Poles

A site plan shall show:

1. **Property lines** with dimensions (lot width x depth)
2. **All buildings** on the property (house, above-grade structures, patio covers, trash enclosures, etc.)
3. **Pools, spas**, and decorative water features
4. **Easements and setbacks** from ultimate property lines and distances between buildings/structures
5. **Driveways**
6. **Streets** with street names and sidewalks adjacent to property
7. **North arrow** orientation
8. **Fence locations** with heights and type (wood, vinyl, block wall)
9. **Location of temporary power pole** with distance from property lines and buildings
10. **Run of conduit** and whether underground or exposed
11. **Main electrical room** and/or point of entry to building

---

## Electrical Plan Requirements

### Single Line Diagram

Shall show at minimum:
- Types of feeder/wiring methods (conductor gauge, type, material, insulation, conduit)
- Equipment voltages, amps, VA/watts, and AIC ratings
- Grounding electrode and conductor types, amounts, and sizes
- Circuit breakers and/or fuses
- Transformers (if applicable)
- Panelboard
- Disconnects

### Load Calculations

- Based on **Article 220** for loads to be supplied
- Include adjustments for continuous loads and largest motor load
- Demonstrate generator/service size is sufficient for the load

### Voltage and Phase Configuration

- Specify ratings: voltage, ampacity, number of phases, wire configuration
- Format example: 240 volt, single phase, 3 wire

---

## Maintenance and Inspection (CFC 605.11)

- Temporary power poles shall be **regularly inspected and tested** for safe operating condition
- Inspections documented; deficiencies corrected immediately
- Signage warning of electrical hazards, emergency shutdown procedures, and responsible personnel contact information required (CFC 605.11.2)

---

## Removal

- Temporary wiring shall be **removed immediately** upon completion of construction or purpose (CEC 590.3(D))
- Temporary power poles and wiring removed when no longer required (CFC 605.12.2)
