# Electrical Requirements for HVAC

Source: 2025 California Electrical Code (Title 24, Part 3), Articles 422 and 440; 2025 California Mechanical Code (Title 24, Part 4), Section 301.4.

## Disconnect Means

### General HVAC Disconnect (CMC 301.4)

Equipment requiring electrical connections of more than 50 volts shall have a **positive means of disconnect adjacent to and in sight from the equipment served**.

**Exception**: Other disconnect means acceptable where in accordance with the CEC.

### A/C and Heat Pump Disconnect (CEC 440.14)

A disconnecting means shall be located **within sight from** and readily accessible from the air-conditioning or refrigerating equipment. The disconnect shall be on the supply side.

"Within sight" = visible and not more than 50 ft from the equipment.

### Furnace Disconnect (CEC 422.31)

A means shall be provided to disconnect the furnace from the power source for servicing. See manufacturer's installation instructions for the approved disconnecting method.

### Appliance Branch Circuit (CEC 422.10)

Furnaces shall be on a **separate dedicated branch circuit**.

> Note: This is consistent with the California amendment requiring dedicated circuits for furnaces.

---

## Service Receptacle (CMC 301.4(2))

A **120V receptacle** shall be located within **25 ft** of the HVAC equipment for service and maintenance purposes.

- The receptacle shall be on the **supply side** of the disconnect switch
- The receptacle need not be on the same level as the equipment

### GFCI Protection

The service receptacle shall be GFCI-protected where required by CEC 210.8 (outdoors, garages, crawlspaces).

---

## Wire Sizing (CEC Table 310.16)

Conductor sizing for HVAC equipment shall be based on the equipment nameplate or manufacturer's data, using CEC Table 310.16 (formerly Table 310.15(B)(16)).

### Breaker Identification (CEC 110.22)

The circuit breaker serving the HVAC equipment shall be clearly **identified** at the electrical panel, indicating the equipment it serves.

---

## A/C Equipment Specific (CEC Article 440)

### Nameplate Requirements (CEC 440.4)

A/C equipment nameplates must show:
- Rated-load current or branch-circuit selection current
- Maximum fuse or circuit breaker size
- Minimum circuit ampacity

### Branch Circuit Sizing

The branch circuit for A/C equipment shall have an ampacity of not less than **125% of the rated-load current** or branch-circuit selection current (whichever is greater).

### Overcurrent Protection (CEC 440.22)

The overcurrent protective device shall not exceed the **maximum** size shown on the equipment nameplate.

### Short-Circuit Ground Fault Protection (CEC 440.22(B))

Where the equipment nameplate specifies "Maximum Fuse Size," only fuses of that type and size may be used (not circuit breakers), unless the nameplate also permits circuit breakers.

---

## Attic Wiring Protection (CEC 334.23, 320.23)

All electrical wiring within **6 ft** of the attic access scuttle (or within **7 ft** if accessible by permanent stairs or ladders) must be:
- Secured and protected by a **guard strip** to prevent damage to the cable
- This applies to NM (Romex) and AC cable types

---

## Working Space (CEC 110.26)

Minimum working space around all electrical equipment (panels, disconnects):
- **30 in. wide** x **36 in. deep** x **6 ft 6 in. high**
- No storage allowed in the working space
