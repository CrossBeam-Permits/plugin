# Ledger Attachment for Attached Patio Covers

Source: 2025 California Residential Code (Title 24, Part 2.5), Section R507.9 (Ledger Connections), Appendix H (Patio Covers).

> **Cross-reference:** This file covers ledger attachment specific to patio covers. The full ledger connection details (fastener spacing tables, placement dimensions, lateral hold-down requirements) are documented in the deck-patio-repair skill's `ledger-connections.md`. The same CRC R507.9 requirements apply to both decks and attached patio covers. This file focuses on the patio-cover-specific details and differences.

---

## When Is a Ledger Required?

| Configuration | Ledger Required? |
|---|---|
| **Attached patio cover** — rafters bear on house wall | Yes — ledger board mounted to house |
| **Freestanding patio cover** — independent posts on all sides | No — ledger not needed |

An attached patio cover transfers its vertical and lateral loads to the house structure through the ledger. This connection is **life-safety critical** — the same failure modes that cause deck collapses apply to attached patio covers.

---

## Ledger Board Requirements

### Ledger Sizing

| Property | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Minimum width | 2x nominal (1-1/2" actual) |
| Depth | Matches rafter depth (e.g., 2x6 ledger for 2x6 rafters, 2x8 for 2x8 rafters) |
| Material | Continuous along full length of patio cover |
| Grade | Douglas Fir-Larch No. 2 or better |
| Treatment | Pressure-preservative-treated or naturally durable wood |

### Ledger Installation

The ledger must be attached directly to the house band joist or structural framing. The stucco or siding must be cut back to expose the structural sheathing and framing.

| Step | Detail |
|---|---|
| 1. Remove wall covering | Cut stucco/siding to expose band joist or structural framing |
| 2. Verify framing | Confirm 2x band joist (solid sawn) or engineered rim board is present |
| 3. Install ledger | Level, with lag screws or through-bolts per spacing table |
| 4. Install flashing | Above ledger per R507.9.1.5 |
| 5. Seal penetrations | Caulk all lag screw/bolt penetrations |

---

## Fastener Requirements

### Lag Screw Method (Most Common for Patio Covers)

| Property | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Lag screw diameter | 1/2 inch |
| Lag screw length | 6 inches minimum (must fully penetrate through ledger, sheathing, and into band joist) |
| Spacing | Per Table R507.9.1.3(1) — based on rafter span and sheathing thickness |
| Pre-drill (through ledger) | 1/2" diameter |
| Pre-drill (into band joist) | 5/16" to 3/8" diameter |
| Washers | Standard washers required |
| Pattern | Staggered top-to-bottom along horizontal run |
| Verification | Lag bolts must go into 2x wood framing (verify on site) |

### Lag Screw Spacing (Table R507.9.1.3(1) — 1/2" Lag Screw, 1/2" Max Sheathing)

These spacings are from the deck code but apply equally to patio cover ledgers. Use the rafter span (equivalent to joist span) to determine spacing.

| Rafter Span | Max Lag Screw Spacing |
|---|---|
| 6 ft | 30" OC |
| 8 ft | 23" OC |
| 10 ft | 18" OC |
| 12 ft | 15" OC |
| 14 ft | 13" OC |
| 16 ft | 11" OC |
| 18 ft | 10" OC |

### Through-Bolt Method (Stronger Alternative)

| Property | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Bolt diameter | 1/2 inch per ASTM A307 |
| Nuts | ASTM A563 |
| Washers | ASTM F844 on both sides |
| Spacing | Per Table R507.9.1.3(1) — through-bolt columns |

Through-bolts are stronger and preferred where access to the interior side of the band joist is available (e.g., unfinished garage wall behind the ledger).

### Fastener Placement (Table R507.9.1.3(2))

| Location | Ledger | Band Joist |
|---|---|---|
| Top edge distance | 2" minimum | 3/4" minimum |
| Bottom edge distance | 3/4" minimum | 2" minimum |
| End distance | 2" minimum (5" maximum) | 2" minimum |
| Row spacing | 1-5/8" minimum (5" maximum) | 1-5/8" minimum (5" maximum) |

## Flashing Requirements (R507.9.1.5)

Flashing above the ledger is **mandatory** to prevent water from entering the wall cavity behind the ledger. This is the same requirement as for deck ledgers.

| Property | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Vertical extension | Minimum 2 inches above top of ledger |
| Horizontal extension | Minimum 4 inches beyond ledger face, OR to ledger face plus 1/4" drip |
| Material | Corrosion-resistant metal min 0.019" thick, OR approved non-metallic |
| Caulking | Flashing and caulking bead at top edge where it meets wall |

### Water-Resistive Barrier (WRB) Integration

- WRB must lap over the vertical leg of the flashing by min 2 inches
- For existing walls without WRB: install WRB behind ledger and flashing with self-adhered counterflashing (R507.9.1.7)

### Exception to Flashing

Flashing is NOT required where the ledger is spaced horizontally from the exterior wall by at least **1/4 inch** to allow drainage and ventilation.

## Solid Roof — Additional Ledger Details

For solid-roof patio covers, the ledger also supports roof sheathing and roofing materials, creating additional water management concerns.

| Component | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Boundary nails | 6" OC from roof sheathing to 2x blocking at ledger |
| Roof covering transition | Approved roof covering laps over flashing at wall junction |
| Drip edge | At eave (bottom of slope, away from house) |
| Step/counter flashing | Where solid roof meets house wall at an angle |

## Lateral Load Connection

Attached patio covers must transfer lateral loads to the house structure, just like attached decks. The same hold-down requirements from CRC R507.9.2 apply:

### Option 1: Two Devices

- Minimum 2 hold-down tension devices per patio cover
- Located within 24 inches of each end
- Each device: ASD capacity >= 1,500 pounds

### Option 2: Four Devices

- Minimum 4 hold-down tension devices
- Each device: ASD capacity >= 750 pounds

> **Critical rule:** Lateral loads must be transferred to the ground or to a structure capable of transmitting them to the ground. Nails alone (including toenails) are NOT acceptable for lateral connections (CRC R318.5).

## Attachment to Different Wall Types

| Wall Type | Ledger Attachment Method |
|---|---|
| Wood frame with wood sheathing | Lag screws or through-bolts per Table R507.9.1.3(1) |
| Wood frame with stucco over sheathing | Remove stucco at ledger location, fasten to framing; reinstall stucco with flashing |
| Concrete/masonry wall | Expansion anchors or epoxy anchors into solid concrete/masonry; engineering typically required |
| Steel frame | Engineering required — prescriptive tables do not cover steel framing |
| Brick/stone veneer over wood frame | Remove veneer at ledger, fasten to wood framing behind. Ledger must NOT bear on veneer (R507.9.1.1) |

---

## Freestanding Patio Covers — No Ledger

Freestanding patio covers do not attach to the house and do not require a ledger. Instead:

- Posts on all sides support beams
- Rafters span between two beams (or a beam and a header beam)
- Lateral stability provided by knee braces at every post
- Independent footings on all sides

> **Advantage of freestanding:** Avoids all ledger flashing, WRB, and wall penetration issues. Recommended when the house wall condition is unknown, when the band joist cannot be verified, or when the homeowner wants to avoid modifying the wall.

## Common Review Findings

1. **Lag screws too far apart** — For a patio cover with 12-foot rafter spans, lag screws must be at 15" OC maximum. Plans showing 24" or 36" spacing are a FAIL.
2. **Lag screws into stucco only** — Lag screws must penetrate into the 2x wood framing, not just stucco and sheathing. Verify on site.
3. **Missing flashing** — Flashing above the ledger is required. Without it, water enters the wall cavity and rots the band joist — the same band joist the ledger depends on for support.
4. **No lateral hold-down devices** — Even with proper lag screws for vertical loads, lateral hold-down devices (e.g., Simpson DTT2Z) are separately required.
5. **Ledger on masonry veneer** — Brick or stone veneer is NOT structural. The ledger must be fastened through the veneer to the structural framing behind it. If not possible, the patio cover must be freestanding.
6. **Existing wall with no WRB** — Older homes may lack a water-resistive barrier. R507.9.1.7 requires installing WRB behind the ledger. FLAG if the scope of work does not address this.
7. **Undersized ledger** — The ledger depth must match the rafter depth. A 2x4 ledger supporting 2x6 rafters is incorrect.
