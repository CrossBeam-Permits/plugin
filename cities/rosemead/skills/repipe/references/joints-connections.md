# CPC Section 605 — Joints and Connections

Source: 2025 California Plumbing Code (Title 24, Part 5), Chapter 6.

---

## Soldered Joints — Copper (605.1.4)

| Requirement | Detail | Code Section |
|-------------|--------|-------------|
| **Standard** | Solder joints shall meet ASTM B828 | CPC 605.1.4 |
| **Lead content** | Solder and flux shall NOT have more than 0.2% lead content (potable water) | CPC 605.1.4 |
| **Flux standard** | Flux shall meet ASTM B813 and shall be rated for potable water | CPC 605.1.4 |
| **Preparation** | Pipe cut square, reamed to full inside diameter, surfaces cleaned bright | CPC 605.1.4 |

---

## Press-Connect Fittings — Copper (605.1.3.2)

Press-connect fittings for copper or copper alloy pipe or tubing shall have an elastomeric o-ring that forms the joint. The pipe or tubing shall be fully inserted into the fitting, marked at the shoulder, cut square, chamfered, and reamed to full inside diameter. The joint shall be pressed using the tool recommended by the manufacturer.

---

## Push Fit Fittings (605.9.3, 605.1.3.3, 605.2.1.1)

Removable and nonremovable push fit fittings that employ a quick assembly push fit connector shall comply with **ASSE 1061**.

---

## CPVC Joints (605.2.2)

| Requirement | Detail | Code Section |
|-------------|--------|-------------|
| **Solvent cement** | Per ASTM F493, with manufacturer's instructions | CPC 605.2.2 |
| **Primer color** | Orange (where primer is required) | CPC 605.2.2 |
| **One-step cement color** | Yellow, green, or red (when no primer is required) | CPC 605.2.2 |
| **Female CPVC threads** | Shall be used with plastic male threads ONLY | CPC 605.2.3 |

---

## PVC Joints (605.12)

| Requirement | Detail | Code Section |
|-------------|--------|-------------|
| **UV exposure** | PVC shall NOT be exposed to direct sunlight | CPC 605.12 |
| **UV exception** | Max 24 in. length, wrapped with 0.04 in. thick UV resistant tape or other UV protection | CPC 605.12 |
| **Threaded PVC** | Minimum Schedule 80 | CPC 605.12.3 |
| **Female PVC threads** | Shall be used with plastic male threads ONLY | CPC 605.12.3 |

---

## Dissimilar Material Connections (605.16, 605.15, 310.6)

### Copper to Galvanized/Threaded Pipe (605.16.1)

Joints from copper or copper alloy pipe to threaded pipe shall use:
- Copper alloy adapter
- Copper alloy nipple (minimum 6 in.)
- Dielectric fitting or dielectric union (per ASSE 1079 or IAPMO PS 66)

### Plastic to Other Materials (605.16.2)

Where connecting plastic pipe to other types of piping, approved types of adapter or transition fittings designed for the specific transition intended shall be used.

### Dielectric Protection (605.15)

Dielectric unions installed where there is a dissimilarity of metals shall be in accordance with ASSE 1079 or IAPMO PS 66.

> For HCD 1 & HCD 2 (residential): Dielectric unions SHALL be used at ALL points of connection where there is a dissimilarity of metals.

### Accessible Locations Only (310.6)

Except for necessary valves, where intermixing of dissimilar metals occurs, the point of connection shall be confined to **exposed or accessible locations**.

---

## Slip Joints (605.14)

In water piping, slip joints shall be permitted ONLY on the **exposed fixture supply**.

---

## Copper Joints Under Concrete Slab (609.3)

Copper pipe joints in or under concrete slab on grade within building shall be installed with **brazed wrought-copper fittings** — soldered joints are NOT permitted under slab.

## Quick Reference — Joint Method by Material

| Material | Primary Joint Methods | Key Rules |
|----------|----------------------|-----------|
| **Copper** | Solder (ASTM B828), Braze, Press-connect, Push-fit (ASSE 1061), Flare | Under-slab = braze only; solder lead max 0.2% |
| **CPVC** | Solvent cement (ASTM F493), Push-fit (ASSE 1061) | Primer orange; one-step yellow/green/red; female threads = plastic male only |
| **PEX** | Crimp, Expansion, Push-fit (ASSE 1061) | Per manufacturer; brass fittings must meet NSF 14 for dezincification |
| **PVC** | Solvent cement (ASTM D2564), Threaded (Sch 80 min) | No direct sunlight; female threads = plastic male only |
| **Galvanized Steel** | Threaded (ASME B1.20.1), Mechanical | Thread sealant on male threads only |
