# Emergency Egress and Window Requirements

> Source: 2025 California Residential Code (Title 24, Part 2.5), 2025 California Building Code (Title 24, Part 2)
> Sections: CRC R319.1-R319.7, R321.2, R325.1-R325.2; CBC 1031.2-1031.5

---

## Overview

Emergency escape and rescue openings (EERO) are required in sleeping rooms and basements. When replacing windows that serve as egress openings, the replacement must maintain adequate escape capability. The CRC provides a practical exception for replacement windows that cannot meet full new-construction egress dimensions.

---

## Where Emergency Egress Is Required (R319.1)

Emergency escape and rescue openings are required in:
- Every **sleeping room** (bedroom)
- **Basements** (at least one per basement)
- **Habitable attics**
- The room to which a **sleeping loft** is open
- Where basements contain sleeping rooms: one EERO per sleeping room

Openings must open directly into a public way, or to a yard or court that opens to a public way.

**Exceptions:**
1. Basements with ceiling height <80" -- no EERO required
2. Sleeping rooms/basements with exit door opening directly to public way, yard, or court
3. Basements without habitable spaces and <=200 SF -- no EERO required
4. Storm shelters per ICC 500
5. Sprinklered dwelling: sleeping rooms in basements may substitute one egress + one EERO, or two means of egress

---

## Minimum EERO Dimensions -- New Construction (R319.2)

| Requirement | Value |
|---|---|
| **Minimum net clear opening area** | 5.7 SF |
| **Minimum net clear opening area (grade floor)** | 5.0 SF |
| **Minimum net clear height** | 24 inches |
| **Minimum net clear width** | 20 inches |
| **Maximum sill height above floor** | 44 inches |

The net clear opening dimensions must result from **normal operation** of the opening (no tools, keys, or special knowledge required).

---

## Replacement Windows for Egress Openings -- THE CRITICAL EXCEPTION (R319.5)

**This is the most commonly applied section for window replacement permits.**

Replacement windows installed in buildings within CRC scope are **exempt from Sections R319.2 (minimum size) and R319.4.4 (bar/grille requirements)** provided BOTH conditions are met:

1. The replacement window is the **manufacturer's largest standard size window** that will fit within the existing frame or existing rough opening
2. The replacement window is **not part of a change of occupancy**

The replacement window may be:
- The same operating style as the existing window, OR
- A style that provides an **equal or greater window opening area** than the existing window

**Practical impact:** A 1950s bedroom window that only provides 4.5 SF of clear opening (below the 5.7 SF minimum) can be replaced with a new window -- even if the new window also doesn't meet 5.7 SF -- as long as the new window is the largest standard size the manufacturer makes that fits the existing opening.

### Window Opening Control Device Height (R319.5.1)
When a window opening control device or fall protection device is installed on a replacement EERO window, it must be located per R319.1.1 or as low as the device can be installed within the existing clear opening.

---

## Operational Requirements (R319.1.1)

Emergency escape openings must:
- Be **free of obstructions** (except allowed devices)
- Be **operational from inside** without keys, tools, or special knowledge
- Window opening control devices and fall prevention devices per **ASTM F2090** are permitted
- Release mechanism must be **operable at all times**
- Control devices must be no more than **70" above finished floor**

### Security Bars / Burglar Bars
- Must have **approved release** operable from inside without keys or tools
- Must have an **approved exterior release device** for fire department use (when required by AHJ)
- On/after July 1, 2000: must comply with California Building Standards Code, Part 12, Chapter 12-3

---

## Window Fall Protection (R321.2)

### Where Required (R321.2.1)
In dwelling units, operable windows must comply when ALL of the following conditions exist:
- Bottom of clear opening is **less than 24"** above finished floor
- Opening is **greater than 72"** above finished grade or exterior surface below

Compliance options:
1. Operable window opening **limits so a 4" sphere cannot pass** in largest opened position
2. Window opening control device or fall prevention device complying with **ASTM F2090**

### Egress Conflict Resolution (R321.2.2)
Where a window serves as EERO AND requires fall protection:
- The fall prevention device, **after release**, must not reduce the net clear opening area below EERO minimums (R319.2.1 and R319.2.2)
- This means the device must be releasable to allow full egress opening

---

## Area Wells for Below-Grade Egress (R319.4)

When an EERO has its bottom below adjacent grade, an area well is required:

| Requirement | Value |
|---|---|
| Minimum horizontal area | 9 SF |
| Minimum projection from wall | 36" |
| Minimum width | 36" |
| Ladder/steps required when | Vertical depth >44" |
| Ladder minimum inside width | 12" |
| Ladder projection from wall | >=3" |
| Ladder rung spacing | <=18" OC |

Area wells must be designed for drainage (connected to foundation drainage system).

## Natural Light Requirements (R325.1.1)

Habitable rooms must have aggregate glazing area of **not less than 8% of the floor area**.

**Exceptions:**
1. Glazing may face into a roofed porch/deck/patio (65% open on longer side, 7 ft min ceiling)
2. Glazing may face into a sunroom
3. Not required where artificial light provides 6 footcandles average at 30" above floor
4. Eave projections do not obstruct required clear space

When replacing windows, verify the replacement maintains adequate glazing area for the room. Reducing glazing area below 8% of floor area is a code violation unless Exception 3 applies.

---

## Natural Ventilation Requirements (R325.1.2)

Habitable rooms must have aggregate openable area of **not less than 4% of the floor area**.

**Exceptions:**
1. Mechanical ventilation system providing 0.35 ACH (except kitchens)
2. Kitchens with local exhaust per CMC
3. Ventilation openings may open into thermally isolated sunroom or roofed porch (40% open perimeter)

When replacing an operable window with a fixed window, verify ventilation compliance is maintained through other openings or mechanical systems.

---

## Bathroom Glazing (R325.2)

Bathrooms require:
- Aggregate glazing area in windows of **not less than 3 square feet**
- **Half must be openable**

**Exception:** Not required where artificial light AND local exhaust are provided (50 cfm intermittent or 20 cfm continuous, exhausted directly outdoors).

---

## Alterations and Repairs to Existing Basements (R319.7)

- **New sleeping rooms** in existing basements: must provide EERO per R319.1
- Other basement alterations/repairs: EERO not required
- Existing operable windows may serve as EERO if they provide:
  - Minimum 4 SF net clear opening (reduced from 5.7 SF)
  - Minimum 22" net clear height (reduced from 24")
  - Minimum 20" net clear width (same)

---

## Dwelling Additions with Sleeping Rooms (R319.6)

New sleeping rooms in additions require EERO. New basements in additions require EERO unless:
1. A sleeping room in the new basement already has one
2. An existing basement EERO is accessible from the new basement
3. An operable window complying with R319.7.1 is acceptable

---

## Practical Review Checklist for Egress

1. Is the window in a **sleeping room**? If yes, EERO requirements apply.
2. Is this a **like-for-like replacement** in an existing egress opening? Apply **R319.5 exception** -- verify it's the manufacturer's largest standard size for the opening.
3. Is the replacement **changing the operating style** (e.g., double-hung to casement)? The new style must provide **equal or greater** opening area.
4. Is the sill height **above 44"**? Not compliant for EERO (but R319.5 may exempt replacement).
5. Does the window also need **fall protection** (bottom <24" above floor, >72" above exterior grade)? If both egress and fall protection apply, the device must be releasable to allow full egress.
6. Is the room's **natural light** maintained at >=8% of floor area?
7. Is the room's **ventilation** maintained at >=4% of floor area (or has mechanical ventilation)?
