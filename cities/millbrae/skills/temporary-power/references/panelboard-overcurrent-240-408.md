# Panelboard, Overcurrent Protection, and Enclosure Requirements

Source: 2025 California Electrical Code (Title 24, Part 3), Articles 240, 312, 408, and 110.

---

## Panelboard Clearances and Location

### Readily Accessible (CEC 240.24(A))

OCPDs shall be installed in locations that are **readily accessible**. The maximum height of the operating handle of a breaker shall be **6 feet 7 inches above the finished floor** (AFF).

### Prohibited Locations

| Location | Code Section |
|----------|-------------|
| Subject to physical damage | CEC 240.24(C) |
| Near easily ignitable materials (e.g., clothes closets) | CEC 240.24(D) |
| Bathrooms of dwellings | CEC 240.24(E) |
| Over the steps of a stairway | CEC 240.24(F) |

---

## Dry, Damp, and Wet Locations

### Weatherproof Enclosures (CEC 312.2)

- Enclosures in wet or damp locations shall be **weatherproof**
- Surface-mounted metal enclosures in wet/damp locations shall maintain **minimum 1/4 inch airspace** between enclosure and wall
- In wet locations, raceways and cables entering above the level of uninsulated live parts shall use **fittings listed for wet locations**

### Protection During Construction (CEC 110.11)

- Equipment rated for dry or damp locations shall be **protected against damage from weather** during construction
- Equipment rated for dry/damp locations or marked "Indoor Use Only" or Enclosure Types 1, 2, 5, 12, 12K, and 13 shall be protected during construction

### Enclosure Marking (CEC 110.28)

Enclosures shall be marked with their **enclosure type**.

---

## Enclosures / Cabinets (CEC 312.3, 312.4)

- Maximum setback in a **noncombustible wall** (e.g., steel studs): **1/4 inch** (CEC 312.3)
- Enclosures shall be **flush to finish surface** in combustible (wood-frame) walls (CEC 312.3)
- Maximum plaster gap at side of flush-mount panel: **1/8 inch** (CEC 312.4)
- Enclosures for OCPDs shall be installed in a **vertical position** (CEC 240.33)
- Panelboards shall NOT be installed in a **face-up position** (CEC 408.43 Exception 2)
- Open knockouts shall be properly filled, except manufacturer holes for mounting (CEC 110.12(A))

---

## Overcurrent Protection Devices (OCPDs)

### Listing and Installation (CEC 110.3(B))

Breakers shall be **listed or classified** and installed according to the approved manufacturer instructions for the panel.

### Back-Fed Breakers (CEC 408.36(D))

Back-fed breakers shall be **secured in place**.

**Exception**: Output circuits from listed utility interactive inverters may not require additional securing (CEC 705.12(E)).

### Position Indication (CEC 240.81)

- Breakers shall indicate whether they are in the **"ON" or "OFF" position**
- If operated vertically, **"UP" shall indicate "ON"**

### Multi-Pole for Shared Yoke (CEC 210.7)

For receptacles on a shared yoke or mounting strap, use either a **2-pole breaker** or **2 single-pole breakers with approved handle ties**.

---

## Bus Bars (CEC 110.12(B), 408.36)

- Bus bars and other internal parts shall be **protected from contamination** (e.g., paint, plaster) during construction
- Subpanels require overcurrent protection on the supply side (CEC 408.36)
  - **Exception**: Existing services in individual residential occupancy settings (CEC 408.36(B))

---

## Covers and Circuit Directories

### Feeder Panel Label (CEC 408.4(B))

Each panel supplied by a feeder shall have a **durable label (NOT handwritten)** indicating where power originates if in a different location.

### Dead-Front Cover (CEC 408.38)

Panels shall have a **dead-front cover**.

### Circuit Directories (CEC 408.4(A))

- Circuit directories shall **distinguish each circuit from all others**
- Descriptions shall **NOT rely on transient conditions** for identification
- **Label spare positions** that contain unused OCPDs
- Fill plates shall be installed in any missing twist-outs without breakers (CEC 110.12(A))
- Empty Edison-base fuse sockets shall NOT be permitted

### Breaker Handle Access (CEC 240.40(B))

Breaker handles do NOT have to be located behind a door.

## Panel Wiring

### Terminal Connections (CEC 110.14(A))

- Only **one wire per terminal** unless terminal is specifically identified for more than one wire
- Panel instructions may allow 2-3 EGCs per terminal

### Neutral Conductors (CEC 408.41)

- Each neutral conductor shall have its **own individual terminal**
- Exception for paralleled conductors on terminals identified for more than one conductor (CEC 408.41 Exception 1)

### EGCs and Neutrals (CEC 408.41)

- EGCs and neutrals **may NOT share the same terminal**, even in service equipment where allowed on the same terminal bar

### Torque (CEC 110.3(B), 110.14(D))

- **Torque all breakers and terminals** according to approved manufacturer instructions
- Use approved means such as **torque screwdrivers** to achieve indicated torque

### Aluminum Conductors (CEC 110.14)

Antioxidant shall be applied on aluminum conductors as required per manufacturer's instructions.

### Cable Securing (CEC 312.5(C))

Each cable shall be secured to the cabinet or enclosure.

---

## Wiring Space Inside Cabinets

### Heat Dissipation (CEC 312.7)

Conductors inside cabinets shall NOT be crowded — proper heat dissipation must be maintained.

### Fill Limits (CEC 312.8)

- Splices and taps within cabinets shall NOT fill more than **40% of available space**
- All conductors together shall NOT exceed **75% of cross-sectional area**

### Power Monitoring Equipment (CEC 312.8(B))

Power-monitoring or energy management equipment acceptable within enclosure if listed and meeting fill requirements.

### Feed-Through Conductors (CEC 312.8(A)(3))

Feed-through conductors may pass through the panel if a **warning label** is applied identifying the power source.

### Wire Bending Space (CEC 408.3(G), 312.6)

- Minimum bending space per **Table 312.6(A)** for "L" bends (conductors not entering/leaving wall opposite terminals)
- Minimum bending space per **Table 312.6(B)** for "S" bends (conductors entering/leaving wall opposite terminals)
- Conductors entering from back wall require specific distance to cover and terminals per Tables 312.6(A) and (B) (CEC 408.55(C))

## Multiwire Branch Circuits (MWBC)

### Requirements

| Requirement | Code Section |
|-------------|-------------|
| Ungrounded conductors shall have voltage potential between them (same pole) | CEC 100 (Definition) |
| All MWBC conductors shall originate from the same panel | CEC 210.4(A) |
| Each neutral identified or grouped with ungrounded conductors when passing through a box without loop/splice | CEC 200.4(B) |
| Grouping not required when obvious (e.g., cable system) | CEC 200.4(B)(1) |
| Continuity of neutral shall NOT depend on a connected device (pigtail required, no feed-through) | CEC 300.13(B) |
| All MWBCs require handle ties or single-handle breaker | CEC 210.4(B) |
| Individual single-pole breakers with handle tie acceptable for line-to-neutral loads only | CEC 240.15(B)(1) |
