# Structural Design, Slope Setbacks, and Drainage for Swimming Pools

Source: 2025 California Building Code (Title 24, Part 2), Chapters 18, 19, 31, 33, Appendix J; ACI 318.

This reference covers all structural, geotechnical, and site drainage requirements for new swimming pool and spa construction in California.

---

## Swimming Pools — CBC 3109

### 3109.1 General
Swimming pools, spas, and hot tubs shall comply with the requirements of Section 3109 and other applicable sections of the California Building Code.

### Permit Required (CBC 105.1)
A building permit is required before construction of any swimming pool or spa can begin. Plans must be reviewed for compliance with building, plumbing, mechanical, and electrical codes, plus state energy conservation and accessibility standards.

## Site Plan Requirements (CBC 107.2)

The site plan submitted for pool construction must include:

| # | Element | Detail Required |
|---|---------|-----------------|
| 1 | **Project location** | APN, tract, and lot number |
| 2 | **Building footprints** | All existing and proposed structures with dimensions and distances to property lines |
| 3 | **Pool/spa location** | Dimensions, shape, setbacks from all property lines and structures |
| 4 | **Property lines** | Labeled with dimensions and ownership boundaries |
| 5 | **Access** | Driveways, existing and proposed |
| 6 | **Setbacks** | Shown from property lines to pool and all structures |
| 7 | **Easements** | Location, purpose, dimensions (flood plain, utilities, ingress/egress, open space) |
| 8 | **Fencing and walls** | Existing and proposed, with heights from grade at all turning and center points |
| 9 | **Cross-sections** | In both directions, extending minimum 5 feet beyond property lines, showing existing slopes and retaining walls |
| 10 | **Utility locations** | Electrical panel, gas meter, water meter, sewer cleanout |
| 11 | **Scale and orientation** | Drawn to scale with north arrow |

### Pool/Spa Plan Details
In addition to the site plan, the pool/spa plan must include:
- Structural details and material specifications
- Cross-section views showing pool shape, depths, steps, benches
- Reinforcing steel schedule (bar size, spacing, cover)
- Equipment pad layout (pump, filter, heater location)
- Plumbing schematic (supply, return, drain, skimmer)
- Electrical plan (bonding, lighting, equipment circuits)

## Slope Setback Requirements (CBC 1808.7.3)

### The Rule
The setback between pools regulated by the building code and slopes shall be equal to **one-half the building footing setback distance** required by Section 1808.7.

### Building Footing Setback Reference (1808.7.1 / 1808.7.2, Figure 1808.7.1)

| Slope Condition | Building Footing Setback | Pool Setback (Half) |
|-----------------|--------------------------|---------------------|
| Ascending slope (toe of slope to footing) | H/2 but need not exceed 15 ft | H/4 but need not exceed 7.5 ft |
| Descending slope (top of slope to footing) | H/3 but need not exceed 40 ft | H/6 but need not exceed 20 ft |

Where **H** = height of the slope.

### Self-Supporting Pool Wall Requirement
That portion of the pool wall within a horizontal distance of **7 feet (2,134 mm)** from the top of the slope shall be capable of **supporting the water in the pool without soil support**.

> This means the pool shell wall near the top of a descending slope must be structurally designed as a retaining wall -- it cannot rely on the surrounding soil to resist the hydrostatic pressure of the pool water.

### When Is a Soil Report Required?
A soil/geotechnical report is required if the pool is located:
- Less than **15 feet** from the top of a slope, OR
- Less than **8 feet** from the toe of a slope

Where the slope is steeper than one unit vertical in three units horizontal (1:3 or approximately 33%).

### Soil Report Exceptions
A soil report is NOT required when ALL of the following are met:
1. Pool is at the **top of slope** with deepened foundation (e.g., keyway) and daylight setback of **H/3 or greater** (minimum 15 ft, maximum 40 ft), OR
2. Pool is at the **bottom of slope** with setback of **H/2 or greater** from the toe (maximum 8 ft)
3. Plumbing pipes and joints accommodate potential soil movement

### Geotechnical Memo Before Steel Inspection
Prior to the steel/rebar inspection, the building inspector will require a memo from the soil/geotechnical engineer stating: *"Pool excavation is suitable for the support of the structure and the design assumptions are considered adequate."*

## Concrete and Shotcrete Specifications

### Concrete for Pool Shells (CBC Chapter 19, ACI 318)

| Parameter | Requirement | Source |
|-----------|-------------|--------|
| Minimum compressive strength (f'c) | **4,500 psi** at 28 days | CBC Ch. 19, ACI 318 Table 19.3.1.1 |
| Cement type | **Type V** (sulfate-resistant) | ACI 318 Table 19.3.1.1 |
| Maximum water/cement ratio | **0.45** | ACI Table 19.3.2.1 |
| Sulfate exposure | Concrete shall be resistant to sulfates | CBC Ch. 19 |
| Alternative | Unless otherwise specified by soil/geotechnical report | -- |

### Expansive Soils

| Condition | Equivalent Fluid Pressure (EFP) |
|-----------|----------------------------------|
| Level lot | 100 PCF |
| Slopes | 125 PCF |
| Soil report available | Per soil report recommendations |

### Special Inspections (CBC 1705.3, 1908, Table 1705.3)

| Inspection Type | When Required | What Is Verified |
|-----------------|---------------|------------------|
| **Shotcrete** | All gunite and shotcrete pool construction | Curing temperature, design mix, placement method, strength testing |
| **Reinforcing steel** | Before shotcrete application | Bar size, spacing, cover, lap splices, tie wire, positioning |
| **Soils** | When geotechnical report is required | Excavation suitability, fill compaction, bearing capacity |
| **Concrete testing** | When non-standard mix used | Compressive strength at 7 and 28 days |

Special inspection for shotcrete is performed by a **special inspector** hired by the owner or engineer of record, NOT by the building inspector. The special inspector must be approved by the building official.

### Structural Calculations
- Required if a **non-standard pool plan** is used
- Standard plans (where approved by the jurisdiction, e.g., "County of Orange approved standard pool plans") may be exempt from separate structural calculations
- Non-standard plans require engineering by a licensed California engineer

## Drainage and Grading

### General Grading Requirement (CBC 1804.4)
- Finished grade shall slope a minimum of **2% (1/4 inch per foot)** away from all existing structures
- Deck surfaces and surrounding grade shall drain away from the pool and from the residence
- Drainage pattern shall not increase runoff to neighboring properties

### Pool Deck and Coping
- Pool deck (coping and surrounding hardscape) shall slope away from the pool edge
- No ponding of water against pool coping or adjacent structures
- Final grading shall prevent ponding of irrigation water or rainfall

### Drainage Away from Pool
- Surface water shall be directed away from the pool to prevent contamination and undermining of the pool structure
- No drainage from the pool area shall flow toward the home foundation

## Erosion and Sediment Control

### During Construction (CBC 3304.1.5)
- An **erosion and sediment control plan** is required before permit issuance
- BMP (Best Management Practices) measures required during construction:
  - Silt fencing around excavation
  - Gravel bags at storm drain inlets
  - Covered stockpiles of excavated soil
  - Stabilized construction entrance
- Graded areas must drain away from the face of slopes at the conclusion of each working day

### NPDES Compliance
- Dewatering of pool excavation must comply with NPDES requirements
- No contaminated groundwater discharge to storm drains
- Sediment-laden water must be filtered or settled before discharge

### Adjacent Property Protection (CBC 3307.1)
- Construction activities shall not cause damage to adjacent properties
- Excavation near property lines requires shoring or sloping per OSHA/Cal-OSHA requirements

## Inspection Sequence (Typical)

| # | Inspection | What Is Verified | When |
|---|-----------|------------------|------|
| 1 | **Pre-construction / Erosion control** | BMP measures in place, NPDES compliance, site access | Before any excavation |
| 2 | **Excavation / Geotechnical** | Excavation suitability memo from geotech engineer, soil conditions | After excavation, before steel |
| 3 | **Steel / Rebar** | Reinforcing bar size, spacing, cover, lap splices, bonding conductor placement, plumbing rough-in in shell | Before shotcrete |
| 4 | **Shotcrete / Gunite** | Special inspection — mix design, placement, curing | During and after application |
| 5 | **Pre-plaster** | Pool equipment set, plumbing connections, bonding verification, deck/coping drainage | Before finish application |
| 6 | **Electrical** | Bonding grid complete, GFCI protection, underwater lighting, motor wiring, disconnect | Before energizing |
| 7 | **Barrier / Fence** | 60" height, gate hardware, self-closing/self-latching, ground clearance, climbability | Before final |
| 8 | **Final** | All systems operational, water in pool, drowning prevention features (2-of-7), drainage verified, property markers visible | Before occupancy/use |
