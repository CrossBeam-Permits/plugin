# Pool Barrier, Enclosure, and Drowning Prevention Requirements

Source: 2025 California Residential Code (Title 24, Part 2.5), Appendix CI -- Swimming Pool Safety Act; California Building Code (Part 2), Section 3109, Section 3119B; California Health and Safety Code Sections 115920-115929.

---

## Applicability

### Who Must Comply?

| Pool Type | Applicable Code | When Triggered |
|---|---|---|
| **Private single-family pool or spa** | Swimming Pool Safety Act (HS Code 115922-115923) / CRC Appendix CI | New construction or remodel of pool/spa |
| **Public swimming pool** | CBC 3119B | New construction or modification |
| **Apartment complex pool** | CBC 3119B (public pool rules) | New construction or modification |
| **Hot tub or spa with locking safety cover** | Exempt from Safety Act (HS Code 115925) | Cover must meet ASTM F1346 |

### Definitions (HS Code 115921)

- **Swimming pool / pool:** Any structure intended for swimming or recreational bathing containing water over 18 inches deep. Includes in-ground, aboveground, hot tubs, spas, portable spas, and nonportable wading pools.
- **Enclosure:** A fence, wall, or other barrier that isolates a swimming pool from access to the home.
- **Public swimming pool:** Pool operated for public use (with or without charge) or for members/guests of a private club. Does NOT include a pool at a private single-family home.

## Private Pool Enclosure Requirements (HS Code 115923)

An enclosure around a private single-family pool must meet ALL of the following:

### Height
- **Minimum 60 inches (5 ft)** measured from the outside of the enclosure

### Ground Clearance
- **Maximum 2 inches** vertical clearance from the ground to the bottom of the enclosure

### Openings
- **No gaps or voids** allowing passage of a sphere **4 inches** or greater in diameter

### Climbability
- Outside surface must be **free of protrusions, cavities, or other physical characteristics** that could serve as handholds or footholds enabling a child under age 5 to climb over

### Gate Requirements
- Access gates must **open away from the swimming pool**
- Gates must be **self-closing** (closes by gravity or spring mechanism)
- Gates must have a **self-latching device** placed **no lower than 60 inches** above the ground
- Gate latch must keep the gate securely closed

### House Wall as Part of Enclosure
- Any walls of a residential structure or accessory structure used to complete the enclosure shall have door openings equipped with **exit alarms** or have a **self-closing, self-latching device 54 inches minimum** above the floor

## Public Pool Enclosure Requirements (CBC 3119B)

### Enclosure (3119B.1)

| Requirement | Specification |
|---|---|
| Minimum height | 60 inches (5 ft) effective perpendicular height measured from the outside |
| Openings | No passage of a 4-inch sphere |
| Construction base | Over hard, permanent material equivalent to concrete |
| Climbability | No horizontal or diagonal members that could serve as a ladder; horizontal members spaced at least **48 inches** apart |
| Chain link | Permitted if openings not greater than **1-3/4 inches** measured horizontally |
| Climbable structures | No planters or other climbable structures within **5 feet** of the outside of the pool enclosure or within a 5-foot arc |
| 5-foot zone | The area 5 feet outside of the pool enclosure shall be a common area open to the public |

**Prohibited as part of pool enclosure:** Doors, openable windows, and gates of living units or associated private premises.

### Gates (3119B.2)

| Requirement | Specification |
|---|---|
| Self-closing / self-latching | Required; must keep gate securely closed |
| Opening direction | Away from pool (unless otherwise prohibited by law) |
| Hardware height | 42 to 44 inches above deck or walkway |
| Lockable | Must be capable of being locked when pool is closed |
| Emergency egress | At least one gate/door allows egress without a key; if not all gates are so equipped, label with "EMERGENCY EXIT" sign in 4" letters |
| Access control | All persons must pass through common pool enclosure gates/doors to access pool |

### Retroactivity (3119B.3)
- Sections 3119B.1 and 3119B.2 apply to pool enclosures constructed on or after July 1, 1994
- No pool fence enclosure shall be less than **4 feet** in height (regardless of construction date)

## Drowning Prevention Features (HS Code 115922)

### Two-of-Seven Requirement

When a building permit is issued for construction of a **new** swimming pool or spa, or **remodeling** of an existing one, at a private single-family home, the pool/spa must be equipped with **at least 2** of the following 7 drowning prevention safety features:

| # | Feature | Standard / Specification |
|---|---------|--------------------------|
| 1 | **Enclosure** meeting HS Code 115923 that isolates pool from the home | See enclosure requirements above |
| 2 | **Removable mesh fencing** with self-closing/self-latching key-lockable gate | ASTM F2286 |
| 3 | **Safety pool cover** (manual or power-operated) | ASTM F1346-23 |
| 4 | **Exit alarms** on doors/windows providing direct pool access without intervening enclosure | Audible continuous alarm or repeating verbal warning (e.g., "the door to the pool is open"); battery or hard-wired |
| 5 | **Self-closing, self-latching device** on home's doors to pool area | Release mechanism no lower than 54 inches above floor |
| 6 | **Pool alarm** detecting accidental/unauthorized water entry | ASTM F2208; must be independently certified; surface motion, pressure, sonar, laser, or infrared type |
| 7 | **Other approved means** of equal or greater protection | Independently verified by approved testing lab; must meet nationally recognized standards; accompanied by verifying label |

### Prohibited Combinations

The following combinations do NOT satisfy the 2-of-7 requirement:

| Combination | Why Prohibited |
|---|---|
| Exit alarm + self-closing/self-latching device **on the same door** | Same door, duplicative |
| Exit alarm + door latch **on separate doors** | Both are door-based features |
| Safety pool cover + pool alarm | Both are pool-based features |

### Personal Devices Not Qualifying
A swimming protection alarm designed for individual use (e.g., alarm attached to a child that sounds when submerged) is **NOT** a qualifying drowning prevention safety feature.

### Inspection Required
Before final approval of permitted construction or remodel, the local building code official must inspect the drowning prevention safety features. Final approval requires no violations found.

## Pool Barrier Material-Specific Requirements

### Chain Link Fencing as Pool Barrier

| Requirement | Specification |
|---|---|
| Minimum height | 60 inches |
| Maximum mesh opening | 1-3/4 inches measured horizontally (CBC 3119B.1) |
| Standard mesh (2") | Does NOT comply -- the diagonal dimension of 2" diamond mesh exceeds 4" |
| Ground clearance | Maximum 2 inches |

**Important:** Standard residential chain link (2-inch mesh) does not comply with pool barrier requirements. Specify 1-3/4 inch mesh or smaller.

### Wrought Iron / Ornamental Metal as Pool Barrier

| Requirement | Specification |
|---|---|
| Minimum height | 60 inches |
| Maximum picket spacing | Less than 4 inches (to prevent passage of 4" sphere) |
| Horizontal rails | Minimize; only top rail and bottom rail. No mid-rails that could serve as climbing aids. |
| Ground clearance | Maximum 2 inches |

### Wood Fence as Pool Barrier

| Requirement | Specification |
|---|---|
| Minimum height | 60 inches |
| Board gaps | Less than 4 inches if present |
| Climbability | No horizontal rails on the outside. Board-on-board with rails on inside is typical. |
| Ground clearance | Maximum 2 inches |

### Masonry / Block Wall as Pool Barrier

| Requirement | Specification |
|---|---|
| Minimum height | 60 inches |
| Openings | None (solid wall inherently meets 4" sphere test) |
| Climbability | Smooth exterior surface -- block wall naturally complies |
| Ground clearance | Typically 0 inches (wall on footing, no gap) |

Masonry walls are generally the most compliant pool barrier material due to their solid, smooth, unclimbable surface.

## Safety Glazing Near Pools (CRC R324.4.5)

Glazing (glass) in walls, enclosures, or fences containing or adjacent to swimming pools is considered a **hazardous location** when:
- The bottom exposed edge of the glazing is **less than 60 inches** above any standing or walking surface
- Applies to both indoor and outdoor pools

### Requirements
- Safety glazing required: **CPSC 16 CFR 1201** (Category II) or **ANSI Z97.1** (Class A)
- Glazing must be tempered, laminated, or otherwise safety-rated
- Glazing must bear manufacturer's designation visible in final installation

### Exception
Glazing that is more than **60 inches** measured horizontally from the water's edge is not considered a hazardous location.

## Quick-Reference: Private vs. Public Pool Barriers

| Feature | Private Pool (HS Code) | Public Pool (CBC 3119B) |
|---|---|---|
| Minimum barrier height | 60 inches | 60 inches |
| Maximum ground clearance | 2 inches | Per 4" sphere test |
| Maximum opening | 4-inch sphere | 4-inch sphere |
| Chain link mesh size | Per 4" sphere test | 1-3/4 inches max |
| Gate swing | Away from pool | Away from pool |
| Gate self-closing/self-latching | Yes | Yes |
| Latch height | 60 inches minimum | 42-44 inches |
| Lockable gates | Not specified | Required when pool is closed |
| Emergency egress | Not specified | At least one gate allows egress without key |
| Anti-climb | No handholds/footholds for children under 5 | No horizontal members within 48"; no climbable structures within 5 ft |
| Drowning prevention features | 2 of 7 required | Not applicable (separate regulations) |
| Building doors as enclosure | Must have exit alarms or self-closing/self-latching device (54" min) | Prohibited |

## Local Government Authority

Per HS Code 115927, the Swimming Pool Safety Act is **subject to local government modification**. Cities may adopt:
- Additional or stricter barrier requirements
- Different gate hardware heights
- Additional drowning prevention measures
- Design standards for barrier appearance

Always verify local requirements at the time of building permit application.
