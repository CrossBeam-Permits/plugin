# Stair Requirements

Source: 2025 California Residential Code (Title 24, Part 2.5), Chapter 3 Section R318 (Means of Egress), Sections R318.7 (Stairways) and R318.3 (Floors and Landings at Exterior Doors).

---

## Applicability

Stair requirements apply to all stairways within or serving a building, porch, or deck (CRC R318.7).

**Exceptions -- these stairs are exempt from R318.7:**
1. Stairways NOT within or serving a building, porch, or deck
2. Stairways leading to nonhabitable attics
3. Stairways leading to crawl spaces

---

## Dimensional Requirements

### Risers (R318.7.5.1)

| Dimension | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Maximum riser height | **7-3/4 inches** (196 mm) |
| Measurement method | Vertically between leading edges of adjacent treads |
| Uniformity | Greatest riser height in a flight shall NOT exceed smallest by more than **3/8 inch** |
| Riser angle | Vertical, or sloped from underside of nosing above at max **30 degrees** from vertical |
| Open risers (>30" above grade) | No passage of **4-inch diameter sphere** |

### Treads (R318.7.5.2)

| Dimension | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Minimum tread depth | **10 inches** (254 mm) |
| Measurement method | Horizontally between vertical planes of foremost projections of adjacent treads |
| Uniformity | Greatest tread depth in a flight shall NOT exceed smallest by more than **3/8 inch** |

### Nosings (R318.7.5.3)

| Dimension | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Nosing projection | **3/4 inch min, 1-1/4 inches max** |
| Nosing uniformity | Greatest projection shall NOT exceed smallest by more than **3/8 inch** within a flight |
| Nosing radius | Max **9/16 inch** radius of curvature, or max **1/2 inch** bevel |

**Exceptions:**
1. Nosing projection NOT required where tread depth is 11 inches or more
2. Where risers are open, max nosing projection may exceed 1-1/4 inches

### Winder Treads (R318.7.5.2.1)

| Dimension | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Depth at walkline | Min **10 inches** |
| Depth at narrowest point (within clear width) | Min **6 inches** |
| Uniformity at walkline | Largest winder tread shall NOT exceed smallest by more than **3/8 inch** |

The walkline is located **12 inches** from the inside of the turn, measured from the widest point of the clear stair width (R318.7.4).

---

## Stair Width (R318.7.1)

| Location | Minimum Clear Width |
|---|---|
| Above handrail height, below headroom | **36 inches** |
| At and below handrail height (one handrail) | **31-1/2 inches** |
| At and below handrail height (two handrails) | **27 inches** |

---

## Headroom (R318.7.2)

**Minimum 6 feet 8 inches** (2032 mm) measured vertically from the sloped line adjoining the tread nosings or from the floor surface of a landing.

**Exception:** Floor openings through which stairs pass may project horizontally into headroom not more than 4-3/4 inches at nosings at the side of the flight.

---

## Vertical Rise (R318.7.3)

Maximum vertical rise per flight: **12 feet 7 inches** (3835 mm) between floor levels or landings.

---

## Landings (R318.7.6)

### General

A floor or landing is required at the **top and bottom** of each flight of stairs.

| Dimension | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Width (perpendicular to travel) | Not less than width of flight served |
| Depth (in direction of travel) -- straight run | Min **36 inches** |
| Non-rectangular landings | Depth at walkline and total area >= quarter circle with radius = required landing width |

### Exceptions

1. Interior stairway top landing may be on other side of door if door does not swing over stairs
2. Enclosed garage top landing may be up to 7-3/4 inches below threshold
3. Exterior stairway of **not more than 2 risers**: top landing NOT required if door does not swing over stairs
4. Exterior stairs to grade with **3 or fewer risers** serving a deck, porch, or patio: bottom landing width min 36 inches (if NOT serving the required egress door)

---

## Exterior Door Landings (R318.3)

### General

A landing or floor is required on each side of each exterior door:
- Width: not less than the door width
- Depth: min **36 inches** in direction of travel
- Slope: max **2 percent** (1/4 unit vertical in 12 units horizontal)

**Exception:** Exterior balconies less than 60 sq ft accessed only from a door may have landing less than 36 inches deep.

### Egress Door Elevation (R318.3.1)

Landing at required egress door: max **1-1/2 inches** below top of threshold.

**Exception:** Exterior landing may be up to **7-3/4 inches** below threshold if door does not swing over the landing.

### Other Exterior Door Elevation (R318.3.2)

Landing at other exterior doors: max **7-3/4 inches** below top of threshold.

**Exception:** No exterior landing required where a stairway of not more than two risers is located on the exterior side and door does not swing over the stairs.

---

## Walking Surface Slope (R318.7.7)

Treads and landings: max **2 percent slope** (1 unit vertical in 48 units horizontal).

**Exception:** Where a landing must drain surface water, max **5 percent slope** (1 unit in 20 units) in direction of travel is permitted.

---

## Handrails at Stairs (R318.7.8)

Required on at least **one side** of each flight with **four or more risers**. See `guards-handrails.md` for full handrail requirements.

---

## Stairway Illumination (R318.7.10)

Stairways must be illuminated per Sections R325.6 and R325.7. For exterior stairs, adequate lighting at the top and bottom landings is required.

---

## Deck Attachment of Stairs (R318.5)

Exterior stairs must be:
- **Positively anchored** to the primary structure for vertical AND lateral forces, or
- Designed to be **self-supporting**
- Attachment NOT by toenails or nails subject to withdrawal

---

## Plastic Composite Stair Treads (R318.7.5.4, R507.2.2)

Plastic composite exterior stair treads must:
- Comply with ASTM D7032
- Bear labels showing compliance, allowable load, and max span
- Have flame spread index <= 200
- Be decay and termite resistant

---

## Spiral Stairways (R318.7.11.1)

| Dimension | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Clear width at/below handrail | Min **26 inches** |
| Walkline radius | Max **24-1/2 inches** |
| Tread depth at walkline | Min **6-3/4 inches** |
| Treads | Identical throughout |
| Max riser height | **9-1/2 inches** |
| Min headroom | **6 feet 6 inches** |

---

## Common Review Findings

1. **Non-uniform risers** -- The 3/8 inch tolerance within a flight is strictly enforced. Where a deck is built on a sloping site, the bottom riser height often differs from the rest of the flight because the landing is set at the wrong elevation. FLAG if riser heights are not shown on plans.
2. **Tread depth less than 10 inches** -- Common on steep stair runs where the homeowner tries to minimize the stair footprint. 10 inches is the absolute minimum. No exceptions for residential stairs (except spiral and alternating tread devices).
3. **Missing bottom landing** -- Even for short flights of 3 or fewer risers, a bottom landing of at least 36 inches wide is required for exterior stairs to grade (Exception 4 of R318.7.6). The only exception is if it serves the required egress door, in which case the full 36" depth landing is required.
4. **Open risers above 30 inches** -- Where the stairway rises more than 30 inches above grade, open risers that allow passage of a 4-inch sphere are a FAIL. This catches many open-riser deck stair designs.
5. **No handrail on short flights** -- Handrails are required on flights with 4 or more risers. Three risers = no handrail required. Four risers = handrail required on at least one side.
6. **Door swinging over stairs** -- At exterior doors, if there are more than 2 risers, the door must NOT swing over the stairway (or a landing must be provided between door and stairs).
