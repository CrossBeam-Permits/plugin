# Accessibility & Path of Travel

Source: 2025 California Building Code (Title 24, Part 2), Chapters 11A and 11B; 2025 California Existing Building Code (Title 24, Part 10).

---

## Applicability

### Existing Buildings (CBC 11B-202)
Alterations, including demolition within a tenant space, that affect the usability of the building trigger accessibility requirements. The "path of travel" from the public way to the area of alteration must be evaluated.

> **FOR LTIA**: Even though LTIA demolition is a "demo only" permit, the path of travel requirements apply because the work affects the usability of the tenant space. The path of travel from the public sidewalk/parking to the tenant space entrance must maintain compliance.

---

## Path of Travel Requirements

### Accessible Routes (CBC 11B-206)
At least one accessible route shall be provided within the site from accessible parking, public transportation stops, passenger drop-off and loading zones, and public streets/sidewalks to the accessible building entrance.

### Accessible Means of Egress (CBC 11B-207)
Accessible means of egress shall be provided in accordance with Section 1009 of the CBC.

### What Constitutes "Path of Travel"

The path of travel includes the following elements from the public way to the area of work:
1. **Main entrance** -- primary entrance to the building/mall
2. **Corridors and walkways** -- from entrance to the tenant space
3. **Restrooms** -- restrooms serving the area
4. **Drinking fountains** -- if provided
5. **Telephones** -- if provided

> **FOR LTIA**: Plans must show the path of travel from the public sidewalk/parking through the main entrance to the tenant space. All elements along this path must be shown to maintain compliance with disabled access requirements during and after demolition.

---

## Maintained Compliance During Demolition

### Plans Required To Show:
1. Main entrance location and accessible route from public sidewalk/parking
2. Path of travel through common areas to tenant space
3. Accessible restrooms serving the area (maintained or alternate provided)
4. Drinking fountains (if provided, maintained or alternate provided)
5. Telephones (if provided, maintained or alternate provided)

### During Construction
Means of egress and required accessible means of egress shall be maintained at all times during construction, demolition, remodeling or alterations (CBC 3310.2, CEBC 1508.2).

**Exception:** Existing means of egress need not be maintained where approved temporary means of egress systems and facilities are provided.

### Note on Plans
Plans must include the note: "All applicable fire, life, safety, and accessibility requirements shall be maintained."

---

## Key Dimensions for Accessible Routes

| Element | Minimum Dimension |
|---------|------------------|
| Accessible route width | 44 inches (corridor), 36 inches (other) |
| Door clear width | 32 inches minimum clear |
| Ramp slope (maximum) | 1:12 (8.33%) |
| Level landing at doors | 60 inches x 60 inches minimum |
| Protruding objects | Max 4 inches into path (above 27 inches) |
| Headroom clearance | 80 inches minimum |

---

## Disproportionate Cost Exception

For alterations to existing buildings, the cost of providing an accessible path of travel is not required to exceed 20% of the cost of the alteration. However, this exception is unlikely to apply to LTIA demolition since the demolition work itself is relatively low-cost and the path of travel is typically through existing common areas that are already accessible.
