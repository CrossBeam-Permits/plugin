# CRC Appendix H — Patio Covers

Source: 2025 California Residential Code (Title 24, Part 2.5), Appendix H (Patio Covers).

> **Note:** CRC Appendix H is adopted in California and provides the primary code path for residential patio cover construction. Cities may adopt additional local amendments. Where Appendix H conflicts with the main body of the CRC, Appendix H governs for patio covers.

---

## Scope and Definition (AH101, AH102)

### What Is a Patio Cover?

A patio cover is a **one-story structure** that:
- Does not exceed **12 feet in height** above the average grade plane
- Is accessory to a **single-family dwelling or individual dwelling unit** in a multi-family building
- Is used for **recreational, outdoor living purposes only**

### What a Patio Cover Is NOT

A patio cover shall **not** be used as:
- A carport
- A garage
- A storage room
- A habitable room

| Property | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Maximum height | 12 feet above average grade plane (one story) |
| Permitted use | Recreational / outdoor living only |
| Prohibited use | Carport, garage, storage, habitable room |
| Dwelling type | Accessory to single-family or individual unit in multi-family |

---

## Open vs Enclosed Classification (AH102, AH103)

This is the most important classification decision. It determines structural loads, enclosure requirements, and the code path for the entire review.

### Open Patio Cover (Lattice / Shade Bar)

An **open** patio cover has:
- A roof covering (lattice, shade bars, or similar) that is **at least 50% open** with a minimum 1-1/2 inch spacing between shade bars (self-spacing)
- No additional roof coverings over the lattice/shade bars
- No walls, or walls that meet the partial enclosure rules below

**Structural design for open covers:**
- Rafters and beams designed for **10 psf live load** (reduced from the standard 20 psf because the open roof does not collect full rain/snow loads)
- Dead load per actual materials used (typically 2-5 psf for lattice)
- Wind and seismic loads per CRC R301

### Enclosed Patio Cover (Solid Roof)

An **enclosed** or solid-roof patio cover has:
- A **solid roof** (plywood sheathing with roofing material — shingles, built-up, tile, etc.)
- Designed for full roof loads per CRC R301

**Structural design for solid covers:**
- **Live load:** 20 psf (standard roof live load per CRC R301.6) — reducible per tributary area
- **Dead load:** per actual assembly weight (light assembly vs heavy assembly — see below)
- Wind and seismic loads per CRC R301

### Light vs Heavy Roof Assembly (Solid Covers)

| Assembly Type | Definition | Typical Weight |
|---|---|---|
| **Light** | Asphalt/fiberglass shingles, cap sheets, built-up roof, hot-mopped — WITHOUT stucco ceiling or soffits | 5-10 psf |
| **Heavy** | Concrete tile roofing (10 psf max), WITHOUT stucco ceiling; OR lightweight roofing WITH stucco ceiling | 10-15 psf |

> The assembly classification affects beam span tables, footing sizes, and knee brace tributary areas.

## Partial Enclosure Rules (AH103)

Patio covers may be **partially enclosed** provided:

| Condition | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Open area | The area of the **longer wall** AND **one additional wall** must be at least **65% open** |
| Screening | Openings may be enclosed with insect screening or readily removable plastic (translucent or transparent) up to 1/8 inch thick |
| Framed windows | **Not permitted** — framed windows are NOT allowed in patio cover walls |

If these conditions are not met, the structure is classified as a **room addition**, not a patio cover, and must comply with the full CRC for habitable space (including energy code, exits, ventilation, etc.).

## Structural Requirements (AH104)

### Design Loads

| Load Type | Open (Lattice) | Solid Roof |
|---|---|---|
| Live load | 10 psf | 20 psf (per CRC R301.6) |
| Dead load | Per actual materials | Per actual assembly |
| Wind load | Per CRC R301.2.1 | Per CRC R301.2.1 |
| Seismic load | Per CRC R301.2.2 | Per CRC R301.2.2 |

### When Engineering Is Required

Engineering (stamped plans from a CA-licensed architect, civil engineer, or structural engineer) is required when ANY of the following is true:

| Trigger | Threshold |
|---|---|
| Height exceeds 12 feet | Above average grade plane |
| Spans exceed prescriptive tables | Rafter or beam span not covered by standard tables |
| Non-standard materials | Steel, aluminum, or other non-wood framing |
| Unusual loading | Snow zone, high-wind zone (>110 mph basic wind speed), or Seismic Design Category D2/E |
| Deviation from standard plans | Any construction detail not matching the jurisdiction's standard plan |
| Enclosed on more than permitted walls | Exceeds 65% open rule — triggers room addition review |

### General Structural Rules

- All structural members shall be **Douglas Fir-Larch No. 2 or better** (or approved equivalent species/grade)
- Concrete shall have a minimum compressive strength of **3,000 psi at 28 days**
- Framing hardware shall be **ICC approved** for the intended use, installed per manufacturer's specifications with all recommended fasteners
- Minimum clearance height from finished floor to underside of beam: **6 feet 8 inches**

## Footings (AH105)

Footings for patio cover posts must comply with CRC R403 (see `footings-posts.md` for details). Appendix H specifies:

| Footing Size | Max Supported Area (Open/Lattice) | Max Supported Area (Solid Roof) |
|---|---|---|
| 12" x 12" x 12" deep | 100 sq ft | 70 sq ft |
| 18" x 18" x 12" deep | 220 sq ft | 150 sq ft |

- Footings may be **circular** (equivalent cross-section area)
- Minimum depth: **12 inches** below undisturbed ground surface
- Footing reinforcement: minimum one #4 rebar each direction for 18" footings

> For larger patio covers, increase footing size or use engineered footings per CRC R403.

---

## Fire Hazard Zone Restrictions (AH106)

Standard patio cover plans (both lattice and solid) **may not comply** with Fuel Modification Zone or Wildland-Urban Interface (WUI) Fire Area construction requirements.

- In WUI fire areas, **noncombustible construction may be required**
- Check local fire authority (e.g., OCFA in Orange County) for additional requirements
- Wood lattice patio covers are generally **not permitted** in fire hazard severity zones without fire-rated alternatives

## Required Inspections (AH107)

### Open (Lattice) Patio Cover
1. **Footing inspection** — after excavation, before concrete is poured
2. **Final inspection** — after all work is completed and all work remains visible

### Solid Roof Patio Cover
1. **Footing inspection** — after excavation, before concrete is poured
2. **Framing inspection** — after all framing is complete; roof sheathing and nailing inspected at this time
3. **Final inspection** — after roof covering is installed; exposed wood has been weather-protected

## Common Review Findings

1. **Calling a room addition a "patio cover"** — If the structure has framed windows, HVAC, or is used as habitable space, it is NOT a patio cover under Appendix H. It must be reviewed as a room addition under the full CRC.
2. **Lattice not meeting 50% open rule** — Shade bars must maintain at least 50% open area with minimum 1-1/2 inch self-spacing. Lattice panels with smaller openings or decorative patterns may not qualify.
3. **Exceeding 12-foot height** — Patio covers exceeding 12 feet above average grade plane require engineered plans.
4. **Enclosing more than permitted** — The longer wall AND one additional wall must remain at least 65% open. Enclosing more triggers room addition classification.
5. **Using carport loads instead of patio cover loads** — Patio covers are NOT carports. Carports have different load requirements. A patio cover cannot be used for vehicle parking.
