# CBC Fire-Rated Assemblies -- Fire Barriers, Fire Partitions, Penetrations & Opening Protectives

Source: 2025 California Building Code (Title 24, Part 2), Chapter 7.

---

## Fire Barriers (CBC 707)

### General (707.1)
Fire barriers installed as required elsewhere in this code or the California Fire Code shall comply with Section 707.

### Fire-Resistance Rating for Fire Areas (707.3.10)

| Occupancy Group | Fire-Resistance Rating (hours) |
|----------------|-------------------------------|
| H-1, H-2 | 4 |
| F-1, H-3, S-1 | 3 |
| A, B, E, F-2, H-4, H-5, I, L, M, R, S-2 | 2 |
| U | 1 |

> **For LTIA**: Most mall/high-rise tenant spaces are Group B (business) or M (mercantile). Fire barriers between fire areas require a **2-hour** fire-resistance rating.

### Continuity (707.5)
Fire barriers shall extend from the top of the foundation or floor/ceiling assembly below to the underside of the floor or roof sheathing, slab or deck above and shall be securely attached thereto. Such fire barriers shall be continuous through concealed spaces, such as the space above a suspended ceiling.

### Openings in Fire Barriers (707.6)
Openings in a fire barrier shall be protected in accordance with Section 716. Openings shall be limited to a maximum aggregate width of 25 percent of the length of the wall, and the maximum area of any single opening shall not exceed 156 square feet.

### Penetrations in Fire Barriers (707.7)
Penetrations of fire barriers shall comply with Section 714.

## Fire Partitions (CBC 708)

### General (708.1)
The following wall assemblies shall comply with Section 708:
1. Walls separating tenant spaces in covered and open mall buildings (Section 402.4.2.1)
2. Corridor walls (Section 1020.2)
3. Walls separating enclosed tenant spaces in high-rise buildings and in buildings of Types I, IIA, IIIA, IV or VA construction of Group A, E, H, I, L and R-2.1 occupancies

> **CRITICAL FOR LTIA**: Demising walls between adjacent tenant spaces in malls are fire partitions per Section 708.1, Item 2. These must NOT be removed during demolition.

### Fire-Resistance Rating (708.3)
Fire partitions shall have a fire-resistance rating of not less than **1 hour**.

**Exceptions:**
1. Corridor walls permitted to have a 1/2-hour fire-resistance rating by Table 1020.2.
2. Dwelling/sleeping unit separations in Types IIB, IIIB and VB construction: not less than 1/2 hour with sprinkler system per 903.3.1.1.

### Continuity (708.4)
Fire partitions shall extend from the top of the foundation or floor/ceiling assembly below and be securely attached to:
1. The underside of the floor or roof sheathing, deck or slab above, OR
2. The underside of a floor/ceiling or roof/ceiling assembly having a fire-resistance rating not less than the fire partition's rating.

### Penetrations in Fire Partitions (708.7)
Penetrations of fire partitions shall comply with Section 714.

## Penetrations (CBC 714)

### Scope (714.1)
Governs the materials and methods of construction used to protect through penetrations and membrane penetrations of horizontal assemblies and fire-resistance-rated wall assemblies.

### Installation (714.2)
A listed penetration firestop system shall be installed in accordance with the manufacturer's installation instructions and the listing criteria.

### Through Penetrations of Fire-Resistance-Rated Walls (714.4.1)
Through penetrations of fire-resistance-rated walls shall be protected using:
1. Systems installed as tested in the approved fire-resistance-rated assembly (714.4.1.1), OR
2. An approved penetration firestop system tested per ASTM E814 or UL 1479, with F rating not less than the required fire-resistance rating of the wall (714.4.1.2).

### Membrane Penetrations (714.4.2)
Membrane penetrations shall comply with Section 714.4.1. Where walls or partitions are required to have a fire-resistance rating, recessed fixtures shall be installed such that the required fire resistance will not be reduced.

> **FOR LTIA DEMOLITION**: Where demolition creates new penetrations in fire-rated walls (e.g., removing fixtures from a demising wall, removing electrical boxes), the penetrations must be properly firestopped per Section 714. All existing penetrations found to be non-conforming during demolition shall also be fire-safed.

## Opening Protectives (CBC 716)

### Fire Door Assemblies (716.2)
Fire door assemblies in fire barriers and fire partitions shall comply with Table 716.1(2):

| Assembly Type | Required Wall Rating | Min. Fire Door Rating |
|--------------|---------------------|----------------------|
| Fire barriers > 1 hour | 2 hours | 1-1/2 hours |
| Fire barriers = 1 hour | 1 hour | 3/4 hour |
| Corridor walls (fire partitions) = 1 hour | 1 hour | 20 minutes (1/3 hour) |
| Corridor walls (fire partitions) = 0.5 hour | 0.5 hour | 20 minutes (1/3 hour) |
| Other fire partitions = 1 hour | 1 hour | 3/4 hour |

> **FOR LTIA**: Where demolition requires removing doors from fire-rated walls, the openings must be properly sealed or protected to maintain the fire-resistance rating. Doors at demising walls and exterior must remain. Door ratings at fire-rated corridors must be maintained.

### Self-Closing and Latching (716.2.6.1, 716.2.6.2)
Fire doors shall be latching and self- or automatic-closing. Single side-hinged swinging fire doors shall be provided with an active latch bolt.
