# Retaining Wall Permit Exemptions, Engineering Triggers, and Guard Requirements

Source: 2025 California Residential Code (Title 24, Part 2.5), Section R105.2 — Work Exempt from Permit; 2025 California Building Code (Title 24, Part 2), Chapter 18 — Soils and Foundations.

---

## Permit Exemptions (CRC R105.2)

### State Code Exemption

CRC R105.2 exempts the following from building permits:

> "Retaining walls that are not over 4 feet (1219 mm) in height measured from the bottom of the footing to the top of the wall, unless supporting a surcharge or impounding Class I, II, or IIIA liquids."

### Permit Threshold Table

| Condition | Permit Required? | Code Section |
|-----------|-----------------|--------------|
| Retaining wall <=4 ft (footing to top), level backfill, no surcharge | NO (exempt) | CRC R105.2, item 3 |
| Retaining wall >4 ft (footing to top) | YES | CRC R105.2 |
| Retaining wall any height supporting surcharge | YES | CRC R105.2 |
| Retaining wall any height impounding flammable liquids (Class I, II, IIIA) | YES | CRC R105.2 |
| Retaining wall in front setback | Often YES (city requirement) | City municipal code |

### Height Measurement Rules

**Critical concept**: The 4 ft measurement is from the **bottom of the footing** to the **top of the wall**, not the exposed face height.

| Measurement | What It Means |
|-------------|---------------|
| **Bottom of footing** | The lowest point of the concrete footing, including any portion below grade |
| **Top of wall** | The highest point of the wall stem (not including any fence or guard on top) |
| **Exposed face** | The visible height from lower grade to top of wall — this is NOT the permit measurement |
| **Total height** | Bottom of footing to top of wall — this IS the permit measurement |

**Example**: A wall with 3 ft exposed face and a 2 ft deep footing has a total height of 5 ft — it requires a permit and engineering even though it "looks like" a 3 ft wall.

### Fence on Top of Retaining Wall

Per the OC Public Works retaining wall permit requirements handout:

| Configuration | Permit Exempt? | Condition |
|---------------|---------------|-----------|
| Retaining wall <=4 ft + fence, total fence <=6 ft, retaining wall <=1 ft exposed | YES | Level backfill, no surcharge |
| Retaining wall <=4 ft, no fence | YES | Level backfill, no surcharge |
| Retaining wall >4 ft + any fence | NO | Permit required |
| Retaining wall <=4 ft + fence where combined height exceeds local limits | NO | Per city code |
| Tiered retaining walls where combined height analyzed as single wall | NO | Within influence zone |

**Note**: When a fence sits on top of a retaining wall, the city may measure the combined height from the lower grade for fence height limit purposes, even if the retaining wall portion alone is under 4 ft. Always check city overlay.

## Engineering Triggers

### When Is Engineering Required?

A registered design professional (California licensed civil engineer, structural engineer, or architect) is typically required when any of the following conditions exist:

| Condition | Engineering Required? | Rationale |
|-----------|----------------------|-----------|
| Height >4 ft (footing to top) | Yes | Exceeds prescriptive limits; lateral loads require analysis |
| Any height with surcharge loads | Yes | Must quantify surcharge and design for increased lateral force |
| Adverse soil conditions (expansive, liquefiable, fill) | Yes | Soils report required per CBC 1803.2 |
| Slope above wall (sloping backfill) | Yes | Increases lateral earth pressure coefficient |
| Slope below wall (sloping toe) | Yes | Reduces passive resistance at toe |
| Tiered/stepped walls within influence zone | Yes | Must analyze as composite system |
| Walls supporting structures, driveways, or utilities | Yes | Surcharge from supported elements |
| Walls adjacent to public right-of-way | Yes | Public safety; city requirement |
| Walls on or near property lines | Often yes | Potential impact on adjacent property |
| Walls in SDC D, E, or F retaining >6 ft of backfill | Yes | Seismic lateral earth pressure required |

### What Is a Surcharge?

A surcharge is any load applied to the ground surface behind (above) the retaining wall that increases the lateral earth pressure on the wall. Common surcharges:

| Surcharge Type | Typical Equivalent Soil Height | Description |
|----------------|-------------------------------|-------------|
| Residential driveway / passenger vehicles | 2 ft (240 psf) | Light vehicle parking or traffic behind wall |
| Heavy vehicles (fire trucks, delivery) | 4 ft (480 psf) | Commercial vehicle access behind wall |
| Buildings / structures | Varies — per structural engineer | Foundation loads transmitted through soil |
| Sloping ground above wall | Increases Ka coefficient | Slope angle changes active pressure calculation |
| Stored materials / equipment | Per actual load | Lumber yards, material storage |
| Adjacent retaining wall (within influence zone) | Per analysis | Treated as composite system |
| Swimming pool | Per structural engineer | Hydrostatic + structural loads |

**Rule of thumb**: If anything other than level, undisturbed ground exists within a horizontal distance equal to the wall height behind the wall, it is a surcharge.

## Tiered (Stepped) Walls

### Influence Zone Rule

When multiple retaining walls are built in a stepped/terraced configuration, they interact structurally if they are close enough together.

**Influence zone = 2x the height of the lower wall**, measured horizontally from the back face of the lower wall.

| Spacing Between Walls | Analysis Required |
|----------------------|-------------------|
| Less than 2x H (lower wall) | Analyze as single composite wall — total retained height is combined |
| Equal to or greater than 2x H (lower wall) | May design each wall independently |

**Example**: Two 3 ft retaining walls. The influence zone is 2 x 3 ft = 6 ft. If the horizontal distance between the back face of the lower wall and the front face of the upper wall is less than 6 ft, they must be designed as a single 6 ft wall system.

### Why This Matters

Applicants frequently propose tiered walls to avoid the 4 ft permit/engineering threshold. Two 3 ft walls appear to be under the limit, but if they are within the influence zone, the total retained height is 6 ft and full engineering is required.

## Geotechnical Investigation (Soils Report) Requirements

### When Required (CBC 1803.2)

A geotechnical investigation is required when:

| Condition | Code Section |
|-----------|--------------|
| Proposed construction on or near slopes steeper than 1 unit vertical in 3 units horizontal (33%) | CBC 1803.5.3 |
| Expansive soils (Expansion Index > 20) | CBC 1803.5.3 |
| Questionable or fill soils | CBC 1803.2 |
| Groundwater within the depth of excavation or foundation | CBC 1803.2 |
| Where required by the building official | CBC 1803.2 |
| Retaining walls >4 ft (typical AHJ requirement) | AHJ policy |

### What the Soils Report Must Include

For retaining wall design, the geotechnical report must provide:

| Parameter | Purpose |
|-----------|---------|
| Allowable bearing pressure (psf) | Footing sizing |
| Equivalent fluid pressure — active (psf/ft) | Lateral load on wall |
| Equivalent fluid pressure — at-rest (psf/ft) | If wall is restrained |
| Coefficient of friction at base | Sliding resistance |
| Passive pressure (psf/ft) | Resistance at toe |
| Seismic lateral earth pressure increment | For SDC D/E/F, walls >6 ft backfill |
| Groundwater elevation | Hydrostatic pressure |
| Soil classification | Design parameters |
| Slope stability analysis | If slopes involved |
| Recommendations for drainage | Drainage system design |

## Guard / Railing Requirements at Retaining Walls (CBC 1807.2.5)

### When Guards Are Required (1807.2.5.1)

Guards are required at retaining walls that meet BOTH conditions:
1. The retaining wall is within **36 inches** (914 mm) horizontally of a walking surface, AND
2. The walking surface at the top is more than **30 inches** (762 mm) above the grade or surface below at any point within 36 inches horizontally of the open side

**Exception**: Guards are not required at retaining walls not accessible to the public.

### Guard Specifications

| Requirement | Residential (CRC R312.1) | Commercial (CBC 1015.3) |
|-------------|--------------------------|------------------------|
| Minimum height | 42 inches above walking surface | 42 inches above walking surface |
| Opening limit | 4-inch sphere shall not pass | 4-inch sphere shall not pass |
| Concentrated load at top | 200 lbs any direction | 200 lbs any direction |
| Infill load | 50 psf | 50 psf |

### Fence as Guard

When a fence is placed on top of a retaining wall and the guard requirement is triggered:
- The fence **must** meet guard height (42" above the walking surface at the top of the wall)
- The fence **must** meet the 4-inch sphere opening limit
- The fence **must** resist 200 lb concentrated load at top
- A standard 6 ft privacy fence typically satisfies these requirements if properly constructed and attached
- Chain link fence with 1-3/4" mesh or smaller satisfies the opening limit
- Wrought iron with pickets at <=4" OC satisfies the opening limit

### Practical Height Check

| Exposed Face Height | Walking Surface at Top? | Guard Required? |
|--------------------|------------------------|----------------|
| <=30" | Yes | No |
| >30" | Yes | Yes — 42" guard |
| >30" | No (not accessible) | No (exception) |
| Any height | No walking surface within 36" | No |

## Grading Permits and Retaining Walls (CBC Appendix J)

Retaining walls are frequently part of grading projects. A separate grading permit may be required in addition to the building permit for the wall.

### Grading Permit Triggers (CBC Appendix J, Section J103)

| Activity | Permit Required? | Threshold |
|----------|-----------------|-----------|
| Grading (cut or fill) | Yes | Exceeds 50 cubic yards OR cut/fill depth exceeds 5 ft |
| Excavation for retaining wall footing | Usually no | Incidental to building permit |
| Cut slope behind retaining wall | May require | If cut exceeds 5 ft or 50 cu yd |
| Fill behind or in front of retaining wall | May require | If fill exceeds 50 cu yd |

**Note**: Many cities have lower grading permit thresholds than the state code. Some cities require a grading permit for any grading over 50 cu yd; others set the threshold as low as 10-25 cu yd. Always check the city overlay.

### Maximum Slopes (CBC Appendix J, Section J107)

| Slope Type | Maximum | Code Section |
|------------|---------|--------------|
| Cut slopes | 2H:1V (50%) unless approved steeper | J107.1 |
| Fill slopes | 2H:1V (50%) unless approved steeper | J107.3 |
| Top of cut to property line | H/2 setback, minimum 2 ft | J107.2 |
| Toe of fill to property line | H/2 setback, minimum 2 ft | J107.4 |

## Common Review Pitfalls

1. **Measuring exposed face instead of total height**: Applicants often measure from lower grade to top of wall and claim it is under 4 ft. The code measures from bottom of footing to top of wall. Always verify.

2. **Ignoring surcharge from a driveway or patio above**: A flat concrete driveway behind a 3 ft retaining wall creates surcharge. Even though the wall is under 4 ft, it requires a permit and engineering because of the surcharge.

3. **Tiered walls designed independently when within influence zone**: Two 3 ft walls 4 ft apart look "small" but must be analyzed as a 6 ft system.

4. **No drainage shown on plans**: The single most common deficiency. Every retaining wall needs drainage — drain rock, perforated pipe, and filter fabric at minimum.

5. **Missing guard at top of wall**: A 4 ft retaining wall with a patio at the top has a walking surface >30" above grade below — a guard is required.

6. **No soils report for adverse conditions**: Expansive soils are common throughout Southern California. If the site is in a mapped expansive soil area, a soils report is required regardless of wall height.
