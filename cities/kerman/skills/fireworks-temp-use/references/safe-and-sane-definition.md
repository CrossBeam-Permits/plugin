# Safe and Sane Fireworks — Definition and Classification

## HSC 12529 — Safe and Sane Fireworks Defined

"Safe and sane fireworks" are fireworks that have been:

1. **Approved by the US Consumer Product Safety Commission** (CPSC) — meets federal safety standards for consumer fireworks
2. **Examined and tested by the State Fire Marshal** — the California OSFM independently tests fireworks submitted by licensed importers
3. **Found to comply** with general and specific standards for **design, construction, performance, and labeling**

Only fireworks meeting ALL three criteria may be classified as "safe and sane" and sold at retail in California.

## What Qualifies as Safe and Sane

Typical safe and sane fireworks include:
- Sparklers (handheld)
- Fountains (ground-based, emit sparks and colored flames)
- Wheels (spin on a nail or stake)
- Snakes and glow worms (small pellets that expand when lit)
- Smoke devices (colored smoke)
- Novelty items (snappers, poppers, party poppers)

**Key characteristic**: Safe and sane fireworks do **not** fly into the air, explode, or travel along the ground in an uncontrollable manner.

## HSC 12505 — Dangerous Fireworks Defined

"Dangerous fireworks" are any fireworks NOT classified as safe and sane. This includes:
- Firecrackers, cherry bombs, M-80s
- Roman candles
- Aerial shells and mortars
- Sky rockets and bottle rockets
- Any firework that explodes, flies, or moves uncontrollably

**Dangerous fireworks are illegal** to sell, possess, transport, or discharge in California without a special pyrotechnic display permit (HSC 12640+), which is a completely separate process from the retail safe-and-sane permit.

## HSC 12560 — State Fire Marshal Approval Required

No person shall sell, offer for sale, or possess for sale any fireworks in California unless they have been **approved by the State Fire Marshal** and bear the SFM approval seal/label.

## HSC 12562 — Labeling

All approved safe and sane fireworks must bear labels indicating:
- SFM approval
- Classification as "safe and sane"
- Manufacturer information
- Usage instructions
- Warning statements per CPSC requirements

## State Fire Marshal Testing

The OSFM maintains a list of approved safe and sane fireworks each year. Importers submit samples for testing before each fireworks season. The testing process verifies:
- The firework performs as described (no aerial flight, no explosion)
- Construction meets safety standards
- Fuse burn time is within safe range
- The device does not produce excessive heat, sparks, or debris outside the safe zone

**Important for permit review**: The permit applicant does not need to prove that individual fireworks are safe and sane — that is handled at the importer/distributor level via the SFM approval process. The retail permittee simply must ensure they only purchase and sell fireworks from licensed distributors carrying SFM-approved products.
