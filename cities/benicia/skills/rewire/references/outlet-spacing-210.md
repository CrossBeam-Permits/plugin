# CEC Section 210.52 — Dwelling Unit Receptacle Outlet Spacing

Source: 2025 California Electrical Code (Title 24, Part 3), Article 210.

A whole-house rewire triggers outlet spacing compliance — if existing spacing doesn't meet current code, new outlets must be added.

---

## General Spacing Rule — 210.52(A)

### The 6-Foot Rule — 210.52(A)(1)

In every kitchen, family room, dining room, living room, parlor, library, den, sunroom, bedroom, recreation room, or similar room:

**No point measured horizontally along the floor line of any wall space shall be more than 6 feet (1.8m) from a receptacle outlet.**

This means receptacles must be spaced a maximum of **12 feet apart** along wall spaces (since the 6-foot measurement applies from each receptacle in both directions).

### What Counts as Wall Space — 210.52(A)(2)

Wall space includes:
1. Any space **2 feet or more in width** (including around corners), unbroken by doorways, fireplaces, stationary appliances, or fixed cabinets without countertops
2. Space occupied by fixed panels in walls (excluding sliding panels)
3. Space afforded by fixed room dividers (freestanding bar-type counters, railings)

### Floor Receptacles — 210.52(A)(3)

Floor receptacles only count toward the required number if located within **18 inches** of the wall.

---

## Kitchen Countertop Spacing — 210.52(C)

### The 24-Inch Rule — 210.52(C)(1)

For countertops and work surfaces **12 inches or wider** in kitchens, pantries, breakfast rooms, dining rooms:

**No point along the wall line shall be more than 24 inches (600mm) measured horizontally from a receptacle outlet.**

Exceptions:
- Not required directly behind a range, counter-mounted cooking unit, or sink
- If a required outlet can't be installed in the wall, it may be installed as close as practicable

### Countertop Receptacle Location — 210.52(C)(3)

Receptacles must be located:
1. On or above, but not more than **20 inches above**, a countertop or work surface
2. In a countertop using receptacle outlet assemblies listed for countertop use
3. In a work surface using listed assemblies

### Island and Peninsular Countertops — 210.52(C)(2)

If a receptacle is installed to serve an island/peninsula, it must follow 210.52(C)(3). If no receptacle is provided, **provisions for future addition** must be provided (conduit stub, etc.).

### Kitchen Branch Circuits — 210.52(B)

- Minimum **two 20-ampere small-appliance branch circuits** required
- Must serve all kitchen wall/floor receptacles, countertop outlets, and refrigerator receptacle
- No small-appliance circuit shall serve more than one kitchen
- Small-appliance circuits shall have no other outlets (some exceptions for clock receptacles and gas appliance accessories)

---

## Room-Specific Receptacle Requirements

### Bathrooms — 210.52(D)

At least one receptacle outlet within **3 feet (900mm)** of the outside edge of each sink:
- On a wall/partition adjacent to the sink or countertop
- On the countertop (listed for countertop use)
- On the side or face of the sink cabinet
- Not more than **12 inches below** the top of the sink or countertop

**Dedicated circuit**: 20A bathroom branch circuit per 210.11(C)(3).

### Outdoor — 210.52(E)

**One-family and two-family dwellings** (at grade level):
- At least one receptacle outlet at **front and back** of dwelling
- Readily accessible from grade, not more than **6.5 feet above grade**

**Balconies, decks, porches** within 4 inches of dwelling:
- At least one receptacle outlet, not more than 6.5 feet above walking surface

### Laundry Areas — 210.52(F)

At least one receptacle outlet in areas designated for laundry equipment.

**Dedicated circuit**: 20A laundry branch circuit per 210.11(C)(2) — no other outlets on this circuit.

### Garages — 210.52(G)(1)

At least one receptacle in each vehicle bay, not more than **5.5 feet above floor**.

**Dedicated circuit**: 20A garage branch circuit per 210.11(C)(4) — no other outlets (may also serve outdoor receptacles).

### Basements — 210.52(G)(3)

At least one receptacle in each separate unfinished portion of a basement.

### Hallways — 210.52(H)

Hallways **10 feet or more** in length: at least one receptacle outlet. Length measured along centerline without passing through a doorway.

### Foyers — 210.52(I)

Foyers greater than **60 sq ft** (not part of a hallway): receptacle(s) in each wall space 3 feet or more in width.

## Dedicated Branch Circuits Summary — 210.11(C)

A whole-house rewire must include all required dedicated circuits:

| Circuit | Min. Rating | Must Serve | No Other Outlets? |
|---------|------------|------------|-------------------|
| Small-appliance (×2 min) | 20A | Kitchen/dining/pantry wall + countertop receptacles | Yes (with exceptions) |
| Laundry | 20A | Laundry receptacle(s) only | Yes |
| Bathroom | 20A | Bathroom receptacle(s) + countertop outlets | Yes (exception for single-bathroom circuit) |
| Garage | 20A | Garage receptacles | Yes (may serve outdoor receptacles) |

---

## Rewire Compliance Trigger

When a whole-house rewire is performed, the existing outlet layout must be evaluated against CEC 210.52. **If outlet spacing does not meet current code, additional outlets must be added to achieve compliance.**

Common deficiencies found in older homes:
- Bedrooms with only one or two outlets (pre-1960s homes)
- Kitchens without GFCI or with insufficient countertop receptacles
- No dedicated 20A circuits for bathroom, laundry, kitchen, or garage
- Missing outdoor receptacles (front and back required)
- No garage receptacle in vehicle bay
- Hallways >10' without a receptacle
