# WUI Exterior Glazing Requirements

> Source: 2025 California Wildland-Urban Interface Code (Title 24, Part 7)
> Sections: WUI Code 504.8-504.9; CRC R337; CBC Chapter 7A

---

## Overview

Buildings in Wildland-Urban Interface (WUI) areas and Fire Hazard Severity Zones (FHSZ) must meet enhanced exterior glazing requirements to resist wildfire exposure. The WUI Code (Part 7) establishes specific glazing construction requirements that go beyond standard building code.

**Key point:** CRC Section R337 and CBC Chapter 7A both redirect to Part 7 (WUI Code) for the actual requirements. The WUI Code applies to all buildings and structures in the WUI or FHSZ.

---

## Exterior Glazing Requirements (WUI Code 504.8)

Exterior windows, window walls, glazed doors, windows within exterior doors, and skylights in WUI areas must be constructed of **any of the following:**

### Option 1: Multi-Pane with Tempered Glass
Multilayered glazed panels with **at least one pane of tempered glass** complying with CBC Section 2406.

This is the **most common compliance path** for residential window replacement in WUI areas. A standard dual-pane insulated glass unit (IGU) with one tempered pane satisfies this requirement.

### Option 2: Glass Block
Glass block construction.

### Option 3: Fire-Protection-Rated Glazing
Glazing with a fire-protection rating of **not less than 20 minutes** when tested according to:
- NFPA 257 (Standard on Fire Test for Window and Glass Block Assemblies), or
- UL 9 (Fire Tests of Window Assemblies)

This glazing is **exempt from the hose stream test**.

### Option 4: SFM Standard 12-7A-2
Glazing meeting the performance requirements of **SFM Standard 12-7A-2** (Exterior Windows -- a fire resistance test consisting of a 150 kW intensity direct flame exposure for an 8-minute duration).

---

## What This Means for Replacement Windows

### Single-Pane Windows Are NOT Compliant
In WUI areas, a single-pane window does not meet any of the four options above. Replacement windows MUST be at least dual-pane with one tempered pane (Option 1) or meet one of the other options.

### Dual-Pane with One Tempered Pane Is the Standard
The most common and cost-effective compliant configuration:
- Dual-pane insulated glass unit (IGU)
- At least one pane is tempered glass
- The other pane may be annealed, tempered, or low-E coated
- This simultaneously satisfies WUI glazing AND energy code requirements (dual-pane is typically needed to meet U-factor targets anyway)

### Triple-Pane Is Also Compliant
Triple-pane windows with at least one tempered pane automatically comply.

## Structural Glass Veneer (504.8.1)

Where structural glass veneer is used on exterior walls in WUI areas, the wall assembly behind the glass must comply with Section 504.5 (exterior wall construction requirements for ignition resistance).

---

## Operable Skylights in WUI Areas (504.8.2)

Operable skylights must be protected by a **noncombustible mesh screen** with openings not exceeding **1/8 inch** in any dimension.

---

## Exterior Doors in WUI Areas (504.9)

Exterior doors must be constructed of one of the following:
1. **Noncombustible construction**
2. **Solid-core wood** not less than 1-3/4" thick
3. **Solid-core wood** with stiles/rails >=1-3/8" and raised panels >=1-1/4" thick
4. **20-minute fire-protection-rated** door (NFPA 252, UL 10B, or UL 10C)
5. Exterior surface/cladding meeting ASTM E2707 performance (Section 504.9.3)
6. Exterior surface/cladding meeting SFM Standard 12-7A-1

**Windows within doors and glazed doors** must comply with Section 504.8 (the exterior glazing requirements above).

### Garage Doors (504.9.1)
Automatic garage door openers must be equipped with **battery backup** in WUI areas.

### Garage Door Gap Control (504.9.2)
Exterior garage doors must prevent gaps >1/8" at bottom, sides, and top using:
- Weather-stripping meeting ASTM D638 tensile strength and UL 94 flammability (V-2 or better)
- Door overlaps onto jambs/headers
- Metal flashing on jambs/headers

## How to Determine If a Project Is in a WUI/FHSZ

### Fire Hazard Severity Zone Maps
Cal Fire maintains official FHSZ maps. Projects may be in:
- **State Responsibility Area (SRA):** Zones designated by Cal Fire
- **Local Responsibility Area (LRA):** Zones designated by local fire authority

### WUI Applicability
The WUI Code applies to:
- Buildings in designated WUI areas (per local or state jurisdiction)
- Buildings in Fire Hazard Severity Zones (Very High, High, or Moderate)
- Check with the local building department or fire authority for zone determination

### Practical Verification
- Many cities include FHSZ/WUI status on permit applications
- Cal Fire FHSZ Viewer: https://osfm.fire.ca.gov/divisions/community-wildfire-preparedness-and-mitigation/fire-hazard-severity-zones/
- Ask the applicant or check the project address against city GIS maps

## Replacement Triggers in WUI Areas (Section 507)

Section 507 of the WUI Code addresses **replacement or repair of roof coverings** but does not have a direct equivalent for window replacement. However, the general principle applies:

When an existing window is replaced in a WUI area, the new window must comply with current WUI Code requirements (Section 504.8), even if the original window was installed before the WUI Code applied.

This means:
- Old single-pane windows being replaced must be upgraded to dual-pane with tempered
- The replacement triggers compliance with current code, not the code in effect when the house was built

## Common WUI Cities and Areas in California

Many cities have partial or full WUI designation:
- **Laguna Beach** -- significant portions in VHFHSZ
- **Malibu** -- nearly entire city in FHSZ
- **Oakland Hills** -- VHFHSZ
- **San Diego** (eastern portions) -- FHSZ
- **Paradise** -- VHFHSZ
- **Santa Rosa** (Fountaingrove, Mark West) -- VHFHSZ
- **Most mountain communities** (Lake Arrowhead, Big Bear, etc.) -- FHSZ

Always verify the specific parcel's FHSZ designation rather than assuming city-wide applicability.

## Practical Review Checklist for WUI Glazing

1. **Is the project in a WUI area or FHSZ?** Check Cal Fire maps or local jurisdiction.
2. **If yes:** Replacement windows must meet WUI Code 504.8 -- at minimum, dual-pane with one tempered pane.
3. **Single-pane replacement?** NOT compliant in WUI areas -- FAIL.
4. **Dual-pane with tempered?** Verify at least one pane is tempered glass per CBC 2406.
5. **Glazed doors?** Must also comply with 504.8 -- plus door itself must meet 504.9 requirements.
6. **Operable skylights?** Require noncombustible mesh screen with <=1/8" openings.
7. **Garage doors?** Must have battery backup openers and gap control <=1/8".
