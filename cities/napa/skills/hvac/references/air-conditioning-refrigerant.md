# Air Conditioning & Refrigerant Piping

Source: 2025 California Mechanical Code (Title 24, Part 4), Chapters 3, 9, and 11.

## System Sizing (CMC 1105.1)

Cooling equipment used for human comfort in residential buildings shall be selected in accordance with:
- **ACCA Manual S** for equipment selection
- **ACCA Manual J** for load calculation (or other approved methods)

> This is a state code requirement -- every residential A/C system must be sized per Manual J/S.

## Equipment Anchoring (CMC 303.4)

All HVAC appliances shall be anchored in place to resist gravity loads, seismic forces, and wind forces.

## Outdoor Unit Pad (CMC 1105.2)

Outdoor condensing units shall be supported on noncombustible materials. In practice, this means a minimum **3 in. raised concrete or composite pad** above grade.

> Note: The 3 in. pad requirement comes from common residential practice and the CRC M1305.1.4 reference. CMC 1105.2 requires noncombustible supports; the pad height prevents flooding and debris contact.

## Blower Capacity (CMC 904.7(1))

Furnace with cooling coil shall have a blower capable of overcoming the resistance of the cooling coil and duct system friction losses.

## Air Filter (CMC 311.2)

Central air conditioning systems shall have an air filter installed. Filters shall be installed in a location that is accessible for maintenance and shall be sized to prevent bypassing of unfiltered air.

## Condenser Clearances

### Distance from Dryer Vent (CMC 311.2)

Condenser shall NOT be installed within **5 ft** of a clothes dryer vent.

### Outdoor Air Intake Clearance (CMC 311.3)

Vent shall maintain minimum clearance from mechanical and gravity outdoor air intake openings per CRC R303.5.

## Electrical Requirements for A/C Equipment

### Disconnect (CMC 301.4)

Equipment requiring electrical connections of more than 50 volts shall have a **positive means of disconnect adjacent to and in sight from the equipment**.

For outdoor condensing units, the disconnect shall be located **within sight** of the condenser (CEC 440.14).

### Service Receptacle (CMC 301.4)

A **120V GFCI-protected receptacle** shall be located within **25 ft** of the equipment for service and maintenance.

## Refrigerant Piping

### Protection (CMC 1109.7)

Refrigerant piping and tubing shall be installed so that it is **not subject to damage from an external source**.

Soft annealed copper tubing shall not exceed 1-3/8 in. nominal size.

### Support (CMC 1109.6)

Piping and tubing shall be securely fastened to a permanent support:
- Within **6 ft** following the first bend after the compressor
- Within **2 ft** of each subsequent bend or angle
- At intervals not more than **15 ft** apart

### Materials (CMC 1109.1)

Refrigerant piping shall be metallic. Copper linesets shall comply with ASTM B280 or ASTM B1003 (CMC 1109.1.2).

### Tamper-Resistant Caps (CMC 1105.11)

Outdoor refrigerant circuit access ports shall have **locking-type tamper-resistant caps**.

**Exception**: Ports in secure locations protected by walls or fencing with key access.

## Condensate Disposal

### Primary Drain (CMC 310.1)

Condensate from air-cooling coils shall be collected and discharged to an approved plumbing fixture or disposal area via indirect waste pipe. The drain pipe shall have a slope of not less than **1/8 in. per foot (1% slope)** and shall be of approved corrosion-resistant material.

### Overflow Protection (CMC 310.2)

Where equipment is installed in a space where condensate overflow could cause damage (attics, above ceilings, etc.), one of the following additional protections is required:

| Method | Description |
|--------|------------|
| 1 | Water level detection device that shuts off equipment |
| 2 | Additional watertight pan with separate drain line |
| 3 | Additional separate drain line connection at higher level |
| 4 | Additional pan with water level detection device |

The additional pan or drain line connection shall have a drain pipe of not less than **3/4 in.** nominal size, discharging at a readily observed point.

### Condensate Pipe Sizing (CMC 310.3)

| Equipment Capacity (Tons) | Min. Condensate Pipe Diameter |
|--------------------------|------------------------------|
| Up to 20 | 3/4 in. |
| 21-40 | 1 in. |
| 41-90 | 1-1/4 in. |
