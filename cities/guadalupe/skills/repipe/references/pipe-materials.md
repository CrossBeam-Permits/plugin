# CPC Section 604 — Water Supply Pipe Materials

Source: 2025 California Plumbing Code (Title 24, Part 5), Chapter 6.

## Approved Materials (604.1 / Table 604.1)

All water supply piping materials must conform to Table 604.1. The following are approved for residential water supply:

### Copper and Copper Alloys
- **ASTM B88** — Seamless copper water tube (Types K, L, M)
- **ASTM B75** — Seamless copper tube
- **ASTM B42** — Seamless copper pipe
- **ASTM B43** — Seamless red brass pipe
- **ASTM B302** — Threadless copper pipe

**Copper Type Requirements (604.3)**:
- **Type L minimum** for underground and general use
- **Type M** permitted aboveground only
- **Type K** required where local jurisdiction specifies (typically for underground service line)

### CPVC (Chlorinated Polyvinyl Chloride)
- **ASTM D2846** — CPVC tubing and fittings (hot and cold)
- **ASTM F441/F442** — CPVC pipe, Schedule 40/80

### PEX (Cross-Linked Polyethylene)
- **ASTM F876** — PEX tubing
- **CSA B137.5** — Cross-linked polyethylene pressure pipe
- **AWWA C904** — Cross-linked polyethylene pressure pipe (4"-6")

### PEX-AL-PEX (Composite)
- **ASTM F1281** — Crosslinked polyethylene/aluminum/crosslinked polyethylene
- **CSA B137.10** — Cross-linked polyethylene/aluminum/cross-linked polyethylene composite pipe

### Galvanized Steel
- **ASTM A53** — Pipe, steel, black and hot-dipped zinc-coated
- Approved but rarely used for new residential repipe

### Other Approved Materials
- **Ductile Iron** — AWWA C151 (typically for service lines, not interior repipe)
- **PVC** — ASTM D1785, D2241 (cold water building supply only, not interior hot water)

## NSF & Lead Content Requirements (604.1, 604.2)

| Requirement | Detail | Code Section |
|-------------|--------|-------------|
| **NSF 61 compliance** | All water supply pipe, fittings, and materials shall comply with NSF 61 | CPC 604.1 |
| **Lead — drinking water** | Max weighted average 0.25% lead content (wetted surfaces) for pipes, fittings, fixtures conveying water for human consumption | CPC 604.2 |
| **Lead — non-drinking** | Max 8% lead content for non-drinking water applications | CPC 604.2.1 |
| **Solder & flux lead** | Max 0.2% lead content for solder and flux in potable water systems | CPC 604.2 |
| **Dezincification** | Copper alloy fittings/valves >15% zinc by weight used with plastic piping shall be resistant to dezincification per NSF 14 | CPC 604.1 |
| **Previously used pipe** | Piping previously used for any purpose other than potable water shall NOT be used for potable water | CPC 604.8 |

## Copper Pipe Markings (604.4)

Hard-drawn copper tubing shall be marked per ASTM B88:
- **Type K** = Green
- **Type L** = Blue
- **Type M** = Red

## Prohibited Materials

| Material | Status | Reason |
|----------|--------|--------|
| **Polybutylene (PB)** | NOT approved for new installation | Removed from approved materials list; known failure issues |
| **Lead pipe** | Prohibited | Lead content exceeds 0.25% threshold per CPC 604.2 |
| **Unlisted/uncertified materials** | Prohibited | Must bear third-party certification mark (604.1) |
| **PEX-AL-PEX** (HCD 1 & HCD 2) | NOT adopted for potable water | Per BSC, DSA-SS, HCD 1 & HCD 2 — not adopted for potable water supply and distribution |

## Dissimilar Metal Protection (605.15, 605.16, 310.6)

Where pipes of different metals are joined, **dielectric protection** is required to prevent galvanic corrosion:

- **Copper to galvanized steel**: Brass pipe, dielectric fitting, or dielectric union (per ASSE 1079 or IAPMO PS 66) required (CPC 605.16.1)
- **Copper to iron/steel**: Same protection required
- **PEX/CPVC to metal**: Approved adapter or transition fittings designed for the specific transition (CPC 605.16.2)
- **Accessible locations**: Except for necessary valves, intermixing of dissimilar metals shall ONLY be at exposed or accessible locations (CPC 310.6)

> Common in repipe projects: new copper connecting to existing galvanized at the water heater or main shutoff.

> For HCD 1 & HCD 2 (residential): Dielectric unions shall be used at ALL points of connection where there is a dissimilarity of metals (CPC 605.15).

## Pipe Support Requirements (313.0)

Pipe support intervals vary by material and orientation:

| Material | Horizontal Support Interval | Vertical Support Interval |
|----------|---------------------------|--------------------------|
| Copper tube (1" and under) | 6 feet | 10 feet |
| Copper tube (1-1/4" and over) | 10 feet | 10 feet |
| CPVC (1" and under) | 3 feet | 5 feet (per manufacturer) |
| CPVC (1-1/4" and over) | 4 feet | 5 feet (per manufacturer) |
| PEX | 32 inches (horizontal runs) | Per manufacturer |
| Steel pipe (3/4" and under) | 10 feet | 12 feet |
| Steel pipe (1" and over) | 12 feet | 12 feet |

**PEX-specific notes**:
- Must be protected from UV exposure (sunlight causes degradation)
- Must not be installed in locations where temperatures exceed 200°F
- Fittings must be same manufacturer system or approved equivalent
- Requires protection from physical damage where exposed

## Third-Party Certification (604.1)

All water supply piping and fittings must bear the mark of a third-party certification agency (e.g., IAPMO UPC, NSF, CSA). Uncertified materials cannot be used regardless of material type.

## Material Selection Guidance

For residential repipe projects, the most common approved materials and their typical applications:

| Material | Typical Use Case | Advantages | Considerations |
|----------|-----------------|------------|----------------|
| **Copper Type L** | Traditional full repipe | Durable, proven, accepted everywhere | Higher cost, requires soldering |
| **PEX** | Modern full repipe | Lower cost, flexible, fewer fittings | UV-sensitive, some jurisdictions require manifold |
| **CPVC** | Budget-friendly repipe | Lower cost than copper, rigid | More brittle than PEX, can crack with age |
| **PEX-AL-PEX** | Specialty applications | Holds shape like copper, PEX flexibility | Higher cost than plain PEX |
