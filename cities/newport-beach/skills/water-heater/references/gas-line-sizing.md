# Gas Line Sizing & Connections

Source: 2025 California Plumbing Code (Title 24, Part 5), Chapter 12 — Fuel Gas Piping.

---

## Typical Water Heater Gas Input Ratings (CPC Table 1208.3.1)

| Appliance Type | Approximate Input (BTU/h) |
|----------------|--------------------------|
| Storage tank, 30-40 gallon | 35,000 |
| Storage tank, 50 gallon | 50,000 |
| Tankless, 2 GPM capacity | 142,800 |
| Tankless, 4 GPM capacity | 285,000 |
| Tankless, 6 GPM capacity | 428,400 |
| Domestic circulating / side-arm | 35,000 |

**Total connected load**: Piping is sized based on the sum of all appliance inputs operating at full capacity simultaneously, unless diversity factors are used (CPC 1208.3.1).

---

## When Gas Line Upgrade Is Needed

Per CPC 1208.1.1, when additional appliances are connected or existing appliances are replaced with higher-capacity units, the existing piping **must be checked for adequate capacity**. If inadequate, the existing system shall be enlarged or a separate adequately-sized line shall be provided.

**Common trigger**: Replacing a 50-gallon tank (50,000 BTU/h) with a tankless unit (199,000-199,900 BTU/h typical residential models). This roughly quadruples the gas demand and will almost always require a gas line upgrade from the meter to the appliance.

## Simplified Pipe Sizing Reference

From CPC Table 1215.2(1) — Schedule 40 steel pipe, natural gas, 0.5 in. w.c. pressure drop, 0.60 specific gravity:

| Pipe Run Length | 1/2" Pipe Capacity | 3/4" Pipe Capacity | 1" Pipe Capacity |
|-----------------|--------------------|--------------------|-------------------|
| 10 ft | 172,000 BTU/h | 360,000 BTU/h | 678,000 BTU/h |
| 20 ft | 118,000 BTU/h | 247,000 BTU/h | 466,000 BTU/h |
| 30 ft | 95,000 BTU/h | 199,000 BTU/h | 374,000 BTU/h |
| 50 ft | 72,000 BTU/h | 151,000 BTU/h | 284,000 BTU/h |
| 80 ft | 56,000 BTU/h | 117,000 BTU/h | 220,000 BTU/h |
| 100 ft | 50,000 BTU/h | 104,000 BTU/h | 195,000 BTU/h |

> Table values are in cubic feet/hour. Multiply by ~1,000 BTU/cu.ft. (natural gas) for approximate BTU/h capacity.

**Rules of thumb for residential**:
- 1/2" pipe handles a standard 40-50 gal tank water heater on short runs (under 30 ft)
- 3/4" pipe handles up to ~175,000 BTU/h on typical residential runs (under 50 ft)
- 1" pipe is needed for high-demand tankless units on longer runs
- For tankless units over 199,000 BTU/h, verify 3/4" is adequate for the actual run length from meter

### Sizing Method (CPC 1215.1.1 — Longest Length Method)

1. Measure the pipe length from the gas meter to the most remote outlet
2. Use that length for sizing ALL sections (conservative approach)
3. For each pipe section, determine the total BTU/h load it carries
4. Look up the pipe size in the appropriate table

## Gas Connector Requirements (CPC 1212.1)

Appliances shall be connected by one of the following:

1. **Rigid metallic pipe and fittings** (black iron, most common)
2. **Semi-rigid metallic tubing** with metallic fittings
3. **Listed appliance connector** (flex connector) per ANSI Z21.24/CSA 6.10
4. **CSST** (corrugated stainless steel tubing) per manufacturer instructions

### Flex Connector Rules

- Only **one connector per appliance**
- Must be in the **same room** as the appliance
- Shall not pass through walls, partitions, ceilings, or floors
- Shall not be concealed
- Maximum length per manufacturer listing (typically 4-6 feet)
- Must be protected against physical and thermal damage

---

## Gas Shutoff Valve (CPC 1212.6)

Each appliance shall have an accessible, approved **manual shutoff valve** with a nondisplaceable valve member.

| Requirement | Specification |
|-------------|---------------|
| **Location** | Within **6 feet** of the appliance it serves |
| **Position** | Upstream of the connector |
| **Union/flange** | Required downstream of the valve for appliance removal |
| **Serves** | Single appliance only |

---

## Sediment Trap / Drip Leg (CPC 1212.9)

A **sediment trap** is required at the appliance connection, installed downstream of the shutoff valve and as close to the appliance inlet as practical.

**Construction**: Typically a tee fitting with a capped nipple in the bottom outlet (minimum 3 inches long). The vertical capped nipple collects moisture and debris before it enters the appliance.

**Exempt appliances**: Gas ranges, clothes dryers, decorative fireplace appliances, outdoor cooking appliances. **Water heaters are NOT exempt** -- a sediment trap is always required.

---

## Acceptable Piping Materials (CPC 1208.5)

| Material | Standard | Notes |
|----------|----------|-------|
| Black steel pipe | ASTM A53, Schedule 40 | Most common residential |
| Copper tubing (Type K or L) | ASTM B88 | Only if gas has < 0.3 grains H2S per 100 scf |
| CSST | CSA/ANSI LC 1/CSA 6.26 | Must be listed; bonding required per CEC |
| Polyethylene (underground only) | ASTM D2513 | Marked "gas" and "ASTM D2513" |

PVC and CPVC pipe shall **not** be used for gas piping.

## Review Checklist — Gas Supply

For gas water heater installations, verify:

- [ ] Gas line sized for total connected load (check if BTU rating changed)
- [ ] Tank-to-tankless upgrade: confirm gas line capacity is adequate
- [ ] Shutoff valve within 6 ft, upstream of connector, accessible
- [ ] Sediment trap (drip leg) installed at appliance connection
- [ ] Flex connector listed, single connector, same room, not concealed
- [ ] Piping material is approved (no PVC/CPVC for gas)
- [ ] CSST properly bonded per electrical code if used
