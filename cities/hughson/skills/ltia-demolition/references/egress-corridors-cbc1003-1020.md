# CBC Means of Egress & Corridors

Source: 2025 California Building Code (Title 24, Part 2), Chapter 10.

---

## General Means of Egress (CBC 1003)

### Applicability (1003.1)
The general requirements of Sections 1003 through 1015 apply to all three elements of the means of egress system: exit access, exit, and exit discharge.

In addition, means of egress that provide access to, or egress from, buildings or facilities where accessibility is required shall also comply with Chapter 11A or Chapter 11B, as applicable.

### Ceiling Height (1003.2)
The means of egress shall have a ceiling height of not less than **7 feet 6 inches** above the finished floor.

### Slip-Resistant Surface (1003.4)
Circulation paths of the means of egress shall have a slip-resistant surface and be securely attached.

### Means of Egress Continuity (1003.6)
The path of egress travel along a means of egress shall not be interrupted by a building element other than a means of egress component. Obstructions shall not be placed in the minimum width or required capacity of a means of egress component. The minimum width or required capacity of a means of egress system shall not be diminished along the path of egress travel.

> **CRITICAL FOR LTIA**: During and after demolition, the egress path through the tenant space, through the mall common areas, and to the building exits must remain uninterrupted and unobstructed. Debris, construction barriers, or other obstructions in the egress path are a code violation.

## Corridors (CBC 1020)

### General (1020.1)
Corridors serving as an exit access component in a means of egress system shall comply with Sections 1020.2 through 1020.7.

### Fire-Resistance Rating of Corridors (1020.2 / Table 1020.2)

| Occupancy | Occupant Load | Without Sprinkler | With Sprinkler |
|-----------|---------------|-------------------|----------------|
| H-1, H-2, H-3 | All | Not Permitted | 1 hour |
| A, B, E, F, M, S, U | Greater than 30 | 1 hour | 0 |
| R-1, R-2 | Greater than 10 | Not Permitted | 1 hour |
| I-2 | Greater than 6 | Not Permitted | 1 hour |

> **For LTIA**: In sprinklered mall/high-rise Group B or M occupancies with corridor occupant load >30, corridor fire-resistance rating is **0 hours** (not required). Without sprinklers, it would be 1 hour. However, fire-rated corridors in high-rises are common and must be maintained.

### Corridor Width (Table 1020.3)

| Occupancy | Minimum Width |
|-----------|--------------|
| Any facility not listed | 44 inches |
| Occupant load < 50 | 36 inches |
| Group E, corridor occupant load >= 100 | 72 inches |
| Group I-2, I-3 bed movement | 96 inches |

### Corridor Obstruction (1020.4)
The minimum width or required capacity of corridors shall be unobstructed.

### Dead-End Corridors (1020.5)
Where more than one exit or exit access doorway is required, dead-end corridors shall not exceed **20 feet** in length.

**Exceptions:**
1. Group I-3: up to 50 feet.
2. Groups B, E, F, M, R-1, R-2, S, U with sprinkler system per 903.3.1.1: up to **50 feet**.

### Corridor Continuity (1020.7)
Fire-resistance-rated corridors shall be continuous from the point of entry to an exit, and shall not be interrupted by intervening rooms.

> **FOR LTIA**: After demolition, the remaining corridor layout must still comply with dead-end limits and continuity requirements. If demolition removes partitions that previously defined a corridor, the resulting open space must still provide a compliant egress path.
