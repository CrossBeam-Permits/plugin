# CEC Articles 312 & 408 — Panel Enclosures, Wiring, and Breakers

Source: 2025 California Electrical Code (Title 24, Part 3), Articles 110, 240, 312, and 408.

These requirements govern the physical installation of panel enclosures, internal wiring practices, breaker installation, circuit directories, and covers. They are among the most frequently cited items during panel upgrade inspections because inspectors can visually verify compliance at the open panel.

---

## Enclosure Installation (Article 312)

### 312.3 — Flush-Mount Installation

| Wall Type | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Combustible (wood-frame)** | Enclosure must be installed **flush** with the finished surface |
| **Noncombustible (steel stud, concrete)** | Maximum setback of **6 mm (1/4 in)** from finished surface |

### 312.4 — Plaster Gap

The maximum gap between the edge of a flush-mounted panel and the surrounding plaster/drywall finish shall not exceed **3 mm (1/8 in)**.

### 110.12(A) — Unused Openings

All unused openings (knockouts) in cabinets and enclosures must be effectively closed to provide protection substantially equivalent to the wall of the enclosure. Exception: openings provided by the manufacturer for mounting purposes.

**Common violation**: Missing knockout fillers where old conduit connections were removed during the upgrade.

## Panel Orientation and Mounting

### 240.33 — Vertical Position

Enclosures for overcurrent devices must be mounted in a **vertical position** unless the construction of the enclosure makes this impractical.

### 408.43 — Face-Up Position Prohibited

Panelboards shall not be installed in a face-up position (i.e., breaker handles pointing upward toward the ceiling with the panel face horizontal).

### Mounting Height (240.24(A))

The maximum height of any breaker handle (in its highest position) shall not exceed **2.0 m (6 ft 7 in)** above the floor or working platform.

## Circuit Directories and Covers

### 408.4(A) — Circuit Directory

Every circuit in the panelboard must be **legibly identified** as to its clear, evident, and specific purpose or use. The identification must be of **sufficient detail** to distinguish each circuit from all others.

Requirements:
- Each circuit shall be identified
- Spare positions containing unused OCPDs shall be labeled as "spare"
- Directory shall be located on the face or inside the door of the panel

### 408.4(B) — Source of Supply

Each panelboard supplied by a feeder in other than one- or two-family dwellings shall have a **durable, legible label** (not handwritten) indicating where the power originates, if the source is in a different location than the panel.

**For panel upgrades**: If the old main panel becomes a subpanel fed from a new main, label the source.

### 408.38 — Dead-Front Cover

Panelboards shall be provided with a **dead-front cover** (the interior cover that prevents access to live bus bars while still allowing breaker operation).

### 110.12(A) — Fill Plates

Fill plates shall be installed in any missing twist-out openings (breaker slots) that do not have breakers installed. This prevents accidental contact with live bus bars.

## Breaker Installation (Overcurrent Protection Devices)

### 110.3(B) — Listed and Installed Per Instructions

All equipment (including breakers) shall be installed and used in accordance with the manufacturer's listing and labeling instructions. This means:
- Only listed breakers that are **classified for the specific panel** may be used
- Torque specifications from the manufacturer must be followed

### 408.36(D) — Back-Fed Breakers

Back-fed breakers (e.g., solar inverter connections, generator interlocks) shall be **secured in place** by an additional fastener that requires other than a pull to release.

**Exception (705.12(E))**: Output circuits from listed utility-interactive inverters may not require additional securing for back-fed breakers.

### 240.81 — Breaker Position Indication

Circuit breakers must clearly indicate whether they are in the **ON**, **OFF**, or **TRIPPED** position.

If a breaker operates **vertically**, the **UP** position must indicate **ON**.

### 210.7 — Shared Yoke Receptacles

For receptacles on a shared yoke or mounting strap, either a **2-pole breaker** or **two single-pole breakers with approved handle ties** must be used.

## Internal Wiring

### 408.41 — One Wire Per Terminal

Only **one conductor per terminal** unless the terminal is specifically listed and labeled for multiple conductors.

**408.41 Exception 1**: Where the terminal is specifically identified for more than one conductor.

**Critical**: Equipment grounding conductors (EGCs) and neutral conductors may NOT share the same terminal, even in service equipment where both may be on the same terminal bar.

### 110.14(A) — Terminal Connections

Terminals for more than one conductor must be so identified. This reinforces the one-wire-per-terminal rule.

### 110.14 — Antioxidant on Aluminum

Antioxidant compound shall be applied on aluminum conductors as required by the manufacturer's installation instructions.

### 110.3(B) — Torque Requirements

All breakers and terminals must be torqued per manufacturer specifications. Inspectors may require torque verification marks or documentation.

### 312.5(C) — Cable Securing

Each cable entering a cabinet or enclosure shall be **secured to the cabinet** with an appropriate cable connector or clamp.

### 312.7 — Conductor Crowding

Conductors inside cabinets shall not be crowded to the point where they impair proper heat dissipation. Wiring must be arranged to allow adequate cooling.

### 312.8 — Splices and Taps Within Cabinets

| Fill Limit | Requirement |
|------------|-------------|
| **Splices and taps** | Shall not fill more than **40%** of the available wiring space |
| **All conductors combined** | Shall not exceed **75%** of the cross-sectional area |

### 312.8(B) — Power Monitoring Equipment

Power-monitoring or energy management equipment and conductors are acceptable within an enclosure if listed and meeting the fill requirements above.

### 312.8(A)(3) — Feed-Through Conductors

Feed-through conductors are allowed to pass through the panel if a **warning label** is applied to the enclosure identifying the power source.

### 312.6 / 408.3(G) — Wire Bending Space

Wire bending space in panelboards must be provided per Table 312.6(A) and Table 312.6(B). The minimum bending space depends on:
- Wire size
- Number of wires per terminal
- Whether the terminal is at the end or mid-point of a run

**For standard residential panels**: The manufacturer designs the enclosure to provide adequate bending space for the listed wire sizes. However, if large conductors (250 kcmil+) are used, verify the panel is rated for that wire size.

## EGC and Neutral Bar Requirements

### 408.40 — Grounding Terminal Bar

A **grounding terminal bar** is required in the panel if wire-type equipment grounding conductors are present. The bar must be:
- Secured directly to the enclosure
- Bonded to the enclosure

### 408.41 — EGC/Neutral Separation

Even though service equipment may have neutral and ground buses that are bonded together, **individual EGCs and neutral conductors must NOT share the same terminal**. Each gets its own terminal connection.

## Common Panel Wiring Inspection Failures

1. **Double-tapped breakers** — two wires on a single-pole breaker terminal not rated for two conductors
2. **Missing knockout fillers** — open holes in panel enclosure
3. **No dead-front cover** — interior cover missing
4. **Missing fill plates** — empty breaker slots without blanks
5. **Back-fed breaker not secured** — solar or generator back-feed breaker can be pulled out
6. **Illegible or missing circuit directory** — circuits not identified or handwritten and unreadable
7. **Aluminum wires without antioxidant** — corrosion risk at terminations
8. **Over-filled panel** — too many conductors, wires crowded, exceeding 75% fill
9. **Breakers not listed for the panel** — wrong manufacturer's breaker in the panel
10. **Panel not mounted vertically** — tilted or face-up installation
