# Fire-Rated Glazing and Fire Separation Requirements

> Source: 2025 California Residential Code (Title 24, Part 2.5), 2025 California Building Code (Title 24, Part 2)
> Sections: CRC R302.1, Tables R302.1(1)-(2); CBC 705, 716.1, Tables 716.1(1)-(3)

---

## Overview

Windows and doors in or near fire-rated walls are subject to strict limitations. The fire separation distance (distance from the wall to the property line or an imaginary line between buildings on the same lot) controls whether openings are allowed, how much wall area they may occupy, and whether fire-rated glazing is required.

**This is critical for window replacements** -- replacing a window in a wall close to a property line may trigger fire-separation compliance that wasn't enforced when the original window was installed.

---

## Fire Separation Distance Rules -- Residential (CRC R302.1)

### Table R302.1(1) -- Standard (No Sprinklers)

| Element | Rating Required | Min Fire Separation Distance |
|---|---|---|
| Walls -- fire-resistance rated | 1-hour (both sides) | 0 feet |
| Walls -- not rated | 0 hours | >= 5 feet |
| **Openings -- NOT ALLOWED** | N/A | **< 3 feet** |
| **Openings -- 25% max of wall area** | 0 hours | **3 feet** |
| **Openings -- unlimited** | 0 hours | **>= 5 feet** |
| Penetrations | Per R302.4 | < 3 feet |
| Penetrations | None required | >= 3 feet |

### Table R302.1(2) -- With Automatic Sprinklers (R309)

| Element | Rating Required | Min Fire Separation Distance |
|---|---|---|
| Walls -- fire-resistance rated | 1-hour (exterior side) | 0 feet |
| Walls -- not rated | 0 hours | >= 3 feet |
| **Openings -- NOT ALLOWED** | N/A | **< 3 feet** |
| **Openings -- unlimited** | 0 hours | **>= 3 feet** |

**Key exception for sprinklered subdivisions:** Where ALL dwellings are sprinklered, and the adjoining lot provides an open setback of >=6 ft on the opposite side of the property line, fire separation distance for unrated walls and openings may be reduced to 0 feet.

## Practical Implications for Window Replacement

### Scenario 1: Wall is >= 5 ft from property line
- **Openings unlimited**, no fire rating required
- Standard replacement windows are acceptable
- No special glazing requirements (beyond safety glazing where applicable)

### Scenario 2: Wall is 3-5 ft from property line
- **Openings limited to 25% of wall area** (without sprinklers)
- Verify total window/door area in the wall does not exceed 25%
- If replacing a window and existing openings already exceed 25%, FLAG for building official
- No fire-rated glazing required (fire protection rating = 0 hours)

### Scenario 3: Wall is < 3 ft from property line
- **NO OPENINGS PERMITTED** (period)
- Cannot install or replace any window or door in this wall
- Existing windows are pre-existing nonconforming -- replacement should be flagged
- Some jurisdictions allow in-kind replacement as a pre-existing nonconformity; others require removal

### Scenario 4: Townhouse common/separation wall
- **No openings permitted** in common walls separating townhouse units (R302.2.2)
- Common walls must extend continuously from foundation to roof sheathing
- No windows, no doors, no penetrations except water-filled sprinkler piping

---

## Perpendicular Wall Exception

Walls, projections, openings, or penetrations in walls **perpendicular to the line** used to determine fire separation distance are exempt from R302.1 requirements (Exception 1). This means a side wall perpendicular to the property line may have unrestricted openings even if the side yard is narrow.

Fire-rated glazing installed in fire door and fire window assemblies must ALSO comply with Chapter 24 safety glazing requirements where applicable. Both fire rating AND safety glazing may be required simultaneously.

## Townhouse and Duplex Separation Walls

### Townhouse Units (R302.2)
- Common walls: **no openings** permitted
- Double walls: each 1-hour fire-resistance rated
- Common walls: 1-hour (with sprinklers) or 2-hour (without) fire-resistance rated
- Continuous from foundation to roof sheathing

### Two-Family Dwellings (R302.3)
- Separation assemblies: 1-hour rated (or 1/2-hour with sprinklers)
- No specific opening restrictions stated for the separation (unlike townhouses), but the fire-resistance rating must be maintained

---

## Garage Separation (R302.5-R302.6)

Openings from garage to dwelling:
- **No opening directly into a sleeping room**
- Other openings: solid wood door >=1-3/8" thick, solid/honeycomb steel door >=1-3/8" thick, or 20-minute fire-rated door
- Doors must be **self-latching and self-closing** (or automatic-closing)
- Exception for sprinklered residences: only self-closing and self-latching required (except to sleeping rooms)

Windows in garage walls separating from dwelling must comply with Table R302.6 separation requirements (1/2" gypsum or equivalent on garage side).

---

## Practical Review Checklist for Fire Separation

1. **Determine fire separation distance** from property line to the wall containing the window
2. **< 3 ft:** No openings allowed -- replacement is nonconforming, FLAG
3. **3-5 ft:** Calculate total opening area as percentage of wall area -- must be <=25%
4. **>= 5 ft:** No restrictions on opening area or rating
5. **Fire-rated wall?** If wall has a fire-resistance rating, replacement glazing must maintain that rating
6. **Townhouse common wall?** No openings permitted -- period
7. **Garage to dwelling?** No opening to sleeping rooms; rated door required for other openings
8. **Commercial building?** Check Table 716.1(3) for fire window assembly rating requirements
