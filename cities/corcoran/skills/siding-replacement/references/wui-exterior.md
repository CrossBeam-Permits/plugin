# WUI Fire-Resistive Exterior Wall Requirements

Source: 2025 California Wildland-Urban Interface Code (Title 24, Part 7), Chapter 5, Sections 503-504.5; California Building Code (Part 2), Chapter 7A; California Residential Code (Part 2.5), Section R337.

---

## Overview

California's Wildland-Urban Interface (WUI) Code imposes additional exterior wall requirements for buildings in designated fire hazard areas. When replacing siding in a WUI zone, the new exterior wall covering must meet ignition-resistant construction standards.

**CRC R337 directs:** All provisions for materials and construction methods for exterior wildfire exposure are in Part 7, California Wildland-Urban Interface Code.

**CBC Chapter 7A directs:** Same -- all WUI provisions are in Part 7.

---

## Applicability

WUI requirements apply when:
1. The project is in a designated **Wildland-Urban Interface area** or **Fire Hazard Severity Zone** (FHSZ)
2. The building is being constructed, **modified**, or relocated into a WUI area
3. The local jurisdiction has adopted WUI requirements

**Key trigger for siding replacement:** "Modified" includes alterations to exterior wall coverings. Replacing siding in a WUI zone triggers compliance with Chapter 5 of the WUI Code.

### How to Determine WUI Status

- Check with the local building department or fire department
- CAL FIRE Fire Hazard Severity Zone maps (frap.fire.ca.gov)
- Local jurisdiction may have additional mapped areas
- Three severity zones: Moderate, High, Very High (VHFHSZ)

## Exterior Wall Covering Requirements (Section 504.5)

### Option 1: Compliant Wall Assembly (504.5 Items 1-9)

If the exterior wall assembly itself meets one of the following, the wall covering can be of ANY compliant material:

1. **1-hour fire-resistance-rated construction** on the exterior side
2. **Noncombustible materials** (tested per ASTM E136)
3. **Heavy timber** -- sawn or glue-lam with min 4" nominal least dimension
4. **Log wall construction**
5. Wall assemblies tested per **ASTM E2707** (10-minute direct flame contact) meeting acceptance criteria in 504.9.3
6. Wall assemblies tested per **SFM Standard 12-7A-1**
7. Wall assemblies with **one layer 5/8" Type X gypsum sheathing** behind exterior wall covering
8. 1-hour fire-resistance-rated assembly rated from exterior side per ASTM E119 or UL 263
9. **Fire-retardant-treated wood** labeled for exterior use per CBC Section 2303.2

The compliant assembly must extend from top of foundation to underside of roof sheathing.

### Option 2: Compliant Exterior Wall Covering (504.5.2)

If using the wall covering itself for compliance, it must be ONE of:

1. **Noncombustible material** (metal siding, fiber cement, stucco, stone, concrete)
2. **Ignition-resistant building material** labeled for exterior use (per Section 503.2)
3. **Fire-retardant-treated wood** labeled for exterior use per CBC 2303.2
4. **Fire-retardant-treated wood shingles/shakes** qualified per CBC 1505.6 as "Class B" roof covering -- acceptable as wall covering when installed over solid sheathing

**504.5.2.1 Extent:** Exterior wall coverings must extend from **top of foundation** to **roof**, and terminate at:
- 2" nominal solid wood blocking between rafters at all roof overhangs, OR
- The enclosure (in case of enclosed eaves)

## Ignition-Resistant Building Material (Section 503.2)

Materials qualifying as "ignition-resistant building material" must meet ALL of the following:

1. **Designed for exterior use and weather exposure**
2. Tested per extended ASTM E84 or UL 723 (30-minute test) or ASTM E2768 on BOTH front and back faces
3. Panel products tested with 1/8" ripped longitudinal gap
4. **Flame spread index not exceeding 25** (503.2.4.1)
5. **Flame front not progressing more than 10'-6"** beyond burner centerline (503.2.4.2)
6. **Weathering durability** maintained per 503.2.4.3

Alternative qualifying materials:
- Noncombustible materials per ASTM E136 (503.2.1)
- Fire-retardant-treated wood for exterior use per CBC 2303.2 (503.2.2)

### Important: Surface Treatments NOT Acceptable (503.2.5)

**Paints, coatings, stains, or other surface treatments are NOT an approved method of protection** under the WUI code. The material itself must be inherently ignition-resistant.

## Common Siding Materials -- WUI Compliance

| Material | Noncombustible? | Ignition-Resistant? | Compliant in WUI? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Fiber cement siding | No (but close) | Yes -- typically meets 503.2 requirements | **Yes** -- most common WUI-compliant siding |
| Metal siding (steel, aluminum) | Yes | N/A | **Yes** |
| Stucco (cement plaster) | Yes | N/A | **Yes** |
| Stone/masonry veneer | Yes | N/A | **Yes** |
| Vinyl siding | No | No | **No** -- combustible, not ignition-resistant |
| Wood siding (untreated) | No | No | **No** -- unless FRT wood |
| Wood siding (fire-retardant-treated) | No | Yes | **Yes** -- must be labeled for exterior use |
| Wood shakes/shingles (FRT) | No | Yes | **Yes** -- must be Class B qualified per CBC 1505.6 over solid sheathing |
| Hardboard siding | No | Varies | **Check** -- must test per 503.2 |
| Polypropylene siding | No | No | **No** -- combustible |

## Flashing at WUI (Section 504.5.1)

A minimum of **6 inches of metal flashing or noncombustible material** applied vertically on the exterior of the wall is required at:
- Ground intersections
- Decking intersections
- Roof intersections

This is in ADDITION to standard flashing requirements.

---

## Labeling Requirements (Section 503.3.3)

Materials tested per WUI standards must bear an identification label showing:
1. Identification mark of the approved testing/inspecting agency
2. Contact and identification information of the manufacturer
3. Model number or product identification
4. Pretest weathering specified in the WUI code
5. Compliance standard per Chapter 7

The label must be issued by a testing/inspecting agency approved by the **State Fire Marshal**.

## Eave and Soffit Requirements (Section 504.3)

When replacing siding, eave and soffit conditions may also need attention:

### Enclosed Eaves (504.3)
Exposed underside must be protected by one of:
- Noncombustible materials
- Ignition-resistant building materials
- 1-hour fire-resistance-rated materials on exterior side
- 2" nominal dimension lumber
- 1" nominal FRT lumber or 3/4" nominal FRT plywood (exterior use)
- Tested assemblies per ASTM E2957 or SFM 12-7A-3

**Fascias required** and must be ignition-resistant, FRT wood, 1-hour rated, or 2" nominal lumber.

### Open Eaves (504.3.1)
Exposed roof deck underside must be protected by:
- Noncombustible material
- Ignition-resistant building material
- FRT wood (exterior use)
- 1-hour rated materials on exterior side
- 5/8" Type X gypsum sheathing behind exterior covering on underside

## Vents in WUI (Section 504.10)

When siding replacement involves vent locations:
- All ventilation openings must be covered with **Wildfire Flame and Ember Resistant vents** (approved/listed by State Fire Marshal) or WUI vents tested per ASTM E2886
- Mesh: min 1/16" to max 1/8" openings
- Noncombustible and corrosion-resistant
- Gable-end and dormer vents: min 10 ft from lot lines

## Practical Guidance for WUI Siding Replacement

1. **Always verify WUI status** of the project address before review
2. **Fiber cement is the go-to** -- most affordable ignition-resistant option
3. **Metal siding** (steel or aluminum) is noncombustible -- always compliant
4. **Stucco** (cement plaster) is noncombustible -- always compliant
5. **Vinyl and untreated wood are NOT permitted** in WUI zones
6. **Do not accept paint/coating as WUI compliance** -- 503.2.5 prohibits this
7. **Check that the wall assembly extends fully** from foundation to roof -- no gaps
8. **Verify product labeling** -- must show State Fire Marshal-approved testing
