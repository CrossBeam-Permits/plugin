# Labeling and Marking Requirements for PV Systems

Source: 2025 California Electrical Code (Title 24, Part 3), Articles 690, 705, 110; 2025 California Fire Code (Title 24, Part 9), Section 1205.4.

---

## Field-Applied Marking Standards (CEC 110.21(B))

All field-applied labels and markings shall:
1. Warn of hazards using effective **words, colors, and symbols**
2. Be **permanently affixed** and **not hand-written**
   - Exception: Variable portions intended to be filled in by installer
3. Be of **sufficient durability** to withstand the installed environment

Reference: ANSI Z535.4-2011 (guidelines for safety signs and labels).

**Minimum recommended type size**: 20 point (3/8" or 9.5 mm) -- considered the minimum for permanency and legibility.

## Required Labels -- Complete Inventory

### 1. Maximum DC Voltage Label (CEC 690.7(D))

**Location** (one of):
- DC PV system disconnecting means
- PV electronic power conversion equipment
- Distribution equipment associated with PV system

**Content**: Highest maximum DC voltage in the PV system (calculated per 690.7)

**Format**: Permanent, readily visible

---

### 2. Rapid Shutdown Label at Service Equipment (CEC 690.12(D) / CFC 1205.4.1)

**Location**: Each service equipment location to which PV systems are connected, or approved readily visible location. Within **3 ft of service disconnecting means** (CFC 1205.4.1.2).

**Content -- Type 1 (Full shutdown -- array + conductors)**:
> SOLAR PV SYSTEM IS EQUIPPED WITH RAPID SHUTDOWN.
> TURN RAPID SHUTDOWN SWITCH TO THE "OFF" POSITION TO SHUT DOWN
> PV SYSTEM AND REDUCE SHOCK HAZARD IN ARRAY.

- First two lines: **black on yellow**, uppercase, min **3/8" (9.5 mm)** height
- Remaining text: **black on white**, uppercase, min **3/16" (5 mm)** height
- Include **simple diagram** of building with roof (CFC 1205.4.1.1)

**Content -- Type 2 (Conductor-only shutdown)**:
> THIS SOLAR PV SYSTEM EQUIPPED WITH RAPID SHUTDOWN.
> TURN RAPID SHUTDOWN SWITCH TO THE "OFF" POSITION TO SHUT DOWN
> CONDUCTORS OUTSIDE THE ARRAY. CONDUCTORS WITHIN ARRAY REMAIN ENERGIZED IN SUNLIGHT.

- First two lines: **white on red**, uppercase, min **3/8"** height
- Remaining text: **black on white**, uppercase, min **3/16"** height
- Diagram: red sections = areas NOT shut down

---

### 3. Rapid Shutdown Switch Label (CEC 690.12(D)(2) / CFC 1205.4.3)

**Location**: On or within **3 ft** of the rapid shutdown switch

**Content**:
> RAPID SHUTDOWN SWITCH FOR SOLAR PV SYSTEM

**Format**: **Reflective**, all capitals, minimum **3/8" (9.5 mm)** height, **white on red** background

---

### 4. PV System Disconnect Label (CEC 690.13(B))

**Location**: Each PV system disconnecting means

**Content**:
> PV SYSTEM DISCONNECT

(or equivalent)

**Additional markings** based on system configuration permitted.

**If line/load terminals energized in open position**:
> WARNING
> ELECTRIC SHOCK HAZARD TERMINALS ON THE LINE AND LOAD SIDES MAY BE ENERGIZED IN THE OPEN POSITION

Per 110.21(B).

---

### 5. DC Circuit Labels -- Raceways and Enclosures (CEC 690.31(D)(2))

**Location**: Exposed raceways, cable trays, wiring methods, covers/enclosures of pull/junction boxes, unused conduit body openings containing PV DC circuit conductors.

**Content**:
> PHOTOVOLTAIC POWER SOURCE

or

> SOLAR PV DC CIRCUIT

**Format**:
- All capitals
- Minimum **3/8" (9.5 mm)** height
- **White on red** background
- Visible after installation
- Suitable for installed environment
- On every section separated by enclosures, walls, partitions, ceilings, floors
- Spacing: **not more than 10 ft (3 m)** between labels

---

### 6. DC Conductor Identification (CEC 690.31(B)(2))

**Location**: All termination, connection, and splice points

**Method**: Color coding, marking tape, tagging, or other approved means.

**Polarity**:
- **Positive**: Imprinted "+" or "POSITIVE" or "POS" on insulation NOT green, white, or gray
- **Negative**: Imprinted "-" or "NEGATIVE" or "NEG" on insulation NOT green, white, gray, or red
- **White wire**: ONLY permitted for solidly grounded systems

Exception: Where conductor identification is evident by spacing or arrangement.

---

### 7. Power Source Directory (CEC 705.10)

**Location**: Each service equipment location, or approved readily visible location

**Content**:
1. Location of each power source disconnecting means
2. Emergency telephone numbers of off-site servicing entities
3. **"CAUTION: MULTIPLE SOURCES OF POWER"** -- per 110.21(B)

Exception: Multiple colocated sources may be identified as a group.

---

### 8. Backfed Breaker Warning (CEC 705.12(B)(2))

**Location**: Distribution equipment, adjacent to back-fed breaker from PV source (for 120% rule installations)

**Content**:
> WARNING: POWER SOURCE OUTPUT DO NOT RELOCATE THIS OVERCURRENT DEVICE.

Per 110.21(B).

---

### 9. Equipment Disconnect Warning (CEC 690.15(B))

**Location**: Non-load-break isolating devices

**Content**:
> DO NOT DISCONNECT UNDER LOAD

or

> NOT FOR CURRENT INTERRUPTING

---

### 10. Mating Connector Warning (CEC 690.33(D)(2))

**Location**: Mating connectors not rated for interrupting current

**Content**:
> DO NOT DISCONNECT UNDER LOAD

or

> NOT FOR CURRENT INTERRUPTING

Requires a tool to open.

## Common Inspection Failures

1. **Missing rapid shutdown label at service panel** -- most common labeling deficiency
2. **DC circuit labels missing or incorrect color** -- must be white on red, not just any sticker
3. **Labels not at 10-ft intervals** on exposed DC conduit runs
4. **Missing power source directory** at service equipment (705.10)
5. **Backfed breaker warning label missing** adjacent to PV breaker in panel
6. **Hand-written labels** -- not permitted (110.21(B))
7. **Labels not durable for environment** -- indoor labels used outdoors, fading
8. **Missing max voltage label** at disconnect or inverter
