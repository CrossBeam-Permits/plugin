# Drainage, Grading & Erosion Control

Source: 2025 California Building Code (Title 24, Part 2), Chapters 18, 33, Appendix J; California Plumbing Code (Title 24, Part 5), Chapter 11.

Pool demolition involves significant site disturbance. Proper drainage, grading, and erosion control are required during construction and after completion.

---

## Post-Demolition Site Grading (CBC 1804.4)

The ground immediately adjacent to the foundation shall be sloped away from the building at a slope of not less than 1 unit vertical in 20 units horizontal (5-percent slope) for a minimum distance of 10 feet measured perpendicular to the face of the wall.

Exception 1: Where climatic or soil conditions warrant, the slope of the ground away from the building foundation shall be permitted to be reduced to not less than 1 unit vertical in 48 units horizontal (2-percent slope).

The procedure used to establish the final ground level adjacent to the foundation shall account for additional settlement of the backfill.

> **Pool demo application**: After backfilling the demolished pool, the finished grade must slope away from the house and any other structures at a minimum of 2% (California exception commonly applied in Southern California). The backfilled area will settle over time, so initial grading should account for this. A 2% minimum slope from existing structures must be established and shown on the site plan.

---

## Storm Water During Construction (CBC 3304.1.5)

Projects which disturb less than one acre of soil and are not part of a larger common plan of development which in total disturbs one acre or more, shall manage storm water drainage during construction in accordance with the California Green Building Standards Code (CALGreen), Chapter 4, Division 4.1.

> **Pool demo application**: Pool demolition disturbs the site and exposes soil. Even though it is typically less than one acre, BMP erosion control measures are required during construction:
> - Silt fencing or fiber rolls around the work area
> - Gravel bags at storm drain inlets
> - Covering stockpiled backfill material
> - Stabilizing exposed soil areas promptly
> This is inspected as part of the NPDES/BMP inspection.

---

## Protection of Adjacent Property (CBC 3307.1)

Adjacent public and private property shall be protected from damage during construction, remodeling and demolition work. Protection shall be provided for footings, foundations, party walls, chimneys, skylights and roofs. Provisions shall be made to control water runoff and erosion during construction or demolition activities.

> **Pool demo application**: The pool demolition must not damage neighboring properties. Water runoff and erosion from the demolition site must be controlled. This is especially important during backfilling when large quantities of soil are being moved and stockpiled.

---

## Drainage Across Property Lines (Appendix J, J109.4)

Drainage across property lines shall not exceed that which existed prior to grading. Excess or concentrated drainage shall be contained on site or directed to an approved drainage facility. Erosion of the ground in the area of discharge shall be prevented by installation of nonerosive down drains or other devices.

> **Pool demo application**: The backfilled pool area cannot create new drainage patterns that send more water to neighboring properties than before. The site plan must demonstrate that drainage after demolition matches or improves the pre-existing drainage pattern.

---

## Erosion Control (Appendix J, J110.1)

The faces of cut and fill slopes shall be prepared and maintained to control erosion. This control shall be permitted to consist of effective planting.

Erosion control for the slopes shall be installed as soon as practicable and prior to calling for final inspection.

> **Pool demo application**: The backfilled area should be planted or otherwise stabilized against erosion before final inspection. Since pool demolition under standard permits restricts the area to landscape/garden use only, planting is the typical erosion control method. The upper 18 inches of planting medium facilitates this.

## Shell Drainage Requirements

While not a specific code section (this is standard practice codified in local standard plans), the drainage through the demolished pool shell is critical to preventing water accumulation (CBC 3303.5):

### Pool Shell Drainage Holes
- **Pool**: Two (2) drainage holes, 36 inches in diameter, drilled or broken into the bottom
- One hole must be at the lowest point of the pool
- Holes should be equally spaced across the deep end and levels
- **Spa**: One (1) drainage hole, 36 inches in diameter, at the lowest point

### Gravel Drainage Layer
- 12-inch layer of #4 rock (1-inch to 2.5-inch Class II permeable free-draining rock)
- Placed over the drain holes and pool shell bottom
- Provides permanent sub-surface drainage path

> **Key principle**: The drainage holes and gravel layer work together to prevent water from being trapped inside the remaining pool shell, which would cause hydrostatic pressure buildup and potential ground heaving or settlement. This directly implements CBC 3303.5 (prevention of water accumulation).

## Backfill Material Storage During Construction

### Public Right-of-Way (CBC 3308.1)

The temporary use of streets or public property for the storage or handling of materials or of equipment required for construction or demolition, and the protection provided to the public shall comply with the provisions of the applicable governing authority.

### Obstruction (CBC 3308.1.1)

Construction materials and equipment shall not be placed or stored so as to obstruct access to fire hydrants, standpipes, fire or police alarm boxes, catch basins or manholes.

> **Pool demo application**: Backfill materials (sand, fill dirt) cannot be stored on public streets or rights-of-way without an encroachment permit. All stockpiled materials on the property must be protected with erosion control measures (covered with tarps, surrounded by silt fencing, etc.).
