# Gas Piping Requirements

Source: 2025 California Plumbing Code (Title 24, Part 5), Chapter 12.

## Appliance Shutoff Valve (CPC 1212.6)

Each gas appliance shall have an accessible, approved **manual shutoff valve** with a nondisplaceable valve member.

Key rules:
- The shutoff valve shall be located within **6 ft** of the appliance it serves
- Shall serve a single appliance only
- Where a connector is used, the valve shall be installed **upstream** of the connector
- A **union or flanged connection** shall be provided downstream from the valve to permit removal of appliance controls

## Sediment Trap (CPC 1212.9)

A sediment trap shall be installed **downstream of the appliance shutoff valve**, as close to the inlet of the appliance as practical.

The sediment trap shall be either:
- A tee fitting with a capped nipple in the bottom outlet (drip leg), OR
- Another device recognized as an effective sediment trap

**Exceptions** (not required): Ranges, clothes dryers, decorative appliances in fireplaces, gas fireplaces, outdoor cooking appliances.

## Appliance Connections (CPC 1212.1)

Appliances shall be connected to building piping by one of:
1. Rigid metallic pipe and fittings
2. Semirigid metallic tubing and metallic fittings
3. Listed connector per ANSI Z21.24/CSA 6.10 (same room, one per appliance)
4. Listed outdoor connector per ANSI Z21.75/CSA 6.27
5. CSST per manufacturer's instructions (fixed appliances only)

### CSST Restriction (CPC 1212.1(5))

CSST shall NOT be directly routed into a metallic appliance enclosure where the appliance is connected to a metallic vent terminating above the roofline.

### Protection (CPC 1212.1.1)

Connectors and tubing shall be protected against physical and thermal damage.

## Gas Piping Installation (CPC 1210.0)

### Support

Gas piping shall be supported at intervals per CPC Table 1210.4:

| Pipe Size | Max Support Interval |
|-----------|---------------------|
| 1/2 in. - 1 in. | 6 ft |
| 1-1/4 in. - 2 in. | 8 ft |
| 2-1/2 in. and larger | 10 ft |

### Protection from Damage

- Piping shall be protected from physical damage
- Piping in concealed locations shall be protected where passing through framing members
- Steel nail plates required where piping is within 1-1/2 in. of face of framing

### Prohibited Locations

- Gas piping shall not be installed in or through air ducts, clothes chutes, elevator shafts, or similar spaces
- Gas piping shall not be installed in solid floors unless enclosed in an approved conduit

## Electrical Bonding (CPC 1211.0)

Each above-ground portion of a gas piping system that is likely to become energized shall be electrically continuous and bonded to an effective ground-fault current path per the California Electrical Code.

**CSST** shall be bonded to the grounding electrode system per the manufacturer's installation instructions and the CEC.

## Pressure Testing (CPC 1213.3)

### Standard Test

Gas piping shall stand a pressure of **not less than 10 psi** gauge pressure for **not less than 15 minutes** with no perceptible drop.

### Welded or High-Pressure Piping

For welded piping or piping carrying gas at pressures exceeding 14 in. w.c.:
- Test pressure: not less than **60 psi**
- Duration: not less than **30 minutes**

### CSST High-Pressure

For CSST at pressures exceeding 14 in. w.c.:
- Test pressure: not less than **30 psi** for **30 minutes**

### Test Medium

Air, nitrogen, CO2, or inert gas. **Oxygen shall NOT be used.**

### Detection

Leaks shall be located by approved gas detector or noncorrosive leak-detecting fluid. Any reduction in test pressure indicates a leak unless attributed to another cause.

## Gas Piping Sizing (CPC 1215.0)

Gas piping shall be sized per CPC Chapter 12 sizing tables, accounting for:
- Total connected BTU/h load
- Length of piping from meter to appliance
- Specific gravity of gas
- Inlet pressure and allowable pressure drop

### Sizing Method

The most common method uses:
- CPC Table 1215.2(3) for natural gas at 0.60 specific gravity
- Based on longest run length and total BTU demand
- Pressure drop of 0.5 in. w.c. (for typical residential)
