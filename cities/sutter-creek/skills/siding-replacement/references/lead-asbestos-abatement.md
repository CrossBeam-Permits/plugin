# Lead Paint and Asbestos Abatement Requirements

Source: California Health and Safety Code, Cal/OSHA Title 8, EPA 40 CFR 745 (Renovation, Repair, and Painting Rule), SCAQMD Rule 1403.

---

## Lead Paint

### When Lead Paint Rules Apply

**Any residential structure built before 1978** is presumed to have lead-based paint unless tested and confirmed otherwise.

Siding replacement on pre-1978 homes triggers lead-safe work practices because:
- Removing siding disturbs painted surfaces
- Scraping, sanding, or demolishing painted components generates lead dust
- Exterior paint is especially likely to contain lead (weathering resistance)

### EPA Renovation, Repair, and Painting (RRP) Rule (40 CFR 745)

| Requirement | Detail |
|---|---|
| Applicability | Renovation of pre-1978 housing and child-occupied facilities |
| Firm certification | Contractor must be EPA-certified renovation firm |
| Certified renovator | At least one EPA-certified renovator on each job |
| Training | Initial 8-hour training + refresher every 5 years |
| Notification | Distribute EPA pamphlet "Renovate Right" to occupants before work begins |
| Work practices | Contain work area, minimize dust, prohibit open-flame burning of lead paint |
| Prohibited methods | Heat guns >1100F, power sanding without HEPA vacuum, dry scraping >2 sq ft |
| Cleaning verification | Visual inspection + cleaning verification using wet cloths |
| Recordkeeping | Maintain records for 3 years |

### California-Specific Lead Requirements

California may have additional requirements beyond EPA RRP:

- **Cal/OSHA Lead in Construction Standard (Title 8, Section 1532.1):** Employer must protect workers from lead exposure. PEL = 50 ug/m3, Action Level = 30 ug/m3
- **DTSC Lead Accreditation:** California Department of Toxic Substances Control accredits lead-related construction workers
- **Disposal:** Lead-contaminated debris must be tested and disposed of properly. May qualify as hazardous waste requiring manifested disposal

### Testing

- **XRF (X-ray fluorescence):** Non-destructive field test, results in seconds
- **Paint chip analysis:** Laboratory test, more definitive
- **Threshold:** >1.0 mg/cm2 by XRF or >0.5% by weight = lead-based paint
- **Presumption:** If not tested, presume lead-based paint for pre-1978 homes

## Asbestos

### When Asbestos Rules Apply

Asbestos was commonly used in building materials from the 1920s through the 1980s. Materials potentially containing asbestos in exterior wall assemblies:

| Material | Asbestos Risk |
|---|---|
| Asbestos-cement siding (Transite) | **High** -- these are asbestos products by definition |
| Older stucco (pre-1980) | **Moderate** -- some mixes contained chrysotile fibers |
| Mastic/adhesives behind siding | **Moderate** -- black mastic often contains asbestos |
| Joint compound at sheathing | **Low to moderate** -- some contained asbestos |
| Caulking at windows/penetrations | **Low to moderate** -- older caulks may contain asbestos |
| Insulation in wall cavity | **Moderate** -- vermiculite insulation may contain asbestos |
| Felt paper/building paper | **Low** -- some older products contained asbestos |

### California Asbestos Requirements

#### SCAQMD Rule 1403 (South Coast Air Quality Management District)

Applies to renovation and demolition in Los Angeles, Orange, Riverside, and San Bernardino counties:

| Requirement | Detail |
|---|---|
| Survey | Asbestos survey required before any renovation/demolition |
| Notification | Written notification to SCAQMD 10 working days before work begins |
| Threshold | Any amount of asbestos-containing material (ACM) triggers requirements |
| NESHAP | Must comply with EPA National Emission Standards for Hazardous Air Pollutants |
| Removal | Regulated ACM must be removed before other demolition begins |
| Disposal | Disposed at approved landfill, wetted, in sealed containers |

#### Cal/OSHA Asbestos in Construction (Title 8, Section 1529)

| Requirement | Detail |
|---|---|
| PEL | 0.1 fiber per cubic centimeter (f/cc) -- 8-hour TWA |
| Excursion limit | 1.0 f/cc -- 30-minute period |
| Survey | Must determine presence, location, and quantity of ACM before renovation |
| Classes of work | Class I (highest risk) through Class IV (lowest risk) |
| Training | Workers must have appropriate class-level training |
| Medical surveillance | Required for workers above PEL |
| Air monitoring | Required during removal activities |

### Asbestos-Cement (Transite) Siding

If the existing siding is asbestos-cement (Transite), special handling is required:

1. **Do NOT break, cut, sand, or drill asbestos-cement siding** -- this releases fibers
2. **Wet removal** -- keep material wet during removal to minimize fiber release
3. **Intact removal** -- remove panels whole if possible (nails cut or carefully removed)
4. **Bagging** -- double-bag in 6-mil polyethylene bags, labeled "DANGER -- CONTAINS ASBESTOS"
5. **Disposal** -- at permitted landfill as asbestos waste
6. **Licensed contractor** -- must hold California DOSH asbestos registration
7. **Air monitoring** -- may be required during removal

### Testing for Asbestos

- **Bulk sample analysis:** Collect material sample (by trained person), send to accredited lab
- **PLM (Polarized Light Microscopy):** Standard method, identifies asbestos fibers in bulk material
- **TEM (Transmission Electron Microscopy):** Higher sensitivity, used for air samples
- **Threshold:** Material containing >1% asbestos by PLM is regulated as ACM in California
- **Presumption:** If not tested, materials in buildings built before 1981 should be presumed to contain asbestos

## Permit Review Implications

### What to Flag on Siding Replacement Applications

1. **Year built before 1978:** Flag for lead paint -- verify RRP compliance
2. **Year built before 1981:** Flag for asbestos -- verify survey completed
3. **Existing material is asbestos-cement (Transite):** Require asbestos abatement plan
4. **Existing stucco on pre-1980 building:** Flag for asbestos testing before removal
5. **Contractor licensing:** Verify appropriate hazardous materials certifications

### Required Documentation

| Document | When Required |
|---|---|
| Lead paint test results or presumption statement | Pre-1978 homes |
| EPA-certified renovator documentation | Pre-1978 homes |
| Asbestos survey results | Pre-1981 buildings or any suspect material |
| SCAQMD/AQMD notification (10-day) | Any renovation disturbing ACM (SoCal) |
| Asbestos abatement plan | When regulated ACM will be disturbed |
| Hazardous waste manifests | For disposal of lead or asbestos waste |

| License | Required For |
|---|---|
| C-22 (Asbestos Abatement) | Removing asbestos-containing materials |
| DOSH Asbestos Registration | Required in addition to C-22 |
| EPA RRP Firm Certification | Lead-safe renovation work |
| EPA Certified Renovator | Individual certification for lead work |
| B (General Building) or C-8 (Concrete) | General siding/stucco work |
