# CPC Section 603 — Backflow Prevention (Cross-Connection Control)

Source: 2025 California Plumbing Code (Title 24, Part 5), Chapter 6.

---

## Overview

Cross-connection control prevents contamination of the potable water supply by backflow (back-siphonage or backpressure). In a residential repipe, the most common backflow scenarios involve hose bibbs, irrigation systems, and pool/spa makeup water connections.

---

## Hose Bibbs (603.5.7)

Potable water outlets with hose attachments (other than water heater drains, boiler drains, and clothes washer connections) shall be protected by one of:

1. **Nonremovable hose bibb-type backflow preventer**
2. **Nonremovable hose bibb-type vacuum breaker**
3. **Atmospheric vacuum breaker** installed not less than 6 inches above the highest point of usage, on the discharge side of the last valve

> In freezing climates: a listed self-draining frost-proof hose bibb with integral backflow preventer or vacuum breaker.

---

## Irrigation Systems (603.5.6)

Systems having NO pumps and NO chemical injection shall be protected by one of:
1. Atmospheric vacuum breaker (AVB)
2. Pressure vacuum breaker backflow prevention assembly (PVB)
3. Spill-resistant pressure vacuum breaker (SVB)
4. Reduced-pressure principle backflow prevention assembly (RP)

### With Chemical Injectors (603.5.6.3)

Where systems include a chemical injector or provisions for chemical injection, protection shall be a **reduced-pressure principle backflow prevention assembly (RP)**.

---

## Swimming Pools, Spas, and Hot Tubs (603.5.21)

Where a pool or spa has a supply line for makeup water, protection shall be:
1. An **air gap**, OR
2. A **reduced-pressure principle backflow prevention assembly (RP)**

---

## Boilers with Conditioning Chemicals (603.5.10)

Potable water connections to steam or hot water boilers shall be protected by:
- Double check valve backflow prevention assembly, OR
- Backflow preventer with intermediate atmospheric vent and PRV, OR
- Reduced-pressure principle backflow prevention assembly (RP)

Where chemicals are introduced: **RP required**.

---

## Fire Sprinkler Systems with Additives (603.5.14.2)

Where antifreeze, corrosion inhibitors, or other chemicals are added to a fire protection system supplied from potable water:
- **RP required**, OR
- Reduced pressure detector fire protection backflow prevention assembly

---

## Quick Reference — Common Residential Backflow Scenarios

| Connection Point | Hazard Level | Required Device | Code Section |
|-----------------|-------------|----------------|-------------|
| **Hose bibb** | High (backsiphonage) | AVB, hose bibb vacuum breaker, or hose bibb backflow preventer | CPC 603.5.7 |
| **Irrigation (no chemicals)** | Low-High | AVB, PVB, SVB, or RP | CPC 603.5.6 |
| **Irrigation (with chemicals)** | High | RP only | CPC 603.5.6.3 |
| **Pool/spa makeup water** | High | Air gap or RP | CPC 603.5.21 |
| **Boiler (no chemicals)** | Low | DC, backflow preventer with vent, or RP | CPC 603.5.10 |
| **Boiler (with chemicals)** | High | RP only | CPC 603.5.10 |
| **Solar heating (with additives)** | High | RP only | CPC 603.5.6.3 |
