# CEC Article 590 — Temporary Installations

Source: 2025 California Electrical Code (Title 24, Part 3), Article 590.

---

## Scope (590.1)

Covers temporary electric power and lighting installations. This includes both temporary power poles (utility-fed) and temporary generators used during construction, events, or emergencies.

## All Wiring Installations (590.2)

### Other Articles (590.2(A))

Except as specifically modified in Article 590, **all other requirements of the CEC for permanent wiring apply to temporary wiring installations**. This is critical — temporary does not mean exempt from the code. It means only the specific relaxations in Article 590 apply.

### Approval (590.2(B))

Temporary wiring methods shall be acceptable only if **approved based on the conditions of use** and any special requirements of the temporary installation.

---

## Time Constraints (590.3)

### During Construction (590.3(A))

Temporary electric power and lighting installations **shall be permitted during the period of construction, remodeling, maintenance, repair, or demolition** of buildings, structures, equipment, or similar activities.

### 90-Day Limit (590.3(B))

Temporary electric power and lighting installations **shall be permitted for a period not to exceed 90 days** for holiday decorative lighting and similar purposes.

> **For generators**: A temporary generator permit is typically valid for 90 days from permit issuance. After 90 days, the generator shall either be removed, the installation formally permitted for permanent installation using permanent wiring methods, or the temporary permit may be renewed.

### Emergencies and Tests (590.3(C))

Temporary electric power and lighting installations shall be permitted during emergencies and for tests, experiments, and developmental work.

**California Amendment (590.3(C.1) — SFM)**: Temporary electrical power and lighting installations shall be permitted during emergencies and for tests, experiments, and developmental work **as approved by the authority having jurisdiction**.

### Removal (590.3(D))

Temporary wiring **shall be removed immediately upon completion** of construction or purpose for which the wiring was installed.

---

## General Requirements (590.4)

### Services (590.4(A))

Services shall be installed in conformance with **Parts I through VIII of Article 230**, as applicable. This means temporary power poles receiving utility service must comply with all service entrance requirements.

### Feeders (590.4(B))

- Overcurrent protection per 240.4, 240.5, 245.26, 445.12, and 445.13
- Conductors permitted within cable assemblies or multiconductor cords/cables identified in **Table 400.4 for hard usage or extra-hard usage**
- **Type NM, NMC, and SE cables** permitted in any dwelling, building, or structure without height limitation or building construction type limitation and without concealment
- **Type SE cable** permitted in raceway in underground installation

**Exception**: Single insulated conductors permitted where installed for emergencies/tests per 590.3(C) and accessible only to qualified persons.

### Branch Circuits (590.4(C))

- All branch circuits shall originate in an **approved power outlet, switchgear, switchboard or panelboard, motor control center, or fused switch enclosure**
- Same conductor and cable allowances as feeders
- Same NM/NMC/SE relaxations as feeders

**Exception for 590.3(B) or (C)**: Branch circuits may be run as single insulated conductors. For holiday lighting (590.3(B)), voltage to ground shall not exceed 150V, wiring not subject to physical damage, conductors supported on insulators at intervals not more than 10 ft.

### Receptacles (590.4(D))

#### All Receptacles (590.4(D)(1))

- **All receptacles shall be of the grounding type**
- Unless installed in continuous metal raceway or metal-covered cable qualifying as EGC per 250.118, all branch circuits shall include a **separate equipment grounding conductor**
- All receptacles shall be electrically connected to the EGC
- **Receptacles on construction sites shall NOT be installed on any branch circuit that supplies temporary lighting**

#### Receptacles in Wet Locations (590.4(D)(2))

All 15- and 20-ampere, 125- and 250-volt receptacles installed in a wet location shall comply with **406.9(B)(1)** (weatherproof enclosure required).

### Disconnecting Means (590.4(E))

- Suitable disconnecting switches or plug connectors shall be installed to permit **disconnection of all ungrounded conductors** of each temporary circuit
- **Multi-wire branch circuits** shall be provided with a means to disconnect simultaneously all ungrounded conductors at the power outlet or panelboard
- Identified handle ties are permitted

### Lamp Protection (590.4(F))

All lamps for general illumination shall be protected from accidental contact or breakage by a suitable luminaire or lampholder with a guard. Metal guarded sockets shall not be used unless the metal guard is connected to the circuit EGC.

### Splices (590.4(G))

A box, conduit body, or other enclosure with a cover installed shall be required for all splices.

**Exception No. 1 (Construction Sites)**: A box is not required where:
1. Circuit conductors being spliced are all from nonmetallic multiconductor cord or cable assemblies, provided EGC continuity is maintained
2. Circuit conductors being spliced are all from metal-sheathed cable assemblies terminated in listed fittings

**Exception No. 2 (Construction Sites, GFCI-protected permanent circuits used for temp power)**:
1. Box cover not required for splices inside junction boxes with plaster rings
2. Listed pigtail-type lampholders permitted in ceiling-mounted junction boxes with plaster rings
3. Finger safe devices permitted for supplying and connection of devices

### Protection from Accidental Damage (590.4(H))

Flexible cords and cables shall be protected from accidental damage. Sharp corners and projections shall be avoided. Where passing through doorways or other pinch points, protection shall be provided.

### Terminations at Devices (590.4(I))

Flexible cords and cables entering enclosures containing devices shall be secured to the box with fittings listed for connecting flexible cords/cables to boxes.

### Support (590.4(J))

- Cable assemblies and flexible cords/cables shall be supported at intervals that protect from physical damage
- Support by staples, cable ties, straps, or similar fittings installed so as not to cause damage
- **Branch circuits and feeders shall NOT be installed on the floor or on the ground**
- Extension cords not required to comply with this section
- **Vegetation shall NOT be used for support** of overhead spans (exception for holiday lighting with strain relief)

## GFCI Protection for Personnel (590.6)

This is one of the most critical sections for temporary power installations. GFCI protection applies to **all temporary wiring installations** — both utility-fed power poles and on-site generators.

### Receptacle Outlets (590.6(A))

#### Not Part of Permanent Wiring (590.6(A)(1))

**All 125-volt, single-phase, 15-, 20-, and 30-ampere receptacle outlets** that are not part of permanent wiring and are in use by personnel shall have **GFCI protection**.

Listed cord sets or devices incorporating listed GFCI protection for portable use are also permitted.

#### Existing or Permanent Wiring (590.6(A)(2))

GFCI protection shall be provided for all 125-volt, single-phase, 15-, 20-, and 30-ampere receptacles installed or existing as part of permanent wiring and **used for temporary electric power**.

#### Receptacles on Portable Generators 15 kW or Less (590.6(A)(3))

**All 125-volt and 125/250-volt, single-phase, 15-, 20-, and 30-ampere receptacle outlets** on a 15-kW or smaller portable generator **shall have GFCI protection**.

All 15- and 20-ampere receptacles in damp or wet locations shall comply with 406.9(A) and (B).

**Exception for industrial establishments**: Where conditions of maintenance and supervision ensure only qualified personnel are involved, an assured equipment grounding conductor program per 590.6(B)(2) is permitted for receptacles supplying equipment that would create greater hazard if power were interrupted or is not compatible with GFCI protection.

### Other Receptacle Outlets (590.6(B))

For receptacles other than those in 590.6(A)(1) through (A)(3), protection shall be provided by either:

1. **GFCI protection** (590.6(B)(1)), OR
2. **Assured equipment grounding conductor program** (590.6(B)(2))

### Assured Equipment Grounding Conductor Program (590.6(B)(2))

A **written program** continuously enforced at the site by designated person(s). Requires:
- All EGCs tested for continuity (electrically continuous)
- Each receptacle and attachment plug tested for correct EGC attachment
- Tests performed: before first use, when damage is evident, before return to service after repairs, and at intervals not exceeding **3 months**
- Tests recorded and available to AHJ
- Program documented and available to AHJ

## Guarding (590.7)

For wiring **over 600 volts nominal**, suitable fencing, barriers, or other effective means shall limit access to authorized and qualified personnel only.

---

## Overcurrent Protective Devices (590.8)

### Reused Devices (590.8(A))

OCPDs previously used and installed in a temporary installation shall be **examined** to ensure proper installation, proper maintenance, and no evidence of impending failure.

### Service OCPDs (590.8(B))

For solidly grounded wye services >150V to ground but not exceeding 1000V phase-to-phase, with available fault current >10,000A, overcurrent protective devices **shall be current limiting**.
