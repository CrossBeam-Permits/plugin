# Sign Electrical Requirements

Source: 2025 California Electrical Code (Title 24, Part 3), Article 600 — Electric Signs and Outline Lighting; CBC Appendix H Section H106.

---

## Scope (CEC 600.1)

Article 600 covers the installation of conductors, equipment, and field wiring for:
- Electric signs
- Retrofit kits
- Outline lighting
- All installations using neon tubing (signs, decorative elements, skeleton tubing, art forms)

This applies regardless of voltage. Illumination systems include cold cathode neon tubing, HID lamps, fluorescent, incandescent, LEDs, electroluminescent, and inductance lighting.

## Listing and Labeling (CEC 600.3)

Fixed, mobile, or portable electric signs, section signs, outline lighting, PV powered signs, and retrofit kits shall be:
- **Listed and labeled** (UL 48 for electric signs)
- Provided with installation instructions
- Installed in conformance with the listing

**Exceptions:**
- Field-installed skeleton tubing does not require listing when installed per the CEC.
- Outline lighting does not require system listing when it consists of listed luminaires wired per Chapter 3.

## Markings (CEC 600.4)

### Required Markings
Signs and outline lighting systems must be marked with:
- Manufacturer's name, trademark, or other identification
- Input voltage and current rating
- Markings must be visible after installation and permanently applied
- May be installed in a location not viewed by the public

### Retrofitted Signs (600.4(B))
Retrofitted signs must be marked that the illumination system has been replaced, with kit provider and installer identification.

### Incandescent Lamp Signs (600.4(C))
Must indicate maximum allowable lamp wattage per lampholder, permanently installed, letters at least 1/4" high, visible during relamping.

## Required Branch Circuits (CEC 600.5)

### Mandatory Sign Outlet (600.5(A))

Each commercial building and each commercial occupancy accessible to pedestrians must have:
- At least **one outlet** at each entrance to each tenant space for sign use
- Supplied by a **dedicated 20-ampere branch circuit** (no other loads)

**Exceptions:**
- Not required at service/delivery entrances intended only for service personnel.
- The branch circuit may supply loads directly related to sign control (electronic/electromechanical controllers).

### Branch Circuit Ratings (600.5(C))

| Sign Type | Maximum Branch Circuit Rating |
|---|---|
| Neon tubing signs | 30 amperes |
| All other signs | 20 amperes |

**Important:** Sign branch circuits are classified as **continuous loads** for calculation purposes. This means conductor and overcurrent device sizing must account for 125% of the load.

### Wiring Methods (600.5(D))

- Supply wiring must terminate within a sign, outline lighting enclosure, suitable box, conduit body, or panelboard.
- Listed sign enclosures may serve as pull/junction boxes for adjacent signs, outline lighting, and sign floodlights (voltages up to 600V).
- Metal or nonmetallic poles supporting signs may enclose supply conductors.

## Disconnect Requirements (CEC 600.6)

### General Rule
Each sign and outline lighting system must be controlled by an **externally operable switch or circuit breaker** that:
- Opens all ungrounded conductors
- Controls no other load

**Exceptions:**
- Exit directional signs within a building do not require a disconnect.
- Cord-connected signs with an attachment plug do not require a separate disconnect.

### Disconnect Location (600.6(A))

The disconnect must be accessible and located per one of the following:

| Location Option | Requirements |
|---|---|
| **At point of entry** (600.6(A)(1)) | At the point the feeder/branch circuit enters the sign enclosure, sign body, or pole. Must open all ungrounded conductors. |
| **Within sight of sign** (600.6(A)(2)) | Within sight of the sign it controls. If out of line of sight from any energized section, must be lockable per 110.25. Permanent label required identifying disconnect location. |
| **Within sight of controller** (600.6(A)(3)) | For signs with external electronic/electromechanical controllers. Disconnect within sight of controller or in same enclosure. Must disconnect both sign and controller. |
| **Remote location** (600.6(A)(4)) | Must be accessible to first responders and service personnel. Location must be marked with a label at the sign location. |

### Control Switch Rating (600.6(B))

Switches controlling transformers and electronic power supplies must be:
- Rated for controlling inductive loads, OR
- Have a current rating not less than **2x** the transformer or power supply current rating.

## Grounding and Bonding (CEC 600.7)

### Equipment Grounding
- Metal equipment of signs, outline lighting, and skeleton tubing must be grounded via the equipment grounding conductor of the supply branch circuit(s) or feeder.
- Size per 250.122.
- **Exception:** Portable cord-connected signs with double insulation do not require equipment grounding conductor.

### Bonding Requirements
- All metal parts and equipment must be bonded together and to the associated transformer/power supply EGC.
- Metal building parts shall NOT be used for bonding sign equipment.
- Listed flexible metal conduit or liquidtight FMC enclosing secondary conductors may serve as bonding means if total length does not exceed 100 ft.
- Small metal parts (<=2" in any dimension), not likely energized, and spaced >=3/4" from neon tubing do not require bonding.
- Bonding conductors: copper min 14 AWG, or copper-clad aluminum min 12 AWG.

### Signs in Fountains (600.7(B)(8))
All metal parts must be bonded to the equipment grounding conductor of the fountain recirculating system branch circuit.

## Enclosures (CEC 600.8)

### General
Live parts (other than lamps and neon tubing) must be enclosed.

### Material
Enclosures must be metal or listed.

### Minimum Metal Thickness

| Material | Minimum Thickness |
|---|---|
| Sheet copper or aluminum | 0.020" (0.51 mm) |
| Sheet steel | 0.016" (0.41 mm) |

Metal parts must be protected from corrosion.

## Location Requirements (CEC 600.9)

### Clearance Heights

| Location | Minimum Clearance |
|---|---|
| Areas accessible to vehicles | 14 ft above grade (unless protected from physical damage) |
| Pedestrian areas (neon tubing) | Must be protected from physical damage |

### Adjacent to Combustible Materials (600.9(C))
- Signs must not subject adjacent combustible materials to temperatures exceeding 194 deg F (90 deg C).
- Minimum 2" spacing between wood/combustible materials and incandescent or HID lamps/lampholders.

### Wet Locations (600.9(D))
Signs and equipment for wet locations must be:
- Listed watertight type, OR
- Weatherproof with drain holes:
  - Drain holes: 1/4" to 1/2" diameter
  - At least one drain hole at every low point
  - Positioned without external obstructions

## Illumination Requirements (CBC H106.1)

Signs shall only be illuminated by **electrical means** (no open spark or flame for display unless specifically approved).

### Internally Illuminated Signs (H106.1.1)

Where internally illuminated signs have facings of wood or approved plastic (per CBC 2606.4):
- Maximum facing area: **120 sq ft** per section
- Wiring must be entirely enclosed in the sign cabinet
- Minimum **2" clearance** from facing material to wiring

**Exception for flexible sign face plastic:** The 120 sq ft limitation does not apply to flame-resistant-coated fabric weighing less than 20 oz/sq yd that passes NFPA 701 Test 1 and Test 2.

## Portable/Mobile Signs (CEC 600.10)

- Must be adequately supported and readily movable without tools.
- Must have an attachment plug for each sign.

### Wet/Damp Location Portable Signs
- Cords: junior hard-service or hard-service type with EGC.
- **GFCI protection required** — listed, integral to the attachment plug or within 12" of it.

### Dry Location Portable Signs
- Cords: SP-2, SPE-2, SPT-2, or heavier.
- Cord maximum length: 15 ft.

## Transformers, Power Supplies, and Ballasts (CEC 600.21-600.24)

### General Requirements
- Must be listed and identified for the use.
- Must be accessible and securely fastened.
- Installed as near to lamps/neon tubing as practicable (keep secondary conductors short).
- Working space: minimum 3 ft x 3 ft x 3 ft at each unit.

### Ballasts (600.22)
- Must be thermally protected.

### Transformers and Electronic Power Supplies (600.23)
- Must have secondary-circuit ground-fault protection (with exceptions for isolated ungrounded secondaries <=7500V and integral porcelain/glass housing units).
- Secondary voltage: max 15,000 volts nominal under any load.
- Voltage to ground of any output terminal: max 7,500 volts.
- Secondary current rating: max 300 mA.
- Secondary outputs shall NOT be connected in parallel or series.

### Class 2 Power Sources (600.24)
- Must be listed for use with electric signs and outline lighting.
- Metal parts connected to equipment grounding conductor.
- Supply-side wiring per Chapter 3.

## Secondary Wiring (CEC 600.30-600.33)

### Neon Secondary Wiring, 1000V or Less (600.31)
- Any Chapter 3 wiring method suitable for conditions.
- Conductors: listed, insulated, min 18 AWG.
- Protected from physical damage.
- Bushings required where wires pass through metal openings.

### Neon Secondary Wiring, Over 1000V (600.32)
- Must be in rigid metal conduit, IMC, LFNC, FMC, LFMC, EMT, metal enclosures, or listed equipment.
- **One conductor per conduit/tubing.**
- Min conduit size: 1/2" trade size.
- Max length of secondary conductor to first electrode: 20 ft (in metal conduit) or 50 ft (in nonmetallic conduit).
- Insulation: listed GTO cable, rated 5/10/15 kV, min 18 AWG, min 105 deg C.
- Min spacing between secondary conductors: 1-1/2".
- Equipment over 1000V open circuit: NOT permitted in dwelling occupancies.
- Splices: only in listed enclosures rated over 1000V, accessible after installation.

### Class 2 Sign Illumination (600.33)
- Wiring per manufacturer's instructions.
- Class 2 cable: listed, min 18 AWG.
- Supported at intervals not exceeding 6 ft.
- Connections in listed insulating devices, accessible after installation.
- Through-wall penetrations protected by listed bushing or raceway.

## PV Powered Signs (CEC 600.34)

- PV components must be listed for PV application.
- Wiring must be listed, labeled, suitable for PV; routed close to sign body; as short as possible; secured at max 3 ft intervals.
- Grounding per Article 690 Part V and 600.7.
- Disconnect per Article 690 Part III and 600.6.
- Battery compartments require a tool to open.

## Retrofit Kits (CEC 600.35)

- Must be listed and labeled.
- Must include installation instructions for field conversion.
- All non-replaced parts must be inspected for damage.
- Retrofitted sign must be marked per 600.4(B).

## Neon Tubing (CEC 600.41-600.42)

### Support
- Supported by listed tube supports.
- Supported within 6" from electrode connection.
- Min 1/4" spacing between tubing and nearest surface (other than support).

### Protection
- Field-installed skeleton tubing must not be subject to physical damage.
- Where accessible to unqualified persons: suitable guards or approved protection required.

### Electrode Connections
- Made by connection device, wire twisting, or electrode receptacle.
- Must be electrically and mechanically secure, in listed enclosure.
- Electrode terminals not accessible to unqualified persons.
- Electrode receptacles must be listed.
- Secondary conductors supported within 6" of electrode connection.
