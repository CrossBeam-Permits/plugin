# Utility Disconnection & Capping

Source: 2025 California Building Code (CBC, Part 2), California Plumbing Code (CPC, Part 5), California Mechanical Code (CMC, Part 4), California Electrical Code (CEC, Part 3).

---

## General Requirement (CBC 3303.6)

Service utility connections shall be discontinued and capped in accordance with the approved rules and the requirements of the applicable governing authority.

> This is the overarching state-level mandate. For interior LTIA demolition, utilities are capped back to the nearest appropriate point, NOT terminated at the building service entrance (the building remains occupied).

---

## Electrical Disconnection (CEC)

### Tying Circuits Back to Panels

When lighting fixtures, receptacles, switches, and other electrical devices are removed during interior demolition, the associated circuits must be properly terminated at the panel. This means:

1. **De-energize circuits** before any demolition work begins.
2. **Remove conductors** from demolished devices back to the nearest junction box or panel.
3. **Terminate conductors** properly -- wire nuts on individual conductors, secured in a junction box with a cover plate, or breaker removed/turned off at the panel.
4. **All junction boxes must remain accessible** per CEC 314.29 -- do not bury junction boxes behind finishes.
5. **Label disconnected circuits** at the panel to indicate they are no longer in use.

### Service Disconnect (CEC 230.70)

The service disconnecting means must remain accessible and operational for the building. LTIA demolition does not disconnect the building service -- only individual branch circuits within the tenant space.

### Working Space (CEC 110.26)

Where electrical panels remain in the demolition area, minimum working clearances must be maintained:
- 30 inches wide
- 36 inches deep (minimum, may be more depending on voltage)
- 6 feet 6 inches headroom

## Plumbing Capping (CPC)

### Capping Water Supply Lines

When plumbing fixtures (sinks, toilets, drinking fountains) are removed, the water supply lines must be:

1. **Shut off** at the nearest accessible valve.
2. **Capped** with appropriate fittings (soldered cap for copper, threaded cap for threaded pipe, solvent-welded cap for CPVC/PVC).
3. **Tested for leaks** after capping.
4. Plumbing stacks (risers serving multiple floors) shall be maintained and reused -- do NOT demolish shared plumbing stacks.

### Capping Drain, Waste & Vent (DWV) Lines

When fixtures connected to the DWV system are removed:

1. **Cap or plug** all open drain and vent connections with approved fittings.
2. Maintain trap seals on any remaining connected fixtures.
3. Open vent pipes must be capped to prevent sewer gas from entering the occupied building.

### Abandoned Sewers (CPC Appendix H, Section H1101.1)

An abandoned building sewer, or part thereof, shall be plugged or capped in an approved manner within 5 feet of the property line.

> For LTIA: Interior plumbing is capped at the stub-out, not at the sewer. The building sewer remains active.

## Gas Piping Capping (CPC 1206 / CMC 1313)

### Capped Outlets (CPC 1206.3)

It shall be **unlawful** to remove or disconnect gas piping or gas appliance without capping or plugging with a screw joint fitting, the outlet from which said pipe or appliance was removed.

Outlets to which gas appliances are not connected shall be left **capped and gastight** on a piping system that has been installed, altered, or repaired.

**Exception:** Where an approved listed quick-disconnect device is used.

### Authority to Disconnect (CPC 1206.1)

The Authority Having Jurisdiction or the serving gas supplier is authorized to disconnect gas piping or appliances that are found defective and in such condition as to endanger life or property.

### Turning Gas On After Work (CMC 1313.5.1)

During the process of turning gas on into a system of new gas piping, the entire system shall be inspected to determine that there are no open fittings or ends and that all valves at unused outlets are closed and plugged or capped.

> **FOR LTIA**: All gas outlets in the demolished tenant space must be capped gastight. The gas supply to the tenant space should be shut off at the branch valve. Any gas piping that remains must be leak-tested.

## HVAC / Mechanical Capping (CMC)

### Duct Capping

When HVAC ductwork serving the demolished tenant space is disconnected:

1. **Cap or seal** all open duct ends with sheet metal caps or listed duct closure systems.
2. **Maintain duct integrity** for any shared ductwork serving adjacent tenants or common areas.
3. Ensure capped ducts do not allow conditioned air to escape into the demolished space or unconditioned air to enter the active HVAC system.

### Fire/Smoke Dampers

Where ductwork penetrates fire-rated assemblies (fire barriers, fire partitions, smoke barriers), fire dampers and smoke dampers must remain in place and functional. Do NOT remove fire dampers during demolition.

### Emergency Disconnect (CMC 106.5)

The Authority Having Jurisdiction has authority to disconnect a mechanical system in case of emergency where necessary to eliminate an immediate hazard to life or property.

## Summary: Utility Capping Checklist for LTIA Demolition

| System | Action Required | Code Reference |
|--------|----------------|---------------|
| **Electrical** | De-energize circuits, remove conductors to panel, terminate at panel or accessible junction box, label at panel | CBC 3303.6, CEC 110.26, 314.29 |
| **Water supply** | Shut off at nearest valve, cap with approved fitting, leak test | CBC 3303.6, CPC |
| **DWV (drain/waste/vent)** | Cap or plug all open connections, maintain trap seals | CBC 3303.6, CPC |
| **Gas piping** | Shut off at branch valve, cap all outlets gastight with screw joint fittings | CPC 1206.3 |
| **HVAC ductwork** | Cap or seal open duct ends, maintain shared ductwork, preserve fire/smoke dampers | CBC 3303.6, CMC |
