# Structural Requirements for Window/Door Openings

> Source: 2025 California Residential Code (Title 24, Part 2.5)
> Sections: CRC R602.7-R602.7.5, R609.7-R609.8, Tables R602.7(1)-(3), R602.7.5

---

## Overview

When a window or door opening is **enlarged** or a **new opening** is created in a wall, structural modifications are required. In bearing walls, a properly sized header must span the opening to transfer loads around it. The CRC provides prescriptive header tables for standard residential construction. Openings exceeding table limits require engineered design.

**Like-for-like replacements** (same size opening, no structural modification) do not trigger header review -- the existing header remains in place.

---

## When Structural Review Is Required

| Scenario | Structural Review? |
|---|---|
| Same-size replacement in existing opening | No -- existing header adequate |
| Wider opening in same wall | Yes -- new/larger header required |
| Taller opening (lowering sill) | Maybe -- existing header stays, but verify cripple studs and sill plate |
| New opening (no previous window) | Yes -- header, jack studs, king studs required |
| Converting window to door | Yes -- sill plate removal, possible header resizing |
| Reducing opening size | No structural concern (may need infill framing) |

## Header Requirements (R602.7)

### Bearing Walls
Headers in bearing walls must be sized per Table R602.7(1) for exterior walls and Table R602.7(2) for interior walls. Header size depends on:
- **Span** of the opening
- **Number of stories** supported above
- **Ground snow load** (30, 50, or 70 psf)
- **Building width** (measured perpendicular to ridge)

### Exterior Bearing Wall Headers -- Table R602.7(1) Summary

Common configurations (30 psf snow load, 24 ft building width):

| Header Size | Roof + Ceiling Only | Roof, Ceiling + 1 Floor (center-bearing) | Roof, Ceiling + 1 Floor (clear-span) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2-2x4 | 3'-1" (1 JS) | 2'-6" (1 JS) | 2'-3" (1 JS) |
| 2-2x6 | 4'-7" (1 JS) | 3'-9" (1 JS) | 3'-4" (2 JS) |
| 2-2x8 | 5'-9" (1 JS) | 4'-10" (2 JS) | 4'-3" (2 JS) |
| 2-2x10 | 6'-10" (2 JS) | 5'-8" (2 JS) | 5'-0" (2 JS) |
| 2-2x12 | 8'-1" (2 JS) | 6'-8" (2 JS) | 5'-11" (2 JS) |
| 3-2x8 | 7'-3" (1 JS) | 6'-0" (1 JS) | 5'-3" (2 JS) |
| 3-2x10 | 8'-7" (1 JS) | 7'-2" (2 JS) | 6'-3" (2 JS) |
| 3-2x12 | 10'-1" (2 JS) | 8'-5" (2 JS) | 7'-5" (2 JS) |

**Important:** Where top of header is NOT laterally braced (e.g., cripple studs bearing on header), tabulated spans for 2x8, 2x10, and 2x12 headers must be **multiplied by 0.70** or the header must be engineered.

## Non-Bearing Walls (R602.7.4)

**Load-bearing headers are NOT required** in interior or exterior non-bearing walls.

A single flat **2x4 member** is permitted as a header for openings up to **8 feet wide** in non-bearing walls, provided the vertical distance to the parallel nailing surface above is not more than 24".

Cripples or blocking above the header are **not required** for non-bearing headers.

---

## Header Construction Types

### Single-Member Headers (R602.7.1)
- Single flat 2" nominal member or wall plate
- Width not less than wall studs
- Face nailed top and bottom with 10d box nails at 12" OC

### Built-Up Headers
- Two or more members with 1/2" spacer
- Face nailed with 16d common at 16" OC each edge, or 16d box at 12" OC each edge

### Rim Board Headers (R602.7.2)
- Supported at each end by full-height studs
- Number of full-height studs = 1 + studs displaced by half of header span

### Wood Structural Panel Box Headers (R602.7.3)
- 15/32" Exposure 1 wood structural panel faces
- Nailed with 8d common at 3" OC, staggered
- Jack studs required for spans >4 ft
- Max spans: 9" depth = 4-7 ft; 15" depth = 3-8 ft (varies by house depth and one/two-sided)

## Jack Stud and Full-Height Stud Requirements

### Jack Studs (R602.7.5)
Headers must be supported on each end by one or more jack studs or approved framing anchors per Tables R602.7(1) and R602.7(2).

Where only 1 jack stud is required, the header may be supported by an approved framing anchor attached to the full-height stud and header.

### Full-Height Studs at Header Ends (Table R602.7.5)

| Max Header Span | High Wind (<140 mph Exp B / <130 mph Exp C) | Standard (<=115 mph Exp B) |
|---|---|---|
| 4 ft | 1 | 1 |
| 6 ft | 2 | 1 |
| 8 ft | 2 | 1 |
| 10 ft | 3 | 2 |
| 12 ft | 3 | 2 |
| 14 ft | 3 | 2 |
| 16 ft | 4 | 2 |
| 18 ft | 4 | 2 |

## Window Anchoring Methods (R609.7)

These requirements apply to anchoring the window or door assembly to the structure -- separate from the header/framing requirement.

### General Requirement (R609.7.1)
Windows and glass doors must be anchored per manufacturer's published recommendations to achieve the design pressure specified.

### Anchoring Methods (R609.7.2)

**Wood or approved framing (R609.7.2.2):**
- Through the frame
- By frame clip
- Through the flange

**Masonry, concrete, or other structural substrate (R609.7.2.1):**
- Wood shim/buck thickness <1.5": anchor through jamb or by jamb clip, embedded directly into substrate
- Wood shim/buck thickness >=1.5" (securely fastened): anchor through jamb, by jamb clip, or through flange to the secured wood buck

---

## Mullions (R609.8)

Mullions connecting multiple window units must be:
- Tested per **AAMA 450**, or
- Engineered per accepted practice

Requirements:
- **Load transfer:** Must transfer design pressure loads to rough opening substrate
- **Deflection:** Must not exceed L/175 under design load
- **Safety factor:** Must resist 1.5x design pressure without exceeding material stress levels

---

## When Engineering Is Required

The prescriptive tables are limited. Engineering design (by a licensed engineer or architect) is required when:

1. Opening span exceeds table maximums
2. Building configuration differs from table assumptions (e.g., unusual roof loads, cantilevers)
3. Concentrated loads bear on the header
4. Header is not laterally braced and span adjustment (0.70 factor) is insufficient
5. Non-standard lumber species or grades
6. Steel lintels or other non-wood header materials
7. Multi-story openings or openings in shear walls
8. Opening is in a braced wall panel (affects lateral bracing -- see R602.10)

## Practical Review Checklist for Structural

1. **Is the opening being modified?** If same size, no structural review needed.
2. **Bearing or non-bearing wall?** Non-bearing: flat 2x4 up to 8 ft, no further review.
3. **What loads are above?** Roof only, roof + 1 floor, roof + 2 floors?
4. **Header span?** Match to Table R602.7(1) or R602.7(2).
5. **Header size adequate?** Include species/grade verification.
6. **Jack studs?** Per table requirements.
7. **Full-height studs?** Per Table R602.7.5.
8. **Span exceed table?** Require engineering.
9. **Is header top laterally braced?** If not, apply 0.70 factor or require engineering.
10. **Anchoring method specified?** Per manufacturer instructions.
