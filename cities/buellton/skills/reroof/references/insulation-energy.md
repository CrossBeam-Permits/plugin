# Roof Insulation and Energy Code Requirements

Source: 2025 California Energy Code (Title 24, Part 6), Sections 150.0(A), 150.1(C)1, 150.2(B)1H.

---

## Overview

When replacing a roof, the California Energy Code may require upgrades to roof/attic insulation. This is separate from the cool roof requirement (reflectance/emittance) and applies specifically to the thermal insulation R-value of the roof assembly.

---

## When Insulation Upgrades Are Triggered

Roof replacement (reroof) is classified as an "alteration" under the Energy Code. Per Section 150.1(C)1:

> When replacing a roof, the new roof assembly shall meet current insulation requirements where feasible.

### Residential (Single-Family) Prescriptive Requirement

The prescriptive requirement for attic and roof deck insulation is:

| Component | Minimum R-Value |
|---|---|
| Attic insulation (above ceiling) | R-22 minimum (varies by CZ) |
| Roof deck insulation (above deck or below rafters) | R-22 minimum |

**Note:** The actual prescriptive R-value depends on climate zone and may be higher (up to R-38 or R-49 in colder zones). R-22 is the minimum floor applicable to most reroof alterations.

### When It Applies

- **Tear-off reroof:** If the roof deck is exposed, rigid insulation above the deck may be required
- **Overlay/recover:** Insulation requirements are less likely to be triggered since the deck is not exposed
- **Practical interpretation:** Most jurisdictions do not require tearing into the attic to add insulation when only the roof covering is being replaced. The requirement is most strictly enforced when:
  - The roof assembly (deck + insulation) is being modified
  - Rigid insulation above the deck is part of the new assembly
  - The existing insulation is clearly deficient (e.g., no insulation at all)

## Commercial Low-Slope Insulation (Energy Code 150.2(B)1H / Table 150.2-F)

For commercial/nonresidential reroof projects, the Energy Code prescribes:

| Climate Zones | Required Continuous Insulation | Max U-Factor |
|---|---|---|
| CZ 1-2, 4, 8-16 | R-14 continuous above deck | 0.039 |
| CZ 3, 5-7 | No insulation requirement | -- |

This is mandatory for low-slope commercial reroof and more strictly enforced than residential requirements.

---

## Reflectance/Insulation Trade-Off (Table 150.2-E)

For commercial low-slope roofs, if the roof covering has lower reflectance than the 0.63 minimum, higher insulation can compensate:

| Aged Solar Reflectance | Required Insulation R-Value |
|---|---|
| >= 0.63 | R-14 (standard) |
| 0.60 | R-21-22 |
| 0.55 | R-18 |
| 0.50 | R-20 |
| 0.45 | R-22 (CZ 6-7 only) |

See `cool-roof-energy.md` for full cool roof requirements.

## Practical Review Notes

1. **Most residential reroof permits do NOT trigger major insulation upgrades** unless the scope includes modifying the roof assembly (adding rigid insulation, changing ventilation, etc.).
2. **If the application mentions adding rigid insulation above the deck**, verify it meets the minimum R-value for the climate zone.
3. **If existing insulation is clearly deficient** (stated as none or minimal), FLAG that energy code compliance should be reviewed by the building department.
4. **Commercial reroof** is more strictly regulated — R-14 continuous insulation is mandatory in most climate zones.
