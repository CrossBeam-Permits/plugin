# Fenestration Energy Code Requirements

> Source: 2025 California Energy Code (Title 24, Part 6)
> Sections: 150.1(c)3A, 150.2(b)1A-B, Table 150.1-A, 110.6

---

## Overview

All replacement windows and doors in California must meet energy code requirements for thermal performance. The Energy Code sets maximum U-factor (heat transfer) and SHGC (Solar Heat Gain Coefficient) values that vary by climate zone. These requirements apply to **alterations** (replacements) as well as new construction, though replacements have a relaxed exception path.

## Replacement Fenestration Requirements (Section 150.2(b)1B)

Replacement fenestration is covered under the **alterations** provisions of Section 150.2(b). The code distinguishes between:

### Added Fenestration (150.2(b)1A)
Alterations that **add** vertical fenestration and skylight area (new openings where none existed) must meet the full prescriptive requirements of Table 150.1-A for U-factor, SHGC, total area, and west-facing area.

**Exception:** Alterations that add up to 16 SF of new fenestration or skylight area are not required to meet the total fenestration area and west-facing fenestration area limits.

### Replacement Fenestration (150.2(b)1B)
New manufactured fenestration products installed to **replace** existing fenestration products of the same total area must meet U-factor and SHGC requirements.

**Exception 1 (the <=75 SF exception):** Replacement of vertical fenestration no greater than **75 square feet** with:
- U-factor no greater than **0.40** in Climate Zones 1-16
- SHGC no greater than **0.35** in Climate Zones 2, 4, and 6-15

**Exception 2 (skylights):** Replaced skylights must meet:
- U-factor no greater than **0.40**
- SHGC no greater than **0.30**

**Exception 3 (CZ 15 SHGC override):** Replacement of vertical fenestration in Climate Zone 15 must have SHGC no greater than **0.23** (overrides the 0.35 from Exception 1).

### Repair vs. Replacement
**Glass replaced in an existing sash and frame**, or **sashes replaced in an existing frame**, are considered **repairs** -- not replacements -- provided the replacement is at least equivalent to the original in performance. Repairs are not subject to the prescriptive U-factor and SHGC requirements.

## NFRC Labeling Requirements

All fenestration products must have ratings determined in accordance with Section 110.6:

- **U-factor:** Determined per NFRC 100
- **SHGC:** Determined per NFRC 200
- **Products must bear an NFRC label** showing the certified U-factor and SHGC values

### Testing and Certification (CRC R609.3)
Exterior windows and sliding doors must be tested by an approved independent laboratory and bear a label identifying:
- Manufacturer
- Performance characteristics (design pressure rating, air/water/structural)
- Approved inspection agency
- Compliance with AAMA/WDMA/CSA 101/I.S.2/A440

Exterior side-hinged doors may comply with AAMA/WDMA/CSA 101/I.S.2/A440 or ANSI WMA 100.

**Exception:** Decorative glazed openings are exempt from testing and labeling.

---

## Wind Resistance (CRC R609.2)

Exterior windows and doors must resist the design wind loads specified in Table R301.2.1(1), adjusted for height and exposure per Table R301.2.1(2), or determined per ASCE 7.

Most of California falls within the **110-115 mph** ultimate design wind speed zone per ASCE 7-22 (Exposure B for most suburban/urban areas).

The performance grade rating on the AAMA/NFRC label must meet or exceed the required design pressure for the project location.

---

## Fenestration Area Limits for Additions

For additions >700 SF: Maximum fenestration area is the greater of 175 SF or 20% of addition floor area.

For additions 400-700 SF: Maximum fenestration area is the greater of 120 SF or 25% of addition floor area.

For additions <=400 SF: Maximum fenestration area is the greater of 75 SF or 30% of addition floor area.

West-facing limit (CZ 2, 4, 6-15): Not greater than 60 SF for additions <=700 SF.

## Performance Approach Alternative (150.2(b)2)

As an alternative to the prescriptive approach, alterations may comply through the **performance approach**. The energy budget is expressed in Long-term System Cost (LSC). The standard design for replacement fenestration (without third-party verification) is based on Table 150.1-A values. With third-party verification, the standard design is based on the existing fenestration U-factor and SHGC values.

This approach is rarely used for simple window replacements but may be useful when the prescriptive path creates hardship (e.g., historic buildings, unusual configurations).

## Exterior Doors (Section 150.2(b)1N, 150.1(c)5)

Alterations that add exterior door area must meet:
- **Max U-factor: 0.20** for all climate zones (Table 150.1-A)
- Glazed door products are treated as fenestration (must meet fenestration U-factor and SHGC requirements)
- Exception: Swinging doors between garage and conditioned space required to have fire protection are not required to meet U-factor requirements
