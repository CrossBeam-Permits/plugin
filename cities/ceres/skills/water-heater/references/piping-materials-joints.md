# Piping Materials, Joints & Connections

Source: 2025 California Plumbing Code (Title 24, Part 5), Chapters 3, 6.

---

## 1. Materials for Potable Water Systems (CPC 604.0)

### NSF 61 Compliance (CPC 604.1)

All pipe, tube, fittings, solvent cement, thread sealants, solders, and flux used in potable water systems intended to supply drinking water shall comply with **NSF/ANSI/CAN 61**.

### Dezincification Resistance (CPC 604.1)

Where pipe fittings and valves are made from copper alloys containing more than **15 percent zinc** by weight and are used in plastic piping systems, they shall be resistant to dezincification and stress corrosion cracking in compliance with **NSF/ANSI 14**.

### Lead Content (CPC 604.2)

- **Pipe, fittings, fixtures**: Max weighted average **0.25% lead** on wetted surfaces
- **Solder and flux**: Max **0.2% lead** in piping for human consumption
- Applies to all potable water connections at the water heater

### Copper Pipe Markings (CPC 604.4)

Hard-drawn copper or copper alloy tubing markings by type:
- **Type K** = Green
- **Type L** = Blue
- **Type M** = Red

### Previously Used Piping (CPC 604.8)

Piping or tubing previously used for a purpose other than potable water **shall not be used** for potable water.

## 2. Water Heater Connectors (CPC 604.13)

Flexible metallic (copper, stainless steel), reinforced flexible, braided stainless steel, or polymer braided with EPDM core connectors shall comply with **ASME A112.18.6/CSA B125.6**.

| Requirement | Specification |
|-------------|---------------|
| **Copper/stainless flex connector max length** | 24 inches (610 mm) |
| **PEX/PEX-AL-PEX/PE-AL-PE/PE-RT proximity** | Shall NOT be installed within first **18 inches** of piping connected to a water heater |
| **Accessibility** | Flexible water connectors shall be installed in accessible locations (CPC 604.5) |

---

## 3. Soldered Joints (CPC 605.1.4)

Soldered joints between copper or copper alloy pipe/tubing and fittings shall comply with **ASTM B828**.

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| **Flux** | Shall conform to **ASTM B813**; noncorrosive and nontoxic after soldering |
| **Solder lead content** | Solder and flux with lead content exceeding **0.2%** are prohibited in potable water piping |
| **Procedure** | Cut square, ream to full inside diameter, clean bright, apply flux, assemble, heat, apply solder by capillary action |

---

## 4. One-Step Cement Joints for CPVC (CPC 605.2.2)

Listed solvent cement for CPVC that does not require primer shall be **yellow, green, or red** in color. Standard primer shall be **orange** in color and comply with ASTM F656.

---

## 5. Press-Connect Fittings (CPC 605.1.3.2)

Press-connect fittings for copper or copper alloy pipe or tubing:
- Shall have an **elastomeric O-ring** that forms the joint
- Pipe shall be **fully inserted** into the fitting and marked at the shoulder
- Joint shall be pressed using the **tool recommended by the manufacturer**
- Installed per manufacturer's installation instructions

---

## 6. Push Fit Fittings (CPC 605.9.3)

Removable and nonremovable push fit fittings that employ a quick assembly push fit connector shall comply with **ASSE 1061**.

---

## 7. Joints Between Dissimilar Materials (CPC 605.16)

### Copper to Threaded Pipe (CPC 605.16.1)

Joints from copper or copper alloy pipe/tubing to threaded pipe shall be made using:
- Copper alloy adapter
- Copper alloy nipple (minimum 6 inches)
- **Dielectric fitting or dielectric union** per ASSE 1079 or IAPMO PS 66

Brass pipe or dielectric fittings are required between copper and galvanized pipes.

### Plastic to Other Materials (CPC 605.16.2)

Approved adapter or transition fittings designed for the specific transition intended shall be used.

### General Rule for Dissimilar Metals (CPC 310.6)

Except for necessary valves, where intermixing of dissimilar metals occurs, the point of connection shall be confined to **exposed or accessible locations**.

---

## 8. Common Violations to Flag

| Violation | Why It Fails |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Solder with >0.2% lead** | CPC 605.1.4 prohibits in potable water systems |
| **PEX within 18 inches of water heater** | CPC 604.13 requires 18-inch minimum clearance |
| **Flex connector over 24 inches** | CPC 604.13 limits copper/stainless flex to 24" max |
| **No dielectric fitting at copper-to-galvanized** | CPC 605.16.1 requires dielectric union or fitting |
| **Dissimilar metal joints in concealed location** | CPC 310.6 requires accessible/exposed locations |
| **Used piping for potable water** | CPC 604.8 prohibits reuse of non-potable piping |
| **Wrong cement color for CPVC** | CPC 605.2.2 one-step cement must be yellow, green, or red |

---

## Review Checklist -- Piping Materials & Joints

- [ ] All materials comply with NSF 61 for potable water
- [ ] Lead content at or below 0.25% (pipe/fittings) and 0.2% (solder/flux)
- [ ] Water heater flex connectors max 24 inches, accessible
- [ ] PEX/PE-RT not within 18 inches of water heater
- [ ] Solder joints per ASTM B828, flux per ASTM B813
- [ ] Dielectric fittings at copper-to-galvanized transitions
- [ ] Dissimilar metal joints at exposed/accessible locations only
- [ ] Press-connect fittings with proper O-ring and manufacturer tool
- [ ] Push fit fittings comply with ASSE 1061
