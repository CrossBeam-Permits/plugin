# Ledger Connections

Source: 2025 California Residential Code (Title 24, Part 2.5), Chapter 5 Section R507.8-R507.9 (Vertical and Lateral Supports).

---

## Why Ledger Connections Matter

The connection between a deck ledger and the house band joist is the **single most common point of catastrophic deck failure**. Improperly attached ledgers allow the deck to pull away from the house, resulting in sudden collapse. This is a life-safety issue and must be thoroughly verified on every attached deck permit.

---

## General Requirements (CRC R507.8, R318.5)

- Decks attached to exterior walls must be **positively anchored** for both vertical AND lateral loads
- Attachment must NOT use toenails or nails subject to withdrawal
- For cantilevered framing, connections must resist **uplift from full live load** on the cantilever
- Where positive connection cannot be verified during inspection, the deck **must be self-supporting**

---

## Ledger Material Requirements (R507.9.1.1)

| Property | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Minimum size | 2x8 nominal |
| Grade | No. 2 or better |
| Treatment | Pressure-preservative-treated (Southern pine, incised hem-fir) OR naturally durable wood |
| Concentrated loads | Ledger shall NOT support concentrated loads from beams or girders |
| Veneer | Ledger shall NOT be supported on stone or masonry veneer |

---

## Band Joist Requirements (R507.9.1.2)

The house band joist that receives the ledger fasteners must meet:

| Property | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Sawn lumber | Min 2" nominal, solid-sawn, spruce-pine-fir or better |
| Engineered | Min 1" nominal engineered wood rim board per R502.1.7 |
| Bearing | Must bear fully on primary structure capable of supporting all loads |

|---|---|---|---|
| 6 | 30" OC | 36" OC | 36" OC |

### Key Notes on Lag Screws

- **1/2-inch diameter**, full-body diameter screws
- Tip must **fully extend beyond the inside face** of the band joist
- Pre-drill: **1/2"** diameter through ledger and sheathing, **5/16" to 3/8"** diameter through band joist (R507.9.1.3)
- Sheathing: wood structural panel or solid sawn lumber only

### Key Notes on Through-Bolts

- **1/2-inch diameter** per ASTM A307
- With ASTM A563 nuts and ASTM F844 washers
- With 1" max sheathing: sheathing may be wood structural panel, gypsum board, fiberboard, lumber, or foam sheathing
- Up to 1/2" of stacked washers may substitute for up to 1/2" of sheathing thickness (with wood panel or lumber sheathing)

## Fastener Placement (Table R507.9.1.3(2))

| Location | Ledger | Band Joist |
|---|---|---|
| Top edge distance | 2" min | 3/4" min |
| Bottom edge distance | 3/4" min | 2" min |
| End distance | 2" min (max 5") | 2" min |
| Row spacing | 1-5/8" min (max 5") | 1-5/8" min (max 5") |

Lag screws or bolts must be **staggered from top to bottom** along the horizontal run of the ledger per Figure R507.9.1.3(1).

## Ledger Flashing (R507.9.1.5)

### Required Installation

Flashing must be installed above the ledger to prevent water entry into wall cavity or behind ledger:

| Dimension | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Vertical extension above ledger | Min 2 inches |
| Horizontal extension | Min 4 inches beyond ledger face, OR extend to ledger face plus min 1/4" down the face |

### Flashing Material (R507.2.4)

- Corrosion-resistant metal: nominal thickness min **0.019 inch** (0.48 mm)
- OR approved non-metallic material compatible with substrate and decking
- Self-adhered membranes for flashing/counterflashing: must comply with FGIA 711

### Exceptions to Flashing

Flashing is NOT required where the ledger is spaced horizontally from the exterior wall covering by at least **1/4 inch** to allow drainage and ventilation behind the ledger.

---

## Water-Resistive Barrier (R507.9.1.6)

The WRB must be:
- **Lapped over** the vertical leg of the ledger flashing or counterflashing by min 2 inches (or the height of the vertical flashing leg, whichever is less)
- **Continued** from the top of the ledger flashing down the wall and behind the ledger flashing and ledger

### Alternate WRB Methods

1. Flashing against face of WRB with self-adhered membrane counterflashing min 2" over flashing leg and 2" onto WRB
2. Flashing in front of WRB and behind exterior wall covering where ledger is spaced 1/4" from wall for drainage

---

## Existing Walls (R507.9.1.7)

Where ledgers attach to existing walls **without water-resistive barriers**:

1. Install WRB behind ledger and ledger flashing
2. WRB extends to top of flashing vertical leg and min 1/2" beyond sides and bottom of ledger
3. Self-adhered membrane counterflashing: min 2" over vertical flashing leg and min 2" onto existing sheathing

---

## Exterior Wall Coverings (R507.9.1.8)

Exterior wall coverings must be **terminated above the finished deck surface** per the covering manufacturer and Chapter 7. Exception: coverings may be behind ledgers where capable of resisting compression from ledger attachment.

---

## Lateral Load Connections (R507.9.2)

### Option 1: Two Devices (Figure R507.9.2(1))

- **Minimum 2 hold-down tension devices** per deck
- Located within **24 inches of each end** of the deck
- Each device: ASD capacity >= **1,500 pounds**

### Option 2: Four Devices (Figure R507.9.2(2))

- **Minimum 4 hold-down tension devices** per deck
- Each device: ASD capacity >= **750 pounds**

### Critical Rule

Lateral loads must be transferred to the ground or to a structure capable of transmitting them to the ground. This is separate from the vertical load connection.

---

## Alternate Ledger Details (R507.9.1.4)

Alternate framing configurations supporting a ledger are permitted provided they are constructed to meet the load requirements of Section R301.5. This allows engineered solutions beyond the prescriptive tables.

---

## Freestanding Decks

Decks not attached to the house do NOT require ledger connections but must still:
- Transfer lateral loads to the ground through their own structural system
- Have positive connections at all structural joints (no toenails)
- Meet all footing, post, beam, and joist requirements

---

## Common Review Findings

1. **Lag screws too far apart** -- The most common error. For a deck with 12-foot joist spans and 1/2" lag screws through 1/2" sheathing, the maximum spacing is 15" OC. Many plans show 24" or even 36" OC, which is a FAIL.
2. **Ledger on masonry veneer** -- Brick veneer is NOT structural. A ledger fastened only to brick veneer will pull out. The ledger must be fastened through veneer to the structural band joist. If that's not possible, the deck must be freestanding.
3. **Missing flashing** -- Flashing above the ledger is required unless the ledger is spaced 1/4" from the wall. Missing flashing causes water intrusion and band joist rot -- the same rot that leads to ledger failure.
4. **No lateral hold-down devices** -- Even with proper vertical lag screw or bolt connections, lateral hold-down devices (e.g., Simpson DTT2Z) are separately required per R507.9.2.
5. **Nails instead of lag screws or bolts** -- Nails are explicitly prohibited for ledger-to-band-joist connections (R318.5). This includes framing nails, ring-shank nails, and all other nail types. Only lag screws or through-bolts per Table R507.9.1.3(1) are permitted.
6. **Existing wall with no WRB** -- On older homes without water-resistive barriers, R507.9.1.7 requires installing WRB behind the ledger and flashing with counterflashing. FLAG if the scope of work does not address this.
