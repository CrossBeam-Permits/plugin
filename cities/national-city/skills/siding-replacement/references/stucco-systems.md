# Stucco (Exterior Plaster) and EIFS Requirements

Source: 2025 California Residential Code (Title 24, Part 2.5), Sections R703.7, R703.9; California Building Code (Part 2), Sections 1404.2, 1407.

---

## Traditional Stucco (Cement Plaster) -- CRC R703.7

### General

Installation must comply with ASTM C926 (application) and ASTM C1063 (lath/furring).

### Number of Coats

| Application | Min Coats | Min Thickness |
|---|---|---|
| Over metal lath or wire lath | **3 coats** | 7/8" total (CBC Table 1404.2) |
| Over masonry, concrete, pressure-treated or decay-resistant wood | **2 coats** | 5/8"-1/2" (varies by substrate, Table R702.1(1)) |
| Over gypsum backing | **2 coats** | 3/4" interior, 7/8" exterior |
| Where completely covered by veneer or concealed | **2 coats** | Per Table R702.1(1) |

### Cement Materials (R703.7.2)

Acceptable cement types:
1. Masonry cement -- ASTM C91, Type M, S, or N
2. Portland cement -- ASTM C150, Type I, II, or III
3. Blended hydraulic cement -- ASTM C595, Type IP, IS (<70), IL, or IT (S<70)
4. Hydraulic cement -- ASTM C1157, Type GU, HE, MS, HS, or MH
5. Plastic (stucco) cement -- ASTM C1328

### Mix Proportions (Table R702.1(3))

| Coat | Cement Type | Volume of Aggregate per Volume of Cement |
|---|---|---|
| First coat | Portland or blended | 2-1/2 to 4 |
| First coat | Masonry or plastic | 2-1/2 to 4 |
| Second coat | Portland or blended | 3 to 5 |
| Second coat | Masonry or plastic | 3 to 5 |
| Finish coat | Portland or blended | 1-1/2 to 3 |
| Finish coat | Masonry or plastic | 1-1/2 to 3 |

**Note:** Lime may be added to Portland cement mixes: 3/4 to 1-1/2 parts per part cement (first coat), up to 2 parts (finish coat).

---

### Lath Requirements (R703.7.1)

- Lath and attachments must be **corrosion-resistant** per ASTM C1063
- Types: expanded metal, welded wire, or woven wire lath
- Attached to wood framing or furring

**Fastening to wood framing:**
- 1-1/2" long, 0.120" diameter (11-gage) nails with 7/16" head, OR
- 7/8" long, 16-gage staples
- Max 7" OC along framing/furring
- Max 24" OC between framing/furring

**Furring (R703.7.1.1):**
- Wood furring strips: min 1x2
- Metal channels: min 3/4"
- Self-furring lath acceptable
- Max 24" OC
- Must be fastened into framing members over wood or steel

**Exception:** Lath not required over masonry, cast-in-place concrete, precast concrete, or stone substrates prepared per ASTM C1063.

---

### Weep Screed (R703.7.2.1) -- CRITICAL

A weep screed is MANDATORY at the base of all exterior stucco walls:

| Requirement | Specification |
|---|---|
| Material | Min 0.019" (No. 26 gage) corrosion-resistant metal or plastic |
| Vertical attachment flange | Min 3-1/2" |
| Height above earth | Min **4 inches** |
| Height above paved surfaces | Min **2 inches** |
| Function | Must allow trapped water to drain to exterior |
| WRB relationship | WRB must LAP the attachment flange |
| Lath relationship | Lath must COVER and TERMINATE on the attachment flange |

---

### Curing Requirements (R703.7.4, R703.7.5)

| Requirement | Two-Coat | Three-Coat |
|---|---|---|
| Moist curing per coat | Min 48 hours | Min 48 hours |
| Wait before second coat | N/A | Min 48 hours after first coat |
| Wait before finish coat | Min 7 days after first coat | Min 7 days after second coat |

**Exception:** Applications installed per ASTM C926 may follow those curing schedules.

---

### Water-Resistive Barrier for Stucco (R703.7.3)

Stucco has STRICTER WRB requirements than other siding types. See `weather-barrier-flashing.md` for full details.

**Summary for dry climates (most of SoCal):**
- Option 1: Two layers of 10-minute Grade D paper, independently installed
- Option 2: One layer of 60-minute Grade D paper, separated from stucco by foam, drainage layer, or non-absorbing layer

## EIFS -- Exterior Insulation and Finish System (CRC R703.9)

### EIFS (over concrete/masonry only) -- R703.9.1

Requirements:
1. Comply with ASTM E2568
2. **Limited to concrete or masonry substrates only**
3. Flashing per R703.4
4. Installed per manufacturer instructions
5. Terminate min 6" above finished ground level
6. Decorative trim NOT face-nailed through EIFS

### EIFS with Drainage (over all other substrates) -- R703.9.2

Required for wood-framed and steel-framed walls:
1. Comply with ASTM E2568
2. Min **90% drainage efficiency** per ASTM E2273
3. WRB per R703.2 or ASTM E2570, applied BETWEEN EIFS and wall sheathing
4. Flashing per R703.4
5. Installed per manufacturer instructions
6. Terminate min 6" above finished ground level
7. Decorative trim NOT face-nailed through EIFS

### CBC EIFS Requirements (Section 1407)

For commercial buildings (Types I-IV construction):
- EIFS containing combustible components on buildings >40 ft above grade: must comply with NFPA 285 (vertical flame propagation test)
- EIFS with drainage required for all wood-frame or steel-frame backing

## Stucco Replacement Considerations

### Removing Existing Stucco

When removing existing stucco:
1. **Lead paint testing required** for homes built before 1978
2. **Asbestos testing** may be required -- some older stucco contains asbestos fibers
3. Remove all layers: finish coat, brown coat, scratch coat, lath, WRB
4. Inspect sheathing and framing for moisture damage, rot, or termite damage
5. Replace damaged sheathing before re-application

### Re-Stuccoing

When re-applying stucco to existing walls:
1. New WRB required per R703.7.3 (two-layer or 60-minute)
2. New lath per R703.7.1
3. Weep screed at base
4. All flashing replaced/updated per R703.4
5. Three-coat application over metal/wire lath
6. Proper curing times between coats
7. If in WUI zone: stucco is noncombustible -- inherently compliant

### Converting From Stucco to Other Siding

When changing from stucco to lap siding, panels, etc.:
1. Remove all stucco layers, lath, and old WRB
2. Inspect and repair sheathing/framing
3. If wall cavity exposed: insulation required per Energy Code
4. Install new WRB per R703.2 (single layer acceptable for non-stucco cladding)
5. Install flashing per R703.4
6. Install new siding per applicable section of R703

### Converting From Other Siding to Stucco

When changing to stucco from other cladding:
1. Remove existing siding
2. Inspect sheathing -- must be solid (no gaps >1/4")
3. Install two-layer or 60-minute WRB per R703.7.3
4. Install lath per R703.7.1
5. Install weep screed per R703.7.2.1
6. Three-coat stucco application
7. All flashing per R703.4
8. Consider weight: stucco at 7/8" weighs approximately 10-11 psf -- verify framing adequacy

## Stucco Repair vs. Replacement

| Scope | Code Treatment |
|---|---|
| Patching cracks or small areas | Repair -- no permit typically required |
| Replacing stucco on one wall | Alteration -- permit and code compliance for altered area |
| Replacing stucco on entire building | Full replacement -- all current code requirements apply |
| Re-coating (new finish coat only) | Repair -- typically no permit, but verify with jurisdiction |
