# Clearance to Combustibles & Furnace Installation

Source: 2025 California Mechanical Code (Title 24, Part 4), Sections 303 and 904.

## General Clearance Rule (CMC 303.1)

Clearance to combustibles shall be in accordance with the **manufacturer's installation instructions**.

> This is the governing rule. The manufacturer's clearances take precedence. Code provides minimum fallback values only when manufacturer data is unavailable.

## Closet and Alcove Installations (CMC 303.2)

- Clearance reductions shall **NOT be allowed** for appliances listed for closet or alcove installation
- Central heating furnaces **NOT listed** for closet or alcove installation shall be installed in a room or space having a volume **not less than 12 times the total volume of the furnace**

## Clearance Reduction Methods (CMC 303.10)

Where the manufacturer's installation instructions do not specify clearances, the code provides Table 303.10.1 with standard clearances and approved reduction methods using:
- Sheet metal shields
- Ceramic fiber blankets
- Approved clearance reduction systems

## Furnace-Specific Requirements

### Prohibited Locations (CMC 904.1)

Fuel-burning appliances shall be prohibited in:
- Bedrooms
- Bathrooms
- Toilet rooms

### Floor Construction (CMC 904.3)

Floor under a furnace shall be of **noncombustible construction**.

**Exception**: When the appliance is listed for installation on a combustible floor.

### Crawl Space Installation (CMC 904.3.1)

Furnaces installed in crawl spaces shall be supported on:
- A concrete slab minimum **3 in. above adjoining ground**; OR
- Suspended from floor framing above per manufacturer's instructions, minimum **6 in. above ground**

### Furnace with Cooling Coil (CMC 904.7(1))

A furnace with an attached cooling coil shall have a blower capable of overcoming the resistance of the cooling coil and duct system friction losses.

### Return Air Requirements (CMC 904.8)

Where a central heating furnace is installed so supply ducts carry air to areas outside the space containing the furnace, the return air shall also be handled by a duct sealed to the furnace casing and terminating outside the furnace space.

### Attic Installation (CMC 904.10)

Furnaces may be installed in an attic or furred space more than **5 ft in height**, provided:
- Required listings are met
- Furnace and duct clearances are observed
- Horizontal furnaces may be installed in attics with proper clearances

## Supply Duct Clearance Near Furnace

### Listed Furnaces (CMC 603.13.2)

Supply air ducts connecting to listed central heating furnaces shall have the same minimum clearance to combustibles as required for the furnace supply plenum for a distance of **not less than 3 ft** from the supply plenum.

### Unlisted Furnaces (CMC 603.13.3)

Supply air ducts connecting to unlisted furnaces with temperature limit controls (max 250 deg F):
- Minimum **6 in. clearance** to combustibles for a distance of **not less than 6 ft** from the supply plenum

---

## Framing Requirements for Attic Furnaces

For furnaces less than 350 lbs on existing conventional roof framing:

| Requirement | Detail |
|------------|--------|
| Weight limit | Must be less than 350 lbs (engineering required if heavier) |
| Truss framing | Engineering design required for trusses |
| Location | Close to at least 2 adjacent walls |
| Modified joists | If 1 ceiling joist is cut for access, adjacent joists shall be doubled |
| Vertical clearance | 30 in. min from top of ceiling framing to underside of roof framing |

> Source: Costa Mesa standard plan reference; this reflects common California practice based on CBC structural requirements.

## Quick Reference — Clearance Summary

| Situation | Clearance | Code |
|-----------|----------|------|
| General rule | Per manufacturer's instructions | CMC 303.1 |
| Non-listed furnace room volume | >= 12x furnace volume | CMC 303.2 |
| Supply duct (listed furnace) | Same as furnace plenum for 3 ft | CMC 603.13.2 |
| Supply duct (unlisted furnace) | 6 in. for 6 ft from plenum | CMC 603.13.3 |
| Single-wall vent pipe | 6 in. to combustibles | CMC 802.6.1 |
| Type B vent | 1 in. to combustibles (or per listing) | CMC 802.6.1 |
| Furnace floor | Noncombustible (unless listed for combustible) | CMC 904.3 |
| Crawl space support | 3 in. above grade (slab) or 6 in. (suspended) | CMC 904.3.1 |
