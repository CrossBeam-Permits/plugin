# Sign Types, Classifications, and Clearances

Source: 2025 California Building Code (Title 24, Part 2), Appendix H; Chapter 31 Sections 3105-3107.

---

## Sign Definitions (CBC H102.1)

| Sign Type | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Wall sign** | Attached to or erected against a wall, exposed face parallel to the wall plane |
| **Projecting sign** | Projects from and is supported by a wall, but is NOT a wall sign (i.e., at an angle to the wall) |
| **Ground sign** | Billboard or similar type supported by uprights, poles, or braces in/on the ground (not a pole or combination sign) |
| **Pole sign** | Wholly supported by a sign structure in the ground |
| **Roof sign** | Erected on or above a roof or parapet |
| **Combination sign** | Incorporates any combination of pole, projecting, and roof sign features |
| **Marquee sign** | Attached to an approved marquee (permanent canopy structure per CBC 3106) |
| **Electric sign** | Contains electrical wiring (NOT including signs illuminated only by exterior light source) |
| **Portable display surface** | Display surface temporarily fixed to a standardized advertising structure, moved at periodic intervals |
| **Display sign** | The area made available by the sign structure for displaying the advertising message |
| **Sign structure** | Any structure that supports or is capable of supporting a sign; may be a single pole |

## Signs Exempt from Permits (CBC H101.2)

The following do NOT require a building permit:
1. Painted nonilluminated signs
2. Temporary signs announcing sale or rent of property
3. Signs erected by transportation authorities
4. Projecting signs not exceeding **2.5 sq ft**
5. Changing moveable parts of an approved changeable-copy sign, or repainting/repositioning display matter (not deemed an alteration)

## General Location Rules

### Prohibited Locations (H101.1, H103.1)
- Must not confuse or obstruct exit signs (Chapter 10) or official traffic signs/signals.
- Must not obstruct fire escapes, windows, or doors used as means of egress.
- Must not prevent free passage on a roof.
- Must not be attached to a fire escape.
- Must not interfere with required ventilation openings.

### Identification (H104.1)
Every outdoor advertising display sign requiring a permit must be marked with:
- Name of the person/firm/corporation erecting and maintaining it
- Permit number (affixed on the front) or other approved method of identification

## Sign Type Requirements Summary

### Wall Signs (CBC H111)

| Requirement | Standard |
|---|---|
| Material (>40 sq ft) | Metal or approved noncombustible (except nailing rails, H106.1.1, H107.1) |
| Material (<=40 sq ft) | No restriction beyond general code |
| Attachment to masonry/concrete/stone | Metal anchors, bolts, or expansion screws, min 3/8" diameter, min 5" embedment |
| Wood block anchorage | Prohibited (except on wood-framed buildings) |
| Unbraced parapet | Must not be supported by anchorages on an unbraced parapet |
| Extension | Must not extend above top of wall or beyond wall ends (or must comply with roof/projecting/ground sign rules) |

### Projecting Signs (CBC H112)

| Requirement | Standard |
|---|---|
| Material | Entirely metal or noncombustible |
| Fasteners | Metal supports (bolts, anchors, chains, guys, steel rods). **Staples and nails prohibited.** |
| Support angle | Min 45 degrees with horizontal (dead load) and 45 degrees with sign face (wind) |
| Min support size | 3/8" net cross-section diameter (chains/guys/steel rods) |
| Lag screws/bolts | Min 5/8" bolt or lag screw with expansion shield |
| Signs >30 sq ft | Min 2 supports per side, max 8 ft apart |
| Height | Must not exceed roof/cornice height (exception: signs perpendicular to wall, max 18" horizontal width, may extend 2 ft above) |

#### Projection Thickness Limits (Table H115.1(2))

| Projection from Wall (ft) | Maximum Sign Thickness (ft) |
|---|---|
| 5 | 2 |
| 4 | 2.5 |
| 3 | 3 |
| 2 | 3.5 |
| 1 | 4 |

### Ground Signs / Monument Signs (CBC H109)

| Requirement | Standard |
|---|---|
| Max height (combustible frame) | 35 ft |
| Max height (noncombustible) | 100 ft |
| Max height (greater) | Only with approval, no public hazard |
| Bottom clearance | Min 3 ft above ground/street level |
| Wood in ground | Must be pressure-treated with approved preservative |

### Pole Signs (CBC H102.1)

- Wholly supported by a sign structure in the ground.
- Subject to same structural requirements as ground signs (H109) and general wind/seismic design.
- Height and clearance requirements per H109.

### Roof Signs (CBC H110)

| Requirement | Standard |
|---|---|
| Material | Entirely metal or noncombustible |
| Clearance (roof to lowest part of sign) | Min 6 ft |
| Clearance between vertical supports | Min 5 ft |
| Projection beyond exterior wall | Not permitted |
| Bearing plates | Must distribute load to masonry walls, steel girders, columns, or beams |

#### Roof Sign Height Limits

| Sign Type | Type I/II Construction | Type III/IV/V Construction |
|---|---|---|
| Solid surface | 24 ft max (all types) | 24 ft max |
| Open (>=40% open area) | 75 ft max | 40 ft max |
| Closed | 50 ft max | 35 ft max |

**Exception for flat roofs:** Signs on flat roofs where every part of the roof is accessible are exempt from the 6 ft clearance requirement.

### Marquee Signs (CBC H113)

| Requirement | Standard |
|---|---|
| Material | Entirely metal or noncombustible |
| Attachment | Must be attached to an approved marquee per CBC 3106 |
| Projection | Must not project beyond the marquee perimeter |
| Height above marquee | Max 6 ft |
| Height below marquee | Max 1 ft |
| Vertical dimension | Max 8 ft |

### Marquee Structure (CBC 3106)

The underlying marquee to which marquee signs are attached must:
- Have a minimum thickness of 3 ft (3106.2) from front to building face at highest point
- Have a noncombustible roof (3106.3) — fire-retardant treated wood or noncombustible with noncombustible surfacing
- Not be located where doors or windows open directly to the marquee area (3106.4)
- Be supported entirely from the building (3106.5)

## Pedestrian Clearance Requirements

### Minimum Heights Above Sidewalks/Driveways

| Sign Location | Minimum Height Above Grade | Code Section |
|---|---|---|
| Over areas accessible to vehicles | 14 ft (per CEC 600.9(A)) | CEC 600.9(A) |
| Projecting signs over pedestrian areas | Per local ordinance (typically 8-10 ft, state code defers to jurisdiction) | Local zoning |
| Ground sign bottom coping | 3 ft | H109.2 |
| Awning/canopy clearance above sidewalk | 7 ft (CBC 3105.1 defers to local code; this is typical) | CBC 3105 / local |
| Roof sign clearance above roof | 6 ft between roof and lowest sign part | H110.1 |

**Note:** The CBC state code sets minimums for vehicle clearance (14 ft via CEC) and ground sign bottom clearance (3 ft). Many specific pedestrian clearance requirements are set by local municipal code (zoning ordinances), not the state building code. City overlay skills will specify local clearance requirements.

## Awning Signs (CBC 3105)

Signs on awnings follow the awning requirements:
- Awnings must comply with CBC 3105.2 (design and construction) and 3105.3 (materials).
- Awning covering materials must be listed and labeled per CPAI-84, or tested per NFPA 701 (flame propagation), or noncombustible per CBC 703.3.
- Metal frames for awnings must be noncombustible and adequately anchored.
- Signage on awnings typically treated as part of the awning structure.

## Temporary Signs and Banners

The CBC does not have a dedicated section for temporary signs beyond H114 (Portable Signs). Temporary signs/banners are generally regulated by:
- Local zoning ordinances (duration limits, size limits, placement)
- If temporary sign uses electrical illumination: CEC Article 600 applies in full
- If temporary sign is a structure: must comply with CBC 3103 (Temporary Structures) if erected for less than 180 days
- Banners on buildings may require engineering if wind load area is significant

### Portable Signs (H114.1)
Must conform to the applicable requirements for ground, roof, projecting, flat, or temporary signs as used in similar capacity. Not required to have permanent foundations/connections where temporary provisions ensure stability for the expected installation duration.

## Sign Type Decision Matrix

Use this to determine which code sections apply:

| If the sign is... | Primary Code Sections | Key Checks |
|---|---|---|
| Wall-mounted, flat against building | H111 | Material (>40 sq ft), attachment, no parapet support, no extension above wall |
| Projecting from building at angle | H112 | All noncombustible, no nails/staples, projection/thickness table, height limit |
| Freestanding on ground (monument) | H109 | Height limit, 3 ft bottom clearance, foundation design, treated wood |
| Pole sign | H109 + H102.1 | Same as ground sign plus pole structural design |
| On roof | H110 | All noncombustible, 6 ft clearance, height limits by type, bearing plates |
| On marquee | H113 | On approved marquee per 3106, within perimeter, height limits |
| On awning | 3105 | Awning material compliance, structural |
| Portable/temporary | H114 | Stability for expected duration, applicable type requirements |
| Internally illuminated | H106.1.1 + CEC 600 | 120 sq ft max facing, 2" wiring clearance, electrical requirements |
| Externally illuminated | CEC 600 (if sign has electrical wiring for lighting) | Disconnect, branch circuit, grounding |
| Channel letters | H111 (typically wall sign) + CEC 600 | Wall attachment, each letter individually or raceway-mounted, electrical |
| Digital/electronic (EMC) | CEC 600 + local zoning | Full electrical, brightness/dimming per local code |
