# Attic Ventilation Requirements

Source: 2025 California Residential Code (Title 24, Part 2.5), Chapter 8, Section R806; California Building Code (Part 2), Section 1203.2.

---

## Overview

Enclosed attic and rafter spaces must have cross ventilation. This is checked on reroof because:
- The roofing contractor has access to inspect and modify ventilation
- Changing roofing material may affect ventilation (e.g., removing ridge vents)
- Energy code compliance on reroof may require verifying ventilation

---

## Vented Attics (CRC R806.1 - R806.4)

### Cross Ventilation Required

Each enclosed attic and enclosed rafter space must have **cross ventilation** — ventilation openings on at least two sides to allow air to flow through.

### Ventilation Opening Requirements (CRC R806.1)

- Openings must open **directly to outside air**
- Openings must be protected against entry of birds, rodents, snakes:
  - Minimum mesh: **1/16"** opening
  - Maximum mesh: **1/4"** opening
- Corrosion-resistant wire cloth, hardware cloth, perforated vinyl, or similar

---

## Minimum Vent Area Calculations (CRC R806.2)

### Default: 1/150 Ratio (Unbalanced)

> Net free ventilating area = **1/150** of the area of the vented space

**Example:** 1,500 SF attic / 150 = **10 SF** of net free vent area = **1,440 sq in NFA**

### Reduced: 1/300 Ratio (Balanced)

The minimum may be reduced to **1/300** when BOTH conditions are met:

1. **40-50% of required ventilation** is provided by ventilators in the **upper portion** of the attic (within 3 feet of the ridge or highest point)
2. The balance is provided by **eave or soffit vents** in the lower third
3. In IECC Climate Zones 6, 7, and 8: a **Class I or II vapor retarder** is installed on the warm-in-winter side of the ceiling

**Example:** 1,500 SF attic / 300 = **5 SF** of net free vent area = **720 sq in NFA**

### Quick-Reference Table

| Attic Floor Area (SF) | Unbalanced NFA (1/150) | Balanced NFA (1/300) |
|---|---|---|
| 500 | 480 sq in | 240 sq in |
| 1,000 | 960 sq in | 480 sq in |
| 1,500 | 1,440 sq in | 720 sq in |
| 2,000 | 1,920 sq in | 960 sq in |
| 2,500 | 2,400 sq in | 1,200 sq in |
| 3,000 | 2,880 sq in | 1,440 sq in |

Formula: Area (SF) / ratio = SF of NFA; SF x 144 = sq in NFA

## Insulation Clearance (CRC R806.3)

- A minimum **1-inch space** must be maintained between insulation and the roof sheathing at eave/cornice vent locations
- Blocking, bridging, and insulation must not obstruct the free flow of air
- Use baffles with loose-fill insulation to maintain the air channel

---

## Vaulted Ceilings / No Attic Space

Where the ceiling and roof are one assembly (cathedral ceiling) with no attic space:

- Each rafter bay must be individually ventilated
- Calculation: bay width x longest length / 150 = NFA per bay
- **Example:** 2 ft bay x 20 ft = 40 SF / 150 = 0.267 SF NFA = 38.4 sq in NFA per bay

---

## Unvented Attic Assemblies (CRC R806.5)

Unvented attics are permitted when ALL of the following are met:

1. Unvented space is **completely within the building thermal envelope**
2. **No Class I vapor retarder** on the ceiling side (attic floor)
3. If wood shingles/shakes: minimum **1/4" vented airspace** between shingles and underlayment above structural sheathing
4. In IECC Climate Zones 5-8: air-impermeable insulation must be Class II vapor retarder or have Class II coating
5. Insulation must comply with one of the approved configurations (air-impermeable in contact with sheathing, or rigid board above sheathing per Table R806.5)

### Table R806.5 — Condensation Control Insulation

| IECC Climate Zone | Min Rigid Board or Air-Impermeable R-Value |
|---|---|
| 2B, 3B (tile roof) | None required |
| 1, 2A, 2B, 3A, 3B, 3C | R-5 |
| 4C | R-10 |
| 4A, 4B | R-15 |
| 5 | R-20 |
| 6 | R-25 |
| 7 | R-30 |
| 8 | R-35 |

**Note:** Most of Southern California falls in IECC Climate Zones 3B or 3C, requiring only R-5 if using an unvented assembly.

## Ventilation Type Reference

| Vent Type | Typical Location | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Ridge vent | Along roof ridge | Best for balanced ventilation (upper) |
| Roof/turtle vent | Near ridge on roof slope | Alternative to ridge vent (upper) |
| Gable vent | In gable end wall | Can serve as upper or lower depending on height |
| Soffit/eave vent | Under eave overhang | Lower ventilation — essential for balanced system |
| Off-ridge vent | On roof slope below ridge | Upper ventilation |
| Powered attic vent | On roof or gable | Not recommended — can cause backdrafting of gas appliances |

## Common Review Findings

1. **No ventilation calculation provided** — FLAG. Reroof application should include NFA calculation showing how ventilation is achieved.
2. **Ridge vent removed but not replaced** — FAIL. If existing ventilation is removed during reroof, it must be replaced or equivalently provided.
3. **Blocked soffit vents** — FLAG if reroof scope includes fascia/soffit work. Insulation must not block eave vents.
4. **Only upper or only lower vents** — Cannot use the 1/300 ratio; must use 1/150 unless balanced.
