# Structural and Building Requirements for Solar PV

Source: 2025 California Residential Code (Title 24, Part 2.5), Section R329.

---

## General (R329.1, R329.3)

Solar energy systems shall comply with CRC R329. PV systems designed and installed per R329.3.1 through R329.8.1 and manufacturer's installation instructions. Electrical per CEC.

---

## Equipment Listings (R329.3.1)

| Equipment | Listing Standard |
|-----------|-----------------|
| PV panels and modules | UL 1703 or both UL 61730-1 and UL 61730-2 |
| Inverters | UL 1741 |
| Grid-tied inverters | UL 1741, listed for utility interaction |
| Mounting systems | UL 2703 -- installed per manufacturer instructions and listings |
| BIPV roof coverings and assemblies | UL 7103 |

## Rooftop-Mounted PV (R329.4)

### Structural Requirements (R329.4.1)

Rooftop PV shall be designed to structurally support the system and withstand applicable gravity loads per Chapter 3. Roof shall be designed for imposed loads per Chapter 8.

### Roof Load Design Cases (R329.4.1.1)

**Two load cases must be evaluated**:

| Load Case | Formula |
|-----------|---------|
| **Case 1** | Dead load (including PV panel weight) + snow load per Table R301.2 |
| **Case 2** | Dead load (excluding PV panel weight) + roof live load or snow load (whichever greater) per R301.6 |

**Key point**: Areas NOT covered by PV are designed per standard dead + live/snow loads (R301.4, R301.6). Areas covered by PV substitute the PV panel dead load for the live load in Case 1.

### Wind Load (R329.4.1.2)

Rooftop PV systems and supports shall resist component and cladding loads per Table R301.2.1(1), adjusted for height and exposure per Table R301.2.1(2).

### Fire Classification (R329.4.2)

PV system fire classification shall **match the roof assembly** rating required in Section R902.

- Class A, B, or C as required by the building's roof assembly
- Tested per UL 1703 or UL 61730 fire classification procedures

### Roof Penetrations (R329.4.3)

All roof penetrations shall be **flashed and sealed** per Chapter 9.

- Standoff/lag bolt penetrations require proper flashing
- Maintain roof weather integrity
- Structural calculations may be required if anchor bolts spaced **>48" on center** (common city requirement)

## Elevated PV Support Structures (R329.7)

Elevated PV support structures used as accessory structures comply with R329.7.1 or R329.7.2. Considered a "roof" for stories and fire separation distances.

### Over Open-Grid or Noncombustible Deck (R329.7.1)
- PV panels listed with fire type rating per UL 1703 or UL 61730-1/-2
- Panels marked "not fire rated" shall NOT be installed

### Over Roof Assembly (R329.7.2)
- Fire classification per R902.4 and R329.4

**Exception**: Elevated PV structures over agricultural use.

## Ground-Mounted PV (R329.8)

### General (R329.8)
Designed and installed per R301 (structural requirements).

### Fire Separation (R329.8.1)
Subject to fire separation distance requirements determined by the enforcing agency.

### California Amendment -- Ground-Mounted Arrays (R329.8.2)
- Comply with CRC and CEC
- Setback requirements **do not apply** to ground-mounted free-standing arrays
- **10-ft clear brush-free area** required

### DC Conductor Routing (R329.8.3)
California amendment [CA]:
- Conduit/wiring as close as possible to ridge/hip/valley
- Directly as possible to outside wall
- Minimize conduit in pathways between arrays
- DC wiring in metallic conduit/raceways in enclosed building spaces
- Conduit along bottom of load-bearing members

## Common Structural Review Items

### What to Check on Plans
1. **PV module weight** specified (typically 2-4 psf for standard modules)
2. **Mounting system** identified with UL 2703 listing
3. **Attachment point spacing** -- structural calcs required if >48" O.C.
4. **Roof framing adequacy** for added dead load (especially older homes)
5. **Wind zone and exposure** identified
6. **Roof penetrations** shown with flashing details
7. **Fire classification** of PV assembly matches roof requirement

### When Structural Engineering Is Required
- Anchor bolts spaced **>48" on center**
- Roof structure does not meet prescriptive span tables with added PV load
- Tile roofs or other non-standard substrates
- Ground-mounted systems requiring foundation design
- Elevated PV structures (carports, trellises)
- High wind zones or unusual exposure conditions
