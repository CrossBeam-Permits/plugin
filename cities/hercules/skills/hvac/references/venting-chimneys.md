# Gas Venting & Chimney Requirements

Source: 2025 California Mechanical Code (Title 24, Part 4), Chapter 8.

## General Venting Requirements

### Listing (CMC 802.1)

- Type B and Type B-W gas vents shall comply with UL 441
- Type L gas vents shall comply with UL 641
- Vents shall have testing agency listing and label
- **Exception**: Single-wall metal pipe and plastic pipe for Category IV as specified by manufacturer

### Installation (CMC 802.1.1)

Listed chimneys and vents shall be installed per this chapter AND the manufacturer's installation instructions.

### Connection Required (CMC 802.2)

All gas appliances shall be connected to a venting system.

**Exceptions** (appliances NOT required to be vented):
- Listed ranges
- Built-in cooking units listed for optional venting
- Listed hot plates
- Listed Type 1 clothes dryers (exhausted per 504.4)
- Listed refrigerators
- Counter appliances
- Room heaters listed for unvented use
- Direct vent appliances (vented per manufacturer)
- Listed gas fireplaces vented per manufacturer

### Conveyance to Outdoors (CMC 802.3)

The venting system shall convey ALL flue and vent gases to the outdoors.

### Fan-Assisted Category I (CMC 803.1)

Induced-draft furnaces shall be classified as "fan-assisted" Category I.

## Vent Locations and Protection

### Shield Above Insulation (CMC 802.6.1.1)

Sheet metal shield shall extend to **2 in. above attic insulation** where vent passes through.

### Screws Prohibited in Inner Wall (CMC 802.6(4))

Screws into the inner wall of double-wall vent are NOT allowed, except at the draft hood outlet or to a single-wall connector.

### Concealed Vents (CMC 802.2.6)

Where concealed vents are less than 1-1/2 in. from the face of framing members, they shall be protected with a minimum **16 ga. steel shield plate** extending a minimum of 4 in. beyond framing inside the wall.

### Prohibited Termination (CMC 802.5.3)

Vents shall NOT terminate through fabricated ducts or plenums.

---

## Vent Termination Requirements

### General (CMC 802.6.2)

Gas vents shall extend above the roof, except for:
- Direct vent appliances
- Appliances with integral vents
- Mechanical draft appliances installed per manufacturer's instructions

### Flashing (CMC 802.6.1(6))

Flashing shall be provided at all roof or wall vent penetrations. All gas vents shall extend through the roof flashing, roof jack, or roof thimble and terminate with a **listed cap or listed roof assembly**.

### Height Above Draft Hood (CMC 802.6.1)

Type B or L vent termination shall be a minimum of **5 ft vertical above the draft hood or flue collar**.

### Height Above Roof

| Condition | Minimum Height | Code Section |
|-----------|---------------|--------------|
| Vent diameter <= 12 in., roof slope < 6:12 | 12 in. above roof | CMC 802.6.2(1A) |
| Vent diameter <= 12 in., roof slope >= 6:12 | Per CMC Table 802.6.1 | CMC 802.6.1 |
| Vent diameter > 12 in. | 2 ft above roof | CMC 802.6.2(1B) |
| Located < 8 ft from vertical surface above roofline | Per CMC Table 802.6.1 | CMC 802.6.1 |

### Clearance to Forced Air Inlet (CMC 802.6.1(7))

Vent shall terminate a minimum of **3 ft above any forced air inlet** located within 10 ft of the vent.

## Clearance to Combustibles — Vents

| Vent Type | Minimum Clearance | Code Section |
|-----------|-------------------|--------------|
| Single-wall metal pipe | 6 in. to combustibles | CMC 802.6.1 |
| Type B double-wall vent | 1 in. to combustibles (or per listing) | CMC 802.6.1 |
| Direct vent | Per manufacturer's installation instructions | CMC 802.6.1 |

---

## Vent Sizing (CMC 803.0)

Category I venting systems shall be sized per CMC Chapter 8, Section 803 tables, which account for:
- Total BTU input
- Vent height (total vertical rise)
- Vent connector length and rise
- Number of elbows/offsets
- Single vs. common venting

### Induced-Draft / Fan-Assisted (CMC 803.1)

Fan-assisted furnaces use the "FAN" columns in the vent sizing tables. Natural draft appliances use the "NAT" columns.

| Type B clearance | 1 in. min to combustibles | CMC 802.6.1 |
