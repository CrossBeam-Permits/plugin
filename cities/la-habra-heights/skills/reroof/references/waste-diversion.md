# Construction Waste Diversion Requirements

Source: 2025 California Green Building Standards Code (Title 24, Part 11 — CALGreen), Section 5.408.

---

## Requirement

All construction projects that generate waste, including reroof tear-offs, must:

> **Recycle or salvage a minimum of 65% of nonhazardous construction and demolition waste.**

This applies to the tear-off debris (old shingles, underlayment, nails, flashing) generated during a roof replacement.

---

## Waste Volume Estimates for Reroof

| Roof Size (squares) | Approximate Tear-Off Weight |
|---|---|
| 10 squares (1,000 sq ft) | 1–2 tons |
| 20 squares (2,000 sq ft) | 2–3 tons |
| 30 squares (3,000 sq ft) | 3–5 tons |
| 50 squares (5,000 sq ft) | 5–8 tons |

Weight varies by material: asphalt shingles ~2–3 lbs/sq ft; tile ~8–15 lbs/sq ft (significantly heavier debris).

---

## Compliance Methods (CALGreen 5.408.1)

Three paths to compliance:

### Method 1: Waste Management Plan
- Submit a construction waste management plan to the enforcing agency
- Plan must identify:
  - Materials to be diverted from disposal
  - Whether waste will be sorted on-site or sent as mixed bulk to a certified facility
  - Diversion facilities to be used (recycling centers, transfer stations)
- Plan must be accessible at the job site during construction

### Method 2: Certified Waste Management Company
- Use a waste management company that provides verifiable documentation of diversion rates
- Company must demonstrate the 65% diversion rate through hauler records

### Method 3: Weight-Based Alternative
- Demonstrate total waste disposed does not exceed **2 lbs per square foot** of building area
- Requires approval from the enforcing agency
- This alternative may be useful for very small projects

---

## Documentation (CALGreen 5.408.2–5.408.3)

- Provide waste management documentation to the enforcing agency
- Keep waste hauling receipts, diversion reports, and facility records
- Documentation should show total waste generated, amount diverted, and amount disposed

## What Counts Toward Diversion?

| Material | Recyclable? | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Asphalt shingles | Yes | Recycled into road paving material |
| Concrete/clay tile | Yes | Crushed for aggregate or fill |
| Metal flashing/edging | Yes | Scrap metal recycling |
| Wood sheathing/lumber | Yes | Biomass or wood recycling |
| Nails/fasteners | Yes | Scrap metal |
| Underlayment (felt) | Varies | Some facilities accept, some don't |
| Plastic packaging | Yes | Standard recycling |

**Not counted / excluded:**
- Excavated soil and land-clearing debris
- Hazardous materials (asbestos, lead paint) — separate disposal requirements
- Demolition waste governed by separate local ordinance

---

## Practical Review Notes

1. **Overlay projects generate minimal waste** — the waste diversion requirement is primarily relevant for tear-off projects.
2. **Most roofing contractors handle this automatically** — they use roll-off dumpsters picked up by waste haulers with documented diversion rates.
3. **The permit reviewer's role** is to confirm that the applicant acknowledges the waste diversion requirement, not to verify the actual diversion rate (that happens at final inspection or project completion).
4. **Buena Park's form** includes a "Waste Disposal Tracking Information" section with links to compliance forms — this satisfies the documentation trigger.
5. **Asbestos warning:** Pre-1980s homes may have asbestos-containing roof materials. If the project involves a pre-1980 structure, FLAG for asbestos survey before tear-off.
