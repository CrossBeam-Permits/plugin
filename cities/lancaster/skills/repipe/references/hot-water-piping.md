# CPC Hot Water Piping, Insulation & General Installation

Source: 2025 California Plumbing Code (Title 24, Part 5), Chapter 6.

---

## Hot Water Pipe Insulation (609.12.1, 609.12.2)

**All domestic hot water piping shall be insulated** (CPC 609.12.1).

### Insulation Thickness (609.12.2)

| Pipe Diameter | Minimum Insulation Thickness |
|--------------|------------------------------|
| Up to 2 in. | Greater than or equal to the pipe diameter |
| 2 in. or more | 2 in. minimum |

**Exceptions:**
1. Piping that penetrates framing members does not require insulation for the distance of the framing penetration.
2. Hot water piping between the fixture control valve or supply stop and the fixture/appliance does not require insulation.

### California Energy Code Requirements (609.12.3)

See California Energy Code Sections 150.0(j)2 and 120.3(c) for additional pipe insulation requirements based on fluid temperature and pipe diameter. The Energy Code requires:
- Piping penetrating metal framing shall use grommets, plugs, wrapping, or other insulating material (no metal contact)
- Insulation shall abut securely against all framing members
- Conditions apply for when wall/attic insulation surrounding pipes counts as pipe insulation

## Maximum Hot Water Developed Length

Hot water piping shall have a maximum developed length of **100 ft.** from the source of hot water to fixtures (CPC 609.11).

> This limit ensures reasonable wait time for hot water at fixtures and reduces water waste.

---

## Water Hammer Arrestors (609.11)

Building water supply systems where **quick-acting valves** are installed shall be provided with water hammer arresters.

| Requirement | Detail | Code Section |
|-------------|--------|-------------|
| **Device standard** | ASSE 1010 or PDI-WH 201 (mechanical devices, NOT air chambers) | CPC 609.11 |
| **Location** | As close as possible to quick-acting valves | CPC 609.11 |
| **Typical locations** | Near dishwasher, clothes washer, ice maker connections | CPC 609.11 |

---

## Dead Legs (309.6)

Dead legs (dead-end pipe sections) shall have a **method of flushing**.

> In repipe projects, ensure any abandoned-in-place stub-outs or dead-end runs are either capped with access for flushing or fully removed.

---

## Flex Connectors (604.5)

| Requirement | Detail | Code Section |
|-------------|--------|-------------|
| **Location** | Shall be installed in accessible locations | CPC 604.5 |
| **Continuous pressure** | Where under continuous pressure, shall comply with ASME A112.18.6/CSA B125.6 | CPC 604.5 |

---

## Burred Ends (609.1, 309.3)

Burred ends of piping shall be **reamed to the full bore** of the pipe or tubing.

---

## Previously Used Piping (604.8)

Piping or tubing previously used for any purpose **other than potable water** shall NOT be used for potable water.

---

## Unions Near Equipment (609.5)

Unions shall be installed within **12 in.** of water heaters, softeners, filters, regulators, and similar equipment that may require removal for servicing.
