# CEC Article 250 — Grounding and Bonding

Source: 2025 California Electrical Code (Title 24, Part 3), Article 250.

Grounding and bonding is one of the most critical and most commonly cited areas in panel upgrade inspections. The entire grounding electrode system must be verified or upgraded when the service panel changes.

---

## 250.50 — Grounding Electrode System

**All grounding electrodes present at the building must be bonded together** to form the grounding electrode system. You cannot pick and choose — if it exists, it must be connected.

The electrodes described in 250.52(A)(1) through (A)(7) that are present shall all be bonded together. If none of those exist, one or more from 250.52(A)(4) through (A)(8) must be installed.

*Exception: Concrete-encased electrodes of existing buildings are not required to be part of the system if the rebar is not accessible without disturbing the concrete.*

---

## 250.52 — Permitted Grounding Electrodes

### Electrodes That Must Be Used If Present

| Electrode | CEC Section | Description |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| **Metal underground water pipe** | 250.52(A)(1) | In direct contact with earth for 3 m (10 ft) or more. Must be supplemented per 250.53(D)(2). |
| **Metal in-ground support structure** | 250.52(A)(2) | Metal building frame in direct contact with earth or concrete-encased electrode. |
| **Concrete-encased electrode (Ufer)** | 250.52(A)(3) | At least 6 m (20 ft) of bare copper #4 AWG or larger, or 13 mm (1/2 in) rebar, encased in at least 50 mm (2 in) of concrete in contact with earth. |
| **Ground ring** | 250.52(A)(4) | Bare copper #2 AWG or larger, at least 6 m (20 ft) long, in direct contact with earth at 750 mm (2.5 ft) depth. |
| **Ground rod** | 250.52(A)(5) | Listed, not less than 2.44 m (8 ft) long, driven to at least 8 ft depth. |
| **Other listed electrodes** | 250.52(A)(6) | Listed grounding electrodes per UL 467. |
| **Plate electrode** | 250.52(A)(7) | At least 0.186 m2 (2 ft2) exposed to soil. |

### Prohibited Electrodes (250.52(B))

The following shall **NOT** be used as grounding electrodes:
- Metal underground **gas piping** systems
- **Aluminum** electrodes
- **Pool or spa shell** bonding grids
- Local metal underground systems such as **tanks** (250.52(A)(8) — these are not permitted as sole electrodes)

### Most Common Residential Configuration

A typical single-family home in Southern California has:
1. **Ground rod(s)** — one or two driven rods (most common)
2. **Metal water pipe** — if the home has a metallic water service line
3. **Concrete-encased (Ufer)** — if accessible (newer construction)

## 250.52 — Detailed Electrode Specifications

### Water Pipe Electrode (250.52(A)(1))

- Must have at least **3 m (10 ft)** of metallic pipe in direct contact with soil
- Must be **supplemented** by another electrode type (250.53(D)(2))
- Bond must be provided **around** water meters, filters, pressure regulators, and similar equipment to ensure continuity (250.53(D)(1))

### Concrete-Encased Electrode / Ufer (250.52(A)(3))

- At least **6 m (20 ft)** of either:
  - Uncoupled **#4 rebar** (minimum), or
  - Bare **#4 AWG copper** wire
- Encased in at least **50 mm (2 in)** of concrete
- Must be in footings or piers in **direct earth contact**

### Metal In-Ground Support Structures (250.52(A)(4))

- Must have at least **3 m (10 ft)** of vertical contact in direct contact with earth
- May be with or without concrete encasement

### Ground Ring (250.52(A)(4))

- Must **encircle** the building or structure
- Must be in direct contact with earth
- Minimum **#2 AWG copper**
- Minimum **6 m (20 ft)** in length in direct contact with earth
- Minimum **750 mm (30 in)** below the surface (250.53(F))

### Ground Rod (250.52(A)(5))

- Copper-clad rods: minimum **16 mm (5/8 in)** diameter unless otherwise listed
- Minimum **2.44 m (8 ft)** in contact with soil
- Must be driven **vertically** and fully below grade (250.53(A)(4))
- Rod ends and clamps above ground must have **protection against physical damage**

### Plate Electrode (250.52(A)(7))

- Ferrous plates: minimum **6 mm (1/4 in)** thick
- Minimum **0.186 m2 (2 sq ft)** of surface in contact with soil
- Must be buried minimum **750 mm (30 in)** below earth surface (250.53(A)(5))

## 250.53 — Grounding Electrode Installation

### Ground Rod Requirements

#### Single Rod Must Be Supplemented (250.53(A)(2))
A single ground rod must be supplemented by an additional electrode (another rod, pipe, plate, or other type from 250.52(A)(2) through (A)(8)).

**Exception**: A second rod is not required if a **certified third-party test report** is submitted verifying resistance to ground of the single rod or plate electrode does not exceed **25 ohms**. In practice, most inspectors require two rods because proving resistance requires testing that is rarely done on residential permits.

#### Electrode Spacing (250.53(A)(3))
If more than one rod/pipe/plate electrode is used, they must be at least **1.83 m (6 ft) apart**. Best practice / preferred spacing: **4.88 m (16 ft)** apart.

#### Supplemental Electrode Bond (250.53(A)(3))
The supplemental electrode bond shall be made to one of the following:
1. A rod, pipe, or plate electrode
2. A grounding electrode conductor
3. A grounded service conductor
4. A grounded service enclosure

#### Metal Water Pipe Supplementation (250.53(D)(2))
A metal underground water pipe electrode must be supplemented by an additional electrode. The water pipe electrode shall **not be the sole electrode**. The supplemental electrode can be bonded to:
1. Grounding electrode conductor
2. Grounded service-entrance conductor
3. Nonflexible grounded service raceway
4. Service enclosure
5. Interior metal water piping within 1.5 m (5 ft) of the point of entrance

### Water Pipe Bonding Continuity (250.53(D)(1))
Bond must be provided **around** water meters, filters, pressure regulators, and similar equipment to ensure continuity of the grounding path.

### Bonding All Electrodes Together (250.53(C))
The bonding jumper(s) connecting electrodes to form the system must be:
- Installed per 250.64(A), (B), and (E)
- Sized per Table 250.122 (for bonding conductors between electrodes)
- Connected per 250.70

**Rebar shall not be used as a conductor to interconnect electrodes.**

## 250.66 — GEC Sizing

The grounding electrode conductor (GEC) connects the service equipment to the grounding electrode system. Size it from Table 250.66 based on the largest ungrounded service conductor.

### Table 250.66 — Grounding Electrode Conductor for AC Systems

| Largest Ungrounded Conductor (Cu) | Largest Ungrounded Conductor (Al) | GEC Size (Cu) | GEC Size (Al) |
|----------------------------------|----------------------------------|---------------|---------------|
| #2 or smaller | #1/0 or smaller | **#8** | **#6** |
| #1 or #1/0 | #2/0 or #3/0 | **#6** | **#4** |
| #2/0 or #3/0 | #4/0 or 250 kcmil | **#4** | **#2** |
| Over #3/0 through 350 kcmil | Over 250 through 500 kcmil | **#2** | **#1/0** |
| Over 350 through 600 kcmil | Over 500 through 900 kcmil | **#1/0** | **#3/0** |
| Over 600 through 1100 kcmil | Over 900 through 1750 kcmil | **#2/0** | **#4/0** |
| Over 1100 kcmil | Over 1750 kcmil | **#3/0** | **250 kcmil** |

### GEC Size Exceptions (Important for Residential)

| Electrode Type | Max GEC Size Required | CEC Section |
|---------------|----------------------|-------------|
| **Rod, pipe, or plate only** | #6 Cu or #4 Al | 250.66(A) |
| **Concrete-encased (Ufer) only** | #4 Cu | 250.66(B) |
| **Ground ring only** | Same as ring conductor | 250.66(C) |

These exceptions apply only when the GEC connects to that electrode type alone and does not extend to other electrodes requiring a larger conductor.

## 250.28 — Main Bonding Jumper

For a grounded system (which includes all standard residential 120/240V services), an unspliced main bonding jumper is required at the service disconnect. It connects:
- Equipment grounding conductors
- Service-disconnect enclosure
- Grounded conductor (neutral)

### Sizing (250.28(D))

The main bonding jumper must not be smaller than specified in **Table 250.102(C)(1)**:

| Largest Ungrounded Conductor (Cu) | Largest Ungrounded Conductor (Al) | Bonding Jumper (Cu) | Bonding Jumper (Al) |
|----------------------------------|----------------------------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| #2 or smaller | #1/0 or smaller | #8 | #6 |
| #1 or #1/0 | #2/0 or #3/0 | #6 | #4 |
| #2/0 or #3/0 | #4/0 or 250 kcmil | #4 | #2 |
| Over #3/0 through 350 kcmil | Over 250 through 500 kcmil | #2 | #1/0 |
| Over 350 through 600 kcmil | Over 500 through 900 kcmil | #1/0 | #3/0 |
| Over 600 through 1100 kcmil | Over 900 through 1750 kcmil | #2/0 | #4/0 |

### Practical Bonding Jumper Sizing

| Service Size | Service Conductor (Cu) | Bonding Jumper (Cu) |
|-------------|----------------------|--------------------|
| 100A | #4 AWG | #8 AWG |
| 125A | #2 AWG | #8 AWG |
| 150A | #1 AWG | #6 AWG |
| 200A | #2/0 AWG | #4 AWG |
| 225A | #3/0 AWG | #4 AWG |
| 320A | 350 kcmil | #2 AWG |
| 400A | 500 kcmil | #1/0 AWG |

**Note**: In most modern residential panels, the main bonding jumper is a factory-installed green bonding screw or strap. If the panel is listed as service equipment and has the bonding screw/strap installed, this check is typically satisfied. Verify it is present and properly installed.

## 250.64 — GEC Installation

The grounding electrode conductor must be:
- **Copper, aluminum, or copper-clad aluminum** — bare, covered, or insulated
- **Continuous** — without splice or joint, except:
  - Irreversible compression connectors listed for grounding
  - Exothermic welding
  - Connections to busbars (min 6mm x 50mm / 1/4" x 2")
- **Protected** if exposed to physical damage
- **Securely fastened** to the surface or protected in a raceway

### Raceway for GEC
If the GEC is enclosed in a ferrous metal raceway (steel conduit), both ends of the raceway must be bonded to the GEC to prevent inductive heating. This is a common inspection item.

## Common Panel Upgrade Grounding Issues

These are the most frequently cited grounding deficiencies in residential panel upgrade inspections:

1. **Missing or broken ground rod bond** — the GEC was disconnected from the old panel and not reconnected to the new one
2. **Water pipe bond missing** — metallic water pipe exists but is not bonded to the grounding system
3. **Single ground rod without supplement** — only one rod, no second rod or other supplemental electrode
4. **GEC undersized for new service** — the old GEC was adequate for 100A but is too small for 200A
5. **Bonding jumper missing or too small** — the green bonding screw is not installed, or the jumper wire is undersized
6. **Neutral-ground bond at subpanel** — if the old main panel becomes a subpanel, the bonding jumper must be REMOVED (grounds and neutrals must be separated at subpanels)
7. **GEC in steel conduit not bonded at both ends** — creates inductive heating hazard
