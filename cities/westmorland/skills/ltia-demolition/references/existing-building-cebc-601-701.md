# CEBC -- Classification of Work & Alterations

Source: 2025 California Existing Building Code (Title 24, Part 10), Chapters 6, 7, and 8.

---

## Classification of Work (CEBC Chapter 6)

### Scope (601.1)
The provisions of Chapter 6 are used in conjunction with Chapters 7 through 11 and apply to the alteration, addition and change of occupancy of existing structures. The work performed on an existing building shall be classified in accordance with this chapter.

### Work Area (601.2)
The work area shall be identified on the construction documents.

> **FOR LTIA**: The demolition plans must clearly identify the work area -- the specific tenant space(s) being demolished, with square footage.

### Alteration -- Level 1 (602.1)
Level 1 alterations include the removal and replacement or the covering of existing materials, elements, equipment or fixtures using new materials that serve the same purpose. Level 1 alterations shall comply with Chapter 7.

### Alteration -- Level 2 (603.1)
Level 2 alterations include the addition or elimination of any door or window, the reconfiguration or extension of any system, or the installation of any additional equipment, and apply where the work area is equal to or less than 50 percent of the building area.

**Exception:** The movement or addition of nonfixed and movable fixtures, cases, racks, counters and partitions not over 5 feet 9 inches in height is not a Level 2 alteration.

### Alteration -- Level 3 (604.1)
Level 3 alterations apply where the work area exceeds 50 percent of the building area.

> **FOR LTIA**: Interior demolition of a tenant space typically classifies as a Level 1 or Level 2 alteration depending on whether doors/windows are being eliminated or systems reconfigured. The subsequent tenant improvement (TI) build-out is always a separate permit.

## Level 1 Alteration Requirements (CEBC Chapter 7)

### Conformance (701.2)
An existing building or portion thereof shall not be altered such that the building becomes **less safe** than its existing condition.

**Exception:** Where the current level of safety or sanitation is proposed to be reduced, the portion altered shall conform to the requirements of the California Building Code.

> **CRITICAL**: This is the foundational principle for LTIA demolition. The demolition must not make the building less safe. Fire ratings, egress paths, structural integrity, and accessibility must all be maintained or improved.

### Materials and Methods (702.7)
New work shall comply with the materials and methods requirements in the California Building Code, California Energy Code, California Mechanical Code and California Plumbing Code, as applicable.

### Fire Protection -- General (703.1)
Alterations shall be done in a manner that maintains the level of fire protection provided.

### Fire Alarm and Detection (703.2)
Replacement devices shall be listed and approved. Existing fire alarm and detection systems shall be maintained per Section 901.7 of the California Fire Code.

### Construction in Existing Buildings (703.3)
On-site fire protection during construction shall be in accordance with Chapter 33 of the CBC and the California Fire Code.

### Means of Egress (704.1)
Alterations shall be done in a manner that maintains the level of protection provided for the means of egress.

## Level 2 Alteration Requirements (CEBC Chapter 8)

### Scope (801.1)
Level 2 alterations as described in Section 603 shall comply with Chapter 8.

### New Construction Compliance (801.4)
New construction elements, components, systems and spaces shall comply with the requirements of the California Building Code.

### Interior Finish in Corridors (802.4)
The interior finish and trim of walls and ceilings in exits and corridors in any work area shall comply with the CBC.

### Fire Alarm and Detection (803.4)
An approved fire alarm system shall be installed in accordance with California Fire Code Sections 907 and 1103.7.

### Means of Egress (804.1)
The requirements apply to work areas that include exits or corridors shared by more than one tenant.

### Dead-End Corridors (804.8)
Dead-end corridors in any work area shall not exceed **35 feet**.

**Exceptions:**
1. Where dead-end corridors of greater length are permitted by the CBC.
2. In other than Group A: up to 50 feet with automatic fire alarm system.
3. In other than Group A: up to 70 feet with automatic sprinkler system.

### Electrical (806.1)
Newly installed electrical equipment and wiring relating to work done in any work area shall comply with all applicable requirements of the California Electrical Code.

### Mechanical (807.1)
Reconfigured spaces intended for occupancy and spaces converted to habitable or occupiable space in any work area shall be provided with natural or mechanical ventilation per the California Mechanical Code.
