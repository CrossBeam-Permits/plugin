# Sign Material and Fire Resistance Requirements

Source: 2025 California Building Code (Title 24, Part 2), Appendix H Sections H107-H113; Chapter 26 (Plastics); CEC Article 600.

---

## Material Requirements by Sign Type

### Summary Table

| Sign Type | Material Requirement | Code Section |
|---|---|---|
| **Wall sign (>40 sq ft)** | Metal or approved noncombustible (except nailing rails) | H111.1 |
| **Wall sign (<=40 sq ft)** | No specific material restriction | H111.1 (by omission) |
| **Projecting sign** | Entirely metal or noncombustible | H112.1 |
| **Ground sign (structural frame)** | Combustible frame: max 35 ft; noncombustible: max 100 ft | H109.1 |
| **Roof sign** | Entirely metal or noncombustible | H110.1 |
| **Marquee sign** | Entirely metal or noncombustible | H113.1 |
| **Portable sign** | Per applicable type (ground, projecting, etc.) | H114.1 |

### Exceptions Allowing Combustible Materials

All sign types that require noncombustible construction allow the following exceptions:

1. **Internally illuminated sign facings** (H106.1.1): Wood or approved plastic per CBC 2606.4, max 120 sq ft facing area per section, with 2" minimum wiring clearance. Flexible sign face plastic (weighing <20 oz/sq yd, passing NFPA 701) is exempt from the 120 sq ft limit.

2. **Combustible trim/elements** (H107.1): Wood, approved plastics, or plastic veneer panels may be used for:
   - Moldings
   - Cappings
   - Nailing blocks
   - Letters
   - Latticing

   These must comply with H109.1 (height restrictions for combustible construction) but shall not be used for other ornamental features unless approved.

## Plastic Materials in Signs

### Approved Plastics (H107.1.1)

Plastics may be used as display surfaces, letters, decorations, and facings if they burn at a rate **not faster than 2.5 inches per minute** when tested per ASTM D635.

### Electric Sign Faces (H107.1.2)

Individual plastic facings of electric signs shall not exceed **200 sq ft** per facing.

### Area Limitation for Large Signs (H107.1.3)

When total display surface exceeds 200 sq ft:
- Plastic area = 200 sq ft + 50% of (total display area - 200 sq ft)
- **Maximum plastic area in any case: 1,100 sq ft**

**Calculation example:**
- Sign display surface = 600 sq ft
- Plastic area allowed = 200 + 50% x (600 - 200) = 200 + 200 = 400 sq ft

### Plastic Appurtenances (H107.1.4)

Letters and decorations mounted ON a plastic facing or display surface may be made of plastics complying with H107.1.1 (2.5 in/min burn rate test).

## Fire Safety — Heat and Combustibility

### Temperature Limits (CEC 600.9(C))

Signs shall not subject adjacent combustible materials to temperatures exceeding **194 deg F (90 deg C)**.

| Spacing Requirement | Minimum Distance |
|---|---|
| Wood or combustible material to incandescent or HID lamp/lampholder | 2 inches |
| Wiring to facing material (internally illuminated signs) | 2 inches (H106.1.1) |

### Illumination — Fire Safety (H106.1)

- Signs shall be illuminated ONLY by electrical means.
- Open sparks or flames are NOT permitted for display purposes unless specifically approved.
- This is a blanket prohibition — gas-lit signs, flaming signs, etc. are not allowed.

## Enclosure Material Requirements (CEC 600.8)

### Live Parts
All live parts (other than lamps and neon tubing) must be enclosed.

### Enclosure Construction
Sign enclosures must be metal or listed.

### Minimum Metal Thickness

| Material | Minimum Thickness |
|---|---|
| Sheet copper or aluminum | 0.020" (0.51 mm) |
| Sheet steel | 0.016" (0.41 mm) |

### Corrosion Protection
All metal parts of equipment must be protected from corrosion. This is especially important for:
- Coastal California installations (salt air)
- Signs in wet locations
- Ground-level signs exposed to irrigation/rain splash

## Wet Location Requirements

### Weatherproofing (CEC 600.9(D))

Signs in wet locations (exterior signs exposed to weather) that are not listed watertight type must be:
- Weatherproof construction
- Provided with drain holes:
  - Size: 1/4" to 1/2" diameter
  - Location: at every low point or isolated section
  - Positioning: no external obstructions to drainage

### Outdoor Signs — General

Virtually all exterior signs are considered "wet location" equipment. This means:
- Enclosures must be weatherproof or watertight
- Wiring and electrical equipment must be rated for wet locations
- Drain holes must be provided in sign cabinets

## Roof Sign Noncombustible Requirement (H110.1)

Roof signs have the strictest material requirements due to fire spread risk:
- Constructed **entirely** of metal or approved noncombustible material
- Electric grounding of metallic parts is mandatory
- Where combustible materials are permitted (letters, ornamental features per H106.1.1 and H107.1):
  - Wiring and tubing must be kept free from and insulated from combustible materials

## Ground Sign Construction (H109)

### Combustible vs. Noncombustible Height Limits

| Material Type | Max Height Above Ground |
|---|---|
| Combustible structural frame | 35 ft |
| Entirely noncombustible | 100 ft |
| Greater (with approval) | Varies — must not create public hazard |

### Clearance Below Sign
The bottom coping must be at least 3 ft above ground. The space below may be filled with:
- Platform decorative trim
- Light wooden construction

### Wood in Ground Contact
Wood anchors or supports embedded in soil must be **pressure treated** with an approved preservative (H109.3).

## Common Sign Materials — Compliance Guide

| Material | Typical Use | Code Compliance Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Aluminum** | Cabinet signs, channel letters, panels | Noncombustible. Min 0.020" thick for enclosures. Corrosion resistant. |
| **Steel (galvanized/painted)** | Sign structures, poles, brackets | Noncombustible. Min 0.016" thick for enclosures. Must be corrosion-protected. |
| **Stainless steel** | High-end letters, trim | Noncombustible. Excellent corrosion resistance. |
| **Acrylic (PMMA)** | Sign faces, channel letter faces | Must pass ASTM D635 (<2.5 in/min burn rate). Max 200 sq ft per electric sign face. |
| **Polycarbonate (Lexan)** | Impact-resistant sign faces | Must pass ASTM D635. Check listing for sign use. |
| **PVC / expanded PVC (Sintra)** | Cut letters, dimensional signs | Must pass ASTM D635. Not permitted where noncombustible is required unless within exempted categories (letters, decorations). |
| **Wood** | Carved signs, rustic signs, frames | Combustible — restricts height to 35 ft (ground signs), prohibited for projecting and roof signs except as trim/letters. |
| **HDU (high-density urethane)** | Carved dimensional signs | Combustible — same restrictions as wood. Often used for letters/decorations per H107.1. |
| **Flex face (vinyl)** | Large cabinet sign faces | Must pass NFPA 701; exempt from 120 sq ft limit if <20 oz/sq yd. |
| **Glass** | Specialty signs, retail | See Table H115.1(1) for thickness by panel size. Wired glass required >144" dimension or >3,600 sq in. |
| **LED modules** | Channel letters, cabinet signs, displays | Electrical components per CEC 600. Must be listed. |
| **Neon tubing** | Channel letters, decorative, art | CEC 600.41-600.42. Listed tube supports. 1/4" min clearance from surfaces. |

## Key Principles

1. **Noncombustible is the default** for projecting signs, roof signs, and marquee signs. Wall signs >40 sq ft also require noncombustible construction. Only ground signs and small wall signs may use combustible framing.

2. **Plastics are widely used but restricted.** The 2.5 in/min burn rate test (ASTM D635) is the gatekeeper. Plastic sign faces are limited to 200 sq ft each, with a sliding-scale area limit for large signs (max 1,100 sq ft total plastic).

3. **Interior illumination adds requirements.** Internally illuminated signs must maintain 2" clearance between wiring and facing, and face sections are limited to 120 sq ft (with the flex-face exception).

4. **Coastal California demands corrosion protection.** All metal parts must be protected from corrosion (CEC 600.8(D)). Galvanized, stainless, or properly coated materials are essential in coastal installations.

5. **Wet location is the norm for exterior signs.** Every outdoor sign needs weatherproof enclosures and drain holes unless listed watertight.
