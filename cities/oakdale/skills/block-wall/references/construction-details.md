# Block Wall Construction Details, Sequence, and Inspections

Source: 2025 California Residential Code (Title 24, Part 2.5), Section R606.3.5; TMS 402/602-22; California Building Code (Part 2), Chapter 21.

---

## Expansion and Control Joints

### Purpose
CMU walls expand and contract with temperature and moisture changes. Without control joints, walls develop random, unsightly cracking. Control joints provide a deliberate weak point where movement occurs in a controlled manner.

### Spacing Requirements

| Source | Maximum Spacing |
|---|---|
| TMS 402 recommendation | Based on L/H ratio; typically **20-25 ft** for a 6 ft wall |
| Common city standard details | 20-30 ft maximum |
| Conservative rule of thumb | **2.5x wall height** (6 ft wall = 15 ft max) |
| More relaxed rule | **3x wall height** (6 ft wall = 18 ft max) |

### Required Locations
Control joints are required at:
1. **Maximum spacing intervals** (per above)
2. **Changes in wall height** (where wall steps up or down)
3. **Changes in wall thickness** (where wall transitions from 8" to 6", etc.)
4. **Changes in wall direction** (corners — use control joint OR provide corner reinforcement)
5. **At or near openings** in the wall (gates, pass-throughs)
6. **Where the wall connects to a building** or other rigid structure
7. **At pilasters** (unless pilaster is part of the wall design)

### Construction Details
- Use a **pre-formed rubber or foam control joint strip** in the mortar joint
- **Rebar does NOT cross** the control joint (the joint is a deliberate break)
- For horizontal reinforcement continuity: use **smooth dowels, greased on one side** to allow horizontal movement
- Seal the exterior face with **backer rod and elastomeric sealant** (typically polyurethane or silicone)
- Joint width: **3/8" to 1/2"** (same as mortar joint, but filled with sealant, not mortar)

### Common Mistakes
- Omitting control joints entirely (leads to random cracking within 1-2 years)
- Running rebar through control joints (negates the joint's purpose)
- Using mortar instead of sealant in the control joint
- Spacing joints too far apart
- Not providing joints at changes in geometry

## Grouting Procedures

### When Grouting is Required
- **All cells containing reinforcement:** Always fully grouted — no exceptions
- **Non-reinforced cells:** Grouting optional for standard walls; recommended for durability and impact resistance
- **SDC D freestanding walls:** Only cells with reinforcement must be grouted (per exception in CBC 2106.1.3)
- **SDC D bearing/shear walls:** All cells fully grouted

### Grout Lift Heights

| Maximum Pour Height | Minimum Grout Space Width | Minimum Cell Dimensions |
|---|---|---|
| 1 ft | 3/4" (fine) / 1.5" (coarse) | 1.5" x 2" (fine) / 1.5" x 3" (coarse) |
| 5 ft | 2" (fine) / 2" (coarse) | 2" x 3" (fine) / 2.5" x 3" (coarse) |
| 12 ft | 2.5" (fine) / 2.5" (coarse) | 2.5" x 3" (fine) / 3" x 3" (coarse) |
| 24 ft | 3" (fine) / 3" (coarse) | 3" x 3" (fine) / 3" x 4" (coarse) |

Source: CRC Table R606.3.5.1

### Grout Consolidation
1. **Initial consolidation:** Immediately after grout placement, consolidate by:
   - **Mechanical vibration** (preferred): Low-amplitude, high-frequency vibrator inserted into each grouted cell
   - **Puddling:** Rod or stick pushed repeatedly into the grout
2. **Reconsolidation:** 3-5 minutes after initial consolidation, vibrate or puddle again to account for grout settling as excess moisture is absorbed by the CMU
3. **Timing:** Grout must be placed before initial set — within 1.5 hours of water addition

### Grout Pour Rules (CRC R606.3.5.1)
- Maximum grout pour height: **8 feet per lift**
- Where total pour exceeds 8 ft: place in lifts not exceeding **64 inches (5 ft 4 in)** with special inspection
- At horizontal construction joints: stop all lifts at same elevation, grout **1 inch below** top of last course
- If work stops for 1 hour or more: form a horizontal construction joint

### California Amendment (CBC 2104.2.4)
For horizontal construction joints between grout pours:
- Terminate grout **minimum 1.5 inches** below a mortar joint (maximum one-half unit height)
- Exception at top of wall
- At bond beams: terminate minimum 1/2 inch below the mortar joint with 1 inch minimum grout cover above rebar

## Cleanout Openings

### When Required
Cleanout openings are required at the **base of grouted cells** where the grout lift exceeds **64 inches (5 ft 4 in)** — per CRC R606.3.5.2.

### Specifications

| Parameter | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Size | Minimum **3" x 3"** opening (one face shell removed at bottom course) |
| Spacing | At each grouted cell, or at 32" OC maximum in solid-grouted walls |
| Purpose | Inspection and removal of mortar droppings before grouting |
| Closure | Seal with a block piece or other approved closure before grouting |

### Walls <=4 ft
Cleanout openings are **typically not required** for walls 4 ft or shorter because:
- The entire wall can be grouted in a single lift (well under 64" limit)
- Reconsolidation adequately handles the short pour height
- Mortar droppings in short pours are less problematic

### Walls 4-6 ft
Gray area — a 6 ft wall is 72 inches, exceeding the 64" threshold:
- **Option 1:** Provide cleanouts at the base per code
- **Option 2:** Pour in two lifts (first 4 ft, then remaining 2 ft) — each under 64", avoiding cleanout requirement
- Most cities accept Option 2 for standard freestanding walls

### Cleanout Procedure
1. At the bottom course, knock out one face shell of each cell to be grouted (or every other cell)
2. After wall is fully laid and reinforcement is in place, inspect through cleanout openings
3. Remove any mortar droppings, debris, or foreign material from the cell bottoms
4. Replace face shell pieces and seal with mortar
5. Allow closure to set before grouting (minimum 1 hour)

## Wall Capping / Top Course

### Bond Beam (Always Required)
The top course of every block wall must be a **bond beam** — U-shaped (open-top) bond beam blocks with:
- Continuous #4 rebar minimum (typically 2-#4 bars in wider bond beam blocks)
- Fully grouted
- This creates a rigid cap that ties the wall together horizontally

### Cap Options

| Cap Type | Description | Pros | Cons |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Exposed bond beam** | Bond beam block with tooled mortar joints, no additional cap | Simple, economical | Water can pool; less durable without sealer |
| **Precast concrete cap** | Factory-made concrete cap piece set in mortar | Clean appearance, sheds water well | Higher cost; joints must be sealed |
| **Natural stone cap** | Flagstone, bluestone, or similar set in mortar | Premium appearance | Expensive; heavy; joints need sealing |
| **Stucco-over-block** | Stucco applied over the entire wall including top | Monolithic appearance | Requires maintenance; cracks at joints |
| **Metal cap flashing** | Sheet metal Z-flashing over top course | Best water protection | Industrial appearance; not common residential |

### Water Management
- Cap must **shed water** away from the wall to prevent moisture intrusion
- **Drip edge or drip groove** recommended at cap edges (prevents water from running down the wall face)
- Stucco or plaster coatings should extend over the top with a positive slope
- Do NOT leave open cells at the top — they collect water and accelerate deterioration

## Construction Sequence

The following sequence represents best practice for standard freestanding CMU block wall construction:

| Step | Action | Key Details |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Excavate footing trench** | Required width and depth; on undisturbed native soil; level bottom; remove loose material |
| 2 | **Place footing rebar** | Set on chairs/supports for proper cover (3" from bottom); lap splices at 48 db |
| 3 | **Set vertical dowels** | Position at correct spacing aligned with future CMU cells; use template; hold plumb |
| 4 | **Pour footing concrete** | f'c >= 2,500 psi; vibrate for consolidation; level and smooth top surface |
| 5 | **Cure footing** | Minimum 24 hours before starting masonry; 3 days preferred; keep moist |
| 6 | **Lay first course (dry-set)** | Dry-fit blocks without mortar to verify spacing, dowel alignment, and cell locations |
| 7 | **Lay first course** | Full bed of mortar; press blocks into mortar firmly; level and plumb every block |
| 8 | **Build wall in courses** | Lay courses in running bond; check level and plumb every 2-3 courses; maintain 3/8" joints |
| 9 | **Install bond beam blocks** | At required intervals (top course always; every 24" for walls >4 ft) |
| 10 | **Place/verify vertical rebar** | Ensure rebar is centered in cells, properly lapped, and held with positioning devices |
| 11 | **Place horizontal rebar** | In bond beam channels; continuous with lap splices; tie to vertical bars at intersections |
| 12 | **Install cleanouts** (if required) | For walls >5 ft; inspect cells; remove debris; seal before grouting |
| 13 | **Grout reinforced cells** | Pour in lifts per code; consolidate with vibrator; reconsolidate after settling |
| 14 | **Install control joints** | At required locations; install sealant backing and joint filler |
| 15 | **Cap the wall** | Bond beam at top fully grouted; install cap/coping; ensure water shedding |
| 16 | **Apply finish** (if required) | Stucco, paint, sealer, or leave exposed; seal control joints |

## Inspections

Most jurisdictions require the following inspections for permitted masonry block walls:

### Standard Inspection Stages

| Inspection | When | What the Inspector Checks |
|---|---|---|
| **Footing** | After excavation and rebar placement, BEFORE concrete pour | Trench dimensions, soil conditions, rebar size/spacing/cover, dowel placement and spacing, form integrity |
| **Reinforcing steel** | After wall is fully laid and rebar is in place, BEFORE grouting | Rebar size, spacing, lap splices, positioning in cells, bond beam rebar, cleanout openings (if required), cell cleanliness |
| **Final** | After grouting, capping, and completion | Wall height and alignment, cap/finish, control joints, overall compliance with approved plans, plumb and level |

### Additional Inspections (May Be Required)

| Inspection | When Required |
|---|---|
| **Grout placement** | For walls over 8 ft or where special inspection is required |
| **Masonry prism testing** | For engineered walls with specified f'm; mortar and grout sampling |
| **Soils/bearing** | When soil conditions are questionable or soils report was required |

### Common Inspection Failures

| Failure | Description | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| **Wrong mortar type** | Type N or O used instead of Type M or S | Remove and rebuild with correct mortar |
| **Rebar missing or wrong size** | #3 used instead of #4; rebar omitted from cells | Add correct rebar (if caught before grouting) |
| **Insufficient lap splice** | Less than 24" overlap for #4 bars | Extend splice or add additional bars |
| **Footing too shallow** | Less than 12" below grade | Excavate deeper and extend footing |
| **Footing too narrow** | Less than required width for wall height | May require redesign or engineering |
| **Cells not grouted** | Reinforced cells left ungrouted | Grout before continuing construction |
| **Poor consolidation** | Voids visible in grouted cells | Reconsolidate or break out and re-grout |
| **No control joints** | Wall exceeds max length without control joints | Cut control joints (less ideal) or note for future |
| **Wrong contractor license** | C-13 (Fencing) instead of C-29 (Masonry) | Contractor must obtain correct license |
