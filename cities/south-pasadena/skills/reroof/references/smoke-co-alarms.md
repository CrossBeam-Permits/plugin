# Smoke and Carbon Monoxide Alarm Requirements

Source: 2025 California Residential Code (Title 24, Part 2.5), Sections R310 (Smoke Alarms) and R311 (Carbon Monoxide Alarms); California Building Code (Part 2), Sections 907 and 915.

---

## Why This Applies to Reroof

Reroof is a permit-triggering alteration. Under CRC R310.2.2, when alterations requiring a permit occur, the dwelling must be equipped with smoke alarms per current code. Similarly, carbon monoxide alarms must comply per R311.2.2.

**Critical exception for reroof:** Both smoke alarm (R310.4 Exception 3, R310.6 Exception 4) and CO alarm (R311.2.2 Exception 1, R311.5 Exception 3, R311.6 Exception 4.3) codes contain exceptions for work limited to exterior surfaces such as roofing replacement. This means:

- **Interconnection** of existing alarms is NOT required for reroof-only projects
- **Hard-wired power source** is NOT required for reroof-only projects (battery-only alarms acceptable)
- **Presence and function** of alarms IS still verified at final inspection

In practice, most jurisdictions verify that working smoke and CO alarms are present in the required locations during the final reroof inspection, but do not require upgrading to hard-wired interconnected units for exterior-only work.

## Smoke Alarms (CRC R310)

### Required Locations (CRC R310.3)

Smoke alarms must be installed in:

| Location | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Each sleeping room (bedroom) | 1 alarm per room |
| Outside each sleeping area | In hallway, within 10 ft of bedroom door |
| Each story of the dwelling | Including basements and habitable attics |
| Room open to hallway serving bedrooms | Where ceiling height exceeds hallway by 24" or more |

### Listing and Standards

- Listed per **UL 217** (smoke alarms)
- Combination smoke/CO alarms: listed per **UL 217 and UL 2034**
- California State Fire Marshal listed and approved

### Technology

- Photoelectric, ionization, or combination
- **Photoelectric recommended** by California code

### Placement Rules (CRC R310.3.3 / NFPA 72)

| Rule | Distance/Restriction |
|---|---|
| From cooking appliances | Min 20 ft (ionization); min 10 ft with hush button; min 6 ft (photoelectric) |
| From bathroom with tub/shower | Min 3 ft horizontal from door |
| From HVAC supply registers | Min 36" horizontal |
| From ceiling fan blades | Min 36" horizontal from blade tip |
| Wall-mounted placement | Max 12" below ceiling |
| Peaked/sloped ceilings | Within 36" horizontal of high side, min 4" from peak |
| Coffered ceilings | On highest portion, max 12" down from highest point |

### Restricted Locations

- NOT in garages, unfinished attics, or spaces where temp < 40F or > 100F
- NOT within 3 ft of steam source (bathroom door)
- NOT in direct airflow from HVAC registers

### Detector Age

- Detectors **older than 10 years** from manufacture date must be replaced
- Must be stamped with manufacturer date and installation date

## Carbon Monoxide Alarms (CRC R311)

### When Required (CRC R311.2.1)

CO alarms are required in any dwelling that has:
1. A **fuel-fired appliance or fireplace** (gas furnace, gas water heater, gas stove, fireplace), OR
2. An **attached garage** with an opening communicating with the dwelling

### Required Locations (CRC R311.3)

| Location | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Outside each sleeping area | In immediate vicinity of bedrooms |
| Every occupiable level | Including basements |
| Bedrooms with fuel-burning appliance | Within the bedroom |

### Listing and Standards

- Listed per **UL 2034** (CO alarms)
- Combination CO/smoke: listed per **UL 217 and UL 2034**
- California State Fire Marshal listed and approved

---

## Interconnection Requirements

### Smoke Alarms (CRC R310.4)

Where more than one smoke alarm is required, all alarms must be interconnected so activation of one triggers all. Wireless interconnection is permitted.

**Exceptions for reroof:**
- Not required when work is limited to exterior surfaces (roofing, siding, windows/doors, porch/deck)
- Not required when no interior wall/ceiling finishes are removed

### CO Alarms (CRC R311.5)

Same interconnection requirement and same exceptions for exterior-only work.

---

## Power Source

### Smoke Alarms (CRC R310.6)

Primary power from building wiring + battery backup is the standard.

**Exceptions for reroof:**
- Battery-only alarms are acceptable when work is limited to exterior surfaces
- Battery-powered detectors must contain a **non-user-serviceable 10-year sealed battery**

### CO Alarms (CRC R311.6)

Same power source requirements and same exceptions for exterior-only work.

## Inspection Checklist for Reroof Final

At final reroof inspection, verify:

| Check | Standard |
|---|---|
| Alarm in each bedroom | Present and functional |
| Alarm outside each sleeping area | Present within 10 ft of bedroom door |
| Alarm on each story | Including basement |
| CO alarm present (if fuel-fired appliance or attached garage) | Outside sleeping areas + each level |
| Detector age | < 10 years from manufacture date |
| Test button functional | Press test — alarm sounds |

---

## Common Review Findings

1. **Missing alarms** — FAIL. Working alarms must be present in all required locations at final inspection.
2. **Alarms older than 10 years** — FAIL. Must be replaced.
3. **No CO alarm but dwelling has gas appliances** — FAIL. CO alarm required.
4. **Applicant claims hardwired required** — For reroof-only projects, battery-powered 10-year sealed alarms satisfy the requirement (exterior work exception).
5. **This is a physical inspection item** — The plan reviewer's role is to note that alarms will be verified at inspection; actual compliance is confirmed in the field.
