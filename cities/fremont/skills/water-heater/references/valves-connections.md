# Valves, Connections & Water Supply

Source: 2025 California Plumbing Code (Title 24, Part 5), Chapters 6.

---

## 1. Shutoff Valves

### Cold Water Supply Valve (CPC 606.2)

A **fullway valve** shall be installed on the cold water supply pipe to each water heater at or near the water heater. The valve shall be accessible.

### Appliance Control Valves (CPC 606.5)

A control valve shall be installed **immediately ahead of** each water-supplied appliance and immediately ahead of each slip joint or appliance supply.

### Accessibility (CPC 606.6)

Required shutoff or control valves shall be **accessible**.

---

## 2. Unions (CPC 609.5)

Unions shall be installed in the water supply piping **not more than 12 inches** from regulating equipment, water heating, conditioning tanks, and similar equipment that requires service by removal or replacement.

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| **Max distance from equipment** | 12 inches |
| **Purpose** | Facilitate ready removal for servicing |
| **Applies to** | Water heaters, softeners, filters, regulators, and similar equipment |

---

## 3. Vacuum Relief Valve (CPC 608.7)

Where a hot-water storage tank or indirect water heater is located at an elevation **above the fixture outlets** in the hot-water system (e.g., attic installation), a vacuum relief valve complying with ANSI Z21.22/CSA 4.4 shall be installed on the storage tank or heater.

**When to flag**: Any attic or elevated installation where the water heater tank is higher than the faucets it serves.

---

## 4. Water Hammer Arrestors (CPC 609.11)

Building water supply systems where **quick-acting valves** are installed shall be provided with water hammer arrestor(s) to absorb high pressures.

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| **Type** | Approved mechanical devices per **ASSE 1010** or **PDI-WH 201** |
| **Location** | As close as possible to quick-acting valves |
| **Air chambers** | Not acceptable (mechanical arrestors required) |
| **Common triggers** | Dishwashers, washing machines, solenoid valves near the water heater |

---

## 5. Hot Water Pipe Insulation (CPC 609.12)

### Requirement (CPC 609.12.1)

Domestic hot water piping **shall be insulated**.

### Thickness by Diameter (CPC 609.12.2)

| Nominal Pipe Diameter | Minimum Insulation Thickness |
|-----------------------|-----------------------------|
| Up to 1 inch | 1 inch |
| Over 1 inch to under 1.5 inches | 1.5 inches |
| 1.5 inches to under 2 inches | 1.5 inches |
| 2 inches and larger | 2 inches |

**Rule of thumb**: Insulation thickness shall be greater than or equal to the pipe diameter for pipes up to 2 inches. For pipes 2 inches and larger, insulation shall be 2 inches thick.

### Exceptions

1. Piping that penetrates framing members: insulation not required for the distance of the framing penetration
2. Hot water piping between fixture control valve/supply stop and the fixture: not required to be insulated

### Energy Code Reference (CPC 609.12.3)

See California Energy Code Sections 150.0(j)2 and 120.3(c) for additional requirements. Energy Code requires:
- Piping penetrating metal framing shall use grommets/plugs/wrapping
- Insulation shall abut securely against all framing members

### Minimum Pipe Insulation by R-Value (California Energy Code)

| Nominal Pipe Diameter (in) | <1 | 1 to <1.5 | 1.5 to <4 | 4 to <8 | 8+ |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Insulation thickness (in)** | 1 | 1.5 | 1.5 | 1.5 | 2 |
| **Min R-Value** | R-7 | R-12.5 | R-11 | R-9 | R-8 |

---

## 6. Hot Water Piping Length (CPC 609.11)

Hot water piping shall have a maximum developed length of **100 feet** from source of hot water to fixtures.

---

## 7. Pipe Sizing at Manifold (CPC 610.8)

Where a water heater is fed from the end of a cold-water manifold, the manifold shall be **one size larger** than the water heater supply line.

---

## 8. Water Pressure Requirements (CPC 608.1)

| Requirement | Specification |
|-------------|---------------|
| **Minimum residual pressure** | 15 PSI after friction and pressure losses |
| **Remedy for low pressure** | Booster pump or other approved means |
| **Higher-demand fixtures** | If fixtures require >15 PSI, that higher minimum applies |

---

## 9. Common Violations to Flag

| Violation | Why It Fails |
|-----------|-------------|
| **No shutoff valve on cold supply** | CPC 606.2 requires fullway valve at water heater |
| **Shutoff valve not accessible** | CPC 606.6 requires accessibility |
| **No unions within 12 inches** | CPC 609.5 requires unions for servicing |
| **Air chambers instead of mechanical arrestors** | CPC 609.11 requires ASSE 1010 devices |
| **Uninsulated hot water piping** | CPC 609.12.1 requires insulation on all hot water piping |
| **No vacuum relief in elevated installation** | CPC 608.7 requires vacuum relief when WH is above fixtures |
| **Manifold not upsized** | CPC 610.8 requires manifold one size larger than supply |

---

## 10. Bonding Jumper

When dielectric fittings are installed (as required at copper-to-galvanized transitions), they can interrupt the metal piping grounding path. A **bonding jumper** across the water heater is commonly required to maintain electrical continuity of the metallic piping system per CEC grounding requirements.

The bonding jumper is typically shown on water heater installation details connecting the hot and cold water piping on either side of the water heater to maintain the grounding path.

---

## Review Checklist -- Valves & Connections

- [ ] Fullway shutoff valve on cold water supply at water heater (accessible)
- [ ] Control valves at appliance supply
- [ ] Unions within 12 inches of water heater for removal
- [ ] Water hammer arrestors (ASSE 1010) near quick-close valves if applicable
- [ ] Hot water piping insulated (thickness per pipe diameter)
- [ ] Hot water piping max 100 ft developed length from source to fixtures
- [ ] Vacuum relief valve if water heater is above fixture outlets
- [ ] Manifold one size larger than WH supply (if end-fed manifold)
- [ ] Water pressure min 15 PSI after losses (booster pump if needed)
- [ ] Bonding jumper across water heater if dielectric fittings interrupt grounding path
