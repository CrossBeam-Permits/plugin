# Scope Verification -- Non-Structural Demolition Only

Source: 2025 California Building Code (Title 24, Part 2); 2025 California Existing Building Code (Title 24, Part 10).

---

## What is LTIA?

**LTIA = Limited Tenant Improvement Area.** This designation is used for interior non-structural demolition of tenant spaces in malls, shopping centers, and high-rise buildings that are within a pre-designated LTIA zone.

### LTIA Demolition Scope Includes:
- Removal of non-load-bearing partition walls (drywall, studs)
- Removal of ceiling tiles and suspended ceiling grid
- Removal of floor finishes (carpet, sheet vinyl, VCT, tile)
- Removal of cabinets, millwork, built-ins, and fixtures
- Removal of plumbing fixtures (sinks, toilets, drinking fountains)
- Removal of light fixtures and electrical devices
- Removal of restroom toilet partitions and accessories
- Removal of doors and frames at demolished walls
- Capping of all electrical, mechanical, plumbing, and gas utilities

### LTIA Demolition Scope Does NOT Include:
- Removal of load-bearing walls or structural elements
- Removal of structural columns
- Modifications to the building envelope (exterior walls, roof, windows)
- Removal of fire-rated assemblies (demising walls, fire barriers, rated corridors)
- Demolition of shared building systems (plumbing stacks, main ductwork, risers)
- Any new construction (that requires a separate TI permit)

## Structural Protection Requirements

### Conformance (CEBC 701.2)
An existing building or portion thereof shall not be altered such that the building becomes less safe than its existing condition.

### Structural Columns (Industry Practice)
Existing structural columns located within embedded walls must be protected and maintained during demolition. The contractor must:
1. Identify all structural columns before demolition begins.
2. Protect columns from damage during partition wall removal.
3. Do NOT remove structural elements -- demo only partition walls, not structural elements.

### Gravity Load-Carrying Elements (CEBC 805.2)
Any existing gravity load-carrying structural element whose gravity load-carrying capacity is decreased as part of the alteration shall be shown to have the capacity to resist the applicable design loads required by the CBC for new structures.

> **FOR LTIA**: Since LTIA demolition is non-structural by definition, this section applies only as a verification check -- confirm that no structural elements are being removed or weakened.

### Lateral Load-Carrying Elements (CEBC 805.3)
Where the alteration decreases the capacity of any existing lateral load-carrying structural element, the lateral force-resisting system of the altered building shall meet current code requirements.

> **FOR LTIA**: If any wall being demolished contributes to the lateral force-resisting system (shear wall), it CANNOT be removed under an LTIA demolition permit. A structural engineer's review would be required and the work would need a different permit type.

## Scope Verification Checklist

When reviewing LTIA demolition plans, verify the following:

| Check | What to Verify |
|-------|---------------|
| **Non-structural scope** | Plans clearly indicate ONLY non-load-bearing partitions are being removed |
| **Structural columns identified** | All columns within or adjacent to demo area are identified and marked for protection |
| **Demising walls remain** | Walls between adjacent tenant spaces are shown to remain in place |
| **Fire-rated walls remain** | All fire separation walls, fire barriers, and rated corridors remain |
| **No exterior work** | No modifications to building envelope, exterior walls, or windows |
| **No roof work** | Unless explicitly included in scope with separate roof plan |
| **Separate TI permit noted** | Plans indicate that tenant improvements will be under a separate permit |
| **Shoring note** | Plans include note: "Contractor is responsible for all temporary shoring prior to the start of demolition" |

## Review and Intent of Demolition

Due to the possibility of discovering unforeseen conditions during demolition, the contractor must study the full intent of the required demolition by incorporating a complete analysis of the proposed new construction (the future TI).

Physical structures and features of the building and tenant improvements that are NOT illustrated by a "dashed" line, or otherwise specifically designated for removal, shall remain "as is."
