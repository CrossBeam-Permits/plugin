# Seismic Strapping Requirements for Water Heaters

## 1. California Mandate (Health & Safety Code 19211)

California law requires **all water heaters** to be braced, anchored, or strapped
to resist falling or horizontal displacement due to earthquake motion.

- **H&S Code 19211(a)**: Applies to new installations, replacements, AND existing
  residential water heaters already in place.
- There is no grandfathering -- existing water heaters that lack strapping must be
  brought into compliance upon discovery (e.g., during a home sale inspection or
  any permit review that touches the water heater).
- At minimum, strapping must comply with the California Plumbing Code (CPC) or
  local amendments adopted under H&S Code 17958.5.

## 2. Two-Strap Placement Rule (CPC 507.2)

CPC 507.2 requires strapping at **two points** on the tank:

| Strap     | Location                              | Notes                                    |
|-----------|---------------------------------------|------------------------------------------|
| Upper     | Within the **upper 1/3** of the tank  | Measured from the top of the unit        |
| Lower     | Within the **lower 1/3** of the tank  | Must be at least **4 inches** (102 mm) above the control valve/thermostat |

The 4-inch clearance above controls on the lower strap prevents damage to the
gas valve or thermostat during seismic movement.

## 3. Strap Material Specifications

Acceptable strapping materials:

- **Plumber's tape (perforated metal strap)**: Minimum **3/4-inch wide x 24-gauge**
  (0.024 in) perforated galvanized steel. Strap shall encircle the tank from
  front and back.
- **Listed strapping kits**: Pre-engineered kits (e.g., Simpson Strong-Tie WHE,
  Holdrite QS-50) that include straps, bolts, and spacer blocks. These are
  preferred by many jurisdictions because they are tested and listed.
- **Conduit or metal banding**: Some jurisdictions accept heavy-gauge conduit
  straps or banding when properly secured, but listed kits are always accepted.

**Not acceptable**: Plastic zip ties, nylon straps, wire, rope, bungee cords,
or duct tape. These will fail during seismic loading.

## 4. Attachment to Structure

Straps must be anchored to the **building structure**, not just surface finishes:

- **Wood-framed walls**: Secure to wall studs with minimum **1/4-inch x 3-inch
  lag screws** (or per kit instructions). Each strap end must engage a stud.
- **Through gypsum wallboard (flat wall)**: Use **1/4-inch x 5-inch lag screws**
  through gypsum and into studs. Use a **2x4 wood ledger** as a backing plate
  with metal washers. Centerline of lag screw aligned with stud center.
- **Corner condition**: Use 1/4-inch x 3-inch lag screws into studs from both
  directions. First stud should not be behind the water heater.
- **Masonry/concrete walls**: Use approved concrete anchors or expansion bolts
  rated for seismic loads.
- **Drywall alone is never acceptable** -- fasteners will pull out during shaking.
- If the water heater is away from a wall, a framed support or listed floor-mount
  bracket must be used.
- **Spacer blocks** (typically included in listed kits or use gypsum wallboard
  shims and 2x4 ledgers) are required between the strap and the wall to keep
  the tank snug against the wall surface and prevent rocking.
- **Strap geometry**: The line connecting points of support must pass through
  the water heater tank (not outside it).

## 5. Flexible Water Connections

CPC 604.5 requires flexible water connectors to be installed in accessible
locations and to comply with ASME A112.18.6/CSA B125.6.

For seismic safety on water heaters:

- **Flexible supply lines** (braided stainless steel) are strongly recommended on
  both hot and cold connections to allow tank movement without rupturing rigid
  piping.
- **Gas connections**: Flexible gas connectors (CSST or listed flex connector) are
  required for gas water heaters to accommodate seismic displacement.
- Rigid copper or galvanized connections directly to the tank are a common
  violation -- they transfer seismic force to the piping system.

## 6. Tankless / Wall-Mounted Units

Tankless water heaters do not require the two-strap method. Instead:

- The unit must be mounted to wall studs (or blocking) using the manufacturer's
  specified mounting bracket and hardware.
- Mounting to drywall alone with toggle bolts is **not acceptable**.
- The manufacturer's bracket, when properly installed to studs, satisfies the
  seismic anchoring requirement of CPC 507.2 and H&S Code 19211.
- Verify that lag screws or structural screws are used per the installation
  manual.

## 7. Exemptions

**There are no residential exemptions.** H&S Code 19211 applies to:

- Every residential water heater in California (new, replacement, or existing)
- All fuel types: gas, electric, heat pump, solar storage tanks
- All sizes: from small point-of-use units to large storage tanks
- All locations: garage, closet, attic, exterior alcove, basement

## 8. Common Violations to Flag

When reviewing plans or inspecting, watch for these frequent deficiencies:

| Violation | Why It Fails |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Single strap only** | CPC 507.2 requires two straps (upper 1/3 and lower 1/3) |
| **Both straps in the middle** | Straps must be in the upper and lower thirds, not clustered at center |
| **Lower strap too close to controls** | Must maintain 4-inch minimum clearance above gas valve/thermostat |
| **Straps not attached to studs** | Drywall-only attachment will fail; must hit framing members |
| **Plastic or nylon straps** | Only metal strapping (24-ga minimum) or listed kits are acceptable |
| **No spacer blocks** | Tank rocks against wall without spacers; straps lose effectiveness |
| **Rigid piping with no flex connectors** | Seismic movement will fracture rigid connections |
| **Tankless unit on drywall only** | Bracket must be secured to studs or blocking behind drywall |
| **Missing strapping on replacement** | Replacement triggers H&S 19211 compliance; cannot reuse old non-compliant setup |

## Quick Review Checklist

- [ ] Two straps present (upper 1/3 and lower 1/3 of tank)
- [ ] Lower strap at least 4 inches above controls
- [ ] Straps are 3/4 in x 24-gauge min perforated steel or listed kit
- [ ] Straps encircle the tank from front and back
- [ ] Each strap end is secured to a wall stud (1/4" lag screws) or masonry anchor
- [ ] Flat wall: 2x4 ledger + 5 in lag screws through gypsum into studs
- [ ] Corner: lag screws into studs from both directions
- [ ] Spacer blocks or ledger used between tank and wall
- [ ] Line of support passes through the tank
- [ ] Max 6 in gap between tank and wall strapping point
- [ ] Flexible connectors on water supply lines
- [ ] Flexible gas connector (for gas units)
- [ ] Tankless units: bracket secured to studs with proper fasteners
