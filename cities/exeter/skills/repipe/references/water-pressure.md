# CPC Section 608 — Water Pressure Requirements

Source: 2025 California Plumbing Code (Title 24, Part 5), Chapter 6.

## Minimum Pressure (608.1)

**Minimum residual pressure at fixtures: 15 psi** (after accounting for friction loss and elevation).

If the available street pressure minus friction and elevation losses results in less than 15 psi at the highest or most remote fixture, a **booster pump** is required.

### Pressure Loss Factors
- Pipe friction: depends on material, size, and flow rate
- Elevation: 0.433 psi per foot of elevation gain
- Meter loss: typically 5-10 psi (varies by meter size)
- PRV loss: typically 5-10 psi

---

## Maximum Pressure / Excessive Pressure (608.2)

**Maximum static pressure: 80 psi**

Where water supply pressure exceeds 80 psi, a **pressure-reducing valve (PRV)** is required:

- PRV must conform to **ASSE 1003** or **AWWA C530**
- PRV must be **preceded by a strainer** (adequate size, accessible for cleaning)
- An **expansion tank** (or approved device conforming to ASSE 1024) must be installed downstream to prevent pressure from exceeding 80 psi due to thermal expansion

### Additional PRV Requirements

| Requirement | Detail | Code Section |
|-------------|--------|-------------|
| **Strainer** | Required ahead of the regulator EXCEPT when regulator serves piping greater than 1-1/2 in. | CPC 608.2 |
| **Regulator & strainer accessibility** | Shall be accessible without removing piping | CPC 608.2 |
| **Pipe sizing with PRV** | Pipe sizing shall be based on **80% of the regulated pressure** | CPC 608.2 |

### Why This Matters for Repipe

When replacing old pipes (especially galvanized), the old pipes may have been restricting flow enough to mask high street pressure. New, smooth-bore pipe (copper or PEX) has much less friction loss, so the actual pressure at fixtures may increase significantly after repipe.

**Best practice**: Always check street pressure when reviewing a repipe permit. If street pressure is >80 psi, confirm PRV is included in scope.

---

## Thermal Expansion (608.3)

Where a **check valve, backflow preventer, or PRV** is installed on the supply line (creating a closed system), thermal expansion of heated water can cause pressure spikes.

**Required**: An expansion tank or approved thermal expansion device conforming to ASSE 1024.

> This is common in repipe projects: if the water utility has installed a backflow preventer at the meter, the system is "closed" and an expansion tank is required on the hot water side.

---

## Pressure Testing

Completed repipe installations must pass a pressure test:
- **Test pressure**: Not less than the working pressure and not less than 50 psi
- **Duration**: Held for not less than 15 minutes
- **Method**: Air test or hydrostatic test, per the Authority Having Jurisdiction (AHJ)
