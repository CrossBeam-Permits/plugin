## Fastener Requirements by Material (Table R703.3(1) Summary)

All fasteners must be corrosion-resistant: aluminum, galvanized, stainless steel, or rust-preventative coated (CRC R703.3.3). Nails and staples per ASTM F1667.

### Wood Siding (CRC R703.5)

| Type | Fastener | Spacing |
|---|---|---|
| Bevel, shiplap, drop, rustic (up to 6" width) | 6d box or siding nail (2" x 0.099") | 1 nail per bearing, face nailed |
| Bevel, shiplap, drop, rustic (8" and wider) | 6d box or siding nail (2" x 0.099") | 2 nails per bearing, face nailed |
| Wood structural panel | 2" x 0.099" siding nail | 6" at panel edges, 12" at intermediate supports |
| Direct to studs | 8d box or siding nail (2-1/2" x 0.113") | Same as stud spacing |

- Vertical wood siding: nail to horizontal nailing strips or blocking at max 24" OC (R703.5.1)
- Panel siding vertical joints: over framing members, shiplapped or covered with batten (R703.5.2)
- Horizontal lap siding: min 1" lap (or 1/2" if rabbeted), ends caulked, battened, or sealed over flashing (R703.5.3)
- Minimum fastener penetration: 1-1/2" into studs, studs plus sheathing, or blocking (R703.3.4)

### Fiber Cement Siding (CRC R703.10)

| Type | Fastener | Spacing |
|---|---|---|
| Panel siding | 6d common nail (2" x 0.113") | 6" at panel edges, 12" at intermediate supports |
| Lap siding (face nailed) | 6d common nail (2" x 0.113") | 1 nail per stud per course |
| Lap siding (concealed nail) | 0.120" dia. (11-gage) roofing nail | 1 nail per stud through top edge |

- Panel joints: over framing, protected with caulking, battens, flashing, or shiplap (R703.10.1)
- Lap siding: min 1-1/4" lap, end joints protected with caulking, H-section, or flashing (R703.10.2)

### Vinyl Siding (CRC R703.11)

| Fastener | Spacing |
|---|---|
| 0.120" nail (shank) with 0.313" head, or 16-gage staple with 3/8"-1/2" crown | 16" OC or per manufacturer |

- Must be certified/labeled per ASTM D3679 (R703.11)
- Not allowed to attach direct to studs without sheathing backing
- Min 6" clearance from ground, 1/2" from adjacent surfaces (R703.3.1)
- Fastener penetration: min 1-1/4" into framing, or per manufacturer (R703.11.2)
- Allow for thermal expansion -- do not drive nails tight

### Hardboard Siding (CRC R703.5)

| Type | Fastener | Spacing |
|---|---|---|
| Panel | 0.120" nail with 0.225" head | 6" at panel edges, 12" at intermediate supports |
| Lap | 0.099" nail with 0.240" head | Same as stud spacing, 2 per bearing |

### Aluminum Siding

| Fastener | Notes |
|---|---|
| Siding nail 1-1/2" x 0.120" (over wood sheathing) | **Aluminum nails only** -- no dissimilar metals |
| Siding nail 2" x 0.120" (over fiberboard or gypsum sheathing) | Into studs required |

- Not allowed direct to studs without sheathing
- 0.019" thickness limited to max 10" panel width, 8" flat area (unbacked)

### Steel Siding

| Fastener | Spacing |
|---|---|
| Siding nail (1-3/4" x 0.113") or 1-3/4" staple (over wood sheathing) | Same as stud spacing |

### Wood Shakes/Shingles on Walls (CRC R703.6)

| Course Type | Fastener | Spacing |
|---|---|---|
| Single course | 2 stainless steel or hot-dip galvanized box nails per shake/shingle | Concealed -- 1" above butt line of succeeding course, 3/4" from edge |
| Double course | 2 face nails per shake/shingle | 2" above butt line, 3/4" from each edge |

- Fasteners within 15 miles of saltwater: stainless steel Type 316 required (R703.6.3)
- Expansion gap: 1/8"-1/4" between shingles; 3/8"-1/2" between shakes
- Furring strips: 1x3 or 1x4, fastened with 7d or 8d box nails (R703.6.1)
- Bottom courses doubled (R703.6.4)

Maximum weather exposure (Table R703.6.1):

| Length | Single Course | Double Course |
|---|---|---|
| 16" shingles | 7" | 12" |
| 18" shingles | 8" | 14" |
| 24" shingles | 10-1/2" | 16" |

| 24" shakes | 10-1/2" | 18" |

## Siding Over Foam Plastic Sheathing (CRC R703.15-R703.17)

When siding is installed over foam plastic insulating sheathing (continuous insulation), additional requirements apply:

- Fasteners must penetrate through foam into framing (R703.3.4 Item 6)
- Fastener length must accommodate foam thickness plus required penetration
- For cladding up to 3 psf (most siding materials): see Table R703.3.3 for optional attachment where stud penetration is not required (wood structural panel sheathing only)
- Maximum foam sheathing thickness varies by fastener type and weight of cladding -- see CBC Tables 1404.5.3.1 and 1404.5.3.2

## Material Compatibility -- Dissimilar Metals

Galvanic corrosion occurs when dissimilar metals are in direct contact in the presence of moisture. Key rules:

| Siding/Flashing Material | Compatible Fasteners | Incompatible Fasteners |
|---|---|---|
| Aluminum siding | Aluminum nails ONLY | Steel, copper, stainless |
| Copper flashing | Copper, brass, bronze, stainless nails | Aluminum, galvanized steel |
| Galvanized steel siding | Galvanized or stainless nails | Copper, aluminum |
| Fiber cement | Galvanized, stainless, or DAC-coated | Copper (staining risk) |
| Vinyl siding | Galvanized, stainless, aluminum | No metal contact issues (vinyl is inert) |

**Rule of thumb:** Use fasteners of the same metal family as the siding/flashing material, or use stainless steel (universally compatible).

## Wind Resistance (CRC R703.1.2, R703.3.2)

Siding and backing materials must resist wind loads per Tables R301.2.1(1) and R301.2.1(2). For most of California (110-115 mph ultimate wind speed, Exposure B), standard attachment per Table R703.3(1) is sufficient without design required (Table R703.3.2 shows "NL" -- Not Limited).

Where design wind pressure exceeds 30 psf or Table R703.3.2 limits are exceeded, engineered attachment design is required using an effective wind area of 10 sq ft.
