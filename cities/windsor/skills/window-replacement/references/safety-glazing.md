# Safety Glazing Requirements

> Source: 2025 California Residential Code (Title 24, Part 2.5), 2025 California Building Code (Title 24, Part 2)
> Sections: CRC R324.1-R324.6; CBC 2406

---

## Overview

Safety glazing (tempered, laminated, or other approved safety glass) is required in specific "hazardous locations" where the risk of human impact with glass is elevated. This applies to ALL window and door replacements -- not just new construction. When replacing a window or door in a hazardous location, the new glazing MUST be safety glazing even if the original was not.

---

## Hazardous Locations Requiring Safety Glazing

### 1. Glazing in Doors (R324.4.1)

ALL glazing in fixed and operable panels of swinging, sliding, and bifold doors is a hazardous location.

**Exceptions:**
- Glazed openings too small for a 3" diameter sphere to pass through
- Decorative glazing

### 2. Glazing Adjacent to Doors (R324.4.2)

Glazing is hazardous when ALL of the following are true:
- Bottom exposed edge of glazing is **less than 60" above the floor**
- Glazing is **within 24" of either side of the door** in the plane of the door (closed position)
- OR glazing is on a wall **less than 180 degrees from the door plane** and within 24" of the hinge side of an in-swinging door

**Exceptions:**
- Decorative glazing
- Intervening wall or permanent barrier between door and glazing
- Access through door is to a closet/storage <=3 ft deep
- Glazing adjacent to the fixed panel of patio doors

### 3. Glazing in Windows -- Large Panes (R324.4.3)

Glazing is hazardous when ALL FOUR of the following conditions are met:
1. Exposed area of individual pane is **larger than 9 square feet**
2. Bottom edge of glazing is **less than 18" above the floor**
3. Top edge of glazing is **more than 36" above the floor**
4. Walking surface is **within 36"** (measured horizontally) of the glazing

**Exceptions:**
- Decorative glazing
- Horizontal rail installed 34-38" above walking surface, capable of withstanding 50 lb/linear ft horizontal load, with cross-sectional height >=1.5"
- Outboard panes in insulating glass units where bottom edge is >=25 ft above grade

### 4. Glazing in Guards and Railings (R324.4.4)

ALL glazing in guards and railings -- structural baluster panels and nonstructural infill panels -- is hazardous **regardless of area or height** above walking surface.

Structural glass baluster panels must be installed with an attached top rail or handrail supported by >=3 glass baluster panels.

**Exception:** Top rail not required where laminated glass with >=2 plies of equal thickness and same type is used.

### 5. Glazing and Wet Surfaces (R324.4.5)

Glazing is hazardous in walls, enclosures, or fences adjacent to:
- Hot tubs, spas, whirlpools
- Saunas, steam rooms
- Bathtubs, showers
- Indoor or outdoor swimming pools

...where the bottom exposed edge is **less than 60"** above any standing or walking surface.

**Exception:** Glazing more than 60" (measured horizontally) from the water's edge or shower/sauna edge.

### 6. Glazing Adjacent to Stairs and Ramps (R324.4.6)

Glazing is hazardous where the bottom exposed edge is **less than 36"** above the walking surface of:
- Flights of stairs
- Ramp runs
- Landings between flights of stairs
- Landings between ramp runs

**Exceptions:**
- Horizontal rail installed 34-38" above walking surface (same specs as window exception)
- Glazing >=36" measured horizontally from the walking surface

### 7. Glazing Adjacent to Bottom Stair Landing (R324.4.7)

Glazing is hazardous when:
- Less than 36" above the landing
- Within a 60" horizontal arc (less than 180 degrees) from the bottom tread nosing

**Exception:** Glazing protected by a guard per R321, with plane of glass >18" from the guard.

## Impact Test Standards

### CPSC 16 CFR 1201 (Primary Standard)

| Exposed Area (one side, one lite) | Storm/Combo Doors | Doors (interior) | Windows (R324.4.3) | Adjacent to Doors (R324.4.2) | Wet Areas (R324.4.5) | Sliding Patio Doors |

| <=9 SF | Category I | Category I | NR | Category I | Category II | Category II |
| >9 SF | Category II | Category II | Category II | Category II | Category II | Category II |

## Identification / Marking Requirements (R324.1)

Each pane of safety glazing in a hazardous location must have a **permanent** manufacturer's designation specifying:
- Who applied the designation
- Type of glass
- Safety glazing standard (CPSC 16 CFR 1201 or ANSI Z97.1)
- Must be **visible in the final installation**

Acceptable marking methods:
- Acid etched
- Sand blasted
- Ceramic fired
- Laser etched
- Embossed
- Non-removable type

**Exception 1:** Non-tempered glass may use certificate/affidavit if approved by building official.
**Exception 2:** Tempered spandrel glass may use a removable paper designation.

### Multi-pane Assemblies (R324.1.1)
Assemblies with individual panes <=1 SF: at least one pane must be fully identified. Other panes labeled "CPSC 16 CFR 1201" or "ANSI Z97.1" as appropriate.

---

## Louvered Windows / Jalousies (R324.2)

- Glass must be >=3/16" nominal thick
- Maximum 48" length
- Exposed edges must be smooth
- Wired glass with exposed wire on longitudinal edges is prohibited

---

## Skylights and Sloped Glazing (R324.6)

Glazing materials for skylights limited to:
1. Laminated glass (>=0.015" PVB interlayer for <=16 SF, >=0.030" for larger/higher)
2. Fully tempered glass
3. Heat-strengthened glass
4. Wired glass
5. Approved rigid plastics

Screens required below fully tempered or heat-strengthened glass unless:
- <=16 SF and <=12 ft above walking surface and <=3/16" thick (tempered)
- Sloped <=30 degrees from vertical and <=10 ft above walking surface

Unit skylights must be tested per AAMA/WDMA/CSA 101/I.S.2/A440.

Curbs required for skylights on roofs <3:12 slope: minimum 4" above roof plane (unless manufacturer specifies otherwise).

## Flashing and Weatherproofing (CRC R703.4, R609.1)

Window and door openings must be flashed in accordance with Section R703.4:

- **Pan flashing** at the sill to collect and drain water to the exterior
- **Side flashing** lapped over the pan flashing at the jambs
- **Head flashing** at the top, with the weather-resistive barrier (WRB) lapped over the head flashing
- Flashing must integrate with the WRB to create a continuous drainage plane
- **Sealant/caulk alone is NOT acceptable flashing**

Windows and doors must be installed per the **fenestration manufacturer's written installation instructions** (R609.1). Written instructions must be provided by the manufacturer for each window or door.

---

## Site-Built Windows (R324.5)

Site-built windows must comply with Section 2404 of the California Building Code, which governs wind load design for glass in buildings, including:
- Glass thickness based on wind load, area, and aspect ratio
- Specific requirements for annealed, heat-strengthened, tempered, and laminated glass
- Altitude correction factors for elevations above 5,000 ft
