# Retaining Wall Drainage and Waterproofing Requirements

Source: 2025 California Building Code (Title 24, Part 2), Section 1805 — Dampproofing and Waterproofing; Section 1807 — Foundation Walls, Retaining Walls; engineering best practices.

---

## Why Drainage Is Critical

Lack of drainage behind a retaining wall is the **single most common cause of retaining wall failure**. Without drainage:

| Effect | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| Hydrostatic pressure builds behind wall | Lateral load can **double or triple** — water weighs 62.4 pcf, adding to the soil's equivalent fluid pressure |
| Saturated soil is heavier | Unit weight increases from ~120 pcf to ~130-140 pcf, increasing lateral pressure |
| Freeze-thaw expansion | Ice lens formation in retained soil pushes against wall (less common in Southern CA but relevant in mountain areas) |
| Soil erosion at base | Water undermines footing, causing settlement and rotation |
| Expansive soil swelling | Clay soils expand when wet, dramatically increasing lateral pressure |

**Design assumption**: Retaining walls are designed assuming the drainage system is functioning and hydrostatic pressure does not develop. If drainage fails, the design loads are exceeded and the wall can fail.

## Drainage System Components

### 1. Drain Rock / Gravel Backfill

| Specification | Requirement |
|---------------|-------------|
| Width behind wall | 12 inches minimum (measured from back face of wall or waterproofing membrane) |
| Material | Clean, free-draining aggregate: 3/4" to 1-1/2" crushed rock or gravel |
| Height | From bottom of wall (at drain pipe) to within 12" of the top of backfill |
| Top cap | 12" layer of compacted native soil or clay cap at top to prevent surface water infiltration into drain rock |
| Function | Collects groundwater and routes it down to the drain pipe at the base |

**Do not use**: Rounded river rock (poor structural interlock), crusher fines (poor drainage), or soil-contaminated aggregate.

### 2. Perforated Drain Pipe

| Specification | Requirement |
|---------------|-------------|
| Diameter | 4 inches minimum (6" for walls >8 ft or high water table) |
| Material | Schedule 40 PVC perforated, or corrugated HDPE perforated |
| Placement | At or near the base of the wall, on top of 4" bed of drain rock |
| Perforations | Holes facing down (gravity drains water into pipe from below) |
| Slope | Minimum 1% (1/8" per foot) to outlet — 2% preferred |
| Filter sock | Wrap pipe in geotextile filter fabric sock to prevent sediment intrusion; or surround with drain rock and wrap drain rock zone in filter fabric |
| Outlet | Must daylight to grade at a lower elevation, connect to storm drain system, or discharge to a sump/catch basin |

**Do not**: Outlet drain pipe to sanitary sewer. Connect to city storm drain only with city approval.

### 3. Filter Fabric (Geotextile)

| Specification | Requirement |
|---------------|-------------|
| Type | Non-woven geotextile, minimum 4 oz/sy |
| Placement | Between drain rock and native soil (wraps around the drain rock zone) |
| Purpose | Prevents fine soil particles from migrating into drain rock and clogging the drainage system |
| Overlap | Minimum 12" overlap at seams; wrap around top of drain rock before placing soil cap |
| Anchoring | Extend filter fabric 12" beyond top of drain rock and fold over; stake or pin to prevent displacement during backfill |

### 4. Weep Holes

| Specification | Requirement |
|---------------|-------------|
| Diameter | 4 inches (PVC pipe through wall face) |
| Spacing | 6-8 ft on center horizontally |
| Location | At or near the base of the wall (lowest practical point) |
| Screen | Install filter fabric or screen on inlet (soil) side to prevent clogging |
| Outlet protection | Provide splash block or riprap at outlet to prevent erosion |
| Function | Supplemental drainage — provides visible evidence that drainage system is working |

**Weep holes alone are not sufficient**. They can clog and do not drain the full height of the backfill. Always provide perforated drain pipe + drain rock as the primary system.

### 5. Surface Drainage

| Specification | Requirement |
|---------------|-------------|
| Grade at top of wall | Minimum 2% slope (1/4" per foot) away from wall for at least 10 ft |
| Swale or berm | Install a swale or berm at the top of the slope to divert surface runoff away from the wall backfill zone |
| Downspout discharge | Do not discharge roof downspouts into the backfill zone behind the wall |
| Irrigation | Minimize irrigation behind retaining walls; drip irrigation preferred over spray |

## Complete Drainage System — Assembly

The complete drainage system behind a retaining wall, from the wall face outward:

```
Wall face
|
|-- Waterproofing membrane (if required)
|
|-- 12" drain rock zone (3/4"-1.5" clean aggregate)
|     |
|     |-- Perforated drain pipe at base (4" min, sloped to daylight)
|     |
|     |-- Filter fabric wrapping drain rock zone
|
|-- Native soil backfill (compacted in lifts, max 8" loose)
|
|-- 12" soil cap at top (over drain rock)
|
|-- Surface graded 2% min away from wall
```

### Drainage System Checklist

Use this checklist when reviewing retaining wall plans for drainage:

| # | Item | Verify |
|---|------|--------|
| 1 | Drain rock shown behind wall? | 12" min width, 3/4"-1.5" aggregate |
| 2 | Perforated pipe at base? | 4" min, sloped 1% min to outlet |
| 3 | Pipe outlet shown? | Must daylight to grade or connect to approved drainage |
| 4 | Filter fabric shown? | Between drain rock and native soil |
| 5 | Weep holes shown? (recommended) | 4" @ 6-8 ft OC, screened inlet |
| 6 | Surface drainage graded away? | 2% min for 10 ft from wall |
| 7 | Roof drainage directed away? | Downspouts not into backfill zone |
| 8 | Soil cap shown at top of drain rock? | 12" native soil or clay cap |

## Waterproofing and Dampproofing (CBC 1805)

### When Required

| Condition | Treatment Required | Code Section |
|-----------|-------------------|--------------|
| Retaining wall enclosing habitable or usable space below grade | Waterproofing | CBC 1805.3 |
| Retaining wall below grade not enclosing habitable space | Dampproofing (minimum) | CBC 1805.2 |
| Freestanding retaining wall (no enclosed space) | Not code-required, but recommended | Engineering practice |
| Walls in areas with high water table | Waterproofing + positive drainage | CBC 1805.3 |

**Note**: Most freestanding retaining walls (not enclosing habitable space) do not technically require waterproofing under CBC 1805. However, applying a waterproofing or dampproofing membrane to the soil side extends the wall's service life and is considered best practice.

### Dampproofing (CBC 1805.2)

Dampproofing resists moisture migration but does not resist hydrostatic pressure.

| Method | Specification |
|--------|---------------|
| Bituminous coating | Applied to soil side of wall below grade; minimum 1/16" dry film thickness |
| Cement-based coating | Portland cement parging, minimum 3/8" thick, troweled smooth |
| Surface preparation | Concrete surfaces must be clean, dry, and free of form release agents |
| Coverage | From finished grade to top of footing on soil side |

### Waterproofing (CBC 1805.3)

Waterproofing resists hydrostatic pressure. Required when the wall encloses habitable or usable space below grade (e.g., basement retaining wall, below-grade storage).

| Method | Specification |
|--------|---------------|
| Membrane waterproofing | Sheet membrane (60 mil min) or liquid-applied membrane; lapped/sealed at joints |
| Bentonite waterproofing | Sodium bentonite panels or spray-applied bentonite |
| Drainage board | Dimpled HDPE sheet (Delta-MS, Platon, etc.) installed over waterproofing membrane; provides drainage path and protection |
| Protection board | 1/8" hardboard or XPS insulation installed over membrane to protect from backfill damage |
| Coverage | From finished grade to top of footing; must be continuous |
| Termination | Mechanically fastened at top with termination bar; sealed |

### Waterproofing vs Dampproofing Decision

| Question | If Yes | If No |
|----------|--------|-------|
| Does the wall enclose habitable or usable space below grade? | Waterproofing required (CBC 1805.3) | Dampproofing minimum (CBC 1805.2) |
| Is there a high water table? | Waterproofing required | Standard dampproofing OK |
| Is the wall a freestanding retaining wall? | Dampproofing recommended (not code-required) | N/A |
| Will the wall be visible / aesthetic finish required? | Consider drainage board to prevent staining | Standard approach |

## Subdrain and Foundation Drain (CBC 1805.4)

### When Required

Foundation drains (subdrains) are required for:
- Buildings with basements or floors below grade
- Crawlspaces below grade
- Any condition where groundwater may reach the foundation

### Specification

| Component | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Drain pipe | 4" minimum perforated pipe |
| Location | Around perimeter of footing, at or below the bottom of the floor slab or crawlspace |
| Bedding | 2" of gravel below pipe; 6" of gravel above pipe |
| Filter fabric | Around gravel bed to prevent soil intrusion |
| Slope | 1% minimum to sump or daylight |
| Sump | Required where gravity discharge is not possible; sump pump to discharge above grade |

## Backfill Behind Retaining Walls

### Backfill Material Selection

| Material | Suitability | Notes |
|----------|-------------|-------|
| Clean sand or gravel (SW, GW) | Excellent | Best drainage, lowest lateral pressure, easy to compact |
| Sand with some fines (SM, SP-SM) | Good | Moderate drainage, moderate lateral pressure |
| Sandy clay (SC, CL) | Fair | Poor drainage, higher lateral pressure, expansive potential |
| Clay (CH, MH) | Poor — avoid | Very poor drainage, highest lateral pressure, expansive |
| On-site native soil | Depends | Acceptable if non-expansive and non-organic; must test |
| Imported structural fill | Good | Must meet project specifications; typically SE 30+ |

### Compaction Requirements

| Zone | Compaction | Method |
|------|-----------|--------|
| Within 12" of wall (drain rock zone) | No compaction of drain rock — loose placement | Hand-placed, not mechanized |
| Native soil backfill (beyond drain rock) | 90% relative compaction (ASTM D1557) | Compact in lifts, max 8" loose thickness |
| Under footings | 95% relative compaction | Proof-roll and verify |
| Adjacent to wall stem | Light compaction only — avoid heavy equipment within 3 ft of wall | Hand tamper or plate compactor max 200 lb |

**Warning**: Heavy compaction equipment directly behind a retaining wall creates temporary surcharge and lateral impact loads that can damage the wall before the concrete has reached full strength. Do not operate heavy compaction equipment within a distance equal to the wall height.

## Drainage Failure Modes

Understanding how drainage fails helps identify plan review concerns:

| Failure Mode | Cause | Prevention | Plan Review Check |
|-------------|-------|------------|-------------------|
| Clogged perforated pipe | Sediment intrusion, no filter fabric | Filter fabric sock on pipe; geotextile around drain rock | Filter fabric shown on plans? |
| Clogged weep holes | Soil intrusion through inlet | Screen on inlet side; never sole drainage method | Weep hole screens shown? |
| Drain pipe has no outlet | Pipe was never connected to daylight or storm drain | Verify outlet location on site plan | Outlet location and elevation shown? |
| Drain pipe backsloped | Installed with reverse slope, water pools instead of draining | Verify 1% minimum slope on section detail | Pipe slope noted on detail? |
| Surface water infiltrating backfill | No grading away from wall, downspouts discharge into backfill | Grade 2% away, divert downspouts | Surface drainage grading shown? |
| Filter fabric deteriorated | UV exposure, root intrusion, poor quality fabric | Use non-woven 4 oz/sy min, bury below grade | Fabric specification noted? |
| Drain rock contaminated with fines | Wrong aggregate used, or soil mixed in during placement | Specify clean 3/4"-1.5" aggregate; inspect before backfill | Aggregate specification on plans? |

## Common Drainage Review Deficiencies

1. **No drainage shown on plans at all**: The most common deficiency. Every retaining wall needs drainage behind it. If no drainage detail is shown, this is a FAIL — request a drainage detail showing drain rock, perforated pipe, filter fabric, and outlet.

2. **Weep holes only, no perforated pipe**: Weep holes alone are insufficient. They clog and do not provide reliable long-term drainage. Require perforated pipe + drain rock as primary system.

3. **No outlet shown for drain pipe**: A perforated pipe with no outlet is useless. The pipe must connect to daylight, a storm drain, or an approved discharge point. Verify the outlet on the site plan.

4. **No filter fabric**: Without filter fabric, fine soil particles migrate into the drain rock and pipe, eventually clogging the entire system. Require geotextile filter fabric between drain rock and native soil.

5. **Surface not graded away from wall**: If the ground surface behind the wall slopes toward the wall or is flat, surface water will infiltrate the backfill and overwhelm the drainage system. Require 2% minimum slope away from wall for at least 10 ft.

6. **Downspouts or irrigation discharging into backfill zone**: Concentrated water sources behind the wall dramatically increase the drainage load. Redirect downspouts and minimize irrigation.
