# Energy Code Wall Insulation Requirements

Source: 2025 California Energy Code (Title 24, Part 6), Section 150.2(b) -- Alterations to Existing Single-Family Residential Buildings.

---

## Overview

When siding is replaced and the wall cavity is exposed or disturbed, the California Energy Code may require insulation to be installed or upgraded. The key question is: **does the siding replacement expose the wall cavity?**

---

## When Insulation Requirements Are Triggered

### Siding Replacement WITHOUT Cavity Exposure

If the existing siding is removed and replaced without removing or disturbing the sheathing or wall cavity:
- **No insulation upgrade required**
- The wall cavity is not considered "altered"
- Example: removing vinyl siding and installing fiber cement over existing sheathing and WRB

### Siding Replacement WITH Cavity Exposure

If the siding replacement involves removing sheathing, exposing framing, or otherwise accessing the wall cavity:
- **Insulation installation is required** in the exposed/altered portion
- The altered wall section must meet prescriptive or performance requirements

---

## Prescriptive Wall Insulation Requirements (Section 150.2(b)1)

### Existing Wood-Framed Walls -- When Siding IS Being Removed or Replaced

Per Section 150.2(a)1A(iv) and 150.2(a)1B(vi), when existing siding IS being removed or replaced:
- Full prescriptive requirements of Section 150.1(c) apply to the altered walls
- This includes both cavity insulation AND continuous insulation

### Existing Wood-Framed Walls -- When Siding Is NOT Being Removed

Per Section 150.2(a)1A(iv) and 150.2(a)1B(vi), when existing siding is NOT being removed or replaced:
- Cavity insulation of **R-15 in 2x4 framing** and **R-21 in 2x6 framing** required
- **Continuous insulation is NOT required**

### Minimum Wall Insulation R-Values (Prescriptive, New Construction Baseline)

| Framing | Cavity Insulation | Continuous Insulation | Total Assembly |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2x4 wall | R-15 | R-4 (varies by CZ) | R-19 approx |
| 2x6 wall | R-21 | R-4 (varies by CZ) | R-25 approx |

**Note:** For alterations (siding replacement), continuous insulation is only required when siding is being removed AND the full prescriptive approach is being applied. In practice, most siding replacement projects trigger the cavity-only requirement.

---

## Climate Zone Variations

California Energy Code climate zones (CZ 1-16) affect specific requirements. For wall insulation in alterations, the requirements are generally uniform across climate zones with these key points:

| Climate Zone | Cavity Insulation (2x4) | Cavity Insulation (2x6) | Continuous Insulation Required? |
|---|---|---|---|
| CZ 1-16 (all) | R-15 | R-21 | Only when siding removed AND full prescriptive applies |

## Continuous Insulation Over Foam Sheathing

When adding continuous insulation (foam board) as part of siding replacement:

- Foam plastic insulating sheathing must comply with CBC Chapter 26 (Plastics)
- Acts as WRB if joints are sealed per manufacturer
- Siding attachment must accommodate foam thickness (see Tables 1404.5.3.1/1404.5.3.2)
- Vapor retarder considerations per CRC R702.7 / CBC 1404.3

### Common Continuous Insulation Products

| Product | Typical R-Value per Inch | WRB Capable? |
|---|---|---|
| Expanded polystyrene (EPS) | R-3.6 to R-4.2 | Some products with sealed joints |
| Extruded polystyrene (XPS) | R-5.0 | Some products with sealed joints |
| Polyisocyanurate (polyiso) | R-5.7 to R-6.5 | Some products with foil facing |
| Mineral wool board | R-4.0 to R-4.3 | No -- requires separate WRB |

## Vapor Retarder Requirements When Adding Insulation (CRC R702.7)

When insulation is added or altered during siding replacement, vapor retarder class must be considered:

### California Climate Zone to IECC Climate Zone Mapping (Table R702.7.3)

| IECC Zone | California Energy Code CZ | Vapor Retarder Required? |
|---|---|---|
| 2 | CZ 14, 16 | No -- Class III permitted, none required |
| 3 | CZ 8-15 (most of SoCal) | No -- Class III permitted, none required |
| 3 (marine) | CZ 1-7, 9, 12, 16 (coastal) | No -- Class III permitted, none required |
| 4 | CZ 2, 12, 13, 16 | Class II or III permitted |
| 4 (marine) | CZ 1, 2, 16 | Class I, II, or III per Table R702.7(2) |
| 5 | CZ 11, 12, 16 | Class I, II, or III per Table R702.7(2) |
| 6 | CZ 16 | Class I, II, or III per Table R702.7(2) |

**For most of Southern California (IECC Zone 3):** No interior vapor retarder is required. A Class III vapor retarder (latex paint) is sufficient.

**Exception:** In Climate Zones 1, 2, and 3, a vapor retarder is explicitly NOT required (R702.7 Exception 4).

## Performance Approach Alternative (Section 150.2(b)2)

As an alternative to prescriptive requirements, the altered wall components can comply through the performance approach:
- Standard design for altered wall insulation: the higher efficiency of existing conditions or the requirements of Table 150.2-G
- Proposed design based on actual values of altered components
- Energy modeling by a HERS Rater or energy consultant

## Practical Guidance for Siding Replacement

### Scenario 1: Replace-in-kind (same material, no cavity access)
- Remove old siding, inspect WRB, install new siding
- **No insulation trigger** -- cavity not disturbed
- Document that sheathing was not removed

### Scenario 2: Remove siding and sheathing (cavity exposed)
- Must install cavity insulation: R-15 (2x4) or R-21 (2x6)
- Consider adding continuous insulation (foam board) under new siding
- If continuous insulation added, verify siding fastener schedule accommodates foam thickness
- Install WRB per R703.2

### Scenario 3: Change from stucco to lap siding (or vice versa)
- Stucco removal typically exposes lath and sheathing (or studs if no sheathing)
- If cavity exposed: insulation required
- New WRB required for new siding type
- Flashing at all openings must be evaluated and replaced if needed

### Scenario 4: Adding continuous insulation as part of re-siding
- Install foam sheathing over existing sheathing/WRB
- New WRB layer over or integrated with foam
- Longer fasteners required to penetrate through foam into framing
- No vapor retarder issues in most of California (IECC Zone 3)

---

## CF-1R/CF-2R Documentation

When insulation is installed or altered as part of a siding replacement:
- **CF-1R** (Certificate of Compliance) must be filed documenting the insulation R-values
- **CF-2R** (Certificate of Installation) must be completed by the installer
- HERS verification may be required depending on scope
