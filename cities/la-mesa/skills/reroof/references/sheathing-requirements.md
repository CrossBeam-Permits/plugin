# Roof Sheathing Requirements

Source: 2025 California Residential Code (Title 24, Part 2.5), Chapter 8 Section R803; Chapter 9 Section R905; California Energy Code (Part 6), Section 150.1(C)2.

---

## General Rule

All damaged, rotten, termite-eaten, or otherwise deteriorated sheathing discovered during tear-off must be replaced before new underlayment and roofing are installed.

---

## Minimum Sheathing Thickness

### Lumber Sheathing (CRC R803.1 / Table R803.1)

| Rafter/Beam Spacing | Minimum Net Thickness |
|---|---|
| 24" | 5/8" |
| 48" | 1-1/2" T&G (min 270Fb, 340,000E) |
| 60" | 1-1/2" T&G (min 420Fb, 660,000E) |
| 72" | 1-1/2" T&G (min 600Fb, 1,150,000E) |

### Wood Structural Panel Sheathing (CRC R803.2)

- Must conform to DOC PS 1, DOC PS 2, CSA O325, or CSA O437
- Must be identified for grade, bond classification, and performance category
- Exposure: exterior-grade or Exposure 1 (interior type bonded with exterior glue)
- Maximum spans per Table R503.2.1.1(1) or APA E30
- Joints: staggered or not staggered per Table R602.3(1)
- Cantilever: max 9" beyond gable endwall unless supported by overhang framing

### Practical Minimum for Reroof

Most jurisdictions enforce a **minimum 1/2" thickness** for replacement sheathing (plywood or OSB). This is the standard Costa Mesa and most Southern California cities apply.

---

## Sheathing Nailing (CRC R803.2.3 / Table R602.3(1))

| Location | Spacing |
|---|---|
| Panel edges (perimeter) | 6" on center |
| Interior framing members (field) | 12" on center |

Fasteners must be appropriate for the panel thickness and framing member (typically 8d common nails for 1/2" sheathing, 8d or 10d for 5/8" and thicker).

---

## Gaps in Existing Spaced Sheathing

Where existing spaced (skip) sheathing is encountered:
- Gaps between boards shall not exceed **1/4"** or the maximum gap permitted by the shingle manufacturer's installation instructions
- Gaps larger than permitted must be filled with **solid sheathing material** and covered
- Spaced lumber sheathing is NOT permitted in Seismic Design Category D2 (CRC R803.1)

**Note:** Spaced sheathing is commonly found on older homes with wood shake roofs. If switching to composition shingles or tile, the spaced sheathing must be converted to solid sheathing (CRC R905.2.1 — asphalt shingles require solid wood structural panels or lumber sheathing).

---

## Radiant Barrier Requirement (Energy Code 150.1(C)2)

When the roof is ventilated with a minimum 1-inch air gap between the roofing material and the deck:

> A **radiant barrier** is required to be installed when reroofing in applicable climate zones.

### When Required

The radiant barrier requirement applies on reroof when there is a vented attic with the required 1-inch air gap. The Energy Code tables specify which climate zones require radiant barriers:

| Climate Zone | Radiant Barrier Required? |
|---|---|
| CZ 2-3 | Option B: Required |
| CZ 4-7 | Option C: Required for vented attics |
| CZ 8-15 | Option C: Required for vented attics |
| CZ 1, 16 | Not required |

### Installation

- Radiant barrier must face an air space of at least 1 inch
- Typically installed on the underside of the roof rafters or on top of the attic insulation (foil side down in Southern California)
- Must not be installed directly on top of attic insulation without an air gap

## Sheathing Inspection Checklist (Tear-Off)

When tear-off exposes the deck, verify:

| Check | What to Look For |
|---|---|
| Structural integrity | No rot, delamination, or insect damage |
| Fastener condition | Nails not backing out; adequate grip |
| Gaps | Within 1/4" or per manufacturer tolerance |
| Water damage | Replace any water-stained or soft areas |
| Thickness | Minimum 1/2" for replacement panels |
| Spaced vs. solid | If converting from shakes, may need solid sheathing overlay |

---

## Common Review Findings

1. **Rotten sheathing discovered on tear-off** — This is expected on older roofs. Contractor should replace with matching or better material. No separate permit required for incidental sheathing replacement.
2. **Spaced sheathing under old shakes** — If new material is asphalt shingles, the spaced sheathing must be covered with solid sheathing. FLAG if the scope of work does not account for this.
3. **Missing radiant barrier** — In CZ 4-15, if the reroof involves a vented attic, a radiant barrier should be installed. This is an energy code requirement often triggered on reroof.
