# Gas Vent Termination and Roof Penetration Requirements

Source: 2025 California Mechanical Code (Title 24, Part 4), Section 802.6; California Plumbing Code (Title 24, Part 5), Section 802.6.

---

## Why This Applies to Reroof

During a reroof, all existing roof penetrations (gas vents, plumbing vents, exhaust vents) must be properly reflashed and their terminations maintained. Reroofing around gas vent pipes requires attention to:
- Minimum height above roof
- Clearance to combustibles
- Proper flashing and roof jack installation
- Vent cap integrity

---

## Gas Vent Termination Heights (CMC 802.6.1 / CPC 802.6.1)

### Type B or L Vents (most residential gas appliances)

| Requirement | Minimum |
|---|---|
| Vertical height above draft hood or flue collar | **5 feet** |
| Above roof surface (vents 12" or less diameter, 8+ ft from wall) | Per Table 802.6.1 (see below) |
| Above roof surface (vents over 12" diameter OR < 8 ft from wall) | **2 feet** above highest point within 10 ft horizontal |
| Above forced air inlet within 10 ft | **3 feet** |

### Roof Slope Table (CMC Table 802.6.1)

Minimum vent termination height (H) above roof for vents 12" or less, 8+ ft from a vertical wall:

| Roof Slope | Minimum H (feet) |
|---|---|
| Flat to 6:12 | 1.0 |
| Over 6:12 to 7:12 | 1.25 |
| Over 7:12 to 8:12 | 1.5 |
| Over 8:12 to 9:12 | 2.0 |
| Over 9:12 to 10:12 | 2.5 |
| Over 10:12 to 11:12 | 3.25 |
| Over 11:12 to 12:12 | 4.0 |
| Over 12:12 to 14:12 | 5.0 |
| Over 14:12 to 16:12 | 6.0 |
| Over 16:12 to 18:12 | 7.0 |
| Over 18:12 to 20:12 | 7.5 |
| Over 20:12 to 21:12 | 8.0 |

For low-slope residential roofs (< 6:12), the practical minimum is **12 inches above the roof deck** at the point of penetration.

---

## Clearance to Combustibles

| Vent Type | Minimum Clearance to Combustibles |
|---|---|
| Single-wall metal pipe | **6 inches** |
| Type B vent (double-wall) | **1 inch** |
| Factory-built chimney | Per manufacturer (typically 2") |

**During reroof:** Ensure that new roofing materials maintain these clearances around vent pipes. Roofing material must not contact the vent pipe directly.

---

## Flashing and Roof Jack Requirements

### General (CMC 802.6.1(6) / CPC 802.6.1(6))

- **Flashing shall be provided** at every roof or wall vent penetration
- All gas vents must extend through the **roof flashing, roof jack, or roof thimble**
- Must terminate with a **listed cap or listed roof assembly**

### Pipe Jack Flashing

- Pipe jack (boot) flashings shall be installed at all through-roof penetrations
- Replace any cracked, deteriorated, or UV-damaged pipe boots during reroof
- Base flashing must integrate with the roofing underlayment and covering
- Metal storm collar or pipe jack must be corrosion-resistant

### Vent Support

- Vents must be **adequately supported** — the vent pipe must not rely on the roofing material for structural support
- Minimum **5 inches** above the water heater collar for Type B/L vents

---

## Plumbing Vent Penetrations

Plumbing vent pipes also penetrate the roof. While not gas vents, they require:
- Proper pipe jack flashing (same as gas vents)
- Must extend above the roof surface
- Boot/jack replacement during reroof is standard practice

---

## Direct Vent Appliances

Direct vent appliances (wall-terminated) do NOT extend through the roof. These are not affected by reroof work unless the wall vent terminates in the soffit/eave area and the reroof scope includes soffit work.

---

## Inspection Checklist for Reroof

| Check | What to Verify |
|---|---|
| Vent height | Meets minimum per roof slope (see table above) |
| Vent cap | Listed cap in place, not damaged |
| Pipe jack/boot | New or in good condition, properly sealed |
| Clearance to combustibles | 6" single-wall, 1" Type B |
| Flashing integration | Pipe jack properly integrated with underlayment and covering |
| Vent support | Pipe adequately supported, not relying on roofing |

---

## Common Review Findings

1. **Cracked pipe boots** — Most common finding. All pipe boots should be replaced during reroof. FLAG if not addressed in scope.
2. **Vent pipe cut too short** — After adding new roofing layers, the vent may no longer meet minimum height. Verify height after new covering is installed.
3. **Missing vent cap** — FAIL. All gas vents must terminate with a listed cap.
4. **Roofing material touching vent pipe** — FAIL. Combustible clearances must be maintained.
