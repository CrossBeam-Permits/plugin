# Waterproofing and Drainage

Source: 2025 California Residential Code (Title 24, Part 2.5), Chapter 3 Section R304 (Decay Protection), Chapter 5 Section R507 (Exterior Decks), Section R318.7.7 (Walking Surface Slope).

---

## When Waterproofing Is Required

Waterproofing and drainage requirements apply in several scenarios:

1. **Elevated decks or balconies over habitable space** -- Where the deck is directly above a room, the structural framing must be protected from water infiltration
2. **Enclosed framing exposed to weather** -- Exterior balcony framing that is enclosed must be ventilated
3. **Ledger connections** -- Flashing and water-resistive barrier requirements at the house connection (see `ledger-connections.md` for full details)

---

## Decks Over Habitable Space (R304.1 Item 6)

### General Rule

Wood structural members supporting **moisture-permeable floors or roofs exposed to the weather** (such as concrete or masonry slabs on a deck over living space) must be:
- Separated from the floor/roof by an **impervious moisture barrier**, OR
- Protected by other approved means

### Impervious Moisture Barrier Requirements

The moisture barrier system must provide **positive drainage** of water that infiltrates the moisture-permeable floor topping. This means:
- The membrane must be continuous and fully waterproof
- It must be sloped to drain water to the edge or to scuppers/drains
- Water that gets past the walking surface (tile, pavers, concrete) must not reach the structural framing

### Practical Application

For a second-story deck or balcony over a habitable room:
1. Install a waterproof membrane over the structural framing
2. Slope the membrane min **1/4 inch per foot** toward drainage points (industry standard, not code-specified)
3. Install drainage channels or scuppers at low points
4. Install the walking surface (tile, pavers, etc.) over the membrane with drainage space
5. Flash all penetrations through the membrane
6. The membrane must extend up walls and be integrated with the wall weather barrier

## Ventilation of Enclosed Framing (R304.1.3)

### Where Required

Enclosed framing in exterior balconies and elevated walking surfaces that are exposed to rain, snow, or drainage from irrigation must have ventilation openings.

### Ventilation Ratio

Net-free cross-ventilation area: min **1/150** of the area of each separate space.

### Practical Application

For a balcony soffit enclosing the framing below:
- Calculate the area of the enclosed space
- Provide screened ventilation openings totaling 1/150 of that area
- Openings should be at opposite ends to promote cross-ventilation
- Use corrosion-resistant screening per climate and exposure

**Example:** A 200 sq ft enclosed balcony space requires 200/150 = 1.33 sq ft = 192 sq in of net-free ventilation area.

## Flashing Material Requirements (R507.2.4)

All flashing used on decks must be:

| Property | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Material type | Corrosion-resistant metal OR approved non-metallic |
| Metal thickness | Min **0.019 inch** (0.48 mm) nominal (approximately 26-gauge) |
| Compatibility | Compatible with the substrate of the structure AND the decking materials |
| Self-adhered membranes | Must comply with **FGIA 711** (formerly AAMA 711) |

### Common Flashing Materials

| Material | Typical Use | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Galvanized steel | Ledger flashing, general | Must be min 0.019" thick; verify compatibility with PT lumber |
| Aluminum | Ledger flashing | NOT recommended in direct contact with pressure-treated wood (galvanic corrosion) |
| Copper | Premium applications | Excellent durability; compatible with most wood |
| Stainless steel | Salt water proximity | Required within 300 ft of salt water |
| Self-adhered membrane | Counterflashing, secondary waterproofing | Must comply with FGIA 711 |

### Galvanic Compatibility Warning

Aluminum flashing in direct contact with pressure-treated wood (copper-based preservatives like ACQ, CA) will corrode rapidly due to galvanic reaction. Use galvanized steel, stainless steel, or copper flashing where it contacts pressure-treated lumber.

## Walking Surface Slope (R318.7.7)

### Stair Treads and Landings

Maximum slope: **2 percent** (1 unit vertical in 48 units horizontal).

**Exception:** Where a landing must drain surface water, max **5 percent** slope in the direction of travel is permitted.

### Exterior Door Landings (R318.3)

Maximum slope at exterior landings: **2 percent** (1/4 unit vertical in 12 units horizontal).

### Deck Surface Drainage

While the CRC does not specify a minimum slope for deck walking surfaces (wood decking typically allows water to drain through gaps between boards), the following practical guidelines apply:

- **Wood plank decking:** Natural gaps between boards provide drainage. Min **1/8 inch** gap between boards is typical industry practice.
- **Composite decking:** Follow manufacturer's instructions for gap spacing and drainage.
- **Solid surface decking** (tile, concrete): Must be sloped to drain. Industry standard is min **1/4 inch per foot** (2% slope) toward the edge or drainage points.

## Decay Protection at Moisture Exposure Points (R304.1)

Wood at these deck-specific moisture exposure points must be pressure-treated or naturally durable:

| Location | Code Reference | Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| Structural members exposed to weather without roof protection | R304.1 Item 8 | Pressure-treated or naturally durable |
| Wood within 6 inches of ground | R304.1 Item 5 | Pressure-treated or naturally durable |
| Wood in contact with ground | R304.1.2 | Pressure-treated for **ground contact use** per AWPA U1 |
| Wood supporting moisture-permeable floors exposed to weather | R304.1 Item 6 | Protected by impervious moisture barrier with positive drainage |
| Wood on concrete or masonry in ground contact | R304.1 Item 3 | Separated by impervious moisture barrier OR pressure-treated |

## Ledger Waterproofing Summary

For complete ledger flashing and waterproofing details, see `ledger-connections.md`. Key points:

- Flashing above ledger: min 2" vertical, min 4" horizontal
- Water-resistive barrier lapped over flashing
- Existing walls without WRB: install WRB with counterflashing
- Exception: flashing not required if ledger spaced 1/4" from wall

## Common Review Findings

1. **Deck over living space with no waterproof membrane** -- This is a critical FAIL. If any habitable space is below the deck, a continuous waterproof membrane with positive drainage must be installed over the structural framing before the walking surface. Missing membranes lead to structural rot and interior water damage.
2. **No ventilation in enclosed balcony soffit** -- Where the underside of a balcony is enclosed (common for aesthetic reasons), the 1/150 ventilation ratio per R304.1.3 must be met. Without ventilation, trapped moisture causes premature rot of the framing.
3. **Aluminum flashing on pressure-treated wood** -- Aluminum corrodes in contact with ACQ or CA-treated lumber. This is a common material incompatibility that leads to flashing failure and water intrusion. Use galvanized steel or stainless steel instead.
4. **Solid deck surface with no slope** -- Tile or concrete walking surfaces on decks must be sloped to drain. A flat surface traps water and promotes structural damage. FLAG if slope is not shown on plans for solid-surface decks.
5. **Missing drainage path** -- Even with a waterproof membrane, water must have somewhere to go. Plans should show drainage channels, scuppers, or edge drainage details. Without a clear drainage path, ponding water will eventually find a way through.
