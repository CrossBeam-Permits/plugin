# CRC R310 / R311 — Smoke and CO Alarm Requirements Triggered by Rewire

Source: 2025 California Residential Code (Title 24, Part 2.5), Chapter 3 — Building Planning.

A whole-house rewire is an alteration requiring a permit, which triggers smoke and CO alarm compliance requirements. The extent of compliance depends on **whether wall or ceiling finishes are removed**.

---

## Smoke Alarms — CRC R310

### When Rewire Triggers Compliance — R310.2.2

> "Where alterations, repairs or additions requiring a permit occur, the individual dwelling unit shall be equipped with smoke alarms located as required for new dwellings."

A rewire permit IS an alteration requiring a permit, so the dwelling must have smoke alarms in all locations required for new construction (R310.3).

### Required Smoke Alarm Locations — R310.3

Smoke alarms must be installed in:
1. **Each sleeping room** (bedroom)
2. **Outside each separate sleeping area** in the immediate vicinity of bedrooms
3. **On each additional story** of the dwelling (including basements, habitable attics; excluding crawl spaces and uninhabitable attics)
4. Not less than **3 feet from bathroom doors** (containing bathtub/shower)
5. In the **hallway AND room open to hallway** where ceiling height of adjacent room exceeds hallway by 24+ inches
6. Within the room to which a **sleeping loft** is open

### Power Source — R310.6

Smoke alarms shall receive primary power from **building wiring** (hardwired) served from commercial source, with **battery backup**.

**Exception 5 (key for rewire)**: Smoke alarms are permitted to be **solely battery operated** when work is limited to electrical systems that **do not result in the removal of interior wall or ceiling finishes exposing the structure**.

> **Translation**: If the rewire removes drywall or ceiling finishes (common for full rewire), smoke alarms MUST be hardwired. If the rewire uses existing pathways without removing finishes (e.g., fishing wire through walls), battery-operated alarms are permitted.

### Interconnection — R310.4

Where more than one smoke alarm is required, all alarms shall be **interconnected** so activation of one activates all alarms in the unit.

**Exception 4**: Interconnection is **not required** when work is limited to electrical systems that **do not result in removal of interior wall or ceiling finishes exposing the structure**.

> **Translation**: Same logic as power source — if finishes are removed during rewire, interconnection is required. If finishes are preserved, existing non-interconnected alarms may remain.

### Combination Alarms — R310.5

Combination smoke and CO alarms may be used in lieu of separate smoke alarms (must be listed per UL 217 and UL 2034).

## Carbon Monoxide Alarms — CRC R311

### When CO Alarms Are Required — R311.2.1

CO alarms are required in dwelling units where either:
1. The dwelling contains a **fuel-fired appliance or fireplace** (gas furnace, gas water heater, gas range, wood fireplace, etc.)
2. The dwelling has an **attached garage** with an opening communicating with the dwelling

> Most California homes meet at least one of these conditions.

### Rewire and CO Alarms — R311.2.2

CO alarms are required when a fuel-burning appliance or fireplace is added. For rewire specifically:

**Exception 2**: "Installation, alteration or repairs of plumbing systems" — does NOT apply to electrical/rewire.

> Rewire does NOT have a blanket exception from CO alarm requirements. If the home has fuel-fired appliances or an attached garage, CO alarms must comply.

### Required CO Alarm Locations — R311.3

1. **Outside each separate sleeping area** in the immediate vicinity of bedrooms
2. **On every occupiable level** of the dwelling (including basements)
3. **Within the bedroom** where a fuel-burning appliance is in the bedroom or attached bathroom

### Interconnection — R311.5

Where more than one CO alarm is required, they must be interconnected (activation of one activates all). Wireless listed alarms that sound upon activation of one alarm satisfy this requirement.

**Exception** (for buildings built before January 1, 2011): Interconnection not required when work is limited to electrical systems that **do not result in removal of interior wall or ceiling finishes** in areas where CO alarms are required.

### Power Source — R311.6

CO alarms shall receive primary power from **building wiring** with **battery backup**.

**Exception 4** (for buildings built before January 1, 2011): Battery-powered or plug-in CO alarms permitted when electrical work does not result in removal of wall/ceiling finishes.

## Decision Matrix: What Does the Rewire Trigger?

| Condition | Smoke Alarm Locations | Hardwired? | Interconnected? | CO Alarms? |
|-----------|----------------------|-----------|-----------------|-----------|
| **Full rewire WITH wall/ceiling removal** | All per R310.3 | **Yes** — must be hardwired | **Yes** — must be interconnected | Yes (if fuel-fired appliance or attached garage) |
| **Full rewire WITHOUT wall/ceiling removal** | All per R310.3 | No — battery OK | No — non-interconnected OK | Yes, but battery/plug-in OK (pre-2011 homes) |
| **Partial rewire WITH wall/ceiling removal** | All per R310.3 | **Yes** in areas where finishes removed | **Yes** where finishes removed | Yes (if applicable) |

## Common Review Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Whole-House Rewire (Typical)
Most whole-house rewires involve opening walls to run new wiring. This triggers full compliance:
- Smoke alarms in every bedroom, outside bedrooms, on every level
- Hardwired with battery backup
- All interconnected
- CO alarms if gas appliances or attached garage

### Scenario 2: Rewire Using Existing Pathways
If the electrician fishes new wire through existing walls without removing finishes:
- Smoke alarms still required in all R310.3 locations
- Battery-operated alarms are acceptable
- Interconnection not required
- CO alarms still required but battery/plug-in acceptable (pre-2011 homes)

### Scenario 3: Rewire Combined with Panel Upgrade
Panel upgrade alone doesn't trigger smoke/CO requirements. But if the rewire portion involves wall/ceiling removal, full compliance applies to the entire dwelling.

---

## Practical Notes

1. **Combination smoke/CO alarms** are the most cost-effective solution — one device per location satisfies both R310 and R311
2. **Wireless interconnection** is available for situations where running interconnect wire between alarms is impractical
3. **Location from cooking appliances**: Smoke alarms must be minimum **20 feet** from permanently installed cooking appliances (photoelectric alarms may be installed at 10+ feet; photoelectric at 6+ feet where no partitions exist)
4. **Stairways**: Smoke alarm required on basement ceiling near stairs entry; not blocked by intervening doors
