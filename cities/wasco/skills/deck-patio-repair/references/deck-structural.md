# Deck Structural Requirements

Source: 2025 California Residential Code (Title 24, Part 2.5), Chapter 5 Section R507 (Exterior Decks), Section R502 (Wood Floor Framing), Section R304 (Decay Protection).

---

## Design Loads

Decks must be designed for the live load required in Section R301.5 or the ground snow load in Table R301.2, whichever is greater. The standard residential deck live load is **40 psf** (CRC R507.1). Dead load is typically 10 psf for wood-framed decks.

---

## Footings (CRC R507.3)

### When Required

Footings are required for all decks EXCEPT:
1. **Grade-bearing joists** -- joists directly supported on grade over their entire length
2. **Small freestanding decks** meeting ALL of:
   - Joists bear directly on precast concrete pier blocks at grade (no beams or posts)
   - Area does not exceed 200 sq ft
   - Walking surface not more than 20 inches above grade within 36 inches of edge

### Minimum Footing Size (Table R507.3.1)

For 40 psf live load on soil with 1,500 psf bearing capacity:

| Tributary Area (sq ft) | Square Footing Side (in) | Round Footing Diameter (in) | Min Concrete Thickness (in) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 20 | 10 | 12 | 6 |
| 40 | 14 | 16 | 6 |
| 60 | 17 | 19 | 6 |
| 80 | 20 | 22 | 7 |
| 100 | 22 | 25 | 8 |
| 120 | 24 | 27 | 9 |
| 140 | 26 | 29 | 10 |
| 160 | 28 | 31 | 11 |

For higher soil bearing capacity (2,000+ psf), footings may be smaller -- see full Table R507.3.1.

### Minimum Depth

- **12 inches** below undisturbed ground surface (R507.3.2)
- Where attached to frost-protected structure: must extend below frost line, to solid rock, or use approved frost protection (R507.3.3)

## Posts (CRC R507.4)

### Post Sizing (Table R507.4)

For 40 psf live load, Douglas fir-larch / Hem-fir / Spruce-pine-fir, No. 2 grade:

| Post Size | Tributary 20 sq ft | Tributary 40 sq ft | Tributary 60 sq ft | Tributary 80 sq ft | Tributary 100 sq ft | Tributary 120 sq ft |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 4x4 | 14'-0" | 13'-6" | 10'-10" | 9'-3" | 8'-0" | 7'-0" |
| 4x6 | 14'-0" | 14'-0" | 13'-10" | 11'-10" | 10'-6" | 9'-5" |
| 6x6 | 14'-0" | 14'-0" | 14'-0" | 14'-0" | 14'-0" | 14'-0" |

**Maximum post height for single-level decks: 14 feet** (measured from underside of beam to top of footing or pier).

### Post-to-Footing Connection (R507.4.1)

Lateral restraint provided by:
- Approved connectors (post bases), OR
- Minimum 12 inches of post embedment in surrounding soils or concrete piers

**Exception:** Expansive, compressible, shifting, or questionable soils shall NOT be relied on for lateral support.

### Post Notching Rule (R507.10.2)

**4x4 posts supporting guard loads shall NOT be notched** at the connection to the supporting structure.

### Beam Spans (Table R507.5(1) -- 40 psf Live Load)

For Douglas fir-larch / Hem-fir / Spruce-pine-fir (incising factor included):

| Beam Size | 6' Joist Span | 8' Joist Span | 10' Joist Span | 12' Joist Span | 14' Joist Span | 16' Joist Span |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2-2x6 | 6'-6" | 5'-3" | 4'-9" | 4'-6" | 4'-4" | 3'-11" |
| 2-2x8 | 8'-8" | 7'-1" | 6'-4" | 6'-0" | 5'-9" | 5'-2" |
| 2-2x10 | 10'-8" | 8'-7" | 7'-9" | 7'-4" | 7'-0" | 6'-6" |
| 2-2x12 | 12'-4" | 10'-0" | 8'-11" | 8'-6" | 8'-2" | 7'-7" |
| 3-2x8 | 10'-11" | 8'-10" | 7'-11" | 7'-7" | 7'-3" | 6'-8" |
| 3-2x10 | 13'-4" | 10'-10" | 9'-8" | 9'-3" | 8'-10" | 8'-2" |
| 3-2x12 | 15'-6" | 12'-7" | 11'-3" | 10'-9" | 10'-3" | 9'-6" |

### Beam Construction

- Beam plies fastened with **two rows of 10d (3" x 0.128") nails at 16" OC** along each edge (R507.5)
- Beam depth must be >= depth of intersecting joist for flush beam connections

### Beam Bearing (R507.5.1)

- Min **1-1/2 inches** on wood or metal
- Min **3 inches** on concrete or masonry
- Beams and individual plies must be continuous between bearing locations
- **Cantilever limit: 1/4 of actual beam span** beyond bearing

### Post-to-Beam Connection (R507.5.2)

- Must prevent lateral displacement
- Use manufactured post-to-beam connectors sized for post and beam
- Bolts must have washers under head AND nut
- Notched post connection per Figure R507.5.2(2) is an approved method

## Joists (CRC R507.6)

### Joist Spans (Table R507.6 -- 40 psf Live Load)

For Douglas fir-larch / Hem-fir / Spruce-pine-fir, No. 2 grade (incising factor included):

| Joist Size | 12" OC | 16" OC | 24" OC |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2x6 | 9'-6" | 8'-4" | 6'-10" |
| 2x8 | 12'-6" | 11'-1" | 9'-1" |
| 2x10 | 15'-8" | 13'-7" | 11'-1" |
| 2x12 | 18'-0" | 15'-9" | 12'-10" |

### Joist Cantilever (Table R507.6)

Maximum cantilever depends on joist size and back span:

| Joist Size | 8' Back Span | 10' Back Span | 12' Back Span | 14' Back Span |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2x8 | 2'-0" | 2'-3" | 2'-0" | N/P |
| 2x10 | 2'-0" | 2'-6" | 3'-0" | 3'-3" |
| 2x12 | 2'-0" | 2'-6" | 3'-0" | 3'-6" |

### Joist Bearing (R507.6.1)

- Min **1-1/2 inches** on wood or metal
- Min **3 inches** on concrete or masonry
- Joists on top of beam/ledger: nailed per Table R602.3(1) (multi-ply) or mechanical connector (single-ply)
- Joists framing into side of beam/ledger: supported by approved joist hangers

### Lateral Restraint (R507.6.2)

- Required at joist ends and bearing locations
- Joist hangers or blocking: depth >= 60% of joist depth
- Rim joist to each joist: min **three 10d nails or three No. 10 x 3" wood screws**

### Cutting, Drilling, and Notching (R502.8)

- Notch depth: max **1/6 of joist depth**
- Notch length: max **1/3 of joist depth**
- No notches in middle 1/3 of span
- End notches: max **1/4 of depth**
- Hole diameter: max **1/3 of depth**
- Holes min **2 inches** from top or bottom edge

## Decking (CRC R507.7)

### Maximum Joist Spacing (Table R507.7)

| Decking Type | Perpendicular Single Span | Perpendicular Multi Span | Diagonal Single Span | Diagonal Multi Span |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1-1/4" wood (5/4 boards) | 12" OC | 16" OC | 8" OC | 12" OC |
| 2" wood (nominal) | 24" OC | 24" OC | 18" OC | 24" OC |

### Fastening

Each deck board attached to each support with:
- Min **two 8d deformed shank nails**, or
- Min **two No. 8 wood screws**

### Plastic Composite Decking (R507.2.2)

- Must comply with ASTM D7032
- Label required showing compliance, allowable load, and max span
- Flame spread index: **not exceeding 200** (ASTM E84 or UL 723)
- Must be decay-resistant per ASTM D7032
- Must be termite-resistant where required (Section R305)
- Installed per code AND manufacturer's instructions

## Material Requirements

### Wood Grade (R507.2.1)

- Sawn lumber for joists, beams, and posts: **No. 2 grade or better**
- Protected from decay per R304.1 and from termites per R305.1
- Wet service factor required for design per AWC NDS

### Decay Protection (CRC R304.1)

Pressure-treated or naturally durable wood required for:
- All deck structural members exposed to weather without adequate roof/eave protection (Item 8)
- Wood within 6 inches of ground (siding rule -- Item 5)
- Wood in contact with ground (Item 1, ground contact use -- R304.1.2)
- Wood supporting moisture-permeable floors exposed to weather (Item 6)

### Quality Mark (R304.2)

Every piece of pressure-treated lumber must bear a quality mark showing:
1. Treating plant identification
2. Preservative type
3. Minimum retention
4. End use designation
5. Treatment standard
6. Inspection agency
7. "Dry" designation if applicable

### Field Treatment (R304.1.1)

Field-cut ends, notches, and drilled holes of pressure-treated wood must be treated per **AWPA M4** (copper naphthenate brush-on treatment is the standard field method).

## Fasteners and Connectors (CRC R507.2.3, R304.3)

### Required Coatings (Table R507.2.3)

| Item | Minimum Finish | Alternate |
|---|---|---|
| Nails | Hot-dipped galvanized per ASTM A153 Class D | Stainless steel, silicon bronze, copper |
| Bolts (incl. nuts, washers) | Hot-dipped galvanized per ASTM A153 Class C/D, or mechanically galv per ASTM B695 Class 55 | Stainless steel, silicon bronze, copper |
| Lag screws (incl. nuts, washers) | Same as bolts | Same as bolts |
| Metal connectors | ASTM A653 type G185 zinc-coated, or post hot-dipped per ASTM A123 (min 2.0 oz/ft2) | Stainless steel |

### Salt Water Proximity

**Within 300 feet of salt water shoreline: all fasteners and connectors must be stainless steel.**

### Through-Bolt Holes

Drilled **1/32 inch to 1/16 inch** larger than bolt diameter (R507.2.3).

## Floor Framing Supporting Guards (CRC R502.11)

Where guards are required, the edge framing must be designed to resist guard loads:

### Conventional Edge Framing (R502.11.1)
- Solid or built-up member: min **3 inches net width**, min **9-1/4 inches net depth**
- Roll brace aligned with each guard post
- Roll brace per R502.11.3

### Timber Edge Framing (R502.11.2)
- Min **6x10 sawn timber** or **5-1/8" x 9-1/4" glulam**
- Roll bracing at 48" OC or less (does not need to align with each guard post)

### Roll Bracing (R502.11.3)
- Joist or blocking matching edge member depth
- Extends perpendicular min **16 inches** from edge
- Blocking end connections: min **six 16d common nails**
- Floor sheathing continuous min **24 inches** from edge
- Sheathing to roll brace: min **twelve 10d common nails**
- Sheathing to edge member: min **twelve 10d common nails** within 12 inches of roll brace

## Common Review Findings

1. **Undersized footings** -- The most frequent error. Verify tributary area calculation and cross-reference Table R507.3.1. A 12x12 deck with a single center post has 72 sq ft tributary -- that requires a 17" square or 19" round footing on 1,500 psf soil.
2. **4x4 posts notched for guards** -- This is a code violation per R507.10.2. Use 4x6, 6x6 posts or use through-bolted guard post connections instead.
3. **Missing field treatment on cut ends** -- Every field cut on pressure-treated lumber must be treated with copper naphthenate per AWPA M4. FLAG if not addressed in scope of work.
4. **Joist span exceeds table values** -- Common when homeowners design their own decks. Verify against Table R507.6 using the ACTUAL species and grade of lumber specified.
5. **No lateral bracing shown** -- Even with a ledger connection, lateral hold-down devices are required per R507.9.2. Plans must show these devices.
