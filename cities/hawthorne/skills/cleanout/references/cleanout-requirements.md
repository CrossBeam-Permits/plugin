# CPC Section 707 — Cleanout Requirements

Source: 2025 California Plumbing Code (Title 24, Part 5), Chapter 7 — Sanitary Drainage.

## Cleanout Plug and Material Types (707.1)

Cleanout plugs must match the piping material:

| Pipe Material | Approved Cleanout Plug |
|---------------|----------------------|
| Cast-iron pipe | Cast-iron or copper alloy body with approved plug |
| Galvanized wrought iron / steel | Plugs per Table 707.1, OR standard weight copper alloy cap, OR approved ABS/PVC plastic plug, OR approved stainless steel |
| Copper / Copper alloy pipe | Same options as galvanized above |
| ABS / PVC plastic pipe | Approved ABS/PVC plastic plug, OR copper alloy, OR stainless steel |

---

## Cleanout Sizing — Table 707.1

Cleanout size is based on the pipe size it serves:

| Pipe Size | Cleanout Size | Threads Per Inch |
|-----------|--------------|-----------------|
| 1-1/2" | 1-1/2" | 11-1/2 |
| 2" | 1-1/2" | 11-1/2 |
| 2-1/2" | 2-1/2" | 8 |
| 3" | 2-1/2" | 8 |
| 4" | 3-1/2" | 8 |
| 5" | 3-1/2" | 8 |
| 6" | 3-1/2" | 8 |
| 8" and larger | 3-1/2" | 8 |

**Key rule**: Maximum cleanout size is 3-1/2" regardless of pipe size (707.10).

---

## Watertight and Gastight (707.3)

All cleanouts shall be designed so as to be **watertight and gastight**. Sewer gas cannot escape through a cleanout.

---

## Where Cleanouts Are Required (707.4)

Each horizontal drainage pipe shall be provided with cleanouts in the following locations:

### Required Locations

1. **Upper terminal** of each horizontal drainage pipe — always required
2. **Every 100 feet** of horizontal run (or fraction thereof exceeding 100 feet)
3. **Direction changes >135°** (aggregate) — at each change of direction
4. **Building drain/sewer junction** — approved two-way cleanout fitting, installed:
   - Inside building wall near the connection, OR
   - Outside building at lower end, extended to grade
5. **Each urinal** — cleanout above fixture connection fitting, regardless of location

### Exceptions (When Cleanouts May Be Omitted)

1. **Short runs**: Horizontal drain lines less than 5 feet in developed length, UNLESS they serve sinks or urinals
2. **Near-vertical connections**: Horizontal drainage pipe installed at 72° or less from vertical (a one-fifth bend or steeper)
3. **Above lowest floor**: Except for building drain, its horizontal branches, kitchen sinks, and urinals — cleanouts are not required above floor level of lowest floor

---

## Cleaning Direction (707.5)

- Cleanouts must open to allow cleaning **in the direction of flow** or at right angles to the pipe
- Must be installed **vertically above the flow line** of the pipe
- **Exception**: Wye branch and end-of-line cleanouts (may be at other angles)

---

## Cleanout Extensions (707.6)

- Cleanout extensions are considered drainage piping (same material and standards apply)
- A 90° cleanout extension must extend from a **wye-type fitting or equivalent** (not a tee)

---

## Above Grade (707.7 / 707.8)

- Cleanouts on stacks shall be located **not less than 42 inches above the floor**
- Unless under an approved cover plate, cleanouts must be **above grade** and **readily accessible**
- Cannot be buried under concrete, dirt, or other covering without approved access cover

---

## Clearance Requirements (707.9)

Adequate clearance must be provided in front of each cleanout for inserting rodding equipment:

### Standard Clearance

| Pipe Size | Minimum Clearance |
|-----------|------------------|
| 2" and under | 18 inches × 18 inches |
| Over 2" | 24 inches × 24 inches |

### Under-Floor Cleanouts

Where cleanouts are installed under floors:
- Must extend to or above the finished floor level, OR
- Must extend to the outside of the building wall
- Where vertical clearance under floor is less than 18 inches:
  - Cleanout must be within **5 feet** of an access door, trap door, or crawl hole
  - Must allow **30 inches horizontal clearance** from means of access

---

## Countersunk Plugs (707.12)

Where a raised cleanout head would create a hazard (e.g., in a floor), **countersunk plugs** shall be used. The cleanout cover or plug must be flush with the surrounding surface.

---

## Hubless Blind Plugs (707.13)

Where hubless (no-hub) blind plugs are used:
- The complete coupling and plug must remain **accessible for removal and replacement**
- Cannot be buried or concealed in a wall without access panel

---

## Common Review Scenarios

### Scenario 1: New Cleanout at Building Drain
- **Type**: Two-way cleanout (707.4)
- **Location**: Inside wall near building drain/sewer junction OR outside at grade
- **Size**: Per Table 707.1 (typically 3-1/2" for 4" building drain)
- **Clearance**: 24" × 24" minimum (pipe >2")

### Scenario 2: Cleanout at Direction Change
- **Type**: One-way cleanout
- **Trigger**: Aggregate direction change exceeds 135°
- **Size**: Per Table 707.1 based on pipe size
- **Access**: Must be accessible, not buried

### Scenario 3: Under-Floor Cleanout
- **Extension**: Must extend to floor level or outside
- **Access**: Within 5 feet of access opening if vertical clearance <18"
- **Clearance**: 30" horizontal from access point

### Scenario 4: Front Yard Cleanout
- **Type**: Typically two-way at building sewer
- **Grade**: Must extend to grade level
- **Cover**: Approved cover plate at grade (countersunk if in walkway)
- **Access**: Readily accessible, not buried under landscaping
