# Combustion Air Requirements

Source: 2025 California Mechanical Code (Title 24, Part 4), Chapter 7.

## Applicability (CMC 701.1)

Air for combustion, ventilation, and dilution of flue gases shall be provided for all appliances installed in buildings using one of the methods in Sections 701.4 through 701.9.3.

**Exceptions**:
1. Direct vent appliances (sealed combustion) -- no combustion air openings required
2. Type 1 clothes dryers with makeup air per Section 504.4.1

### Other Appliance Types (CMC 701.1.1)

Appliances NOT designated as Category I (e.g., power burners, non-natural draft) shall be provided combustion air per the **manufacturer's installation instructions**.

### Draft Hood (CMC 701.2)

The draft hood or barometric draft regulator shall be in the **same room or enclosure** as the appliance served.

---

## Method 1: Indoor Air Source Only (CMC 701.4)

Allowed only if the room infiltration rate is >= 0.40 ACH.

### Standard Method (CMC 701.4.1)

Minimum required volume: **50 cu ft per 1,000 BTU/h** of total input rating.

### Known Infiltration Method (CMC 701.4.2)

For fan-assisted appliances: Required volume >= (15 cu ft / ACH) x (Input / 1,000 BTU/h)
For other appliances: Required volume >= (21 cu ft / ACH) x (Input / 1,000 BTU/h)

Maximum infiltration rate for calculation: 0.60 ACH.

---

## Method 2: Outdoor Combustion Air (CMC 701.6)

### Two Permanent Openings (CMC 701.6.1)

Two openings required:
- One within 12 in. of the **top** of the enclosure
- One within 12 in. of the **bottom** of the enclosure

| Duct Type | Minimum Free Area per Opening |
|-----------|-------------------------------|
| Direct exterior or vertical ducts | 1 sq in. per 4,000 BTU/h |
| Horizontal ducts | 1 sq in. per 2,000 BTU/h |

### One Permanent Opening (CMC 701.6.2)

Single opening within 12 in. of the top of the enclosure. Appliance must have:
- Minimum 1 in. clearance on all sides and back
- Minimum 6 in. clearance in front

Minimum free area:
- 1 sq in. per 3,000 BTU/h of total input rating
- Not less than the sum of all vent connector areas

---

## Method 3: Mechanical Combustion Air (CMC 701.9)

Minimum supply rate: **0.35 CFM per 1,000 BTU/h** for all appliances in the space.

### Interlock (CMC 701.9.2)

Each appliance shall be **interlocked** to the mechanical air supply system to prevent main burner operation when the air supply is not running.

---

## Indoor Openings Between Spaces (CMC 701.5)

When combining spaces on the same story:
- Each opening: minimum 1 sq in. per 1,000 BTU/h, but not less than **100 sq in.**
- One opening within 12 in. of top, one within 12 in. of bottom
- Minimum dimension of any opening: **3 in.**

---

## Louvers, Grilles, and Screens (CMC 701.10)

Free area assumptions when actual free area is not known:
- Metal louvers: **75% free area**
- Wood louvers: **25% free area**

Nonmotorized louvers/grilles shall be **fixed in the open position**.

### Screen Mesh (CMC 701.10.1)

Screens on outside air openings shall be >= **1/4 in. mesh**.

### Motorized Louvers (CMC 701.10.2)

Motorized louvers shall be:
- Interlocked with the appliance
- Proven in the **fully open position** prior to main burner ignition
- Must prevent ignition if louver fails to open
- Must shut down main burner if louvers close during operation

---

## Combustion Air Ducts (CMC 701.11)

| # | Requirement |
|---|------------|
| 1 | Constructed of galvanized steel or equivalent |
| 2 | Terminate in an unobstructed space |
| 3 | Serve a single space only |
| 4 | Same duct shall NOT serve both upper and lower openings |
| 5 | Ducts shall NOT be screened where terminating in an attic |
| 6 | Horizontal upper ducts shall NOT slope downward toward air source |
| 7 | Remaining space around chimney shall NOT be used for combustion air |
| 8 | Exterior intake openings shall be >= 12 in. above finished grade |

### Dampers Prohibited (CMC 701.12)

Manual dampers shall NOT be installed in combustion air openings.

---

## Attic and Crawl Space as Combustion Air Sources

Ventilated attics and crawl spaces are considered **equivalent to outdoors** (CMC 701.6.1).

- Screens shall NOT be used on ducts terminating in attic (CMC 701.11(5))
- Crawl space below shall be used ONLY for lower combustion air, not upper (CMC 701.11(7))

---

## Quick Reference — Combustion Air Sizing

| Method | Opening Location | Sizing Rule |
|--------|-----------------|-------------|
| Indoor air only | N/A (room volume) | 50 cu ft per 1,000 BTU/h |
| Two openings, direct exterior | Top & bottom 12 in. | 1 sq in. per 4,000 BTU/h each |
| Two openings, horizontal ducts | Top & bottom 12 in. | 1 sq in. per 2,000 BTU/h each |
| Single opening, direct exterior | Top 12 in. | 1 sq in. per 3,000 BTU/h |
| Mechanical supply | N/A (fan) | 0.35 CFM per 1,000 BTU/h |
