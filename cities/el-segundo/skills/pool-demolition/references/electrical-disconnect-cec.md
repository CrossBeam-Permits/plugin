# CEC Article 680 — Electrical Disconnection for Pool Demolition

Source: 2025 California Electrical Code (Title 24, Part 3), Article 680 — Swimming Pools, Fountains, and Similar Installations.

When a pool or spa is demolished, all electrical systems serving the pool must be properly disconnected and removed. This reference covers the existing installation requirements that inform what must be properly decommissioned.

---

## Maintenance Disconnecting Means (680.13)

One or more means to simultaneously disconnect all ungrounded conductors shall be provided for all utilization equipment other than lighting. Each means shall be readily accessible and within sight from its equipment and shall be located not less than 1.5 m (5 ft) horizontally from the inside walls of a pool, spa, fountain, or hot tub unless separated from the open water by a permanently installed barrier that provides a 1.5 m (5 ft) reach path or greater.

> **Pool demo application**: The existing pool equipment disconnect is the starting point for decommissioning. The disconnect must be opened (de-energized) before any demolition work begins. The entire sub-panel and all related conductors feeding pool equipment must be removed back to the service supply connection point.

---

## Equipment Rooms and Drainage (680.12)

### (A) Drainage
Electrical equipment shall not be installed in rooms, vaults, or pits that do not have drainage that prevents water accumulation during normal operation or maintenance unless the equipment is rated and identified for submersion.

### (B) Receptacles
At least one GFCI-protected 125-volt, 15- or 20-ampere receptacle supplied from a general purpose branch circuit shall be located within an equipment room.

> **Pool demo application**: When removing pool equipment rooms/areas, any GFCI-protected receptacles that remain must still comply with code. If the equipment room/pad is being removed entirely, all wiring must be traced back and properly terminated.

---

## Corrosive Environments (680.14)

### (A) Wiring Methods
Wiring methods shall be suitable for use in corrosive environments. Rigid metal conduit, intermediate metal conduit, rigid polyvinyl chloride conduit, reinforced thermosetting resin conduit, and liquidtight flexible nonmetallic conduit shall be considered suitable for use. Aluminum conduit and tubing shall not be permitted.

### (B) Other Equipment
Other equipment shall be suitable for use in corrosive environments or be installed in identified corrosion-resistant enclosures.

> **Pool demo application**: Pool wiring often uses PVC conduit or liquidtight flexible nonmetallic conduit due to the corrosive (chlorine) environment. During demolition, all underground conduit and wiring serving pool equipment must be removed or properly abandoned. Conductors must be removed back to the service supply connection.

---

## Equipotential Bonding (680.26)

### (B)(1) Conductive Pool Shells
Pool shells with structural reinforcing steel must have the rebar bonded together. A copper conductor grid or copper ring may be used where rebar is encapsulated.

### (B)(2) Perimeter Surfaces
Equipotential bonding extends to perimeter surfaces within 3 feet of the pool edge.

### (B)(3) Metallic Components
All metallic parts of the pool structure, including reinforcing metal, shall be bonded.

> **Pool demo application**: The existing bonding system (typically #8 AWG solid copper bonding conductor connected to pool rebar, metal parts, and equipment) must be disconnected and removed as part of the demolition. The bonding conductor that connects to the pool shell rebar is typically severed when the bond beam is removed. All bonding connections at the sub-panel or equipment pad must be properly terminated.

---

## Decommissioning Requirements Summary

Based on CEC Article 680 and CBC 3303.6 (utility connections shall be discontinued and capped), pool electrical demolition requires:

1. **De-energize**: Open the disconnect means (680.13) and lock out/tag out before any work.
2. **Remove sub-panel**: The electrical sub-panel serving pool equipment must be removed entirely.
3. **Remove conductors**: All related electrical conductors must be removed back to the service supply connection point (the main panel or junction where the pool circuit originates).
4. **Remove bonding**: The equipotential bonding system (680.26) is removed with the pool structure.
5. **Cap at service**: The circuit breaker feeding the pool sub-panel should be removed from the main panel, and the wiring properly terminated or removed.
6. **Separate permit for reuse**: If any part of the existing pool electrical system is to be converted for other uses (e.g., landscape lighting, outdoor kitchen), a separate electrical permit is required.

> **Key principle**: The electrical system is removed, not just disconnected. Conductors go back to the service supply connection. This is more extensive than simply capping -- the entire pool electrical infrastructure is decommissioned.
