# Gas Water Heater Venting & Combustion Air

## 1. Appliance Venting Categories

Gas appliances are classified into four venting categories based on flue gas pressure and condensation behavior:

| Category | Flue Pressure | Condensation | Typical Residential Water Heater |
|----------|--------------|--------------|----------------------------------|
| I | Negative (natural draft) | Non-condensing | Standard atmospheric tank WH (40-gal, 50-gal with draft hood) |
| II | Negative | Condensing | Rare in residential |
| III | Positive | Non-condensing | Some power-vent tank WH |
| IV | Positive | Condensing | Tankless (Rinnai, Navien, etc.), condensing tank WH |

**Key rule:** Category I appliances use Type B gas vent, chimney, or single-wall metal pipe (CPC Table 509.4). Categories II-IV use venting as specified by the appliance manufacturer (CPC 509.4.1). Direct-vent appliances are installed per manufacturer instructions and bypass most venting rules (CPC 509.2.6).

## 2. Vent Types

### Type B Gas Vent (Double-Wall)
- Listed to UL 441 (CPC 509.1)
- **Required for:** Category I appliances in residential occupancies -- this is the standard for tank water heaters
- Double-wall construction: aluminum inner liner, galvanized steel outer
- Clearance to combustibles: as listed (typically 1 inch)
- Screws/rivets shall not penetrate the inner wall except at the transition from draft hood/flue collar (CPC 509.6(4))

### Single-Wall Metal Connector
- Galvanized sheet steel, min 0.0304 inch thick (CPC 509.7)
- **Single-wall metal pipe shall NOT be used as a vent in dwellings and residential occupancies** (CPC 509.7.3)
- May only be used as a **vent connector** (the horizontal run from appliance to vertical vent) in conditioned spaces
- Cannot pass through attics, inside walls, concealed spaces, or floors (CPC 509.7.3.2)
- Clearance to combustibles: 6 inches for listed appliances with draft hoods (Table 509.7.3.4(1))
- Cannot be used outdoors where 99% winter design temp is below 32F (CPC 509.7.1)

### Key Distinction
- **Vent connector** = horizontal run from appliance draft hood to vertical vent/chimney
- **Vent** = the vertical portion terminating through the roof
- Single-wall is allowed for connectors in conditioned space; Type B is required for the vertical vent in residential

## 3. Vent Connector Sizing Rules

### Single Appliance (CPC 509.6.2.1, 509.10.2)
- Connector size shall NOT be less than the draft hood outlet or flue collar diameter
- Connector size shall NOT exceed 7x the draft hood outlet area (CPC 509.5.5(2))
- For fan-assisted appliances, size per Section 510.0 tables

### Two Appliances Sharing a Common Vent (CPC 509.5.5(3))
- Common vent area >= area of larger draft hood outlet + 50% of smaller draft hood outlet area
- Common vent area shall NOT exceed 7x the smaller draft hood outlet area

### Other Connector Rules
- Manifolded draft hoods: min 1-foot connector rise (CPC 509.10.2.1)
- Connectors shall NOT be upsized more than 2 sizes above draft hood/flue collar (CPC 510.2.21)
- Corrugated connectors shall NOT be smaller than draft hood/flue collar (CPC 510.1.11)
- Vent downsizing below draft hood size allowed only when vent height >= 10 ft, reduction is one table size max for outlets <= 12 in, and outlet is > 4 in diameter (CPC 510.1.1)

## 4. Vent Termination Heights Above Roof

### Type B Gas Vent (12 inches or less, 8+ feet from wall) -- Table 509.6.1 / Table 802.6.1

| Roof Slope | Min Height Above Roof (ft) |
|-----------|---------------------------|
| Flat to 6/12 | 1.0 |
| Over 6/12 to 7/12 | 1.25 |
| Over 7/12 to 8/12 | 1.5 |
| Over 8/12 to 9/12 | 2.0 |
| Over 9/12 to 10/12 | 2.5 |
| Over 10/12 to 11/12 | 3.25 |
| Over 11/12 to 12/12 | 4.0 |
| Over 12/12 to 14/12 | 5.0 |
| Over 14/12 to 16/12 | 6.0 |

### Additional Termination Rules
- **Type B/L vent:** minimum 5 feet vertical above highest connected draft hood or flue collar (CPC 509.6.1(2))
- **Chimneys:** at least 3 feet above roof penetration AND 2 feet above any building surface within 10 feet horizontal (CPC 509.5.4)
- **Chimney height:** at least 5 feet above highest connected draft hood outlet (CPC 509.5.4.2)
- **Vents >12 inches or <8 feet from wall:** terminate at least 2 feet above roof penetration AND 2 feet above any building within 10 feet horizontal (CPC 509.6.1(1)(b))
- Vent must terminate at least 3 feet above any forced air inlet within 10 feet (CPC 509.6.1(7))
- All gas vents must terminate with a listed cap or roof assembly (CPC 509.6.1(6))

## 5. Critical Connector and Vent Rules

### No Reduction in Direction of Flow
- Vent connectors sized per tables shall not reduce in the direction of flow unless specific downsizing rules are met (CPC 510.1.1)

### Connector Slope
- Minimum 1/4 inch per foot upward slope toward the vent or chimney (CPC 509.10.7)
- No dips or sags permitted
- Exception: connectors attached to mechanical draft systems per manufacturer instructions

### Maximum Connector Length
- **Single-wall connector:** max horizontal length = 75% of chimney/vent height (CPC 509.10.8.1)
- **Type B double-wall connector:** max horizontal length = 100% of chimney/vent height (CPC 509.10.8.2)

### Connector Maximum Horizontal Length by Diameter (Table 510.2)
| Connector Diameter (in) | Max Horizontal Length (ft) |
|------------------------|--------------------------|
| 3 | 4.5 |
| 4 | 6 |
| 5 | 7.5 |
| 6 | 9 |
| 7 | 10.5 |
| 8 | 12 |

### Elbows and Joints
- Table lateral lengths already include two 90-degree elbows; each additional elbow up to 45 deg reduces max capacity by 5%, each 45-90 deg elbow by 10% (CPC 510.1.2)
- Joints: min 3 sheet-metal screws equally spaced, or listed vent material per manufacturer (CPC 509.10.5)

## 6. Combustion Air Requirements (CPC 506.0 / CPC 701.0 / CMC 701.0)

Combustion air rules appear in CPC 506, CPC 701, and CMC 701, which largely mirror each other. The CPC sections govern water heater installations specifically.

### General Rule (CPC 701.1 / CMC 701.1)
- Combustion air shall be provided in **all spaces containing Category I appliances** (most standard tank water heaters)
- Other appliances (not Category I) shall provide combustion air per manufacturer's instructions (CPC 701.1.1)
- Draft hood shall be in the **same room/space** as the appliance (CPC 701.2)

### Key Warning for Tightly Constructed Homes
Indoor air alone is NOT a reliable sole source of combustion air in modern, tightly constructed homes. The 0.40 ACH infiltration rate required for indoor air compliance is not directly comparable to energy code ACH requirements, and most homes have a lower rate than 0.40 ACH under passive conditions. **Outdoor openings are typically needed for combustion air.**

---

### Combustion Air Option Summary

| Option | Method | Sizing | Key Requirement |
|--------|--------|--------|-----------------|
| **1. Single Opening** | 1 opening in upper 12 in of exterior wall | Min 1 sq in / 3,000 BTU/h | Opening >= sum of vent connector areas; 1" sides/back, 6" front clearance (CPC 701.6.2) |
| **2. Two Exterior Openings** | 2 openings in exterior wall (upper + lower 12 in) | Min 1 sq in / 4,000 BTU/h each (direct) | Both openings direct to outdoors (CPC 701.6.1) |
| **3. All Indoor Air** | Room volume sufficient | Min 50 cu ft / 1,000 BTU/h | Infiltration >= 0.40 ACH required (CPC 701.4) |
| **4. Openings to Indoor Space** | Openings from enclosed space (upper + lower 12 in) | Min 1 sq in / 1,000 BTU/h, not less than 100 sq in | Connected space must have adequate volume (CPC 506.3) |
| **5. Crawl Space & Attic** | Openings to ventilated attic and crawl space | Min 1 sq in / 4,000 BTU/h each | Crawl space below cannot be upper air source (CPC 701.11(6)) |
| **6. Vertical Ducts to Attic** | 2 openings ducted to ventilated attic | Min 1 sq in / 4,000 BTU/h each, sleeved min 6 in above joist | Crawl space below cannot be upper air source |

### Horizontal Ducts (CPC 701.6.1)
When 2 horizontal ducts are used, sizing increases to **1 sq in per 2,000 BTU/h** each (double the vertical/direct rate).

### Indoor Air Source (CPC 701.4)
- Indoor air alone for combustion air is allowed **only if infiltration >= 0.40 ACH** (CPC 701.4)
- Minimum required air volume: **50 cu ft per 1,000 BTU/h** (CPC 701.4.1)
- Communicating rooms (openings without doors) count toward volume

### Openings (CPC 701.6)
- Minimum dimension of any air opening: **3 inches** (CPC 701.6)
- Screens with mesh >= 1/4 inch shall be provided for outside air openings (CPC 701.10.1)
- Metal louvers: assume **75% free area**; wood louvers: assume **25% free area** (CPC 701.10)
- Nonmotorized louvers/dampers shall be in **fixed, open position** (CPC 701.10)
- Motorized louvers shall be interlocked with appliance, fully open before ignition (CPC 701.10.2)

### Combustion Air Ducts (CPC 701.11 / CPC 506.9)
- Shall be **galvanized metal** or material of equivalent performance (CPC 701.11(1))
- Exterior openings shall be no less than **12 inches above finished grade** (CPC 701.11(8))
- Each duct shall serve only **one enclosure** or appliance space (CPC 701.11(3))
- Same duct shall NOT serve both upper and lower openings (CPC 701.11(4))
- Screens shall NOT be on ducts terminating in attic (CPC 701.11(5))
- Remaining space around chimney shall NOT be used for combustion air (CPC 701.11(7))
- Manual dampers or fire dampers ARE allowed in combustion air ducts (CMC 701.12)

### Attic and Crawl Space Sources (CPC 701.6.1)
- Ventilated attics and crawl spaces are considered **equivalent to outdoors** (CPC 701.6.1)
- Crawl space below shall be used **only for lower combustion air**, NOT upper (CPC 701.11(6))

## 6A. Vent Location & Protection (CPC 802.6 / CMC 802.6)

### General (CPC 802.3 / CMC 802.3)
- Venting system shall convey **all flue and vent gases to outdoors**
- Induced-draft furnaces shall be classified as "fan-assisted" Category I (CPC 803.1)
- Vents shall have testing agency listing and label, except single-wall metal and plastic for Category IV per manufacturer (CPC 802.1)

### Protection in Attic Spaces (CPC 802.6.1.1)
- Sheet metal shield shall extend to **2 inches above attic insulation**

### Screw Restriction (CPC 802.6(4))
- Screws into inner wall of double-wall vent **shall NOT be allowed** except at draft hood outlet or to single-wall connector

### Concealed Vent Protection (CMC 802.2.6)
- When less than 1.5 inches from face of framing members, protect with minimum **16-gauge steel shield plates** extending min 4 inches beyond framing inside wall

### Vent Shall Not Terminate Through (CPC 802.5.3)
- Fabricated ducts or plenums

### Vent Termination Requirements (CPC 802.6.2 / CMC 802.6.2)

| Requirement | Specification |
|-------------|---------------|
| **Type B/L vent min height** | 5 ft vertical above draft hood or flue collar (CPC 802.6.1) |
| **Roof penetration (vents <=12 in)** | Per Table 509.6.1 by roof slope (min 1 ft for flat to 6:12) |
| **Roof penetration (vents >12 in)** | Min 2 ft above roof (CPC 802.6.2(1B)) |
| **Forced air inlet clearance** | Min 3 ft above any forced air inlet within 10 ft (CPC 802.6.1(7)) |
| **Roof flashing** | Required at roof or wall vent penetration (CPC 802.6.1(6)) |
| **Listed cap** | All gas vents shall terminate with a listed cap or listed roof assembly (CPC 802.6.1(6)) |

## 7. Direct-Vent Appliances (Sealed Combustion)

Direct-vent (sealed combustion) appliances draw combustion air from outdoors and exhaust directly outdoors through a concentric or two-pipe system. They are exempt from standard venting and combustion air rules.

### Key Rules (CPC 509.2.6)
- Installed per manufacturer instructions -- no Section 509 venting rules apply
- **Combustion air provisions (Section 506) do not apply** to direct-vent appliances (CPC 506.1 Exception)
- Through-the-wall termination clearances per Table 509.8.1

### Through-the-Wall Termination Clearances (Table 509.8.1)
| Location | Direct Vent | Non-Direct Vent |
|----------|------------|----------------|
| Above grade/deck/porch | 12 in | 12 in |
| To openable window/door (<=50k BTU/hr) | 9 in | 4 ft below/to side, 1 ft above |
| To openable window/door (>50k to <=150k) | 12 in | 4 ft below/to side, 1 ft above |
| To mechanical air inlet | 10 ft horiz or 3 ft above | 10 ft horiz or 3 ft above |
| Above public sidewalk | 7 ft | 7 ft |
| Under veranda/porch/deck | 12 in (2 sides open) | 12 in (2 sides open) |

Wall penetrations for direct-vent pipes must be permanently sealed (CPC 509.8.2).

## 8. Common Residential Scenarios

### 40-Gallon Tank in Garage (Category I, ~40,000 BTU/hr)
- **Venting:** 4-inch Type B vent through roof; single-wall connector OK in garage for the horizontal run
- **Termination:** 1 ft above roof (flat to 6/12 slope); 5 ft min above draft hood
- **Combustion air:** Garage doors typically provide adequate infiltration; enclosed closet within garage needs two openings per 506.4.1
- **Elevation:** Burner/ignition 18 inches above floor unless FVIR-listed (CPC 507.13)
- **Connector slope:** 1/4 in/ft upward; **Seismic:** two straps, upper/lower 1/3 (CPC 507.2)

### 50-Gallon Tank in Interior Closet (~50,000 BTU/hr)
- **Venting:** Type B vent through roof
- **Combustion air (two-opening):** Each opening min 100 sq in (506.3 minimum), one high one low
- **Combustion air (single-opening):** Min 16.7 sq in (50k/3000), at top of closet, direct to outdoors
- **Clearances:** 30 in working space (CPC 507.25); draft hood in same room (CPC 506.1.2)

### Tankless Water Heater (Category IV, ~199,000 BTU/hr)
- **Venting:** Per manufacturer -- typically PVC/CPVC or stainless steel concentric vent
- **Direct-vent models:** Sealed combustion, no combustion air openings needed in room
- **Power-vent models (indoor air):** Need combustion air per 506.0
- **Termination:** Through-the-wall per Table 509.8.1 or through roof per manufacturer
- **Condensate drain:** Required for Category IV (CPC 509.9)
- **Wall termination:** Min 12 inches above grade; 9 inches from openable window (<=50k) or 12 inches (>50k to <=150k)
- **Category IV wall termination:** Min 10 feet horizontal from operable opening in adjacent building (CPC 509.8.3)
