# Guards and Handrails

Source: 2025 California Residential Code (Title 24, Part 2.5), Chapter 3 Sections R320 (Handrails) and R321 (Guards and Window Fall Protection), Chapter 5 Sections R507.10 (Exterior Guards) and R502.11 (Floor Framing Supporting Guards).

---

## Guards (Guardrails)

### Where Required (R321.1.1)

Guards are required at **open-sided walking surfaces** -- including floors, stairs, ramps, and landings -- where the surface is more than **30 inches** above the floor or grade below, measured vertically at any point within **36 inches** horizontally to the edge of the open side.

**Insect screening does NOT count as a guard.**

### Height Requirements (R321.1.2)

| Location | Minimum Guard Height |
|---|---|
| Deck, balcony, porch, landing | **42 inches** from walking surface |
| Open side of stairs (general) | **34 inches** from nosing line |
| Open side of stairs (top rail IS handrail) | **34 to 38 inches** from nosing line |

**Note:** The 2025 CRC increased the residential guard height from 36" to **42 inches**. This is a significant change from earlier code cycles. Some jurisdictions may still reference the previous 36" requirement for existing structures, but new construction and replacement must meet 42".

### Opening Limitations (R321.1.3)

| Location | Maximum Opening |
|---|---|
| Walking surface to guard height (general) | No passage of **4-inch diameter sphere** |
| Open side of stairs (general) | No passage of **4-3/8 inch diameter sphere** |
| Stair triangle (riser + tread + bottom rail) | No passage of **6-inch diameter sphere** |

The 4-inch sphere rule is a child safety standard -- it prevents a small child's head from passing through guard openings.

### Baluster Spacing Rule of Thumb

To meet the 4-inch sphere requirement:
- Maximum clear spacing between balusters: **less than 4 inches**
- In practice, typical baluster spacing is **3-1/2 inches** clear (using standard dimensional lumber)

## Exterior Deck Guards (R507.10)

### General

Guards on decks must meet both R321 (guard requirements) and R301.5 (structural loads) plus the additional provisions of R507.10.

### Guard Load Path (R507.10.1)

Where guards are supported on deck framing:
- Guard loads must be transferred to the deck framing with a **continuous load path to the deck joists**
- Guards connected to interior or exterior side of a joist or beam: the joist/beam must be connected to adjacent joists to **prevent rotation** (R507.10.1.1)
- Connections relying only on **fasteners in end grain withdrawal are NOT permitted**
- Guards mounted on top of decking must be connected to framing or blocking per manufacturer instructions (R507.10.1.2)

### Wood Post Rules (R507.10.2)

**4x4 wood posts supporting guard loads at the top of the guard shall NOT be notched** at the connection to the supporting structure.

This is a critical rule -- notching a 4x4 post at the deck frame weakens it precisely where the highest moment stress occurs. Use 4x6 or 6x6 posts, or use through-bolted connections without notching.

### Plastic Composite Guards (R507.10.3)

Must comply with R507.2.2:
- ASTM D7032 compliance
- Flame spread index <= 200 (ASTM E84 or UL 723)
- Labeled with max allowable span
- Decay and termite resistant per ASTM D7032

## Floor Framing Supporting Guards (R502.11)

For guard assemblies not exceeding 44 inches in height, the framing at the open edge of a floor must comply with one of:

### Option 1: Conventional Edge Framing (R502.11.1)

- Roll brace aligned with **each guard post**
- Edge member: solid or built-up lumber, glulam, or structural composite
  - Min **3 inches net width**
  - Min **9-1/4 inches net depth**

### Option 2: Timber Edge Framing (R502.11.2)

- Roll braces at **48 inches OC or less** (need NOT align with each guard post)
- Edge member: min **6x10 sawn timber** or min **5-1/8" x 9-1/4" glulam**

### Roll Bracing Details (R502.11.3)

Each roll brace must be:
- A joist or blocking matching the depth of the edge member
- Extending perpendicular to edge member min **16 inches** from edge
- Blocking end connections: min **six 16d common nails**
- Floor sheathing continuous min **24 inches** from edge
- Sheathing-to-roll-brace: min **twelve 10d common nails**
- Sheathing-to-edge-member: min **twelve 10d common nails** within 12 inches of roll brace

## Handrails (R320)

### Where Required (R318.7.8)

Handrails required on **at least one side** of each flight of stairs with **four or more risers**.

### Height (R320.2)

| Measurement | Requirement |
|---|---|
| From stair nosing line | **34 inches min, 38 inches max** |
| On alternating tread devices / ship's ladders | **30 inches min, 34 inches max** |

### Grip Size (R320.6)

#### Type I Handrails

| Shape | Dimension |
|---|---|
| Circular | OD: **1-1/4" to 2"** |
| Non-circular | Perimeter: **4" to 6-1/4"**, max cross-section: **2-1/4"** |
| Edge radius | Min **0.01 inch** |

#### Type II Handrails (perimeter > 6-1/4")

Must have graspable finger recess on both sides:
- Finger recess begins within **3/4 inch** from top of profile
- Recess depth: min **5/16 inch** within **7/8 inch** below widest point
- Continues min **3/8 inch** to level min **1-3/4 inches** below top
- Width above recess: **1-1/4" to 2-3/4"**

### Continuity (R320.5)

- Continuous for **full length of flight** (from landing nosing at top to lowest nosing at bottom)
- For ramps: continuous for **full length of ramp**
- End must be **returned continuously** to itself, a wall, guard, or walking surface
- Return gap to adjacent wall: max **1/4 inch**

**Exceptions:**
1. Continuity may be interrupted by a **newel post** at a turn in winders, at a landing, or over the lowest tread
2. A **volute, turnout, or starting easing** may terminate over the lowest tread

### Wall Clearance (R320.4)

Min **1-1/2 inches** between wall and handrail.

### Projection (R320.3)

Max **4-1/2 inches** projection on either side of stairway or ramp.

**Exception:** Where landing/floor nosings project into stairway reducing clearance at passing handrails, handrails may project up to 6-1/2 inches provided stair width and clearance are maintained.

### Plastic Composite Handrails (R320.7)

Exterior plastic composite handrails must also comply with R507.2.2 (ASTM D7032, flame spread, decay resistance).

## Common Review Findings

1. **Guard height at 36 inches instead of 42 inches** -- The 2025 CRC requires 42 inches. Plans showing 36" guards are a FAIL unless the project is a repair to an existing guard under the existing building provisions.
2. **Baluster spacing exceeds 4 inches** -- The most common guard deficiency. Verify that no opening allows passage of a 4-inch sphere. Horizontal rail systems are particularly prone to this error.
3. **4x4 posts notched at deck connection** -- Prohibited by R507.10.2. This is the most common structural deficiency in guard installations. The notch creates a stress concentration exactly where bending moment is highest.
4. **No roll bracing at guard posts** -- Edge framing supporting guards must be braced per R502.11. Without roll bracing, the edge member can rotate under guard loads, leading to guard failure.
5. **Handrail not graspable** -- A flat 2x4 cap rail is NOT a code-compliant handrail. The handrail must meet Type I or Type II grip size requirements. If the top of a guard serves as a handrail, it must be graspable.
6. **Handrail not returned** -- Handrail ends must be returned to themselves, a wall, guard, or walking surface. An exposed handrail end is a snag hazard and a code violation.
7. **Guard not required confusion** -- Guards are only required where the surface is >30" above grade. A deck that is 28" above grade at its highest point within 36" of the edge does NOT require guards -- but this must be verified by measurement, not assumption.
