# Weather-Resistive Barrier and Flashing Requirements

Source: 2025 California Residential Code (Title 24, Part 2.5), Sections R703.1.1, R703.2, R703.4, R703.7.3; California Building Code (Part 2), Sections 1402.2, 1403.2, 1404.4.

---

## Water-Resistive Barrier (WRB) -- General Requirements

### When Required

A WRB is required behind all exterior wall cladding on wood-framed and steel-framed walls (CRC R703.2, CBC 1403.2).

**Exceptions:**
- Concrete or masonry walls designed per Chapter 6/19/21 and flashed per R703.4 (CRC R703.1.1)
- Wall assemblies demonstrated to resist wind-driven rain per ASTM E331 testing (CRC R703.1.1)
- Unconditioned detached tool sheds, storage sheds, playhouses under ALL conditions: siding attached direct to studs, walls uninsulated, no interior covering (CRC R703.2 Exception)

### Acceptable WRB Materials (CRC R703.2, CBC 1403.2)

| Material | Standard | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| #15 asphalt felt | ASTM D226, Type 1 | Most common -- the "building paper" baseline |
| Housewrap | ASTM E2556, Type 1 or 2 | Synthetic WRB products (Tyvek, etc.) |
| Foam plastic insulating sheathing | Per R703.1.1 | With joints sealed per manufacturer |
| ASTM E331 tested system | Per R703.1.1 | Proprietary wall assemblies |
| Other approved materials | Per manufacturer | Installed per manufacturer instructions |

### Installation Requirements

- Applied horizontally over studs or sheathing
- Upper layer lapped over lower layer **minimum 2 inches**
- Where joints occur, lapped **minimum 6 inches**
- Must be **continuous to the top of walls**
- Must be terminated at penetrations and building appendages to maintain continuity
- Where WRB also functions as air barrier, installed per California Energy Code

## WRB for Stucco (Exterior Plaster) -- Special Requirements

Stucco has stricter WRB requirements because the cladding absorbs water. Requirements depend on climate zone (CRC R703.7.3).

### Dry Climates (Most of Southern California) -- R703.7.3.1

Option 1: **Two layers of 10-minute Grade D paper** (or equivalent ASTM E2556 Type I)
- Each layer installed independently as a separate continuous plane
- Flashing drains BETWEEN the two layers

Option 2: **One layer of 60-minute Grade D paper** (or equivalent ASTM E2556 Type II)
- Must be SEPARATED from stucco by:
  - Foam plastic insulating sheathing, OR
  - Non-water-absorbing layer, OR
  - Drainage space or means of drainage per R703.7.3.2
- Flashing drains to EXTERIOR side of WRB

### Moist/Marine Climates -- R703.7.3.2

In IECC Climate Zones 3 (marine) and 4 (marine) -- includes parts of coastal Northern California:
- Must comply with dry climate requirements PLUS:
  - Add drainage space or material min 3/16" depth on exterior side of WRB, OR
  - Drainage efficiency min 90% per ASTM E2273

### California Climate Zone Mapping for Stucco WRB

Most of Southern California (CZ 6-10, 14-15) falls in IECC Zone 3 (non-marine), so the **dry climate** requirements apply. Coastal areas from San Luis Obispo north may fall in IECC Zone 3 (marine) requiring the drainage enhancement.

## Flashing -- Required Locations (CRC R703.4)

Approved corrosion-resistant flashing is required at ALL of the following locations:

1. **Exterior window and door openings** -- per R703.4.1
2. **Chimney/masonry intersections with frame or stucco walls** -- with projecting lips on both sides under stucco copings
3. **Under and at ends of masonry, wood, or metal copings and sills**
4. **Continuously above all projecting wood trim**
5. **Exterior porches, decks, or stairs** attached to wall or floor assembly
6. **Wall and roof intersections**
7. **Built-in gutters**

### Flashing at Windows and Doors (CRC R703.4.1)

Flashing must extend to the surface of the exterior wall finish or to the WRB for subsequent drainage. Installation methods (in order of preference):

1. Per fenestration manufacturer's installation and flashing instructions
2. Per flashing or WRB manufacturer's instructions
3. Per registered design professional's flashing design
4. Other approved methods

**Where instructions not provided:** Pan flashing at sill of openings is required:
- Sealed or sloped to direct water outward
- Head and side protection also required

**Self-adhered membrane flashings:** Must comply with AAMA 711
**Fluid-applied membrane flashings:** Must comply with AAMA 714
**Mechanically attached flexible flashings:** Must comply with AAMA 712

### Air Sealing at Openings

Air sealing must be installed around ALL window and door openings on the **interior side** of the rough opening gap (R703.4.1).

## Flashing Materials

| Material | Min Thickness | Standard |
|---|---|---|
| Galvanized steel | 0.019" (26 gage) | -- |
| Aluminum | 0.024" | -- |
| Copper | 0.0216" (16 oz/sq ft) | ASTM B370 |
| Stainless steel | 28 gage | -- |
| Self-adhered membrane | Per manufacturer | AAMA 711 |
| Fluid-applied membrane | Per manufacturer | AAMA 714 |

Flashing must be:
- **Corrosion-resistant** (R703.4)
- Applied in **shingle fashion** (overlapped with upper over lower)
- Extended to surface of exterior wall finish

## Flashing at Stucco -- Weep Screed (CRC R703.7.2.1)

For stucco (exterior plaster) installations, a weep screed is mandatory:

- Min 0.019" (No. 26 galvanized sheet gage) corrosion-resistant metal or plastic
- Min 3-1/2" vertical attachment flange
- Placed at or below the foundation plate line
- Not less than **4 inches above earth** or **2 inches above paved areas**
- WRB must lap the attachment flange
- Exterior lath must cover and terminate on the attachment flange
- Must allow trapped water to drain to exterior

## Drainage Path Design

The exterior wall assembly must be designed to prevent water accumulation within the assembly (CRC R703.1.1, CBC 1402.2):

1. WRB behind exterior cladding creates first line of defense
2. Flashing at all penetrations and intersections redirects water outward
3. Means of draining water that enters the assembly to exterior

### Key Drainage Principles for Siding Replacement

- **Lap siding** (wood, fiber cement, vinyl): naturally sheds water by overlap -- WRB behind catches any penetrating moisture
- **Panel siding**: vertical joints must be over framing and sealed (caulk, batten, or shiplap)
- **Stucco**: requires deliberate drainage layer or dual WRB
- **Masonry veneer**: requires 1" min airspace between veneer and sheathing, with weepholes at bottom (max 33" OC)
- **All materials**: flashing at transitions (wall/roof, wall/deck, wall/foundation) directs water outward

## Critical Siding Replacement Considerations

When replacing siding, the condition of the existing WRB and flashing must be evaluated:

1. **If WRB is damaged, deteriorated, or missing:** Must be replaced before new siding installation
2. **If WRB does not exist (older homes):** Must be installed per current code
3. **If changing siding type:** WRB requirements may change (e.g., wood to stucco requires two-layer or 60-minute WRB)
4. **Window/door flashing:** Must be inspected and replaced if damaged -- this is the most common source of moisture intrusion
5. **Kick-out flashing:** Required at wall/roof eave intersections to prevent water from running behind siding
6. **Z-flashing:** Required at horizontal joints in panel siding where not shiplapped
