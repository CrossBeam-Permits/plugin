# Roof Framing & Span Tables

Source: 2025 California Residential Code (Title 24, Part 2.5), Chapter 8 (Roof-Ceiling Construction), Appendix H (Patio Covers), and California Building Code (Title 24, Part 2) Section 1507 (Roof Assemblies).

---

## Design Loads for Patio Covers

### Open (Lattice) Patio Covers

| Load Type | Value | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Live load | 10 psf | CRC Appendix H |
| Dead load | 2-5 psf (actual materials) | Per design |
| Wind load | Per CRC R301.2.1 | Risk Category II typical |
| Seismic load | Per CRC R301.2.2 | SDC D typical in SoCal |

### Solid Roof Patio Covers

| Load Type | Value | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Live load | 20 psf | CRC R301.6 |
| Dead load — light assembly | 5-10 psf | Shingles/cap sheet, no stucco ceiling |
| Dead load — heavy assembly | 10-15 psf | Concrete tile, or lightweight w/ stucco ceiling |
| Wind load | Per CRC R301.2.1 | Risk Category II typical |
| Seismic load | Per CRC R301.2.2 | SDC D typical in SoCal |

## Allowable Rafter Spans

Rafter spans are measured **horizontally** from the face of the ledger (or house wall) to the center of the supporting beam. All values assume Douglas Fir-Larch No. 2 or better.

### Freestanding (N.F.Z. — No Frost Zone) Rafter Spans

These spans apply to both lattice and solid roof covers when rafters are **not** attached to the house (freestanding with beams on both ends).

| Rafter Size | Spacing 24" OC | Spacing 32" OC | Spacing 48" OC |
|---|---|---|---|
| 4x4 | 11'-0" | 10'-0" | 8'-6" |
| 4x6 | 17'-4" | 15'-8" | 13'-8" |
| 4x8 | 20'-0" | 20'-0" | 18'-0" |

### Attached (F.Z. — Frost Zone / Ledger-Connected) Rafter Spans

These spans apply when one end of the rafter is supported by a ledger attached to the house.

| Rafter Size | Spacing 16" OC | Spacing 24" OC | Spacing 32" OC | Spacing 48" OC |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2x4 | 9'-6" | 8'-4" (F.Z.) | 7'-3" | — |
| 2x6 | 13'-1" (16") | 11'-4" (24") | 10'-0" (32") | — |
| 2x8 | 17'-4" (16") | 15'-0" (24") | 13'-0" (32") | — |

> **Maximum rafter span for solid roof patio cover: 12 feet** per OC standard plans. Spans exceeding 12 feet require engineering or deeper rafters.

### Rafter Overhang

| Dimension | Limit |
|---|---|
| Maximum rafter overhang (past beam) | 6 times the rafter depth, maximum 48 inches |
| Maximum overhang (past beam, alternate rule) | Backspan / 5 |

## Beam Span Tables

Beam spans are measured between **post centers**. All values assume Douglas Fir-Larch No. 2 or better. Spans are listed for rafter spans up to 12 feet and up to 20 feet.

### Sawn Lumber Beams

| Beam Size | Rafter Spans up to 12' | Rafter Spans up to 20' |
|---|---|---|
| 4x4 | 6'-0" | 5'-0" |
| 4x6 | 9'-6" | 8'-0" |
| 4x8 | 12'-0" | 10'-6" |
| 4x10 | 16'-0" | 13'-0" |
| 4x12 | 19'-0" | 16'-0" |
| 4x14 | 22'-0" | 18'-0" |

### Built-Up / Glulam Beams

| Beam Size | Rafter Spans up to 12' | Rafter Spans up to 20' |
|---|---|---|
| 6x8 #1 | 16'-0" | 14'-0" |
| 6x10 #1 | 22'-6" | 18'-0" |
| 6x12 #1 | 25'-0" | 22'-0" |

> **Note:** "#1" designation indicates No. 1 grade or better, which has higher allowable stresses than No. 2. Built-up beams (two or more plies nailed together) may use No. 2 grade per ply if total section modulus matches the table requirements.

## Rafter-to-Ledger Connection (Attached Covers)

| Component | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Ledger size | Continuous 2x, depth matching rafter depth (e.g., 2x6 ledger for 2x6 rafters) |
| Framing clips | 18 gauge framing clip each rafter with 3-8d nails each leg |
| Approved hanger | Joist hanger sized for rafter (alternative to framing clip) |

---

## Rafter-to-Beam Connection

| Component | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Toenails | 3-8d toenails per rafter to beam AND at blocking to beam |
| Blocking | 2" solid blocking between 2x rafters (may be omitted with 4x rafters) |
| Framing clips | 18 gauge framing clip each rafter with 3-8d nails each leg (rafter to beam) |
| Hurricane ties | Recommended in high-wind areas (Simpson H2.5 or equivalent) |

## Roof Sheathing (Solid Covers Only)

Roof sheathing is required for solid-roof patio covers. The sheathing must span continuously over 2 or more rafters, with face grain perpendicular to rafters.

| Sheathing Type | Span Rating | Max Rafter Spacing | Nailing |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1/2" Structural I plywood or OSB | 24/0 | 24" OC | 8d common |
| 5/8" Structural I plywood or OSB | 40/20 | 32" OC | 10d common |
| 3/4" Structural I plywood or OSB | 48/24 | 36" OC | 10d common |
| 1-1/8" Structural I plywood or OSB | 60/48 | 48" OC | 10d common |
| 1x nominal lumber | — | 24" OC | 2-10d at each lap |

**Nailing schedule:** 6 inches on center at edges and 12 inches on center in field.

---

## Roof Slope Requirements (Solid Covers Only)

| Roof Covering | Minimum Slope |
|---|---|
| Cap sheet / built-up roof | 1/4" per 12" (1/4:12) |
| Asphalt shingles | 4" per 12" (4:12) minimum; 2:12 with 2 layers 15# felt |
| Concrete or clay tile | Per ICC-ES report for specific product |

---

## Roofing Material Requirements (Solid Covers — CBC 1507)

| Requirement | Standard |
|---|---|
| Fire rating | Class B or better fire retardant (per CRC R902.1 / CBC 1505.1) |
| Tile weight | Concrete tile: max 10 psf (classified as "heavy" assembly) |
| Roof tile | NOT permitted on standard-plan patio covers per some jurisdictions (verify with city) |
| Flashing at house wall | Metal flashing and caulking bead at ledger/wall junction |
| Drip edge | Required at eaves and rakes |

> **Important:** The OC standard plan notes that "roof tile is not permitted" on standard roofed patio covers. Concrete or clay tile may require engineering due to increased dead load and seismic mass.

| Component | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Fascia board | Optional but recommended for solid covers |
| Attachment | Nailed to rafter tails |
| Material | Same species/treatment as rafters if exposed to weather |

## Boundary Nailing (Solid Covers)

Where the patio cover roof sheathing meets the house wall:
- Boundary nails at **6 inches on center** to 2x blocking at ledger
- Roof covering laps over flashing at wall junction

## Common Review Findings

1. **Rafter span exceeds table** — The most common error. A 2x4 rafter at 24" OC can only span 8'-4". Many plans show 2x4 rafters at 10' or 12' spans — FAIL, upsize to 2x6 or reduce spacing.
2. **Beam undersized for span** — A 4x6 beam spans only 9'-6" with rafters up to 12'. Wider patio covers need 4x8, 4x10, or larger beams.
3. **Missing blocking between rafters** — 2x rafters require solid blocking at the beam. Only 4x rafters may omit blocking.
4. **No sheathing nailing schedule** — Solid covers must specify 6" edge / 12" field nailing. Plans that omit this are incomplete.
5. **Roof slope too low for shingle type** — Asphalt shingles require 4:12 minimum slope (or 2:12 with double underlayment). Cap sheet/built-up is the only option at 1/4:12.
6. **Missing flashing at house wall** — Where solid roof meets the house, flashing and caulking bead are mandatory to prevent water intrusion into the wall cavity.
