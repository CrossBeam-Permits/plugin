# Clearances, Location & Access Requirements

Source: 2025 California Mechanical Code (Title 24, Part 4), Chapters 3 and 9; 2025 California Plumbing Code (Title 24, Part 5), Chapter 5.

---

## Clearances to Combustible Materials (CMC 303.10)

Appliances and vent connectors shall be installed with clearances from combustible material so their operation does not create a hazard.

**Listed appliances**: Install per listing and manufacturer's instructions. The listing clearances govern and shall not be reduced beyond what the listing allows.

**Unlisted appliances**: Minimum clearances are 6 inches on sides and back. Refer to the manufacturer's installation instructions and CMC Chapter 8 / Chapter 9 for specific appliance types.

### Reduced Clearances (CMC Table 303.10.1)

Clearances may be reduced when approved protection is applied to the combustible surface. Common reduction methods include:

| Protection Method | Typical Reduction |
|-------------------|-------------------|
| 1/4" noncombustible board + 1" ventilated air space | Up to 2/3 reduction |
| 24-gauge sheet metal + 1" ventilated air space | Up to 2/3 reduction |
| 3.5" thick masonry wall (no air space) | Up to 1/3 reduction |

**Key rules for reduced clearances**:
- Protection must extend far enough in each direction so that the unprotected distance equals the original required clearance
- At least 1 inch air gap between the protection system and the combustible wall
- Bottom, top, or side edges must be left open for ventilation
- Reduction shall not interfere with combustion air, draft hood clearance, or servicing access

## Flammable Storage Prohibition (CPC 507.12)

Appliances shall **not** be installed in areas where the open use, handling, or dispensing of flammable liquids occurs, unless the design, operation, or installation reduces the potential of ignition of the flammable vapors.

---

## Ground Support (CPC 507.4)

A water heater supported from the earth shall rest on **level concrete or other approved base extending not less than 3 inches above the adjoining ground level**.

---

## Garage Installation (CMC 305.1)

Appliances in residential garages (and adjacent spaces opening to the garage that are not part of living space) shall have all burners, burner-ignition devices, heating elements, and switches located **not less than 18 inches above the floor**.

**Exception**: Listed **FVIR (Flammable Vapor Ignition Resistant)** appliances are exempt from the 18-inch elevation requirement. Most water heaters manufactured after July 2003 are FVIR-compliant.

**Physical protection (CMC 305.1.1)**: Garage-installed appliances shall be guarded against vehicle damage by protective barriers, elevation, or location out of the normal path of vehicles.

**Separate enclosure (CMC 305.1.2)**: Where the appliance is in a separate enclosed space with access only from outside the garage, it may be installed at floor level provided combustion air is taken from the exterior of the garage.

---

## Bedroom and Bathroom Location (CPC 504.1)

Fuel-burning water heaters installed in bedrooms or bathrooms must comply with one of the following:

1. **Direct-vent type** -- sealed combustion, no air drawn from the room
2. **Enclosed in a dedicated closet** meeting all of the following:
   - Listed, gasketed door assembly
   - Listed self-closing device (no hold-open feature)
   - Threshold and bottom door seal
   - Combustion air obtained from outdoors (per CPC 506.4)
   - Closet used exclusively for the water heater

> Electric water heaters do not have these restrictions since they produce no combustion byproducts.

---

## Working Space and Servicing Access (CMC 304)

Unless otherwise specified, maintain a minimum **30 inches in depth, width, and height** of clear working space around the service side of the appliance (CMC 304.1). On roofs with slope 4:12 or greater, provide a level **30" x 30"** platform at the service side (CMC 304.2).

---

## Attic, Under-Floor, and Roof Installations (CMC 304.4 / CPC 508)

### Access Opening

The access opening and passageway must be larger than the largest component of the appliance, and not less than **22 inches x 30 inches**.

### Passageway Requirements

| Requirement | Specification |
|-------------|---------------|
| **Passageway width** | Min. 24 inches, unobstructed, solid flooring |
| **Max distance to appliance** (height < 6 ft) | 20 feet from access opening |
| **Max distance to appliance** (height >= 6 ft) | 50 feet from access opening |

### Work Platform (CMC 304.4.3 / CPC 508.4.3)

A level working platform not less than **30 inches x 30 inches** shall be provided in front of the service side of the appliance.

**Exception (CMC only)**: A platform is not required if the appliance can be serviced from the access opening and the service side is within 12 inches of the opening.

### Lighting and Receptacle (CMC 304.4.4 / CPC 508.4.4)

- A permanent **120V receptacle outlet** and a **luminaire** shall be installed near the appliance
- The **light switch** shall be located at the entrance to the passageway

### Roof Access (CMC 304.3)

Buildings over 15 feet in height must have an inside means of access to the roof (permanent or foldaway stairway/ladder). Scuttles or trapdoors shall be at least **22 inches x 24 inches**. At least **6 feet** of clearance between the access opening and edge of roof (or install 42-inch guards).

Permanent lighting required at roof access, with the switch inside the building.

---

## Drainage Pan (CMC 305.5)

Required when a water heater is installed in an attic, on a ceiling assembly, floor-ceiling assembly, floor-subfloor assembly, or any location where leakage could cause damage.

- Pan material: corrosion-resistant
- Drain pipe: min. 3/4 inch diameter to an approved, readily visible location
- Pan depth: min. 1-1/2 inches
- Drain pipe material rated for the water heater temperature
- **Relief valve discharge into the drainage pan is prohibited**

---

## Closet Installations — Combustion Air

Fuel-burning water heaters in a closet or confined space require combustion air per CPC 506 / CMC 701:

- **Indoor air**: Two permanent openings to an adequate-volume space -- one within 12" of top, one within 12" of bottom
- **Outdoor air**: Two permanent openings to outdoors -- min. free area of 1 sq. in. per 4,000 BTU/h (direct) or 1 sq. in. per 2,000 BTU/h (ducted)
- A sealed closet with no combustion air openings is only acceptable for direct-vent or electric water heaters

---

## Review Checklist

For any water heater installation, verify:

- [ ] Clearances to combustibles per listing or code minimums
- [ ] Garage: 18" elevation or FVIR-compliant unit, vehicle protection
- [ ] Bedroom/bathroom: direct-vent or sealed closet with gasketed self-closing door
- [ ] Working space: 30" x 30" platform at service side
- [ ] Attic/under-floor: access opening, walkway, platform, light, switch, receptacle
- [ ] Drainage pan where required (attic, floor assembly, damage-prone locations)
- [ ] Combustion air provided for closet/confined-space installations
