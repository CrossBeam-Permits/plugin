# Retaining Wall Materials and Construction Methods

Source: 2025 California Building Code (Title 24, Part 2), Chapter 19 — Concrete, Chapter 21 — Masonry; ACI 318-19 (concrete design); TMS 402/602 (masonry design); ASTM standards.

---

## Material Comparison

| Material | Typical Height | Advantages | Disadvantages | Engineering Required? |
|----------|---------------|------------|---------------|----------------------|
| Poured concrete (cantilever) | 4-12+ ft | Strongest, most versatile, monolithic | Formwork cost, curing time | Yes (>4 ft) |
| CMU / concrete block | 4-8 ft | No formwork, familiar to masons | Requires grouting and rebar, slower | Yes (>4 ft) |
| Segmental (SRW blocks) | Up to 15-20 ft | No concrete/mortar, fast, attractive | Requires geogrid for height, limited structural capacity | Yes (>4 ft) |
| Timber | Up to 4 ft | Low cost, easy to build, natural look | Short lifespan (15-20 yr), limited height | No (if <=4 ft, no surcharge) |
| Gabion (rock-filled wire baskets) | Up to 10 ft | Permeable (self-draining), natural look | Large footprint, aesthetic limitations | Yes (>4 ft) |
| Soldier pile + lagging | Any height | Good for deep cuts, temporary or permanent | Expensive, specialty contractor | Always |

## Poured-in-Place Concrete

### Concrete Specifications

| Property | Requirement | Code Section |
|----------|-------------|--------------|
| Minimum compressive strength (f'c) | 2,500 psi (3,000 psi typical for retaining walls) | CBC 1904.2.1 |
| Maximum water-cement ratio | 0.45 for exposure to deicing chemicals; 0.50 for moderate exposure | ACI 318 Table 19.3.2.1 |
| Air entrainment | 4-7% for freeze-thaw exposure (mountain areas) | ACI 318 Table 19.3.3.1 |
| Slump | 4-6 inches (typical for wall pours) | ACI 318 |
| Cement type | Type II or V for sulfate exposure (common in Southern CA soils) | ACI 318 Table 19.3.2.1 |
| Cover over reinforcement | 2" (cast against earth); 1.5" (exposed to weather); 3/4" (not exposed) | ACI 318 Table 20.6.1.3.1 |

### Reinforcement Specifications

| Property | Requirement | Code Section |
|----------|-------------|--------------|
| Rebar grade | ASTM A615 or A706, Grade 60 (fy = 60 ksi) | ACI 318 |
| Minimum stem vertical rebar | Per structural analysis; #4 @ 12" OC minimum for walls >4 ft | ACI 318 |
| Minimum stem horizontal rebar | #4 @ 18" OC (temperature/shrinkage) | ACI 318 7.6.1 |
| Lap splice length | Per ACI 318 Chapter 25; minimum Class B: 1.3 x development length | ACI 318 25.5 |
| Typical lap splice (#4 bar) | 24" minimum (Class B splice, normal weight concrete, f'c = 3,000 psi) | ACI 318 25.5 |
| Typical lap splice (#5 bar) | 30" minimum | ACI 318 25.5 |
| Dowels (footing to stem) | Match vertical bar size and spacing; extend into footing per development length | ACI 318 |
| Hook (standard 90-degree) | #4: 8" extension; #5: 10" extension | ACI 318 25.3.1 |

### Construction Sequence — Poured Concrete

1. **Excavate** to footing depth; verify bearing soil with geotechnical engineer
2. **Install forms** for footing; place rebar per plans (chairs for bottom mat, tie bars)
3. **Place footing concrete**; vibrate to consolidate; leave roughened top surface and projecting dowels for stem
4. **Cure footing** minimum 7 days before loading
5. **Install stem forms** — one side at a time for access to rebar; place stem vertical and horizontal rebar, tie to dowels
6. **Install drain pipe** and begin drain rock placement behind forms
7. **Pour stem concrete** in one lift if <=8 ft; maximum pour height per ACI 318 without cold joint
8. **Strip forms** after minimum 24 hours (longer in cold weather)
9. **Apply waterproofing/dampproofing** to soil side if required
10. **Complete drain rock** and filter fabric installation
11. **Backfill** in 8" loose lifts, compacting each lift (light compaction near wall)
12. **Install guard/fence** at top if required
13. **Grade surface** away from wall at 2% minimum

## Concrete Masonry Units (CMU)

### Material Specifications

| Material | Standard | Requirement |
|----------|----------|-------------|
| CMU blocks | ASTM C90 | Hollow load-bearing units, Grade N (moisture-controlled) |
| Minimum block compressive strength | ASTM C90 | 1,900 psi (net area average) |
| Block weight | ASTM C90 | Normal weight (125+ pcf) preferred for retaining walls |
| Mortar | ASTM C270 | Type M (2,500 psi) for ground contact; Type S (1,800 psi) for above grade |
| Grout | ASTM C476 | f'g >= 2,000 psi (fine or coarse grout) |
| Reinforcement | ASTM A615/A706 | Grade 60 rebar |

### Wall Thickness

| Retaining Height | Minimum Block Width | Notes |
|-----------------|--------------------|----|
| Up to 4 ft | 8" CMU (7-5/8" actual) | Prescriptive OK for no surcharge |
| 4-6 ft | 8" CMU | Engineered design required |
| 6-8 ft | 10" or 12" CMU | Engineered design; 8" may work with closely spaced rebar |
| Over 8 ft | 12" CMU or pilasters + 8" infill | Engineered design |

### Reinforcement for CMU Retaining Walls

| Component | <=4 ft (prescriptive) | 4-6 ft (typical engineered) | 6-8 ft (typical engineered) |
|-----------|----------------------|----------------------------|----------------------------|
| Vertical rebar | #4 @ 24" OC | #4 @ 16" OC or #5 @ 24" OC | #5 @ 16" OC or #6 @ 24" OC |
| Horizontal rebar | #4 @ bond beam at top + every 24" OC | #4 @ every 16"-24" OC | #4 @ every 16" OC |
| Bond beams | Top course minimum | Top + mid-height minimum | Top + every 32" OC minimum |
| All rebar cells | Fully grouted | Fully grouted | Fully grouted |

### CMU Construction Requirements

| Requirement | Specification | Code Section |
|-------------|---------------|--------------|
| Running bond | Required (half-bond overlap) | TMS 402 |
| Mortar joints | 3/8" typical; tool/compress for weather resistance | TMS 402 |
| Grouting | All cells with rebar fully grouted; pour in lifts <=4 ft; consolidate by rodding or vibration | TMS 402 |
| Cleanout openings | Required at base of cells to be grouted >5 ft (remove mortar droppings before grouting) | TMS 402 |
| Lap splices | 48 bar diameters minimum (#4 = 24", #5 = 30", #6 = 36") | TMS 402 |
| Rebar positioning | Center in cell; minimum 5/8" clear from cell wall | TMS 402 |
| Control joints | Maximum 20-25 ft spacing; at changes in height, thickness, or direction | TMS 402 |
| Expansion joints | As needed for thermal movement | TMS 402 |

### Footing for CMU Retaining Walls

| Wall Height | Footing Width | Footing Depth | Dowels | Footing Rebar |
|-------------|---------------|---------------|--------|---------------|
| Up to 3 ft | 18"-24" | 12" | #4 @ 24" OC, match wall verticals | 2-#4 continuous, top and bottom |
| 3-4 ft | 24"-30" | 12" | #4 @ 24" OC | 2-#4 continuous, top and bottom |
| 4-6 ft | 30"-36" | 12"-18" | Per engineered design | Per engineered design |
| 6-8 ft | 36"-48" | 18"-24" | Per engineered design | Per engineered design |

### CMU Construction Sequence

1. **Excavate and prepare** footing subgrade
2. **Form and pour footing** with dowels projecting at vertical rebar spacing
3. **Cure footing** minimum 24 hours before laying block
4. **Lay first course** of CMU on mortar bed; level carefully (sets alignment for entire wall)
5. **Build wall** in running bond; install horizontal rebar in bond beam courses
6. **Grout cells** with rebar in lifts not exceeding 4 ft; rod or vibrate to consolidate
7. **Install drainage** (drain rock, pipe, filter fabric) behind wall as courses rise
8. **Apply dampproofing** to soil side
9. **Backfill** in 8" lifts with light compaction
10. **Install cap** (solid cap block, poured concrete cap, or stone cap)

## Segmental Retaining Walls (SRW)

### Block Specifications (ASTM C1372)

| Property | Requirement |
|----------|-------------|
| Material standard | ASTM C1372 — Standard Specification for Segmental Retaining Wall Units |
| Minimum compressive strength | 3,000 psi average (3 units); 2,500 psi individual unit |
| Maximum water absorption | 10 lb/ft^3 average; 13 lb/ft^3 individual |
| Freeze-thaw durability | Must pass 100 cycles (weight loss <1%) — or 150 cycles for severe exposure |
| Common brands | Allan Block, Keystone, Versa-Lok, Risi Stone, Belgard |

### SRW Construction Components

| Component | Specification |
|-----------|---------------|
| Leveling pad | 6" thick compacted crushed stone (3/4" minus), 24" wide minimum, extending 6" beyond front and back of block |
| Base course | First course of blocks set on leveling pad; critical for alignment — must be level |
| Batter (setback) | Typically 3/4" to 1-1/4" per course (manufacturer-specific); provides stability through self-weight |
| Geogrid | High-strength polyester or polypropylene grid; placed between courses at specified elevations |
| Geogrid embedment | Extends behind block face into backfill zone; minimum length per manufacturer/engineer |
| Drain rock | 12" minimum behind blocks, full height |
| Perforated pipe | 4" minimum at base, behind first course |
| Cap units | Adhesive-bonded cap blocks on top course |
| Backfill over geogrid | Granular fill (not clay) within reinforced zone |

### Geogrid Reinforcement Schedule

| Wall Height | Number of Geogrid Layers | Typical Spacing | Minimum Embedment |
|-------------|--------------------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Up to 3 ft | 0 (gravity wall) | N/A | N/A |
| 3-4 ft | 1 | At mid-height | 4 ft min |
| 4-6 ft | 1-2 | Every 16"-24" | 4-6 ft min |
| 6-8 ft | 2-3 | Every 16"-24" | 6-8 ft min |
| 8-12 ft | 3-5 | Every 16"-20" | 8-10 ft min |
| Over 12 ft | Per engineered design | Per engineer | Per engineer |

*Geogrid type, strength, and spacing per manufacturer design tables or site-specific structural engineering. Most SRW manufacturers provide free design software.*

### SRW Construction Sequence

1. **Excavate** trench for leveling pad + first course (typically 12-18" below lower grade)
2. **Install and compact leveling pad** — 6" compacted crushed stone, level within 1/8" over 10 ft
3. **Place first course** of blocks on leveling pad; check level and alignment
4. **Install perforated drain pipe** behind first course on top of 4" drain rock bed
5. **Place drain rock** behind blocks (12" min width)
6. **Place and compact backfill** in 8" lifts behind drain rock zone
7. **Stack subsequent courses** with batter (setback); check level each course
8. **Install geogrid** at specified course heights — pull taut, stake, and backfill over grid
9. **Continue drain rock and backfill** with each course
10. **Install filter fabric** between drain rock and native soil at each lift
11. **Set cap blocks** with adhesive
12. **Grade surface** away from wall at top

## Timber Retaining Walls

### When Timber Is Appropriate

Timber retaining walls are limited to:
- Maximum height: 4 ft (to remain within permit-exempt threshold)
- Level backfill only (no surcharge)
- Non-critical applications (landscape walls, garden terraces)
- Not supporting structures, driveways, or utilities

### Material Requirements

| Material | Specification | Code Section |
|----------|---------------|--------------|
| Lumber treatment | Pressure-treated per AWPA U1, Use Category 4B (ground contact, heavy duty) | CRC R304.4 |
| Treatment chemical | ACQ, CA-B, or CCA (CCA restricted to non-residential per EPA) | AWPA U1 |
| Timber grade | #2 or better Southern Yellow Pine, Douglas Fir, or Hem-Fir | NDS |
| Hardware | Hot-dip galvanized or stainless steel (no electro-galvanized near treated wood) | Industry practice |
| Landscape timbers | 6x6 or 6x8 minimum for structural walls | Industry practice |
| Railroad ties | Not recommended — creosote environmental concerns; verify local regulations | Local code |

### Typical Timber Wall Construction

| Component | Specification |
|-----------|---------------|
| Wall members | 6x6 or 6x8 pressure-treated timbers, stacked horizontally |
| Connection | 1/2" or 5/8" galvanized rebar driven through pre-drilled holes, every other course, 4 ft OC |
| Deadman (tieback) | 6x6 timbers extending 4-6 ft back into soil, attached to wall with 1/2" galvanized hardware, spaced 4-6 ft OC and every 2 courses (for walls >2 ft) |
| Base | Timbers set in 6" trench of compacted gravel |
| Drainage | Drain rock behind wall, perforated pipe at base — same as concrete walls |
| Filter fabric | Between drain rock and native soil |
| Maximum height | 4 ft (without engineering) |

### Timber Lifespan

| Treatment | Expected Lifespan | Notes |
|-----------|-------------------|-------|
| ACQ or CA-B treated | 15-25 years | Most common; compatible with stainless hardware |
| CCA treated (non-residential) | 25-40 years | Best longevity but restricted use |
| Untreated (naturally durable — redwood heartwood, cedar) | 10-15 years in ground contact | Not recommended for retaining walls |
| Railroad ties (creosote) | 20-30 years | Environmental concerns; check local regulations |

## Connection Details

### Footing-to-Stem Connection (Dowels)

| Detail | Specification |
|--------|---------------|
| Dowel size | Match vertical rebar size in stem (#4 or #5 typical) |
| Dowel spacing | Match vertical rebar spacing |
| Embedment into footing | Per ACI 318 development length; minimum 12" for #4, 15" for #5 |
| Projection above footing | Lap splice length above footing top (24" min for #4, 30" min for #5) |
| Hook | Standard 90-degree hook in footing if space is limited |
| Placement | Set in wet concrete or drilled and epoxied after footing cures |

### Shear Key

| Detail | Specification |
|--------|---------------|
| Purpose | Increases sliding resistance by engaging passive soil pressure below the footing |
| Location | At stem or slightly toward heel |
| Size | 4-6" wide x 6-12" deep (below bottom of footing) |
| Reinforcement | #4 or #5 dowels connecting key to footing |
| Construction | Form a keyway in the bottom of the footing pour; or pour as separate element |

### Expansion / Construction Joints

| Joint Type | Location | Detail |
|-----------|----------|--------|
| Construction joint | Where concrete pours meet (footing-to-stem, between pours) | Roughen surface, install rebar continuously through joint, apply bonding agent |
| Expansion joint | Every 40-60 ft in long walls | Full-depth joint with compressible filler (1/2" preformed), sealant at exposed face, waterstop at soil side |
| Contraction joint | Every 20-30 ft (alternative to expansion joint) | Saw-cut or formed groove, 1/4 wall thickness depth, sealant at face |

## Material Specification Checklist

When reviewing retaining wall plans, verify the following material specifications are shown:

| # | Item | What to Verify |
|---|------|---------------|
| 1 | Concrete strength (f'c) | Minimum 2,500 psi; 3,000 psi typical; sulfate-resistant cement if required by soils report |
| 2 | Rebar grade | ASTM A615 or A706 Grade 60 |
| 3 | Rebar clear cover | 2" against earth, 1.5" exposed to weather |
| 4 | CMU block type | ASTM C90, normal weight, Grade N (if CMU wall) |
| 5 | Mortar type | Type M for below grade / ground contact; Type S for above grade |
| 6 | Grout strength | f'g >= 2,000 psi (if CMU wall) |
| 7 | SRW block standard | ASTM C1372 (if segmental wall) |
| 8 | Timber treatment | AWPA U1, Use Category 4B (if timber wall) |
| 9 | Geogrid type/strength | Per manufacturer or engineer (if SRW or MSE wall) |
| 10 | Drain rock specification | 3/4"-1.5" clean crushed aggregate |
| 11 | Filter fabric | Non-woven geotextile, minimum 4 oz/sy |
| 12 | Drain pipe | 4" min perforated PVC or HDPE |
