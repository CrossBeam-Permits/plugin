# Smoke and Carbon Monoxide Alarm Requirements

Source: 2025 California Residential Code (Title 24, Part 2.5), Sections R310 and R311.

## When Triggered by HVAC Work

### Smoke Alarms (CRC R310.2.2)

Where alterations, repairs, or additions **requiring a permit** occur, the individual dwelling unit shall be equipped with smoke alarms located as required for new dwellings.

> This means: any HVAC permit triggers retrofit smoke alarm compliance for the entire dwelling unit.

### Carbon Monoxide Alarms (CRC R311.2.2)

Where a fuel-burning heater, appliance, or fireplace is **added to** an existing dwelling not previously required to have CO alarms, new CO alarms shall be installed.

**Exceptions** (CO alarm upgrade NOT triggered):
1. Work limited to exterior surfaces (roofing, siding, windows, doors)
2. Plumbing system work only
3. Mechanical systems that are **not fuel-fired** (e.g., electric heat pump replacement)

### When CO Alarms ARE Required (CRC R311.2.1)

CO alarms are required where either or both conditions exist:
1. The dwelling unit contains a **fuel-fired appliance** or fireplace
2. The dwelling unit has an **attached garage** with an opening to the dwelling

---

## Smoke Alarm Location (CRC R310.3)

Smoke alarms shall be installed in:
1. Each **sleeping room**
2. **Outside** each separate sleeping area in the immediate vicinity of bedrooms
3. On **each additional story** of the dwelling (including basements, habitable attics; NOT crawlspaces or uninhabitable attics)
4. Not less than **3 ft** from the door of a bathroom with tub/shower
5. In the hallway and room open to hallway where ceiling height exceeds hallway by 24 in.

### Placement Near HVAC

- Smoke alarms shall NOT be installed within **36 in.** of a supply register of a forced air heating or cooling system (NFPA 72 29.8.3.4(6))
- Smoke alarms shall NOT be installed within **36 in.** of a ceiling fan blade tip

---

## Carbon Monoxide Alarm Location (CRC R311.3)

CO alarms shall be installed in:
1. **Outside of each separate sleeping area** in the immediate vicinity of bedrooms
2. On **every occupiable level** of the dwelling, including basements
3. **Within a bedroom** if a fuel-burning appliance is located within the bedroom or its attached bathroom

---

## Interconnection

### Smoke Alarms (CRC R310.4)

Where more than one smoke alarm is required, they shall be **interconnected** so activation of one activates all alarms in the unit.

**Exceptions** (interconnection not required):
1. Buildings not undergoing any work
2. Existing areas where work does not remove interior wall/ceiling finishes exposing structure (unless attic/crawlspace/basement access available)
3. Exterior-only work (roofing, siding, windows, doors)
4. **Plumbing or mechanical system work** that does not remove interior finishes

> Important: HVAC work that does NOT remove drywall or ceiling finishes does not trigger interconnection requirements. Most HVAC change-outs fall under Exception 4.

### Carbon Monoxide Alarms (CRC R311.5)

Same interconnection requirement and exceptions as smoke alarms. Wireless listed alarms are acceptable.

---

## Power Source

### Smoke Alarms (CRC R310.6)

Primary power from building wiring (hardwired) with **battery backup**.

**Exceptions** (battery-only permitted):
1. Buildings without commercial power
2. Existing buildings with no construction
3. Alterations not removing interior finishes (unless attic/crawl/basement access exists)
4. Exterior-only work
5. **Plumbing or mechanical system work** not removing interior finishes

> For most HVAC change-outs: if no drywall is opened, existing battery-only alarms may remain. If drywall IS opened, hardwired alarms with battery backup are required.

### Carbon Monoxide Alarms (CRC R311.6)

Same hardwired + battery backup requirement, with the same exceptions for existing buildings.

## Quick Reference — HVAC Permit Alarm Requirements

| Scenario | Smoke Alarms | CO Alarms |
|----------|-------------|-----------|
| Gas furnace replacement (no drywall work) | Location compliance required; existing power source OK | Required in dwelling with fuel appliances |
| Gas furnace replacement (drywall opened) | Hardwired + battery, interconnected | Hardwired + battery, interconnected |
| Heat pump (electric only) replacement | Location compliance required | NOT triggered (Exception 3 to R311.2.2) |
| New gas furnace in previously all-electric home | Full compliance (location + hardwired) | Full CO alarm installation required |
| A/C condenser replacement only | Location compliance required | Not triggered if no fuel appliance change |
