# Fire Setbacks, Access Pathways, and Smoke Ventilation for Solar PV

Source: 2025 California Fire Code (Title 24, Part 9), Chapter 12 (Section 1205); 2025 California Residential Code (Title 24, Part 2.5), Section R329.6.

---

## General (CFC 1205.1)

Solar PV systems shall be installed per CBC or CRC. Electrical per CEC. Rooftop-mounted systems per CFC 1205.2 through 1205.4.3. Ground-mounted per CFC 1205.5.

---

## Residential (Group R-3) Roof Access and Pathways (CFC 1205.2.1 / CRC R329.6)

### Exceptions -- These Rules Do NOT Apply To:
1. Detached nonhabitable structures (garages, carports, parking shades, solar trellises)
2. Structures where fire code official determines rooftop operations will not be employed
3. **Roofs with slope <=2:12** (16.7% or less -- flat/low-slope)
4. BIPV listed per UL 3741 where cutting/removal does not expose firefighters to shock

### Pathways to Ridge (CFC 1205.2.1.1 / CRC R329.6.1)

- Minimum **two** pathways on **separate roof planes** from lowest roof edge to ridge
- Each pathway minimum **36 inches (914 mm) wide**
- At least **one pathway on the street or driveway side**
- For each roof plane with a PV array: one 36" pathway on same plane, adjacent plane, or straddling both
- Pathways over areas **capable of supporting firefighters**
- Located in areas with **minimal obstructions** (vent pipes, conduit, mechanical equipment)

### Ridge Setbacks (CFC 1205.2.1.2 / CRC R329.6.2)

| Array Coverage (% of plan view roof area) | Setback on Both Sides of Ridge |
|-------------------------------------------|-------------------------------|
| **<=33%** | 18 inches (457 mm) |
| **>33%** | 36 inches (914 mm) |

### Alternative Ridge Setbacks -- With Sprinklers (CFC 1205.2.1.3 / CRC R329.6.2.1)

Where automatic sprinkler system installed per 903.3.1.3 (NFPA 13D or CRC R309):

| Array Coverage (% of plan view roof area) | Setback on Both Sides of Ridge |
|-------------------------------------------|-------------------------------|
| **<=66%** | 18 inches (457 mm) |
| **>66%** | 36 inches (914 mm) |

### Emergency Escape and Rescue Openings (CFC 1205.2.2 / CRC R329.6.3)

- **No panels below** emergency escape and rescue openings
- 36-inch (914 mm) minimum pathway to the opening
- Exception: BIPV per UL 3741

### Hip and Valley Pathways (CFC Appendix BB103.2.2)

- **18-inch (457 mm) minimum** pathway along each side of a hip or valley where PV arrays are located
- Required for roofs with slope >2:12

### Pathway Spacing (CFC Appendix BB103.2.3)

- Located for most direct route to roof access points
- Structurally sound locations
- **3-foot (914 mm) clear pathway** on each roof plane where PV is installed

### Flat Roof Setbacks (CFC Appendix BB103.2.4)

- Minimum **4-foot (1219 mm) wide** pathway along structural load-bearing lines and along perimeter

## Smoke Ventilation (CFC Appendix BB103.4 / CFC 1205.3.3)

### Residential (CFC Appendix BB103.4)
- Clear area of **3 ft x 3 ft (914 mm x 914 mm)** on each side of ridge or horizontal roof feature
- Panel layouts designed to provide required clear spaces for ventilation cuts

### Commercial/Other (CFC 1205.3.3)
- Nongravity smoke/heat vents: 4-ft pathway bordering all sides
- Gravity dropout vents: 4-ft pathway on at least one side
- Between arrays: 8-ft pathway OR 4-ft pathway with 4x8-ft venting cutouts every 20 ft

---

## BIPV System Marking (CFC 1205.2.3 / CRC R329.6.4)

Where BIPV creates hidden electrical hazard areas:
- Markings to identify hazardous areas for ladder placement avoidance
- **Reflective**, visible from grade beneath eaves
- Exception: BIPV per UL 3741

---

## DC Conductor Routing on Rooftops (CFC 1205.2.4 / CRC R329.8.3)

California amendment [CA]:
- Conduit, wiring, raceways located **as close as possible to ridge, hip, or valley**
- From hip/valley **directly as possible to outside wall**
- Purpose: reduce trip hazards, maximize ventilation opportunities
- Conduit runs between sub-arrays and DC combiner boxes: **shortest path**
- DC combiner boxes located to **minimize conduit in pathways between arrays**
- DC wiring **in metallic conduit or raceways** when in enclosed spaces in buildings
- Conduit along **bottom of load-bearing members**

---

## Commercial/Non-R-3 Buildings (CFC 1205.3)

### Perimeter Pathways (CFC 1205.3.1)
- **6-foot (1829 mm) minimum** clear perimeter around roof edges
- Exception: <=250 ft in either axis: may reduce to 4 ft (1219 mm)

### Interior Pathways (CFC 1205.3.2)
- At intervals <=150 ft throughout length and width
- 4-ft minimum to roof standpipes or ventilation hatches
- 4-ft minimum around roof access hatches, with path to parapet/roof edge

## Rapid Shutdown Labeling for Fire Response (CFC 1205.4)

### Rapid Shutdown Type Labels (CFC 1205.4.1)

**Type 1 -- Full shutdown (array + conductors)**:
- Black on yellow background (first two lines, 3/8" min uppercase)
- Remaining text black on white (3/16" min uppercase)
- Text: "SOLAR PV SYSTEM EQUIPPED WITH RAPID SHUTDOWN. TURN RAPID SHUTDOWN SWITCH TO THE 'OFF' POSITION TO SHUT DOWN PV SYSTEM AND REDUCE SHOCK HAZARD IN ARRAY."

**Type 2 -- Conductor-only shutdown (array remains energized)**:
- White on red background (first two lines, 3/8" min uppercase)
- Remaining text black on white (3/16" min uppercase)
- Text: "THIS SOLAR PV SYSTEM EQUIPPED WITH RAPID SHUTDOWN. TURN RAPID SHUTDOWN SWITCH TO THE 'OFF' POSITION TO SHUT DOWN CONDUCTORS OUTSIDE THE ARRAY. CONDUCTORS WITHIN ARRAY REMAIN ENERGIZED IN SUNLIGHT."

### Label Requirements
- Include simple diagram of building with roof (CFC 1205.4.1.1)
- Red sections = not shut down when switch is off
- Location: within **3 ft of service disconnecting means** (CFC 1205.4.1.2)
- Indicate location of all rapid shutdown switches if not at same location

### Rapid Shutdown Switch Label (CFC 1205.4.3)
Within 3 ft of switch: **"RAPID SHUTDOWN SWITCH FOR SOLAR PV SYSTEM"**

---

## Ground-Mounted PV (CFC 1205.5)

- Setback requirements do not apply to ground-mounted free-standing arrays
- **Vegetation control (CFC 1205.5.1)**: 10-ft (3048 mm) clear brush-free area around perimeter
- Maintained vegetative surface or noncombustible base under arrays and associated equipment
- CRC R329.8.2: 10-ft clear brush-free area required; setback requirements do not apply
