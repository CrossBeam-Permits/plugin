# CALGreen Section 5.408 -- Construction Waste Management

Source: 2025 California Green Building Standards Code (Title 24, Part 11), Chapter 5.

---

## Construction Waste Management (5.408.1)

Recycle and/or salvage for reuse a minimum of **65 percent** of the nonhazardous construction and demolition waste in accordance with Section 5.408.1.1, 5.408.1.2 or 5.408.1.3; or meet a local construction and demolition waste management ordinance, whichever is more stringent.

> **CRITICAL FOR LTIA**: This 65% diversion requirement applies to all demolition waste generated during interior demo -- drywall, ceiling tiles, flooring, fixtures, millwork, etc.

### Construction Waste Management Plan (5.408.1.1)

Where a local jurisdiction does not have a construction and demolition waste management ordinance that is more stringent, submit a construction waste management plan that:

1. Identifies the construction and demolition waste materials to be diverted from disposal by efficient usage, recycling, reuse on the project or salvage for future use or sale.
2. Determines if construction and demolition waste materials will be sorted on-site (source-separated) or bulk mixed (single stream).
3. Identifies diversion facilities where construction and demolition waste material collected will be taken.
4. Specifies that the amount of construction and demolition waste materials diverted shall be calculated by weight or volume, but not by both.

### Waste Management Company (5.408.1.2)

Utilize a waste management company that can provide verifiable documentation that the percentage of construction and demolition waste material diverted from the landfill complies with this section.

**Exceptions:**
1. Excavated soil and land-clearing debris.
2. Alternate waste reduction methods developed by working with local agencies if diversion or recycle facilities capable of compliance do not exist.
3. Demolition waste meeting local ordinance or calculated in consideration of local recycling facilities and markets.

### Waste Stream Reduction Alternative (5.408.1.3)

The combined weight of new construction disposal that does not exceed **two pounds per square foot** of building area may be deemed to meet the 65 percent minimum requirement as approved by the enforcing agency.

### Documentation (5.408.1.4)

Documentation shall be provided to the enforcing agency which demonstrates compliance. The waste management plan shall be updated as necessary and shall be accessible during construction for examination by the enforcing agency.

> **FOR LTIA**: Waste hauler receipts will be required for final inspection sign-off. These receipts must document the weight/volume of materials and the diversion rate achieved.

## Universal Waste (5.408.2)

Additions and alterations to a building or tenant space shall require verification that Universal Waste items such as:
- Fluorescent lamps and ballasts
- Mercury-containing thermostats
- Other California prohibited Universal Waste materials

are disposed of properly and are **diverted from landfills**.

A list of prohibited Universal Waste materials shall be included in the construction documents.

> **FOR LTIA**: Fluorescent light fixtures and ballasts removed during demolition are regulated Universal Waste. They must NOT be thrown in general demolition debris. They require separate collection and proper disposal through certified handlers.

## Hazardous Materials Context

### Asbestos

Asbestos-containing materials (ACM) are regulated by the South Coast Air Quality Management District (SCAQMD) Rule 1403 and Cal/OSHA regulations, NOT by the building code directly. However:

- Buildings constructed before 1980 may contain asbestos in flooring, ceiling tiles, pipe insulation, textured coatings, and other materials.
- An asbestos survey by a certified assessor may be required before demolition begins.
- SCAQMD/AQMD notification is typically required before demolition permits are issued.

### Lead-Based Paint

- Buildings constructed before 1978 may contain lead-based paint.
- Lead testing for demolition of construction materials built in 1978 or prior years may be required.
- Lead-based paint removal must comply with Cal/OSHA Title 8, Section 1532.1.

> **Note**: Both asbestos and lead requirements are typically addressed through separate regulatory channels (AQMD notification, certified testing labs) but jurisdictions often require documentation before issuing the demolition permit.
