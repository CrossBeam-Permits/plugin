# CEC Article 680 — Swimming Pool Electrical Requirements

Source: 2025 California Electrical Code (Title 24, Part 3), Article 680 -- Swimming Pools, Fountains, and Similar Installations.

This reference covers all electrical requirements for new swimming pool and spa construction, including equipotential bonding, GFCI protection, underwater lighting, motor/pump circuits, wiring methods, and overhead conductor clearances.

---

## Equipotential Bonding (680.26)

Equipotential bonding is the single most critical electrical safety requirement for swimming pools. It creates a common voltage reference for all metallic and conductive components in and around the pool, preventing dangerous voltage gradients that cause electrocution.

### (A) Performance
The equipotential bonding required by 680.26 shall be installed to reduce voltage gradients in the pool area as prescribed.

### (B) Bonded Parts

#### (B)(1) — Metallic Parts of the Pool Structure
- All metallic parts of the pool structure, including reinforcing metal, shall be bonded
- **Pool shells with structural reinforcing steel**: Rebar must be bonded together by the usual steel tie wires or an equivalent listed method
- **Where reinforcing steel is encapsulated** in a nonconductive compound: A copper conductor grid shall be provided. Grid must conform to the contour of the pool and be arranged in a 12-inch by 12-inch network of minimum #8 AWG solid copper conductors, secured within or under the shell within 6 inches of the outer contour
- A copper conductor **ring** (min #8 AWG solid copper) may be used as an alternative to the grid, installed within 18 inches of the inside pool wall perimeter

#### (B)(2) — Perimeter Surfaces
- Equipotential bonding extends to perimeter surfaces within **3 feet horizontally** of the inside walls of the pool
- Bonding to perimeter paved and unpaved surfaces shall be provided per 680.26(B)(2)(a) or (B)(2)(b):
  - **(a)** Structural reinforcing steel bonded together by steel tie wires
  - **(b)** A minimum #8 AWG solid copper conductor, attached to the pool reinforcing steel or copper conductor grid at a minimum of **four (4) points uniformly spaced** around the perimeter of the pool
- The perimeter bonding conductor shall follow the contour of the perimeter surface, 18 inches to 24 inches from the inside wall of the pool

#### (B)(3) — Metallic Components
All metallic parts of the pool structure, equipment, and fittings:
- Metal fittings within or attached to the pool structure (ladders, rails, handholds, diving board supports)
- Metal parts of electric equipment associated with the pool water circulating system (pump motors, metal conduit, metal piping)
- Metal-sheathed cables and raceways, metal piping, and fixed metal parts within 5 feet horizontally of the inside walls of the pool and within 12 feet above the maximum water level or observation stand/tower/platform

#### (B)(4) — Underwater Lighting
Metal forming shells for wet-niche underwater lighting fixtures shall be bonded.

#### (B)(5) — Metal Fittings
Metal fittings within or attached to the pool structure shall be bonded. Common sense -- covers handrails, ladder brackets, light niches, skimmer bodies, main drain frames.

### Bonding Conductor Specifications

| Parameter | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Minimum conductor size | #8 AWG solid copper |
| Connection method | Listed pressure connectors, clamps, or other listed means (no solder-only connections) |
| Conductor protection | Protected against physical damage where exposed |
| Connection to rebar | At minimum 4 points uniformly spaced around pool perimeter |
| Perimeter bonding location | 18" to 24" from inside pool wall |

### Bonding vs. Grounding
Bonding (680.26) and grounding (680.23, 680.24, 250) are separate requirements:
- **Bonding** connects all metallic parts to each other to equalize potential (prevent shock)
- **Grounding** connects equipment to the grounding electrode system (to clear faults)
- Both are required. A pool can be properly grounded but improperly bonded, which is still dangerous.

## GFCI Protection Requirements

### Receptacles (680.22)

#### (A) Receptacles Within 20 Feet
- **All 125-volt, single-phase receptacles** within **20 feet** of the inside walls of a pool or fountain shall have **GFCI protection for personnel**
- Measured as the shortest path a supply cord could follow without piercing a wall, floor, ceiling, door, or window

#### (A)(1) — Required Receptacle
- At least **one 125-volt, 15- or 20-ampere receptacle** on a general-purpose branch circuit shall be located a minimum of **6 feet** and not more than **20 feet** from the inside wall of the pool

#### (A)(2) — Prohibited Zone
- **No receptacles** shall be installed within **6 feet** of the inside walls of the pool

#### (A)(3) — Existing Receptacles
- Where existing receptacles are within the restricted zone and cannot be relocated, GFCI protection is an acceptable modification

#### (A)(4) — Indoor Pools
- Same 6-20 ft receptacle requirement applies to indoor pools

### Switches and Switching Devices (680.22(C))
- Switches controlling pool lighting, motors, or other loads shall be located at least **5 feet** from the inside walls of the pool
- Exception: Listed pool-rated switching devices integral to or attached to listed pool equipment

### Luminaire and Ceiling Fan Clearances (680.22(B))
- In outdoor pool areas: luminaires, lighting outlets, and ceiling-suspended fans not less than **12 feet** above the maximum water level

## Motor and Pump Requirements

### Motors (680.21)

#### (A) Wiring Methods
- Branch circuit wiring from the panelboard to the pool equipment shall be installed in:
  - Rigid metal conduit (RMC)
  - Intermediate metal conduit (IMC)
  - Liquidtight flexible nonmetallic conduit (LFNC)
  - Rigid polyvinyl chloride conduit (PVC)
  - Reinforced thermosetting resin conduit (RTRC)
  - Type MC cable (where listing permits wet locations)
- **Aluminum conduit shall NOT be permitted** in pool areas (680.14(A))

#### (C) GFCI Protection for Pump Motors
- All pool pump motors rated 15 or 20 amperes, 125V or 240V, single phase, whether permanently connected or cord-and-plug connected, shall be provided with **Class A GFCI protection**

### Maintenance Disconnecting Means (680.13)
- One or more means to simultaneously disconnect all ungrounded conductors shall be provided for all utilization equipment other than lighting
- Each disconnect shall be:
  - **Readily accessible**
  - **Within sight** from its equipment
  - Located not less than **5 feet horizontally** from the inside walls of the pool
- Exception: A disconnect separated from the pool by a permanently installed barrier providing a 5 ft reach path is acceptable

## Underwater Lighting (680.23)

### General Requirements
- All underwater luminaires operating at **more than the low-voltage contact limit (15 volts)** shall be GFCI-protected, regardless of voltage
- Maximum voltage: **150 volts** between conductors for wet-niche fixtures

### Wet-Niche Fixtures (680.23(B))
- Forming shell shall be **grounded** to the equipment grounding conductor
- Equipment grounding conductor shall be an insulated copper conductor not smaller than **#12 AWG**
- Forming shell installed in pool wall, with conduit run to junction box
- Junction box: located not less than **4 inches** above the ground level or pool deck, not less than **4 feet** from the pool edge, or **8 inches** above maximum pool water level (whichever provides greater elevation)

### Low-Voltage Lighting Systems (680.23(A)(2))
- Listed low-voltage lighting systems (15 volts or less) with a listed transformer or power supply are permitted
- Low-voltage LED pool/spa lights are increasingly common and simplify compliance
- The transformer/power supply shall be listed for pool use

### No-Niche Fixtures (680.23(C))
- Listed for the purpose
- Installed per manufacturer's instructions
- GFCI-protected

### Cord-Connected Lighting (680.23(B)(6))
- Where a forming shell is used with a cord-connected luminaire, the cord shall:
  - Not exceed **12 feet** in length
  - Include an equipment grounding conductor not smaller than #12 AWG
  - Be terminated in a grounding-type attachment plug

## Overhead Conductor Clearances (680.8)

### Clearance From Pool Water Surface

| Voltage | Minimum Clearance Above Water / Diving Structure |
|---------|--------------------------------------------------|
| 0-750V insulated supply or service drop | **14.5 feet** |
| 0-750V uninsulated supply or service drop | **22.5 feet** |
| Over 750V-15kV insulated | **14.5 feet** |
| Over 750V-15kV uninsulated | **22.5 feet** |
| Over 15kV-50kV | **25.5 feet** |

### Horizontal Clearances
- Overhead conductors shall not pass directly over the pool or within **10 feet** measured horizontally from the inside wall of the pool, diving structure, observation stand, tower, or platform

### Communication Cables
- Communication, CATV, and network-powered broadband cables shall maintain the same clearances as service drop conductors

## Pool Heater Electrical (680.9)

All pool water heaters shall be listed and installed per manufacturer instructions.

### Electric Pool Heaters
- Connected to a dedicated branch circuit
- Sized per continuous load (125% of nameplate rating)
- GFCI protection not required for hardwired heaters (but required for cord-connected units)

### Heat Pump Pool Heaters
- Outdoor heat pump pool/spa heaters: a cover shall be installed per California Energy Code Section 110.4 if the pool/spa has a heat pump or gas heater
- Disconnect within sight and within 50 feet of the unit

## Equipment Rooms and Drainage (680.12)

### (A) Drainage
Electrical equipment shall not be installed in rooms, vaults, or pits that do not have drainage to prevent water accumulation during normal operation or maintenance, unless the equipment is rated for submersion.

### (B) Receptacles
At least one GFCI-protected 125-volt, 15- or 20-ampere receptacle shall be located within an equipment room.

---

## Corrosive Environments (680.14)

### (A) Wiring Methods
- Rigid metal conduit, intermediate metal conduit, rigid PVC conduit, reinforced thermosetting resin conduit, and liquidtight flexible nonmetallic conduit are suitable
- **Aluminum conduit and tubing shall NOT be permitted** -- the chlorine environment corrodes aluminum

### (B) Other Equipment
Equipment shall be suitable for corrosive environments or installed in corrosion-resistant enclosures.

## Quick-Reference: Pool Electrical Zone Summary

| Zone | Distance | What's Allowed | What's Prohibited |
|------|----------|----------------|-------------------|
| **Zone 0** (in water) | Pool water / wet-niche | Listed underwater fixtures (GFCI, max 150V), low-voltage LED lights (15V) | Receptacles, switches, standard fixtures |
| **Zone 1** (0-5 ft from pool wall) | Pool edge to 5 ft | Listed pool equipment only. Bonding required for all metallic parts. | Receptacles, switches, disconnects, luminaires below 12 ft |
| **Zone 2** (5-6 ft) | 5 ft to 6 ft | Disconnects (within sight of equipment). Switches for pool loads. | Receptacles |
| **Zone 3** (6-20 ft) | 6 ft to 20 ft | GFCI-protected receptacles (min 1 required). Switches. Equipment. | Non-GFCI receptacles |
| **Zone 4** (>20 ft) | Beyond 20 ft | Standard electrical rules apply | -- |
| **Overhead** | Above pool + 10 ft horizontal | Per clearance table (min 14.5 ft insulated, 22.5 ft uninsulated) | Lines below clearance minimums |

## Bonding Inspection Checklist

When reviewing pool electrical plans, verify all of the following are shown:

| # | Item | Requirement |
|---|------|-------------|
| 1 | Pool shell rebar bonding | All rebar tied together (typical), or #8 AWG copper grid if rebar is encapsulated |
| 2 | Perimeter surface bonding | #8 AWG solid copper, 18-24" from pool wall, within 3 ft of pool edge |
| 3 | Perimeter connection points | Min 4 points uniformly spaced around pool perimeter |
| 4 | Metal fittings bonded | Ladders, rails, diving board supports, light niches, skimmers, drain frames |
| 5 | Equipment bonded | Pump motor, metal conduit, metal piping within 5 ft |
| 6 | Conductor size | Min #8 AWG solid copper throughout |
| 7 | Connection method | Listed pressure connectors or clamps (no solder-only) |
| 8 | Underwater light forming shells | Bonded to bonding grid and grounded to EGC |
