# Pool Plumbing, Filtration, and Anti-Entrapment Requirements

Source: California Health & Safety Code Sections 115928-115929; 2025 California Plumbing Code (Title 24, Part 5), Chapters 7 and 12; 2025 California Electrical Code (Title 24, Part 3), Section 680.9; California Energy Code (Title 24, Part 6), Section 110.4; Virginia Graeme Baker Pool and Spa Safety Act (federal).

This reference covers pool plumbing systems, filtration, suction outlet anti-entrapment, drain configuration, water supply, pool heaters, and water discharge requirements.

---

## Suction Outlet Anti-Entrapment (HS Code 115928)

### The Critical Safety Issue
Suction entrapment occurs when a swimmer's body, hair, or limbs are trapped against a drain or suction outlet by the suction force of the pool circulation pump. This can cause drowning, evisceration, or limb entrapment. Both federal and California state law mandate specific protections.

### California Requirements (HS Code 115928)

| Requirement | Specification |
|-------------|---------------|
| **Minimum suction outlets per pump** | **2** circulation suction outlets per pump |
| **Hydraulic balance** | The two outlets must be **hydraulically balanced** (equal flow through each) |
| **Minimum separation** | Outlets separated by at least **3 feet** |
| **Anti-entrapment grates** | Must comply with **ANSI/APSP-16** (or successor CPSC standard) |
| **Grate removal** | Removable **only with tools** |
| **Grate openings** | Must prevent physical entrapment and suction hazard |
| **Approval** | All drains, grates, protective devices, and skimmer covers must be **approved by the enforcing agency** prior to installation |

### Virginia Graeme Baker Pool and Spa Safety Act (Federal — P.L. 110-140, Section 1404)
The federal VGBA Act requires:
- All pool and spa drain covers to comply with **ASME/ANSI A112.19.8** (now APSP-16)
- Single main drain pools/spas must have at least one additional anti-entrapment device:
  - **Safety Vacuum Release System (SVRS)** conforming to ASME/ANSI A112.19.17 or ASTM F2387
  - **Suction-limiting vent system**
  - **Gravity drainage system** operating without suction
  - **Automatic pump shut-off** system
  - **Drain disablement** (no main drain)
  - Any other system that the CPSC determines to be equally protective

### Dual Main Drain Configuration
The dual suction outlet requirement eliminates the single-drain entrapment risk:
- If one outlet is blocked by a body, the second outlet maintains flow and prevents dangerous suction buildup
- The two outlets must be far enough apart (3 ft minimum) that a single person cannot cover both simultaneously

## Return Drain Configuration

### Drain Layout Requirements

| Component | Specification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Number of return drains** | **2 required** |
| **Drain 1 location** | At the **bottom** of the pool (deepest point) |
| **Drain 2 location** | At the **side**, within **2 inches** of the bottom |
| **Minimum separation** | **4 feet** apart |
| **Covers** | **Anti-vortex covers** required on all drains |
| **Cover standard** | ANSI/APSP-16 compliant |

### Anti-Vortex Covers
Anti-vortex drain covers are dome-shaped or otherwise designed to:
- Prevent the formation of a vortex (whirlpool) over the drain
- Distribute suction force over a larger area
- Prevent hair entrapment by keeping hair away from the suction point
- Allow water flow even if partially obstructed

## Pool Water Supply

### Backflow Prevention (CPC Chapter 6)
- The water supply to the pool fill line or autofill system must include an **approved backflow prevention device**
- Pool water is a **contamination hazard** (chemicals, biological) — requires at minimum an **air gap** or **reduced pressure principle backflow prevention assembly (RP)**
- An **atmospheric vacuum breaker** is acceptable for hose-bib pool fill connections
- The fill line cannot be submerged below the pool waterline without an approved backflow device

### Water Supply Sizing
- Pool fill/autofill lines are typically sized per the pool contractor's specifications
- No specific CPC sizing requirement for pool fill beyond standard fixture supply sizing
- The autofill float valve must be accessible for inspection and maintenance

## Pool Filtration System

### General Requirements
- All swimming pools must have a **filtration system** to maintain water clarity and sanitation
- The filtration system must be sized for the pool volume — typically designed to turn over the entire pool volume within **6 to 8 hours**
- Equipment must be **listed** and installed per **manufacturer's instructions**

### Filtration Components

| Component | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Pump** | Listed, properly sized for pool volume and head pressure. Motor GFCI-protected per CEC 680.21(C). |
| **Filter** | Listed (sand, cartridge, or DE type). Backwash capability required for sand and DE filters. |
| **Skimmer** | At least one surface skimmer. Skimmer cover/grate must be approved before installation. |
| **Return inlets** | Adequate number to provide proper circulation. Located to promote circulation and avoid dead spots. |
| **Chemical feeder** | If installed, must be downstream of filter and heater. Listed for pool use. |

### Equipment Pad
- Equipment pad location shown on site plan
- Minimum **5 feet** from pool edge for disconnect compliance (CEC 680.13)
- Drainage so that water from equipment does not flow toward structures
- Equipment accessible for maintenance and inspection

## Pool Heater Requirements

### Gas Pool Heaters (CPC Chapter 12)

| Requirement | Specification |
|-------------|---------------|
| **Installation** | Per CPC and manufacturer's instructions |
| **Gas supply** | Sized per CPC gas pipe sizing tables for BTU input |
| **Venting** | Per manufacturer specifications and CPC Chapter 8 |
| **Clearances** | Per manufacturer's listing and CPC |
| **Certification** | Manufacturer must certify system per California Energy Code 110.4 |
| **Cover** | Pool/spa cover required if outdoor pool/spa has a gas heater (Cal Energy Code 110.4) |
| **Accessibility** | Gas shut-off valve accessible, labeled |

### Electric Pool Heaters / Heat Pumps (CEC 680.9)

| Requirement | Specification |
|-------------|---------------|
| **Listing** | Must be listed for pool/spa use |
| **Installation** | Per manufacturer's instructions |
| **Circuit sizing** | Dedicated branch circuit, sized at 125% of nameplate for continuous load |
| **Disconnect** | Within sight and within 50 feet of unit |
| **Cover** | Pool/spa cover required if outdoor pool/spa has a heat pump (Cal Energy Code 110.4) |
| **GFCI** | Not required for permanently connected (hardwired) heaters; required for cord-connected |

### California Energy Code 110.4 — Pool/Spa Heating
- Pool or spa heating system or equipment shall be installed only if the **manufacturer has certified** the system per Energy Code 110.4
- An **outdoor pool or spa with a heat pump or gas heater** must have a pool/spa cover installed
- This is an energy conservation requirement — the cover reduces heat loss and energy consumption

## Water Discharge and Dewatering

### Pool Excavation Dewatering
- Groundwater encountered during excavation must be handled per NPDES requirements
- **No contaminated groundwater** discharge to storm drains
- Dewatering of non-contaminated groundwater requires a **National Pollutant Discharge Elimination System Permit** from the State Regional Water Quality Control Board
- Sediment-laden water must be filtered or settled before any permitted discharge

### Pool Water Discharge (Initial Fill Test / Maintenance)
- Pool water containing **chlorine or other chemicals** must be **de-chlorinated** before discharge
- Maximum residual chlorine for discharge: typically **0.1 mg/L** (check local requirements)
- Discharge to sanitary sewer is preferred for treated pool water (coordinate with local sewer agency)
- **No filter backwash** or cleaning wastewater to storm drains

### Construction Water Management

| Discharge Type | Permitted Destination | Conditions |
|----------------|----------------------|------------|
| Clean groundwater | Storm drain (with NPDES permit) | Must be non-contaminated, sediment-free |
| Sediment-laden water | Settling basin, then permitted discharge | Filter/settle before discharge |
| Chlorinated pool water | Sanitary sewer (preferred) or de-chlorinate for storm drain | De-chlorinate to < 0.1 mg/L |
| Filter backwash | Sanitary sewer | Never to storm drain |
| Chemical rinse water | Sanitary sewer or licensed hauler | Never to storm drain |
| Contaminated groundwater | Licensed hauler / off-site disposal | No discharge permitted |

## Equipment Listing and Installation

### General (CEC 110.3(B), CBC 3109)
- All pool equipment — pumps, filters, heaters, lighting, GFCI devices, chemical feeders, automation systems — must be **listed by a nationally recognized testing laboratory** (UL, ETL, CSA, etc.)
- All equipment must be installed per **manufacturer's instructions**
- Equipment labeling must be durable, weather-resistant, and visible after installation

### Approval Before Installation
Per the OC swimming pool note sheet and standard practice:
- All drains, grates, protective devices, and skimmer covers for swimming pools/spas must be **approved by the enforcing agency prior to installation**
- This means submittals/cut sheets for these safety-critical components are reviewed during plan check, not discovered at inspection

## Plumbing Inspection Points

| # | Inspection | What Is Verified |
|---|-----------|------------------|
| 1 | **Rough plumbing** | Pool plumbing laid in excavation — pipe sizing, material, routing, suction outlet locations, return locations, skimmer placement, main drain configuration (dual drains, 4 ft apart, anti-vortex covers) |
| 2 | **Pressure test** | All pool plumbing pressure-tested before backfill/shotcrete. Typical: 40 PSI for 24 hours with no loss. |
| 3 | **Equipment set** | Pump, filter, heater properly connected. Gas line connected (if gas heater). Backflow prevention on fill line. Chemical feeder downstream of filter and heater. |
| 4 | **Anti-entrapment verification** | Dual suction outlets installed, 3 ft separation verified, ANSI/APSP-16 grates installed, removable only with tools. SVRS or equivalent if single drain (not typical for new construction). |
| 5 | **Final plumbing** | System operational, no leaks, proper drainage, equipment accessible. |
