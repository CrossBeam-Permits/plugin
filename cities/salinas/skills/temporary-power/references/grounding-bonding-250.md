# Grounding Electrode System, GECs, EGCs, and Bonding

Source: 2025 California Electrical Code (Title 24, Part 3), Article 250.

---

## Grounding Electrode System (GES)

### Use All Available Electrodes (CEC 250.50)

All grounding electrodes described in 250.52(A)(1) through (A)(7) that are **present at each building or structure** shall be bonded together and utilized.

**Exception**: If an existing building has concrete-encased electrodes that cannot be accessed without disturbing the concrete, they need not be included.

### Types of Grounding Electrodes (CEC 250.52)

| Electrode Type | Code Section | Key Requirements |
|---------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Metal underground water pipe | 250.52(A)(1) | At least 10 ft in contact with soil |
| Metal in-ground support structures | 250.52(A)(4) | At least 10 ft vertical contact with earth |
| Concrete-encased electrode (Ufer) | 250.52(A)(3) | 20 ft uncoupled rebar or bare Cu, min. 2 in. concrete encasement in footings/piers in direct earth contact |
| Ground ring | 250.52(A)(4) | Min. #2 AWG Cu, 20 ft in earth contact, encircle building |
| Rod electrodes | 250.52(A)(5) | Cu-clad min. 5/8 in. diameter, 8 ft in soil contact |
| Plate electrodes | 250.52(A)(7) | Ferrous: min. 1/4 in. thick, 2 sq ft soil contact, buried 30 in. below surface |
| Listed electrodes | 250.52(A)(6) | Per manufacturer instructions |

### Prohibited Electrodes (CEC 250.52(B))

- Metal underground gas piping systems
- Aluminum electrodes
- Pool or spa shell bonding grids

### Bond All Electrodes Together (CEC 250.50)

Bond all electrodes together to form the continuous GES.

## Grounding Electrode Installation

### Water Pipe Electrodes (CEC 250.53(D))

- Bond around water meters, filters, pressure regulators, and similar equipment to ensure continuity (250.53(D)(1))
- Water pipe electrode shall **NOT be the sole electrode** — supplemented by another type (250.53(D)(2))

### Rod and Plate Electrodes (CEC 250.53(A))

- Rods driven **vertically and fully below grade** (250.53(A)(4))
  - **Exception**: If bedrock encountered, rod may be buried horizontally 2-1/2 ft deep or driven at max 45-degree angle
- Rod ends and clamps above ground shall have **physical damage protection** (250.53(A)(4))
- Plate electrodes buried **minimum 30 inches** below surface (250.53(A)(5))

### Supplemental Electrode (CEC 250.53(A)(2))

A supplemental electrode is required if:
- A single rod or plate electrode exceeds **25 ohms resistance** to earth
- Multiple rods: minimum **6 ft spacing** (16 ft preferred) (250.53(A)(3))

### Concrete-Encased Electrode (Ufer) (CEC 250.52(A)(3))

- 20 ft of uncoupled rebar or bare copper wire
- Minimum **2 inches of concrete** encasement
- In footings or piers in direct earth contact
- Minimum rebar size: **#4** or copper wire **#4 AWG**
- Shorter rebar sections may be connected using steel tie wires or welding
- Ufer shall be present during construction (exception: existing buildings)
- Where multiple Ufers present, only one required to be bonded to GES

### Ground Ring (CEC 250.52(A)(4))

- Encircle building/structure, direct earth contact
- Minimum **#2 AWG copper**, 20 ft length in earth contact
- Minimum **30 inches** below surface (250.53(F))

## Grounding Electrode Conductor (GEC)

### Material (CEC 250.62)

Copper, aluminum, copper-clad aluminum, or items per 250.68(C). Resistant to corrosion or protected against it.

### Sizing (CEC 250.66)

GEC sized per service conductor size using Table 250.66.

| GEC Termination | Maximum GEC Size Required |
|----------------|--------------------------|
| Rod, pipe, or plate electrode only | #6 Cu or #4 Al (250.66(A)) |
| Concrete-encased electrode (Ufer) | #4 Cu (250.66(B)) |
| Ground ring | #2 Cu (250.66(C)) |

### Installation (CEC 250.64)

- Installed in **one continuous length** without splice or joint (250.64(C))
  - Exceptions: listed irreversible compression connectors, exothermic welding, listed connections to busbars
- All GEC connections shall be **accessible** except where buried or encased in concrete (250.68(A))
- Splices not allowed except as noted above

### Protection (CEC 250.64(B))

- Raceway or armor required for **#8 GEC** (250.64(B)(3))
- Protection required unless #8 GECs are NOT exposed to damage (250.64(B)(1))
- GECs subject to physical damage require protection: RMC, IMC, PVC 80, RTRC, EMT, or cable armor (250.64(B)(2))
- Each end of ferrous raceway enclosing GEC shall be **bonded** to enclosure, electrode, or GEC (250.64(E)(1))

### Connection to Electrodes (CEC 250.70)

- Buried clamps shall be listed and labeled for **direct burial ("DB")** (250.70)
- Copper water tubing clamps listed for tubing
- Ufer clamps listed for rebar and encasement
- Maximum **one conductor per clamp** unless listed for more
- Solder alone shall NOT be used (250.8(B))
- Nonconductive coatings (paint, enamel, lacquer) shall be **removed from contact surfaces** (250.12)

## Equipment Grounding Conductors (EGC)

### Sizing (CEC Table 250.122)

| Overcurrent Device Rating (A) | Min. EGC — Copper (AWG) | Min. EGC — Aluminum (AWG) |
|-------------------------------|------------------------|--------------------------|
| 15 | 14 | 12 |
| 20 | 12 | 10 |
| 60 | 10 | 8 |
| 100 | 8 | 6 |
| 200 | 6 | 4 |
| 300 | 4 | 2 |
| 400 | 3 | 1 |

> Where necessary to comply with 250.4(A)(5) or (B)(4), the EGC shall be sized larger than this table.

### Types and Identification (CEC 250.118, 250.119)

- Wire EGCs: copper, aluminum, or copper-clad aluminum; solid or stranded; bare, covered, or insulated (250.118(1))
- Conductors **#6 AWG or smaller**: factory insulated with **green** (or green with yellow stripes) (250.119)
- Conductors **#4 AWG or larger**: identified with **green tape or labels** encircling conductor at each end and every accessible point (250.119(A))
- **Green shall NEVER be used for neutral or ungrounded conductors** (250.119)
- Acceptable EGC types: copper/aluminum conductors, RMC, IMC, EMT, AC cable armor, electrically continuous raceways (250.118)

### Purpose and Routing (CEC 250.134)

- EGCs shall run with other conductors of the circuit they serve (250.134(C))
- A GEC shall NOT be used as an EGC (250.121(A))
- Metal building frames shall NOT serve as EGCs (250.121(B))
- Earth is NOT an effective ground-fault current path (250.4(A)(5))

### EGCs in Boxes (CEC 250.148)

- Splices require listed devices (250.8(A))
- EGCs connected within box or bonded to box (250.148(B))
- Removal of a device shall NOT interrupt EGC continuity (pigtails may be required) (250.148(B))
- Dedicated connections shall bond EGCs to a metal box (250.148(C))

## Neutral Conductors and EGCs

### Service Panels (CEC 250.24(B))

Neutral, EGCs, and enclosure shall be **bonded** in service panels.

### Subpanels (CEC 250.24(A)(5))

Neutrals shall **NOT be bonded** in subpanels.

### Neutral Conductor Rules

| Rule | Code Section |
|------|-------------|
| Continuity shall NOT depend on enclosures | CEC 200.2(B) |
| Each neutral on its own terminal | CEC 408.41 |
| Neutral shall NOT serve more than one circuit or MWBC | CEC 200.4(A) |
| Factory-applied white or gray; #4 AWG+ may use white/gray tape | CEC 200.6(A), (B) |
| White conductors NOT permitted on ungrounded conductors | CEC 200.7(A) |
| Exception: white in cable assembly acceptable as ungrounded if taped (not white, gray, green) | CEC 200.7(C) |
| EGCs and neutrals may NOT share same terminal | CEC 408.41 |
| EGCs shall NOT terminate on neutral bar in subpanels | CEC 250.24(A)(5) |
| Grounding terminal bars required if wire EGCs present in panel | CEC 408.40 |
| OCPDs shall NOT be in series with neutral | CEC 240.22 |

## Supply-Side Bonding (CEC 250.92)

- All service equipment, raceways, cable armor, and enclosures containing service conductors shall be bonded (250.92(A))
- Threaded couplings or listed threaded hubs made wrench-tight are acceptable (250.92(B)(2))
- **Standard locknuts NOT acceptable** on supply side of service (250.92(B)(2))
- Bonding locknuts acceptable where no remaining concentric knockouts present (250.92(B)(4))
- Jumpers required around impaired connections (concentric knockouts, reducing washers) on supply side (250.92(B)(2))

### Main Bonding Jumper Sizing (CEC Table 250.102(C)(1))

| Largest Ungrounded Conductor (Cu) | Largest Ungrounded Conductor (Al) | Bonding Jumper (Cu) | Bonding Jumper (Al) |
|-----------------------------------|-----------------------------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| 2 or smaller | 1/0 or smaller | 8 | 6 |
| 1 or 1/0 | 2/0 or 3/0 | 6 | 4 |
| 2/0 or 3/0 | 4/0 or 250 | 4 | 2 |
| Over 3/0 through 350 | Over 250 through 500 | 2 | 1/0 |
| Over 350 through 600 | Over 500 through 900 | 1/0 | 3/0 |
| Over 600 through 1100 | Over 900 through 1750 | 2/0 | 4/0 |

## Intersystem Bonding (CEC 250.94)

- IBT device shall be external to service equipment (250.94(A)(1))
- **Minimum 3 terminals** for intersystem bond conductors (250.94(A)(2))
- IBT shall NOT interfere with opening enclosure cover (250.94(A)(3))
- IBT shall be listed as grounding and bonding equipment (250.94(A)(6))
- Separate grounding electrodes for phone, CATV, etc.: minimum **#6 copper** to power GES (CEC 800.100(D))

---

## Bonding of Piping Systems (CEC 250.104)

- Water pipe bonding sized per Table 250.102(C)(1) (250.104(A)(1))
- Any metal piping system capable of becoming energized shall be bonded (including gas, metal ducts) (250.104(B))
- Gas piping bonding per Table 250.122 (250.104(B))
