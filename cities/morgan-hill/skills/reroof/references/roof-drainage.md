# Roof Drainage, Overflow Drains, and Scuppers

Source: 2025 California Residential Code (Title 24, Part 2.5), Section R903.4; California Building Code (Part 2), Section 1503.4.

---

## Primary Roof Drainage (CRC R903.4 / CBC 1503.4)

### General Rule

Unless roofs are sloped to drain over roof edges (e.g., to gutters or ground), **roof drains shall be installed at each low point of the roof.**

### Residential Steep-Slope

Most residential steep-slope roofs drain naturally over the eaves to gutters or ground. No internal roof drains are typically needed. The key requirement is:
- Roof drainage must **not discharge onto adjacent property**
- Gutters and downspouts should direct water appropriately

### Low-Slope / Flat Roofs

Low-slope roofs (common on additions, covered patios, carports, garages) require:
- Roof drains at low points
- Positive drainage (water does not pond)

---

## Secondary (Emergency Overflow) Drains or Scuppers (CRC R903.4.1 / CBC 1503.4.1)

### When Required

Secondary overflow drainage is required where:
- Roof drains are required (low-slope/flat roofs), AND
- The **roof perimeter extends above the roof surface** (parapets) such that water would be entrapped if primary drains fail

### Overflow Drain Option

- Same size as primary roof drain
- Inlet flow line located **2 inches above the low point** of the roof
- Must discharge **separately** from main roof drains (not into the same leader)

### Scupper Option

- **Three times the size** of the roof drain opening
- Minimum opening height: **4 inches**
- Located in the parapet wall
- Inlet flow located **2 inches above the low point** of the roof

---

## Roof Drain Sizing

Per California Plumbing Code (CPC) Table D101.1, roof drains are sized based on rainfall intensity:

- Southern California (Costa Mesa vicinity): approximately **2 inches per hour** maximum 1-hour rainfall rate
- Drain sizing is calculated per CPC drainage tables based on roof area served and rainfall rate

---

## Reroof Considerations

### Existing Drainage

When reroofing, the existing drainage system must be maintained or improved:
- Do not block or cover existing roof drains
- Replace deteriorated drain components
- Ensure positive drainage is maintained after new covering installation

### New Requirements on Reroof

Per CRC R908.1 (materials and installation same as new roofs):
- If the existing roof has positive drainage, **secondary overflow drains are not required** for a reroof/recover
- If drainage is being modified or parapets are involved, overflow drainage may be required

---

## Common Review Findings

1. **Flat roof addition with parapet but no overflow drain** — FLAG. If parapet traps water and primary drain fails, flooding can occur. Verify overflow provision.
2. **Roof drain blocked by new roofing material** — FAIL. Contractor must maintain drain access.
3. **Drainage onto neighbor's property** — FAIL. Redirected drainage must stay on the property or into public storm drain.
