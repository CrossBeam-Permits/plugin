# Block Wall Foundation and Footing Requirements

Source: 2025 California Residential Code (Title 24, Part 2.5), Section R403; California Building Code (Part 2), Sections 1807.1.6 and 1809; ACI 318; TMS 402/602-22.

---

## General Requirements

Every freestanding masonry block wall requires a **continuous reinforced concrete footing**. The footing:
- Transfers wall loads (self-weight, wind, seismic) to the soil
- Resists overturning forces trying to tip the wall
- Anchors the wall to the ground via dowel connections
- Must bear on undisturbed native soil or compacted engineered fill

---

## Footing Dimensions by Wall Height

### Prescriptive Footing Sizing

| Wall Height | Min Footing Width | Min Footing Depth | Footing Rebar (continuous) | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Up to 3 ft | 12" | 12" | 2-#4 continuous | Standard garden walls |
| 3 ft to 4 ft | 12"-18" | 12" | 2-#4 continuous | Property line walls |
| 4 ft to 5 ft | 18"-24" | 18" | 2-#4 continuous | Increased overturning |
| 5 ft to 6 ft | 24"-30" | 18"-24" | 2-#5 or 3-#4 continuous | Maximum prescriptive height |
| Over 6 ft | Per engineering | Per engineering | Per engineering | Always engineered |

### Footing Width Rules of Thumb
- **Minimum:** 2x the wall thickness (8" wall = 16" minimum footing)
- **Typical:** 2x to 2.5x wall thickness (8" wall = 16"-20" footing)
- **For taller walls (5-6 ft):** 3x wall thickness or more (8" wall = 24"-30")

The footing must be wide enough so the wall is centered with **minimum 6 inches** of footing on each side of the wall.

Example: 8" wall on 20" footing = 6" on each side. 8" wall on 24" footing = 8" on each side.

## Footing Reinforcement

### Longitudinal Bars

| Wall Height | Minimum Footing Rebar | Placement |
|---|---|---|
| Up to 4 ft | 2-#4 bars continuous | 3" from bottom, 3" from top |
| 4-6 ft | 2-#5 bars or 3-#4 continuous | 3" from bottom, 3" from top |
| Over 6 ft | Per engineering | Per engineering |

- Bars run the **full length** of the footing, continuous
- Lap splices: 48 bar diameters (#4 = 24", #5 = 30")
- At corners: bars turn the corner with a standard hook or continuous bar
- Provide 3" clear cover to soil on all sides

### Transverse Bars (Optional for Standard Walls)
For taller walls (>4 ft) or wide footings (>24"):
- #3 or #4 stirrups/ties at 12"-24" OC perpendicular to wall
- Helps resist shear and distributes bearing loads

## Footing Dowels (Wall-to-Footing Connection)

The connection between the footing and the wall above is made by **dowels** — vertical reinforcing bars that are embedded in the footing and extend up into the wall.

### Dowel Requirements

| Parameter | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Bar size | Match vertical wall rebar size (typically #4) |
| Spacing | Match vertical wall rebar spacing (24"-32" OC) |
| Embedment into footing | **24" minimum** (hooked bar) or full development length (straight bar) |
| Extension above footing | **24" minimum** into wall (to lap with vertical wall rebar) |
| Hook | Standard 90-degree hook at bottom of footing preferred |
| Positioning | Centered in the cell location where wall vertical rebar will be placed |

### Dowel Installation
1. Set dowels into the footing **before** concrete is poured
2. Use a template or string line to position dowels at correct spacing aligned with future CMU cell locations
3. Dowels must be plumb and securely held during concrete placement
4. After footing is poured and cured, dowels project above the footing at the correct locations for the CMU cells

### Alternative: Drilled and Epoxied Dowels
When adding a wall to an existing footing or slab:
- Core-drill holes at required spacing
- Install rebar with structural epoxy adhesive (e.g., Hilti HIT-RE 500 V4 or Simpson SET-3G)
- Embedment per adhesive manufacturer's specifications (typically 6-8 bar diameters)
- Must be designed by an engineer when used structurally

## Concrete Specifications

| Property | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Minimum f'c | **2,500 psi** (3,000 psi is common and preferred) |
| Slump | 4-6 inches for standard placement |
| Maximum aggregate size | 3/4" to 1" |
| Air entrainment | Not typically required in California (mild freeze-thaw) |
| Admixtures | As needed for workability; no calcium chloride if rebar present |

### Expansive Soil / Sulfate Conditions
Some cities (e.g., Laguna Niguel — city-wide expansive soils, high sulfate) require:
- **Type V cement** (sulfate-resistant)
- Minimum **4,500 psi** concrete
- Deeper footings (24"+ below grade)
- Soils report may be required

## Footing Placement

### Depth Below Grade
- **Minimum 12"** of soil cover over the top of footing
- Top of footing at or slightly above finished grade on the high side
- For frost areas: below frost line (minimal concern in most of California; <6" frost depth in nearly all areas)

### Centering
- Wall must be **centered** on the footing
- Equal footing width on each side of the wall
- Misaligned walls (wall offset on footing) create eccentric loading and can fail

### Soil Requirements
- Footing must bear on **undisturbed native soil** or **compacted engineered fill**
- Do not pour on loose fill, topsoil, organic matter, or saturated soil
- Trench bottom must be level, clean, and dry
- **Minimum soil bearing capacity assumed:** 1,500 psf (clay/silt) per CRC Table R401.4.1(1)
- Where bearing is questionable: request soils investigation

### CRC Table R401.4.1(1) — Presumptive Soil Bearing Values

| Soil Type | Bearing Value (psf) |
|---|---|
| Crystalline bedrock | 12,000 |
| Sedimentary/foliated rock | 4,000 |
| Sandy gravel, gravel (GW, GP) | 3,000 |
| Sand, silty sand, clayey sand (SW, SP, SM, SC) | 2,000 |
| Clay, sandy clay, silty clay, clayey silt (CL, ML, MH, CH) | **1,500** |
| Uncontrolled fill | **Not permitted** — must be engineered |

## Stepped Footings (Slopes)

On sloping terrain, the footing may be stepped to follow the slope:

| Requirement | Value |
|---|---|
| Each step must be level | Horizontal |
| Minimum step length | 12" |
| Maximum step height | Height of one CMU course (8" nominal) preferred |
| Rebar continuity | Footing rebar bends at each step to remain continuous |
| Wall height | Measured at each step segment from grade to top of wall |

### Step Footing Rules
1. The footing at each step must meet minimum width and depth for the wall height above it
2. Vertical dowels at each step must align with CMU cell locations
3. Steps should coincide with CMU course heights for clean block layout
4. Lower footing sections may need to be wider/deeper if overall wall height (from lowest grade) is taller

## Overturning Resistance

The footing must resist overturning from wind and seismic forces. For prescriptive designs, the footing dimensions above provide adequate resistance for standard conditions. For engineered designs:

### Overturning Moment Resistance
The resisting moment comes from:
1. **Dead load of the wall** (weight of block, grout, mortar) x distance from toe
2. **Dead load of the footing** (concrete weight) x distance from toe
3. **Soil weight on footing** (if footing has a heel extending behind the wall)

The overturning moment comes from:
1. **Wind pressure** on the wall face x height to centroid of pressure area
2. **Seismic force** (wall weight x seismic coefficient) x height to center of mass

Safety factor: **1.5 minimum** (static) or **1.1 minimum** (with seismic)

### Typical Wall Weights (for overturning calculations)

| CMU Type | Weight (psf of wall face) |
|---|---|
| 8" CMU, partially grouted (rebar cells only) | 55-60 psf |
| 8" CMU, fully grouted | 78-84 psf |
| 6" CMU, partially grouted | 40-45 psf |
| 6" CMU, fully grouted | 55-60 psf |
