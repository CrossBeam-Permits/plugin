# Energy Code & Efficiency Requirements for Water Heater Permits

## 1. Federal Appliance Standards (Baseline)

Water heaters sold in the US must meet DOE minimum efficiency standards (10 CFR 430). California additionally requires compliance with Title 20 Appliance Efficiency Regulations (CCR Title 20, §1601 et seq.). Per Part 6 §110.1(a), no appliance may be installed unless it fully complies with these regulations.

**Practical minimum UEF values (federal, effective 2015/updated 2023):**
- Gas storage, up to 55 gal: UEF 0.64 (varies by draw pattern and first-hour rating)
- Gas storage, over 55 gal: UEF 0.77 (condensing technology effectively required)
- Electric storage, up to 55 gal: UEF 0.93
- Electric storage, over 55 gal: UEF 2.00 (heat pump technology effectively required)
- Gas tankless (instantaneous): UEF 0.87
- Electric tankless: UEF 0.93

Key point for reviewers: manufacturers cannot legally sell non-compliant units in the US. If the unit has a current nameplate and appears in the CEC appliance database or an equivalent federal/trade directory (§110.1(b)), it meets minimum efficiency. The inspector verifies the nameplate data and model number.

## 2. California Energy Code Requirements for Replacements (§150.2)

Section 150.2(b) governs alterations to existing single-family residential buildings. For water heater replacements, §150.2(b)1H applies:

**Permitted replacement water heating systems:**
- (a) Natural gas or propane water heater (like-for-like gas replacement is allowed)
- (b) Heat pump water heater (HPWH) with storage tank indoors, placed on R-10 insulated surface, and equipped with a communication interface meeting §110.12(a) or ANSI/CTA-2045-B port
- (c) HPWH meeting NEEA Advanced Water Heater Specification Tier 3 or higher
- (d) If existing unit is electric resistance, a consumer electric water heater is permitted
- (e) A system determined by the Executive Director to use no more energy than option (a) above; or if no gas is connected, no more energy than option (d)

**Additional alteration requirements triggered by a water heater replacement:**
- Pipe insulation: All newly installed and existing accessible hot water piping must meet §150.0(j)1 insulation requirements (§150.2(b)1Hi)
- Recirculation systems: If a recirculation system serves an individual dwelling unit, only demand recirculation with manual on/off control is permitted (§150.2(b)1Hii)

## 3. New Construction Water Heating (§150.1(c)8)

New single-family homes must install one of the following (or comply via performance approach):
- (A) 240V heat pump water heater in garage or conditioned space, with compact distribution in CZ 1 and 16, and drain water heat recovery in CZ 16
- (B) 240V HPWH meeting NEEA Tier 3+, with drain water heat recovery in CZ 16
- (C) Solar water heating with electric backup, minimum 0.70 solar savings fraction

Exception: 120V HPWH allowed for units with one bedroom or fewer. Electric resistance with point-of-use distribution allowed for units 500 sq ft or less.

## 4. Gas-to-HPWH Readiness (§150.0(n))

When a gas or propane water heater is installed in new construction, the builder must provide a designated HPWH-ready space (at least 2.5 ft x 2.5 ft x 7 ft) with:
- 240V-capable electrical circuit (120/240V 3-conductor, 30A minimum)
- Reserved breaker space labeled "Future 240V Use"
- Condensate drain no more than 2 inches above base of water heater
- If the ready space is more than 3 ft from the water heater: dedicated cold water supply routed through the space, hot water supply routed through the space first, and exposed accessible piping

This does not apply to replacement permits directly, but reviewers should be aware of it for new construction and ADU projects.

## 6. Heat Pump Water Heaters — Key Installation Requirements

HPWHs are increasingly common in both new and replacement scenarios. Part 6 §110.3(c)7 mandates:
- **Backup heat**: Required when inlet air is unconditioned, unless compressor cut-off temp is below the Heating Winter Median of Extremes for the location (JA2 Table 2-3)
- **Ventilation**: Consumer integrated HPWHs need adequate air volume — minimum 100 cu ft per kBtu/hr of compressor capacity, or manufacturer-specified ducting/louver arrangements
- **Indoor placement**: Storage tank must not be located outdoors (per §150.2(b)1Hiiib and §150.1(c)8)
- **Insulated base**: Placed on incompressible, rigid insulated surface with minimum R-10 thermal resistance
- **Demand response**: Must have communication interface per §110.12(a) or ANSI/CTA-2045-B port

## 7. When Replacement Triggers Additional Requirements

**Simple like-for-like gas replacement:**
- Must be a compliant appliance (nameplate/CEC database)
- Pipe insulation on accessible piping (§150.0(j)1)
- If recirculation system exists, must be demand-controlled (manual on/off)
- No additional attic insulation or duct sealing triggered (those are triggered by HVAC changes, not water heater changes)

**Fuel-switching replacement (e.g., gas to heat pump):**
- All of the above, plus HPWH-specific requirements (ventilation, insulated base, communication interface)
- Electrical work requires separate electrical permit
- May require structural assessment if unit is significantly heavier

**When it does NOT trigger broader energy upgrades:**
- Unlike HVAC system replacement (which triggers duct sealing and attic insulation per §150.2(b)1E and 1J), a water heater replacement alone does not trigger envelope upgrades
- Exception: if the project includes both water heater and HVAC replacement, the HVAC triggers apply

## 8. Mandatory Equipment Requirements (§110.3)

All service water-heating equipment must meet:
- Manufacturer certification of compliance (§110.3(a))
- Efficiency per Appliance Efficiency Regulations (§110.3(b), referencing §110.1)
- Storage tank insulation: R-3.5 external, or R-16 combined internal/external, or surface heat loss below 6.5 BTU/hr/sq ft at 80F delta (§110.3(c)3)
- Circulation system controls capable of automatic shutoff (§110.3(c)2)
- Instantaneous heaters over 6.8 kBTU/hr must have isolation valves and hose bibbs for flushing (§110.3(c)6)

## 9. Practical Reviewer Guidance

For the vast majority of residential tank water heater replacements:
1. **Verify nameplate**: Confirm model appears in CEC Appliance Database or federal equivalent
2. **Check fuel type**: Gas-to-gas is straightforward; electric resistance replacement only if existing was electric resistance; HPWH always allowed
3. **Pipe insulation**: Flag if accessible hot water piping is uninsulated — it must be insulated as part of the replacement permit
4. **Recirculation**: If present, must be demand-controlled with manual on/off
5. **HPWH specifics**: If heat pump, verify ventilation, indoor placement, insulated base, and communication interface
6. **No envelope triggers**: Water heater replacement alone does not require attic insulation, duct sealing, or cool roof upgrades — those are HVAC-triggered
