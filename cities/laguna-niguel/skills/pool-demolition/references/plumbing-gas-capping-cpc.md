# CPC Plumbing & Gas Line Capping Requirements

Source: 2025 California Plumbing Code (Title 24, Part 5), Chapters 3, 6, 7, 12.

Pool demolition requires disconnecting and capping three types of plumbing/gas lines: water supply, sewer/drain, and gas. Each has specific code requirements.

---

## Gas Line Disconnection and Capping

### Authority to Disconnect (CPC 1206.1)

The Authority Having Jurisdiction or the serving gas supplier is hereby authorized to disconnect gas piping or appliance or both that shall be found not to be in accordance with the requirements of this code or that are found defective and in such condition as to endanger life or property.

### Capped Outlets (CPC 1206.3)

It shall be unlawful to remove or disconnect gas piping or gas appliance without capping or plugging with a screw joint fitting, the outlet from which said pipe or appliance was removed. Outlets to which gas appliances are not connected shall be left capped and gastight on a piping system that has been installed, altered, or repaired.

Exception: Where an approved listed quick-disconnect device is used.

### Cap Outlets at Installation Points (CPC 1210.8.1)

Each outlet, including a valve, shall be closed gastight with a threaded plug or cap immediately after installation and shall be left closed until the appliance or equipment is connected thereto. When an appliance or equipment is disconnected from an outlet and the outlet is not to be used again immediately, it shall be capped or plugged gastight.

> **Pool demo application**: The gas line serving the pool heater must be disconnected and capped at BOTH the gas meter and the equipment location. The cap must be a screw joint fitting and must be gastight. This is a two-point capping requirement -- at the source (meter) and at the removed equipment location. The gas utility should be notified.

## Water Supply Line Capping

### General Water Supply (CPC 601.1, 601.2)

Each plumbing fixture shall be provided with an adequate supply of potable running water. The water supply system must be maintained to prevent cross-connections and backflow.

### Dead Legs (CPC 309.6)

Dead legs shall have a method of flushing.

> **Pool demo application**: When the water supply line to the pool fill valve or autofill is disconnected, it must be capped to prevent leaks and maintain system pressure. The water line should be capped at the nearest appropriate point -- typically at or near the main supply branch. A shut-off valve should be closed and the line downstream capped with an appropriate fitting. The capped line becomes a dead leg and should have a method of flushing, or preferably the line should be removed back to the nearest active branch and capped there.

## Sewer/Drain Line Capping

### Sanitary Drainage (CPC 701.1)

The sanitary drainage system governs materials, design, and installation. All fixtures connected to the drainage system must be properly connected or disconnected.

### Damage to Drainage System (CPC 305.1)

It shall be unlawful for a person to deposit, by any means whatsoever, into a plumbing fixture, floor drain, interceptor, sump, receptor, or device, which is connected to a drainage system, public sewer, private sewer, septic tank, or cesspool, any ashes; cinders; solids; rags; inflammable, poisonous, or explosive liquids or gases; oils; grease; or any other thing whatsoever that is capable of causing damage to the drainage system or public sewer.

> **Pool demo application**: The pool drain line connecting to the sanitary sewer must be properly capped. This is typically done at the building sewer connection point. The cap must prevent sewer gas from escaping and prevent debris from entering the sewer system. A mechanical plug or permanent cap fitting is required. This inspection is commonly called the "sewer cap" inspection.

## Trenching and Backfill for Plumbing (CPC 314.4)

Excavations shall be completely backfilled as soon after inspection as practicable. Precaution shall be taken to ensure compactness of backfill around piping without damage to such piping. Trenches shall be backfilled in thin layers to 12 inches above the top of the piping with clean earth, which shall not contain stones, boulders, cinder fill, frozen earth, construction debris, or other materials that will damage or break the piping or cause corrosive action.

> **Pool demo application**: Where plumbing lines are being capped and trenches are involved, the backfill around the capped pipe must be clean earth placed in thin layers. This applies to the gas line trench, water line trench, and sewer line trench where they are exposed during pool demolition.

## Pool Water Drainage Requirements

Pool water discharge is governed by local NPDES (National Pollutant Discharge Elimination System) requirements and the State Water Resources Control Board:

1. **De-chlorinated water**: Pool water must be de-chlorinated before discharge. De-chlorinated water may be discharged on-site by percolation or into the storm drainage system.
2. **Chlorinated water**: Chlorinated or contaminated water must NOT be discharged to the storm drain. It must be transported off-site to an approved containment facility.
3. **Cleaning wastewater**: Filter backwash and cleaning wastewater are NOT permitted to be drained to storm water.
4. **Debris-free**: Pool water must be free of debris and contaminants before draining.

> **Key principle for pool water**: De-chlorinated = OK for storm drain. Chlorinated or contaminated = must go off-site. This is an NPDES/BMP compliance item, not a plumbing code item, but it is inspected as part of the pool demolition process.

## Summary of Capping Requirements

| Utility | Cap Location(s) | Method | Code Section |
|---------|-----------------|--------|-------------|
| **Gas line** | Both at meter AND at equipment location | Screw joint fitting, gastight | CPC 1206.3, 1210.8.1 |
| **Water supply** | Nearest active branch or supply point | Appropriate cap fitting, shut-off valve closed | CPC 601.1, 309.6 |
| **Sewer/drain** | Building sewer connection point | Mechanical plug or permanent cap, prevent sewer gas | CPC 305.1, 701.1 |
| **Electrical** | Removed back to service supply (see electrical reference) | N/A -- removal, not capping | CEC 680.13, CBC 3303.6 |
