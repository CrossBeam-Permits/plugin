## Plan-Check Checklist (applies to both modes)

Every item here is verified at plan-check time. Severity drives reviewer-mode output.

| # | Check | Severity | What to verify | Code |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Permit required?** | ❌ **fail** | Height >4 ft (footing to top), or any height with surcharge, or impounding flammable liquids. Measure from bottom of footing to top of wall, not exposed face only. | CRC R105.2 |
| 2 | **Engineering required?** | ❌ **fail** | Walls >4 ft, surcharge loads, adverse soils, slopes above or below, tiered walls within influence zone, walls supporting structures/driveways, walls near ROW or property lines. Must be designed by CA licensed civil/structural engineer or architect. | CBC 1807.2 |
| 3 | **Soils report provided?** | ❌ **fail** | Required when: adverse soil conditions (expansive, liquefiable, fill), slopes, or per AHJ. Report must include allowable bearing pressure, lateral soil parameters, groundwater level, and recommendations. | CBC 1803.2, 1803.5.3 |
| 4 | **Lateral soil loads calculated?** | ❌ **fail** | Design for lateral earth pressure using equivalent fluid method or Rankine/Coulomb. Must account for at-rest vs active conditions, soil type, groundwater, and surcharge. Seismic lateral earth pressure for walls >6 ft in SDC D/E/F. | CBC 1807.2.2, 1610; ASCE 7-22 |
| 5 | **Surcharge loads accounted for?** | ❌ **fail** | Identify all surcharges: vehicles/driveways, buildings, slopes, stored materials, adjacent walls. Surcharge adds equivalent height to lateral pressure calculation. Typical: 2 ft equivalent for residential driveways, 4 ft for heavy vehicles. | CBC 1807.2.2 |
| 6 | **Stability analysis adequate?** | ❌ **fail** | Check safety factors: overturning >=1.5 (1.1 with seismic), sliding >=1.5 (1.1 with seismic). Bearing pressure within allowable. Uses 0.7x nominal earthquake loads + 1.0x other nominal loads. Standard load combinations of CBC 1605 do NOT apply to retaining wall stability. | CBC 1807.2.3 |
| 7 | **Footing design adequate?** | ❌ **fail** | Footing width, depth, reinforcement, and key (if provided). Footing below frost line (minimal in most CA) and minimum 12" below lower grade. Bearing area sufficient for soil capacity. | CBC 1807.2, 1809 |
| 8 | **Wall stem design adequate?** | ❌ **fail** | Stem thickness, vertical and horizontal reinforcement, concrete or masonry strength, dowel connections to footing. CMU walls: cells grouted, bond beams provided. | CBC 1807.2; ACI 318 / TMS 402 |
| 9 | **Drainage system shown?** | ❌ **fail** | Drainage behind wall is critical. Verify: drain rock (12" min width), perforated drain pipe at base (4" min, sloped to daylight), filter fabric between drain rock and native soil. Weep holes as supplemental (4" dia, 6-8 ft OC). Surface drainage graded away from wall top. | Engineering practice; CBC 1805 |
| 10 | **Waterproofing addressed?** | ⚠️ **flag** | Below-grade portions: dampproofing or waterproofing per CBC 1805 where wall retains habitable space or where required by design. Waterproofing membrane on soil side of wall below grade. | CBC 1805 |
| 11 | **Guards/railings required?** | ❌ **fail** | Guards required where retaining wall is within 36" of a walking surface AND the drop exceeds 30" from the walking surface to grade below. Guard height: 42" min above walking surface. Openings: 4" sphere test. Loads: 200 lb concentrated at top. | CBC 1807.2.5; CRC R312.1 |
| 12 | **Material specifications shown?** | ❌ **fail** | Concrete: f'c >= 2,500 psi (typical 3,000). CMU: ASTM C90. Mortar: Type M or S. Grout: f'c >= 2,000 psi. Rebar: ASTM A615 Grade 60. SRW blocks: ASTM C1372. Timber: pressure-treated per AWPA U1. | CBC 1904, 2103; ASTM |
| 13 | **Tiered walls analyzed correctly?** | ❌ **fail** | If multiple walls, check spacing. Influence zone = 2x height of lower wall measured from base of lower wall. Walls within influence zone must be analyzed as a single composite wall. Walls outside influence zone may be designed independently. | CBC 1807.2 |
| 14 | **Grading plan consistent?** | ⚠️ **flag** | If retaining wall is part of a grading project, verify consistency with grading plan. Grading permits per CBC Appendix J when cut/fill exceeds thresholds (typically 50 cu yd or 5 ft depth of cut). | CBC Appendix J |
| 15 | **Contractor licensing adequate?** | ❌ **fail** | B (General Building) or C-8 (Concrete) for concrete walls. C-29 (Masonry) for CMU walls. C-12 (Earthwork/Paving) for grading. A (General Engineering) for walls with engineered grading. | B&P Code 7028 |
| 1 | **Engineer of record — walls >4 ft** `[SCMC]` | ⚠️ **flag** | Walls with exposed height greater than 4 ft require structural calculations prepared and wet-stamped by a California-licensed civil or structural engineer; applies to both masonry and cast-in-place concrete walls |  |
| 2 | **Seismic Design Category D** `[SCMC]` | ⚠️ **flag** | San Clemente is in SDC D (coastal Orange County); seismic analysis must reflect SDC D parameters in accordance with 2025 CBC Chapter 16; provide geotechnical report with site-specific soil parameters for engineered walls |  |
| 3 | **Grading coordination — Ch. 15.36** `[SCMC]` | ⚠️ **flag** | Walls that retain significant soil cuts or are associated with overall site grading work may also trigger a separate grading permit from Public Works under SCMC Ch. 15.36; confirm at pre-submittal whether the scope requires concurrent grading permit issuance — city Building Division and Public Works Land Development coordinate on combined grading/retaining wall projects |  |
| 4 | **Masonry vs. cast-in-place** `[SCMC]` | ⚠️ **flag** | Both CMU masonry and cast-in-place concrete retaining walls are permitted; engineer of record is required for walls >4 ft exposed height regardless of material type |  |
| 5 | **Coastal Zone — CDP required** `[SCMC]` | ⚠️ **flag** | Retaining walls located within the California Coastal Zone that alter topography or drainage require a Coastal Development Permit (CDP) from the California Coastal Commission (CCC) — the city cannot issue CDPs until the LCP Implementation Plan is fully certified (~2026); obtain CCC CDP before city building permit for affected walls; confirm parcel's Coastal Zone status at coastal.ca.gov before submittal |  |
| 6 | **Coastal Zone — new walls** `[SCMC]` | ⚠️ **flag** | New retaining walls that create new topographic alteration (not maintenance of an existing wall in-kind) are subject to CDP review; PRC §30610 exemptions for "improvements to existing SFR" typically do not cover new retaining walls in different locations or with expanded dimensions — verify exemption applicability with CCC prior to submittal |  |
| 7 | **VHFHSZ — hillside areas** `[SCMC]` | ⚠️ **flag** | Retaining walls in Very High Fire Hazard Severity Zones (Talega, Forster Ranch, inland canyon areas) are subject to OCFA Fuel Modification requirements; combustible landscaping setback from the structure applies per the approved Fuel Mod Plan; verify VHFHSZ status via CAL FIRE or OC GIS |  |
| 8 | **Specific Plans — Forster Ranch / Talega** `[SCMC]` | ⚠️ **flag** | Parcels within the Forster Ranch and Talega Specific Plans may have supplemental grading and retaining wall design standards beyond Title 17 base zoning; review the applicable Specific Plan document for additional height limits, material requirements, or slope grading standards; confirm Specific Plan status for all hillside parcels at intake |  |
| 9 | **3-set paper submittal** `[SCMC]` | ⚠️ **flag** | Retaining wall permits require 3 sets of plans submitted in person or by mail to 910 Calle Negocio, Suite 100; electronic plan submission via email (building@san-clemente.org) or eTRAKiT should be confirmed with the Building Division as current policy — in-person or mail is the documented baseline |  |
| 10 | **Geotechnical report** `[SCMC]` | ⚠️ **flag** | Site-specific geotechnical/soils report required for walls >4 ft exposed height or walls associated with grading; report must address bearing capacity, lateral earth pressure, seismic loads (SDC D), and drainage recommendations; engineer of record must incorporate geotechnical recommendations into structural design |  |
| 11 | **Drainage and waterproofing** `[SCMC]` | ⚠️ **flag** | Plans must show positive drainage behind the wall; waterproofing and weep holes required per CBC; walls adjacent to structures require additional drainage detail — include on structural plans |  |
| 12 | **Plan check — no OTC** `[SCMC]` | ⚠️ **flag** | Retaining wall permits require plan check review; over-the-counter (OTC) issuance is not available for retaining wall permits; standard plan review turnaround applies — confirm current review times with Building Division |  |
| 13 | **Inspections** `[SCMC]` | ⚠️ **flag** | Footing, reinforcing steel (prior to concrete pour), and final inspections required; schedule via eTRAKiT portal or by phone; do not pour concrete until reinforcing inspection is approved |  |
