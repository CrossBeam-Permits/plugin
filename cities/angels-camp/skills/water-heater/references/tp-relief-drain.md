# T&P Relief Valve, Discharge Pipe, Drain Pan & Expansion Tank

## 1. Temperature & Pressure (T&P) Relief Valve

**Code basis:** CPC 504.4, 504.5, 608.3, 608.4

- A **pressure relief device** shall be installed at the water heater (CPC 504.4).
- A **temperature limiting device** shall also be installed (CPC 504.5).
- The temperature probe shall be installed in the **top 6 inches** of water storage (CPC 504.5).
- A combination temperature-and-pressure-relief (T&P) valve is **required on every storage water heater**, regardless of fuel type (gas, electric, oil, heat pump).
- **Exception:** Listed non-storage (tankless/instantaneous) water heaters with an inside diameter of 3 inches or less are exempt from the T&P requirement (CPC 608.3).
- The T&P valve must be ASME-rated and listed by an approved testing agency.
- Temperature limiting device shall open at: **150 PSI** or **210 deg F** (CPC 608.4).
- Must be installed in a location specified by the manufacturer's installation instructions and its listing.
- **No shutoff valve or check valve** shall be installed before or after the T&P valve (CPC 504.6, 608.4).
- Storage water heaters using fuels other than gas must also have an over-temperature safety protection device in addition to the T&P valve (CPC 505.2).

### Reviewer checklist
- T&P valve shown on plans and called out with rating.
- Valve location matches manufacturer specs (typically top or upper side of tank).
- Temperature probe in top 6 inches of tank.
- No isolation/shutoff/check valve between T&P and tank or after T&P.

## 2. Discharge Pipe Requirements

**Code basis:** CPC 608.5

The discharge pipe from a T&P valve must comply with **all** of the following:

| Requirement | Code detail |
|---|---|
| Requirement | Code detail |
|---|---|
| **Size** | Min 3/4 inch; not less than the valve outlet size; must discharge full size to the flood level (CPC 608.5(1)). No reduction in pipe diameter allowed. |
| **Material** | Rated for the operating temperature of the system. Must comply with ASME A112.4.1 (CPC 608.5(3)). Hard-drawn copper or galvanized pipe are typical. |
| **Termination height** | Discharge end must be **6 to 24 inches above the floor or finished grade**, pointing downward (CPC 608.5(2)). |
| **Gravity flow** | Must discharge independently by gravity through an air gap into drainage system or outside the building (CPC 608.5(2)). |
| **No trapping** | No part of the pipe shall be trapped or subject to freezing; shall not run uphill (CPC 608.5(3&5)). |
| **No threading** | Terminal end of the pipe shall **not be threaded** (CPC 608.5(6)). |
| **Observable** | Discharge termination point must be readily observable (CPC 608.5(8)). |
| **No valves or obstructions** | No shutoff or check valves shall be installed before or after the T&P valve (CPC 504.6). No valves, obstructions, or means of isolation in discharge piping. |
| **No injury or damage** | Must discharge in a manner that does not cause personal injury or structural damage (CPC 608.5(4)). |
| **Not into drain pan** | Discharge from a relief valve into a water heater pan is **prohibited** (CPC 608.5(7)). |
| **Not into crawlspace** | Drainage shall not terminate at crawl space (CPC 608.5(3)). |
| **Independent piping** | Each T&P valve shall be piped independently; shall NOT be shared with condensate drain or relief valves of other systems (CPC 608.5(7)). |
| **End pointing down** | End of pipe shall be pointing down (CPC 608.5(1)). |

### Acceptable discharge locations
- **Exterior** -- to grade, 6-24 inches above ground, pointing down.
- **Floor drain** -- through an air gap into the drainage system.
- **Indirect waste receptor** -- per CPC Chapter 8 indirect waste rules.
- **NOT** directly connected to the sanitary sewer (must have air gap).

### Common deficiencies to flag
- Discharge pipe reduced in size (e.g., 3/4 inch valve to 1/2 inch pipe).
- Pipe terminates into a wall cavity, crawl space, or concealed area.
- Pipe routed upward creating a trap or has a low point that could trap water.
- Terminal end is threaded or capped.
- Pipe discharges into the drain pan (explicitly prohibited).
- Discharge directed where it could scald occupants or damage finishes.

## 3. Drain Pan Requirements

**Code basis:** CMC 305.5

### When required
A watertight drain pan is required beneath the water heater when it is located:
- In an **attic** or on an attic-ceiling assembly.
- On a **floor-ceiling assembly** or **floor-subfloor assembly** (i.e., above living space).
- **Anywhere leakage would cause damage** to the structure or finish below.

### When NOT required
- Water heater on a **garage slab** or ground-floor concrete slab where leakage drains harmlessly.
- Exterior installation where water drains to grade.

### Pan specifications
| Requirement | Value |
|---|---|
| Material | Corrosion-resistant (galvanized steel, aluminum, plastic) |
| Minimum depth | 1-1/2 inches (38 mm) |
| Drain pipe diameter | Not less than 3/4 inch (20 mm) |
| Drain termination | Approved location; terminating end must be readily visible |
| Pipe material | Rated for the temperature of the water heater |

### Critical prohibition
- **Discharge from the T&P relief valve into the drain pan is prohibited** (CMC 305.5(4) and CPC 608.5(7)). The T&P discharge pipe and the pan drain are two separate systems.

## 4. Expansion Tank

**Code basis:** CPC 608.2, 608.3

### When required
An expansion tank (or approved equivalent device) is **required** whenever the water system is a "closed system" -- meaning thermal expansion cannot dissipate back into the water main. This occurs when any of the following are installed on the supply side:
- **Check valve**
- **Backflow preventer** (RP, DC, or PVB assembly)
- **Pressure regulator** (CPC 608.2 -- a PRV creates a de facto closed system)
- Any other **normally closed device** that prevents backflow

CPC 608.2 specifically states: "An approved expansion tank shall be installed in the cold water distribution piping downstream of each such regulator to prevent pressure from exceeding 80 psi due to thermal expansion."

**Exception:** An expansion tank is not required for an instantaneous (tankless) non-storage water heater (CPC 608.3 Exception).

### When NOT required
- Open system with no check valve, backflow preventer, or PRV -- thermal expansion can push back into the city main.
- Tankless/instantaneous heaters (they store minimal water volume).

### Installation requirements
| Requirement | Detail |
|---|---|
| Location | On the **cold water supply** line, building side of the check valve, backflow preventer, or PRV |
| Sizing | Adequately sized for the water heater capacity per manufacturer tables |
| Mounting | Securely fastened to the structure |
| Standard | Must comply with IAPMO/ANSI Z1088 (pre-pressurized tanks) |
| Potable rating | Must comply with NSF/ANSI/CAN 61 for potable water systems |
| Installation | Per manufacturer's installation instructions and listing |

### Alternative to expansion tank
A licensed plumbing contractor or registered design professional may specify an approved **pressure relief valve** (set at max 100 psi) in lieu of an expansion tank (CPC 608.2). This is uncommon in residential work.

### Practical guidance for reviewers
- If plans show a PRV at the meter or a backflow preventer, **require an expansion tank** on the cold water line near the water heater.
- Most California homes built after ~2000 have PRVs; assume a closed system unless plans clearly show otherwise.
- The expansion tank must be on the cold side upstream of the water heater, not on the hot side.
- Common sizes: 2-gallon tank for up to 50-gallon water heaters, 4.5-gallon for 80-gallon units. Always verify against manufacturer sizing charts.
