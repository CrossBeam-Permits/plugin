# Ductwork Requirements

Source: 2025 California Mechanical Code (Title 24, Part 4), Chapter 6; 2025 California Energy Code (Title 24, Part 6), Sections 150.0(m) and 150.2(b).

## Duct Sizing (CMC 601.2)

Residential duct systems shall be sized in accordance with:
- **ACCA Manual D** (supply duct sizing)
- **ACCA Manual Zr** (as applicable for zoned systems)
- Or other approved methods

### Return Duct Sizing (CMC 601.2.1)

California Energy Code Tables 150.0-B and 150.0-C specify return duct sizing requirements for single-return and multiple-return systems.

## Duct Materials

### Metallic Ducts (CMC 602.4)

Metal ducts shall comply with **SMACNA HVAC Duct Construction Standards**. Flexible metallic ducts shall comply with **UL 181**.

### Flexible Air Ducts (CMC 603.4)

Flexible air ducts shall comply with UL 181 and be installed per manufacturer's instructions and SMACNA standards.

Key installation rules:
1. Use minimum required length to make connection
2. Horizontal runs supported at not more than **4 ft intervals**
3. Vertical risers supported at not more than **6 ft intervals**
4. Sag between supports shall not exceed **1/2 in. per foot** of support spacing
5. Supports shall be rigid and not less than **1-1/2 in. wide** at contact
6. Bends shall be not less than **one duct diameter** bend radius
7. Inner liner installed at least 1 in. on the collar and past the bead
8. Outer vapor barrier secured with two wraps of approved tape

### Residential Exception (CMC 603.4.1)

Flexible air ducts in non-residential settings shall be no more than 5 ft in length. **Exception: Residential occupancies** (no length limitation, but minimum length is always required).

## Duct Sealing (CMC 603.9)

All ducts shall be sealed to **Seal Class A** regardless of pressure classification.

Joints and seams shall be made substantially airtight using:
- Tapes (UL 181A or 181B listed)
- Mastics
- Gasketing
- Other approved means

Crimp joints for round ducts: minimum 1-1/2 in. contact lap with at least 3 sheet-metal screws.

## Duct Insulation (CMC 605.1)

Air ducts conveying air at temperatures exceeding 140 deg F shall be insulated to limit surface temperature to 140 deg F.

### Energy Code Duct Insulation for Alterations (Table 150.2-D)

| Climate Zone | Minimum Duct R-Value |
|-------------|---------------------|
| 3, 5 through 7 | R-6 |
| 1, 2, 4, 8 through 16 | R-8 |

### New/Replacement FAU Requirement

When an FAU is replaced, ducts (existing and/or new) require:
- **R-6 minimum duct insulation** (Energy Code 150.2(b)1E reference)
- A **duct leakage test** is required

### New Ducting > 40 ft Trigger

On existing systems, if more than **40 linear feet** of new ducting is installed during a replacement or extension project:
- Minimum insulation of R-6 on new ducting (per Table 150.2-D)
- Duct leakage test required

## Duct Leakage Testing (Energy Code 150.2(b))

### When Equipment is Replaced (150.2(b)1E)

When a space-conditioning system is altered by replacement of equipment (air handler, outdoor condensing unit, or coil), the connected duct system shall be sealed and tested:

| Compliance Path | Leakage Limit |
|----------------|--------------|
| Total duct leakage | <= 10% of system air handler airflow |
| Duct leakage to outside | <= 7% of system air handler airflow |
| Visual + smoke test (if above cannot be met) | All accessible leaks sealed, verified by ECC-Rater |

**Exceptions to duct sealing on equipment replacement**:
1. Ducts previously tested and documented as sealed
2. Duct systems with less than 40 linear feet
3. Existing ducts constructed/insulated/sealed with asbestos

### Entirely New or Replacement Duct System (150.2(b)1Diia)

| Compliance Path | Leakage Limit |
|----------------|--------------|
| New/replacement duct system | <= 5% of system air handler airflow |

### Ducts in Garage Spaces (150.2(b)1Diic)

When ducts, air-handling units, coils, or plenums are located in a garage:
- System duct leakage <= 6% of air handler airflow; OR
- All accessible leaks in garage sealed, verified by ECC-Rater

## Furnace Plenums and Air Ducts (CMC 604.0)

### Return Air (CMC 604.4)

Where a furnace supplies air to areas outside the space containing the furnace, return air shall also be handled by a duct sealed to the furnace casing and terminating outside the furnace space. **Return air shall not be taken from the mechanical room** containing the furnace.

### Supply Duct Clearance to Combustibles (CMC 603.13.2)

Supply air ducts connecting to listed central heating furnaces shall have the same minimum clearance to combustibles as the furnace supply plenum for a distance of not less than **3 ft from the supply plenum**.
