# CBC Demolition & Structural Requirements

Source: 2025 California Building Code (Title 24, Part 2), Chapters 33, 18, 17, and Appendix J.

---

## Demolition General Requirements (CBC Section 3303)

### 3303.1 Construction Documents

Construction documents and a schedule for demolition shall be submitted where required by the building official. Where such information is required, work shall not be done until such construction documents or schedule, or both, are approved.

### 3303.4 Vacant Lot

Where a structure has been demolished or removed, the vacant lot shall be filled and maintained to the existing grade or in accordance with the ordinances of the jurisdiction having authority.

> **Pool demo application**: After the pool shell is partially demolished (coping/bond beam removed, drainage holes punched), the excavation must be backfilled and the area restored to grade. The site cannot be left as an open hole.

### 3303.5 Water Accumulation

Provision shall be made to prevent the accumulation of water or damage to any foundations on the premises or on adjacent property.

> **Pool demo application**: Drainage holes in the pool shell bottom and a gravel drainage layer are the standard methods to comply with this section. The backfilled area must drain properly and not cause water accumulation that could damage the house foundation or neighboring properties.

### 3303.6 Utility Connections

Service utility connections shall be discontinued and capped in accordance with the approved rules and the requirements of the applicable governing authority.

> **Pool demo application**: This is the governing section requiring that all pool utilities -- electrical, gas, water, and sewer -- be properly disconnected and capped. This applies to the pool pump/heater electrical sub-panel, gas line to the pool heater, water supply lines, and drain/sewer connections.

## Safeguards During Construction (CBC Section 3301, 3304)

### 3301.2 Storage and Placement of Construction Equipment and Materials

Construction equipment and materials shall be stored and placed so as not to endanger the public, the workers or adjoining property for the duration of the construction project.

### 3301.5 Removal of Waste Materials

Waste materials shall be removed in a manner that prevents injury or damage to persons, adjacent properties and public rights-of-way.

> **Pool demo application**: Demolished concrete, rebar, coping, and bond beam debris must be removed safely. Demolition debris from coping and bond beam may be placed on the gravel layer in the pool bottom prior to backfilling, provided pieces do not exceed 3 inches per standard practice.

### 3304.1 Excavation and Fill

Excavation and fill for buildings and structures shall be constructed or protected so as not to endanger life or property.

### 3304.1.5 Storm Water Drainage During Construction [CA] [HCD 1]

Projects which disturb less than one acre of soil and are not part of a larger common plan of development which in total disturbs one acre or more, shall manage storm water drainage during construction in accordance with the California Green Building Standards Code (CALGreen), Chapter 4, Division 4.1.

> **Pool demo application**: Pool demolition sites typically disturb less than one acre. BMP (Best Management Practice) erosion controls are required during construction -- silt fencing, gravel bags, etc. to prevent sediment from entering storm drains.

## Backfill Placement (CBC Section 1804)

### 1804.3 Placement of Backfill

The excavation outside the foundation shall be backfilled with soil that is free of organic material, construction debris, cobbles and boulders or with a controlled low-strength material (CLSM). The backfill shall be placed in lifts and compacted in a manner that does not damage the foundation or the waterproofing or dampproofing material.

Exception: CLSM need not be compacted.

> **Pool demo application**: Backfill material for pool demolition must be clean fill -- free of organic material and construction debris. Standard practice is self-compacting fill sand (SE25 type), clean fill dirt, or material specified in a soils report. Fill is placed in lifts (typically 6-inch lifts) and compacted. The upper portion (typically 18 inches) may use planting medium for landscaping.

### 1804.4 Site Grading

The ground immediately adjacent to the foundation shall be sloped away from the building at a slope of not less than 1 unit vertical in 20 units horizontal (5-percent slope) for a minimum distance of 10 feet measured perpendicular to the face of the wall.

Exception 1: Where climatic or soil conditions warrant, the slope of the ground away from the building foundation shall be permitted to be reduced to not less than 1 unit vertical in 48 units horizontal (2-percent slope).

> **Pool demo application**: After backfill, the finished grade must slope away from the house at minimum 2% (California exception). The backfilled pool area must be graded to re-establish proper drainage away from all existing structures.

### 1804.6 Compacted Fill Material

Where shallow foundations will bear on compacted fill material, the compacted fill shall comply with the provisions of an approved geotechnical report, as set forth in Section 1803.

Exception: Compacted fill material 12 inches (305 mm) in depth or less need not comply with an approved report, provided that the in-place dry density is not less than 90 percent of the maximum dry density at optimum moisture content determined in accordance with ASTM D1557. The compaction shall be verified by special inspection in accordance with Section 1705.6.

> **Pool demo application**: Pool backfill exceeds 12 inches, so a geotechnical report or soil compaction report is typically required. Standard practice is 90-95% compaction per ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor). A soil compaction report from a licensed geotechnical engineer is required at final inspection.

## Special Inspections for Soils (CBC Section 1705.6)

### 1705.6 Soils

Special inspections and tests of existing site soil conditions, fill placement and load-bearing requirements shall be performed in accordance with this section and Table 1705.6. The approved geotechnical report and the construction documents prepared by the registered design professionals shall be used to determine compliance.

Exception: Where Section 1803 does not require reporting of materials and procedures for fill placement, the special inspector shall verify that the in-place dry density of the compacted fill is not less than 90 percent of the maximum dry density at optimum moisture content determined in accordance with ASTM D1557.

Table 1705.6 requires:
- Periodic inspection to verify excavations reach proper depth and material
- Periodic inspection to classify and test compacted fill materials
- Continuous inspection during fill placement to verify proper materials, procedures, densities, and lift thicknesses
- Periodic inspection prior to placement to verify subgrade preparation

> **Pool demo application**: For pool demolition, a soil compaction test (typically 90-95% per Modified Proctor / ASTM D1557) is the standard requirement. The compaction report must be available at final inspection.

## Fill Material and Compaction (Appendix J)

### J107.4 Fill Material

Fill material shall not include organic, frozen or other deleterious materials. Rock or similar irreducible material greater than 12 inches (305 mm) in any dimension shall not be included in fills.

> **Pool demo application**: Backfill cannot contain organic material, and no rock/debris pieces larger than 12 inches. Demolished bond beam and coping fragments placed on the gravel layer should not exceed 3 inches per standard practice (more conservative than the 12-inch code limit for general fills).

### J107.5 Compaction

All fill material shall be compacted to 90 percent of maximum density as determined by ASTM D1557, Modified Proctor, in lifts not exceeding 12 inches (305 mm) in depth.

> **Pool demo application**: This is the baseline compaction standard -- 90% Modified Proctor in 12-inch lifts maximum. Many jurisdictions require 90-95% for pool demolition. Hand compaction equipment on 6-inch lifts with prudent use of water is the standard method.

## Permits (CBC Section 105)

### 105.1 Required

Any owner or owner's authorized agent who intends to construct, enlarge, alter, repair, move, demolish or change the occupancy of a building or structure, or to erect, install, enlarge, alter, repair, remove, convert or replace any electrical, gas, mechanical or plumbing system, the installation of which is regulated by this code, or to cause any such work to be performed, shall first make application to the building official and obtain the required permit.

> **Pool demo application**: A demolition permit is required for pool/spa removal. This is NOT exempt work under 105.2. The permit covers structural demolition, utility disconnection, backfill, and grading.
